Course Description

SYDE 599:

This course deals with selected topics at the undergraduate level in environmental and societal systems, mechatronics and physical systems, or human factors and ergonomics.

View requirements for SYDE 599

In July 2024, it was announced that the new regional hospital for Kitchener-Waterloo, a joint project of the Grand River Hospital and St. Mary’s General Hospital, would be located on north campus of the University of Waterloo (https://futureofcaretogether.ca/our-new-hospital/).  More than a physical state-of-the-art hospital is envisioned.  Transformational innovation in healthcare that embodies community, education, research and ultimately the complete tenets of sustainability is desired.  The coalition planning the hospital is in the functional programming phase.  The site plan is not yet determined.

One overarching goal of the coalition is to gather student conceptual designs that embody sustainability.  This class is structured as a collaborative forum within which students will undertake robust explorations of both sustainability concepts and hospital/health systems. 

Three frameworks or tools ground the course in systems design engineering: 

  • defining sustainability (with metrics); 
  • systems thinking (with mapping); and 
  • structured design methods with a strong focus on problem definition and evaluation of alternative designs.  

Students will develop and critically evaluate alternative site designs within the context of facilitating hospital sustainability.  Students will focus on one of the following aspects of sustainability:

  • access and transportation; 
  • green energy; or 
  • integrated well-being with the surrounding community.

Together as a learning collaborative, students, with the instructor, will contribute documentation to that will focus on the hospital through several products:

  • Co-laboratory definitions of sustainability and hospital systems;
  • Notes on design elements raised by class lectures and invitedspeakers;
  • Summaries and reviews of hospital sustainability literature; 
  • Short design reports of alternative hospital site designs; and
  • Posters for stakeholder review.

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Identify and conceptualize the role of a hospital system in a community and region.
Appreciate the complexity of defining sustainability for a hospital, a complex social technical system.
Use terminology in sustainable planning and design.
Clearly define the hospital site as a design problem with a sustainability-based objective.
Identify multiple conceptual design alternatives for the Waterloo hospital site and evaluate the extent to which each meets sustainability goals and objectives using metrics.
Clearly articulate a position or argument with associated justification, evidence or data.
Use systems thinking to frame a complex problem and communicate the elements of a system using mapping.
Work effectively in a collaborative team environment by co-developing definitions, documents, and system representations of a complex system.
Practice effective communications with professional and community stakeholders.

This is not a typical course.  There are no homework problems, mid-terms, exams or even a standard class project. We will work together on a real-world problem to learn, think critically and design alternative solutions for which real-world stakeholders want our input.  As such, marks are accumulated by: a) participating, b) contributing, and c) completing the assignment.  The best marks are earned when contributions are a) creative/comprehensive, b) grounded in sound arguments, both technical and non-technical, and c) effective in terms of communication approach (graphic, oral or written).

Grading rubrics are provided with assignments because in real life when you write a proposal for a contract or grant, the evaluation metrics or “point” system is usually provided.

Some assignments may be completed in pairs.  As pairs can be self-selected and change over the term, all deliverables must be uploaded by both people in order for the grade book to be managed.

Marks are not adjusted or curved in this course.  It is feasible for everyone to get 100% or for everyone to fail – both highly unlikely. It is possible to skip one co-note exercise without penalty (this is why points add to 102 in the class Schedule). 

Late work is in general never accepted out of respect for those who make the due date.  In the event of illness or emergency as defined by Faculty and University policy, extensions may be permitted or deliverables excluded.  Note that the rapid return of marked work and the compilation of individual contributions into joint documents used by the whole class requires a commitment to each other for timely completion of deliverables.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
