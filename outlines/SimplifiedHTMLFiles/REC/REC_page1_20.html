Course Description

REC 420:

Students will acquire knowledge and skills to plan and conduct evaluations of leisure services and programs. The course will cover pre-evaluation, needs assessment, goals and outcomes, evaluation designs, process and outcome evaluation, report preparation, evaluation politics, and ethics.

View requirements for REC 420

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Design and conduct needs assessments.
Carry out an evaluability assessment of an agency or service.
Design and apply logic models in the planning of evaluations.
Design and conduct program evaluations using a variety of information gathering
Identify problems with program performance and diagnose theory and program failures.
Write an evaluation report and recommendations.
Develop and implement utilization strategies for making the results of program evaluations relevant, known, and used by stakeholders.
Identify the appropriate behaviours and ethics required of an evaluator when dealing with agencies, stakeholders, and service participants.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Case Studies 90%
Intersectionality Learning Experience 10%

1) Case Studies (90%)                                                                                                                      

** Group Assignments 10% each (Case studies assigned on January 20, 27, February 3, 10, March 3, 10, 17, 24)                     

  *Final Individual Assignment 10% (March 31)                              

This course is comprised of online recorded lectures and in-person tutorials. During each week, a recorded lecture will be posted to Learn (typically on Monday afternoon). During the following tutorial, the class will be given a brief case that everyone must address. Your job will be to apply insights gathered from the recorded lecture and in the readings to resolve issues identified in the case. This process is intended to help you integrate materials we have covered in class. On the dates listed above, you will have devoted class time to work on your assigned tasks with the instructor on hand to offer any guidance you might require. For all but one case studies (i.e., March 31), students will discuss their case response with their fellow group members. The group then uses insight from all its members’ reports to create an ultimate response to the case. This group response is submitted on LEARN (one submission per group) prior to Friday at 11:59pm. A grade is assigned to the group report. This process protects the individual student because responses have all been edited, enhanced, and approved by several classmates prior to submission. The only exception to this process is March 31 where a final individual assignment will be submitted. 

Following each assignment, groups will have the opportunity to submit a mark redistribution form. This form is only required of those groups whose members collectively agree work was not distributed fairly within their group. If your group members collectively indicate that you did not participate fully in the group submissions, you will not receive full credit. As a result, if you are having issues or difficulty within the group, it is critical that you address this immediately. If further mediation is required, please contact the course instructor (Brendan) as soon as possible.

2) Intersectionality Learning Experience (10%)

This course will encourage you to think critically about topics related to program evaluation in leisure services. Therefore, it is important to examine how our own unique perspectives and experiences may impact our social and political identities. The purpose of the intersectionality learning experience is to identify the advantages and disadvantages we have faced by exploring concepts such as implicit bias, equity, diversity, inclusion, and privilege. You will have access to this online module through LEARN. Due on February 24th at 8:30am.

Administrative Policy

Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI)

Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) that is trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code (e.g., Chat GPT, DALL-E, GitHub CoPilot) is permitted for some uses in this course. Proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement of GenAI use is required as it is for any other source you may consult and reference (e.g., journal articles, textbooks). 

If you choose to use GenAI in your course work, it should NOT replace your own ideas, work, or voice.

Unauthorized use of GenAI in this course, such as submitting content generated by Artificial Intelligence (AI) without proper attribution, or representing content generated by AI as your own idea or work will be considered a violation of Policy 71 and treated as such.

Permitted uses of GenAI:

  • Using GenAI to brainstorm or generate prompts/examples that encourage reflection, to refine an original idea, or support your editing process.

Prohibited uses of GenAI:

  • Representing content generated by GenAI as your own without proper attribution.
  • Sharing copyright-protected materials with open access GenAI (e.g., inputting any course materials, such as lecture content, into a GenAI tool).

If you choose to use GenAI, you must ensure you:

  • Maintain primary authorship and your own voice throughout your work.
  • Critically assess and evaluate whether output from GenAI is factual, verifiable, and relevant.
  • Do not rely on GenAI as your primary or sole source of information. 
  • Attribute GenAI use properly using APA for all submissions.
  • Maintain documentation of where/how you’ve used GenAI in case proof is requested by the course instructor or Associate Dean.
  • Remember that you remain responsible for ensuring that your use of AI tools complies with copyright and intellectual property laws.

Resources for using GenAI:

  • To learn more about documenting and citing Generative Artificial Intelligence, visit the Writing and Communication Centre's AI and the Writing Process – Documenting and Citing page or the Library's Research Guide on Generative Artificial Intelligence.
  • The Writing and Communication Centre has a great resource on Using ChatGPT and other Text-Generating Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) and how to be productive and ethical in your use of GenAI as well as cautions and risks to consider.
