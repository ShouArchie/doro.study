Course Description

CO 331:

A first course in error-correcting codes. Linear block codes, Hamming-Golay codes, and multiple error-correcting BCH codes are studied. Various encoding and decoding schemes are considered.

View requirements for CO 331

  1. Syllabus: We will cover some algebraic methods for devising error-correcting codes. These codes are used, for example, in satellite broadcasts, CD/DVD/Blu-ray players, memory chips, two-dimensional bar codes (including QR codes), and digital video broadcasting. The mathematical ingredients for the course include linear algebra, groups, rings, ideals, and finite fields. The necessary abstract algebra will be introduced as needed.
  2. Prerequisites: Math 239 can be used to satisfy the Math 225/235/245 prerequisite.
  3. CO 331 will be offered online, and supported by the University's Centre for Extended Learning (CEL).
  4. Video lectures are prerecorded. The videos,  slides and practice problems are available here.

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Demonstrate a fundamental understanding of the binary symmetric channel, decoding strategies, and the challenges with designing good codes;
Construct codes, and devise efficient encoding and decoding algorithms for them as a means of gaining exposure to the applications of linear algebra and abstract algebra;
Analyze the properties of major families of algebraic codes including linear codes, Hamming codes, Golay codes, cyclic codes, BCH codes, and Reed-Solomon codes.

Administrative Policy

SELF-DECLARED SHORT-TERM ABSENCE:  After completing the self‐declaration on Quest, send the instructor an email requesting accommodation for an assignment or quiz that is due during the two (2) day period of your absence. You can submit solutions to a subset of questions by the assignment due date. (I post solutions on LEARN shortly after the submission deadline, so extensions will not be granted.) The portion of your assignment grade for questions whose solutions you did not submit will be moved to the ﬁnal exam. The subset you submit must be comprised of solutions to full questions (not parts of questions), and need not be a proper subset. Your request should include the list of questions that you have elected not to submit.

OFFICE HOURS: Please make use of TA and instructor office hours and Piazza throughout the semester. You can use the office hours for assignment questions as well as for questions about the lecture material.

PIAZZA: You are encouraged to use Piazza to ask questions about the video lectures and to discuss the course material. You may ask for general clariﬁcation about assignment questions. However, please do not discuss solutions to assignment questions on Piazza, or make posts that reveal part of a solution. In particular, please do not ask for hints on Piazza. Instead, please see the instructor or one of the TA's during their oﬃce hours or make a private post on Piazza.

EMAIL QUERIES: Please restrict your email queries to questions of a personal nature, and questions that have short (e.g., YES/NO) answers. Questions that may have longer answers are best handled during office hours or on Piazza.

COLLABORATION ON ASSIGNMENTS: One question on each assignment will permit no collaboration of any kind; more details will be provided on the assignment. For the remaining questions, you are welcome (and encouraged) to collaborate on assignments in groups of size at most five (5) with other students presently enrolled in CO 331. However, solutions must be written up by yourself. If you do collaborate, you must acknowledge your collaborators in the write-up for each problem. You are not permitted to solicit help from websites such as Course Hero and Chegg, online discussion groups including Discord, LLMs including ChatGPT, or solutions from previous offerings of the course.

ASSIGNMENT DEADLINES: The material for each assignment problem will be covered well in advance of the assignment due date, so you will have adequate time to work on assignment problems. Late assignments will not be accepted except in very special circumstances (usually a documented illness of a serious nature). High workloads because of midterms and assignments in other courses will not qualify as a special circumstance.

GRADE APPEALS: If you have any concerns with the marking of assignment questions, please email your instructor together with a clear and detailed description of your appeal(s). If your marked assignment was returned to you on day X, then you should email appeals to me by the end of day X + 7 (and no later). Solutions to assignments will be posted on the course website shortly after the assignment submission deadline.

CONTINUITY PLANS: If an in-person final exam is not possible, the final exam will be held online using Crowdmark and LEARN.
