Course Description

EARTH 333:

An introduction to sediment properties, sedimentation processes, and sedimentary deposits in a variety of depositional environments and climatic conditions. Processes affecting sediments after initial deposition, such as those involved in the transformation of sediments into sedimentary rocks are also examined. Laboratories will focus on the different methods to describe and interpret sediments and sedimentary rocks.

View requirements for EARTH 333

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Know the main principles, concepts and approaches pertaining to the study of sediments and sedimentary rocks
Understand the basic processes of weathering, erosion, sediment transport and deposition, as well as post-depositional processes
Know the main modern sedimentary environments
Interpret past sedimentary environments from the sedimentary record
Appreciate societal relevance of sedimentology and know some of the possible applications
