Instructor & TA (Teaching Assistant) Information

Sana Ahmad has a BSc degree in Honours Co-op Environmental Science with a Geoscience Specialization from the University of Waterloo. She is currently pursuing an MSc degree in Earth Science at the same institution while working at the University as an Instructor.

At the University of Waterloo, Sana teaches a variety of Earth Science courses and labs including Earth 121 – Introductory Earth Sciences, Earth 122 – Introductory Environmental Sciences, Earth 231 – Mineralogy, and Earth 232 – Introductory Petrography.

For her research, Sana focuses on geomicrobiology and microbe-mineral interactions. Specifically, this involves investigating whether natural microbial metabolisms are able to bioremediate metal-contaminated mine sites through mineral precipitation.

When not busy with research or teaching, Sana loves to collect rocks, thrift, and work on her paint-by-numbers.

For students taking EARTH122. Laboratory exercises cover selected topics from EARTH122 lectures.

View requirements for EARTH 122L

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Discuss how anthropogenic actions on our environment such as air and water pollution, unsustainable extraction and use of natural resources, and climate change, adversely impact the Earth and our own lives
Use GPS devices and GIS software to locate points of interest and create a map
Use simple water quality measurement tools (e.g. pH meter, chemical analyses kits) to analyse and interpret water quality data

The format of the final exam will be run digitally through the LEARN Quiz system in the last week of the course, not during the formal exam period. Expect a series of True/False, Multiple Choice, and/or similar type questions. To keep the academic integrity of the course, you will NOT be able to move back and forth through the Exam questions or pages, it is a linear progression through the test. This is to prevent the easiest methods of cheating available. All course work will count towards your final grade, and only exceptions will be made via a proper and accepted Verification of Illness or Student Declaration form and instructor consent.

  • The Final Exam will cover the entire course from the first to last lab.
  • As the Final Exam will be held online through the LEARN Quiz system, it will also be an 'open-book' exam.
  • More details will be provided as we get closer to the end of term.

Administrative Policy

LATE POLICY

Your marks will be based on weekly lab assignments and a final exam. Assignments must be finished by the end of your lab session and submitted to your lab TA or proper Dropbox within our LEARN site.  Assignments not submitted to your TA or the Dropbox in time will not be accepted, and a late deduction could be applied.

Late deductions are typically -5% per day.

No lab assignments will be accepted after 1 Week Late without a VIF or acceptable documentation.

Alternative arrangements for missed labs will be granted only in cases where the student provides an acceptable Verification of Illness Form or Student Declaration or has consent from the instructor. Please contact the course instructor asap if you think you will miss a lab!

Accommodations may be given for valid and documented reasons but students must complete the 2 step process described below:

  1. Email the course instructor within 24 hours of the missed work due date.
    • Please note emails can be received 24 hours per day
    • Email subject line must include “Earth 122L – Missed Course Work" with your “Full Name” and “Student ID number”. This will alert me to the importance of the email.
    • Repeat the above information within the email message with a brief explanation of why the term work was missed.
  2. Email a copy of the documentation to the course instructor within one week.
    1. Medical Examination On Campus
      • Print a Verification of Illness Form (VIF) and bring to medical professional to complete or fill out the online Student Declaration form.
      • Get examined at the University of Waterloo campus Health Services. Health Services will complete a Verification of Illness form (VIF) for the student.
      • Assessment of illness must be based on an actual examination before or within 24 hours of the missed term work rather than an account of how the student felt several days prior.
      • These can be completed digitally now!
    2. Medical Examination Off Campus
      • If the student is ill on a weekend, during off-hours, while out-of-town or receiving ongoing care from a family physician or specialist, it is acceptable to provide documentation from other health service providers
      • Information should include date of physician assessment, dates of illness, level of incapacitation and whether the diagnosis was made by the physician or based on description by the student.
      • If the student does not have the University of Waterloo's VIF with them at the time they are seen by the external health service provider, or the documentation provided does not include the required information, the student will need to return and have them complete and sign the VIF. This official verification is necessary before any action can be taken and can be downloaded from the Health Services website.
      • Please note instructors may contact physicians to verify student illness.
    3. Situation where VIFs are not Appropriate
      • For other extenuating circumstances that are not illness related, for which the Verification of Illness Form is not appropriate, official documented proof that something has occurred will be required (consult with the instructor). It is understood that sometimes events occur beyond the student’s control so if in doubt, contact the course instructor if the student has a compelling reason. But it is the student’s responsibility to provide valid supporting evidence in writing.

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI. If you use any generative AI in assignments or lab reports, you MUST cite that you used it. 

Example citations:

"Lab Report 1 by Student LastName

Note: ChatGPT Version X was used for editing answers 1.1 through 1.11."

or "Studies have found that air pollution can lead to various illnesses such as cancer (OpenAI, 2025)" with the reference at the bottom like this:

"References

OpenAI. (2025). ChatGPT (Mar 14 version) [Large language model]. https://chat.openai.com/chat"

[I would not recommend using a generative AI as a source of info as they are not always right! It's probably just faster and more accurate to do a web search and pick a reputable website, e.g. a university, government, or scientific article page.]
