Course Description

EARTH 471:

An introduction to the principles and theories of mineral deposit genesis. Basic technical, economic, legal, and ethical aspects of mineral exploration and responsible development of natural resources are also covered. Laboratories involve hand sample and ore petrology of suites from diverse deposits.

View requirements for EARTH 471

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1) Identify metallic ore minerals in hand samples (Module 1, Labs)
2) Describe the formation of magmatic, hydrothermal and sedimentary ore deposits (Modules 2–4)
3) Integrate geochemical and geological data into mineral resource estimates (Module 5, Project)
4) Synthesize the life cycle of mining and responsible mineral exploration in Canada (Module 5)

Tentative Course Schedule

Module schedule

Module 1: Introduction to mineral deposits. Week 1. 

Module 2: Metallogeny and magmatic mineral deposits. Weeks 2–3. 

Module 3: Hydrothermal mineral deposits. Weeks 4–6. 

Module 4: Sedimentary and other mineral deposits. Weeks 8–10. 

Module 5: Responsible mining and the mining cycle Weeks 11–13.

Week 13: Optional review of class material 

Time commitment

Students should be prepared to spend 3 hours/week for in-person lecture participation (or alternate asynchronous materials), ~1 hour/week for quizzes and additional reading, and ~3 hours/week for labs.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Lab assignments (5 x 5%) 25%
Exploration project 25%
Module quizzes (5 x 4%) 20%
Deposit summary 10%
Final exam 20%
  1. Lab assignments (5 x 5% = 25%): Students have one week to complete each lab (L1–L5). The mark of each lab report will go down 5% for each 24 hours (or portion thereof) following the due date.
  2. Exploration project (25%): Students will participate in a mock exploration program. The final product is a report in the style of an NI 43-101 technical report that synthesizes the results of core logging, geochemical data analysis, a resource estimate and a summary of findings and recommendations. The final mark on the exploration project report will go down 5% for each 24 hours (or portion thereof) following the due date, which is the last day of classes.
  3. Module quizzes (5 x 4% = 20%): Through LEARN. Unlimited attempts allowed. I keep the highest mark.
  4. Deposit summary (10%): Individually, prepare a summary of a major magmatic or hydrothermal mineral deposit type targeted to a 2A Geoscience student knowledge level. Written or video options.
  5. Final exam (20%): Scheduled by the Registrar; a comprehensive examination of the course content related to the learning objectives.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

You have the option of using generative AI for written assignments in Earth 471. You are wholly accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class and for appropriate referencing of materials. 

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
