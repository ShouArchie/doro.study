Course Description

EARTH 270:

The course will examine the physical causes and affects of natural disasters including landslides, snow avalanches, earthquakes, tsunamis, hurricanes, tornadoes, flooding, subsidence, volcanic eruptions, and asteroid impacts. Illustrated by case histories, the course will analyze the factors that lead to disasters. The effect of climate change, human activity, and population growth on the magnitude and frequency of disasters will be explored. The course will introduce the basic principles of Geo-Risk management and its applications in natural hazards engineering, in the development of mitigation strategies and in the re-insurance industry.

View requirements for EARTH 270

There are no prerequisites for this course.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the various natural hazard processes and their impacts on human health, property, and the natural environment.
Explain the geological and/or hydrological systems involved in the most common natural hazard events.
Connect the role of climate change on current and future natural hazards.
Describe secondary impacts from the common natural hazards as well as possible mitigation techniques.

Administrative Policy

Course Content Warning

This online course involves viewing, reading, and discussing natural hazard processes and disasters that may directly or indirectly impact humans in a negative way.  Some students may find specific hazards/disasters disturbing or even traumatizing, as such, you are encouraged to prepare yourself emotionally before studying the material.  If you believe that you will find a certain part of this course too difficult to engage with, please contact the instructor to provide an alternate learning option as you will still be responsible for understanding all of the course material.

Late Policy

All term work (i.e., Quizzes, Discussion posts, and Assignments) submitted after the due date given on the Course Schedule will receive a score of zero. It is YOUR responsibility to understand and follow the course schedule and meet all posted due dates. Accommodations may be given for valid and documented reasons, but students must complete the 2-step process described below:

  1. Email the course instructor within 24 hours of the missed work due date.
    • Please note emails can be received 24 hours per day
    • Email subject line must include “EARTH270 – Missed Course Work" with your “Full Name” and “Student ID number”. This will alert the instructor to the importance of the email.
    • Repeat the above information within the email message with a “brief explanation” of why the work was missed.
  2. Email a copy of and important documentation to the course instructor as soon as possible, and not more than 1 week after the event.
    • Medical Examination On Campus
      • Print a Verification of Illness Form (VIF) and bring to medical professional to complete.
      • Get examined at the University of Waterloo campus Health Services. Health Services will complete a Verification of Illness form (VIF) for the student.
      • Assessment of illness must be based on an actual examination before or within 24 hours of the missed term work rather than an account of how the student felt several days prior.
    • Medical Examination Off Campus
      • If the student is ill on a weekend, during off-hours, while out-of-town or receiving ongoing care from a family physician or specialist, it is acceptable to provide documentation from other health service providers
      • Information should include date of physician assessment, dates of illness, level of incapacitation and whether the diagnosis was made by the physician or based on description by the student.
      • If the student does not have the University of Waterloo's VIF with them at the time they are seen by the external health service provider, or the documentation provided does not include the required information, the student will need to return and have them complete and sign the VIF. (Please see Accommodation for illness or extenuating circumstances for additional information)
      • Please note instructors may contact physicians or academic advisers to verify student illness.
    • Situation where VIFs are not Appropriate
      • For other extenuating circumstances that are not illness related, for which the Verification of Illness Form is not appropriate, official documented proof that something serious has occurred will be required (consult with the instructor). It is understood that sometimes events occur beyond the student’s control so if in doubt, contact the course instructor if the student has a compelling reason. But it is the student’s responsibility to provide valid supporting evidence in writing.

Use of Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will be discussed in class.

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the library: ChatGPT and Generative Artificial Intelligence (AI) – Research Guides at University of Waterloo. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. Therefore, it is your responsibility to double-check all AI outputs you wish to build upon in your project. Do not just assume it’s always correct!

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: Generative Artificial Intelligence – University of Waterloo.

You will be accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
