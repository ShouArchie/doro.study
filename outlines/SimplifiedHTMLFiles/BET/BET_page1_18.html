Course Description

BET 430:

The one thing every new venture needs whether small or large, not-for-profit or for-profit, startup or part of an already mature business, is sales. This course is designed to help create and grow a successful business venture through personal marketing and sales efforts. Coursework examines key sales technologies, networking techniques, the use of marketing in sales, the sales process, and assessing the value of a particular sale from both the perspective of customer impact and the financial and strategic impact on the organization.

View requirements for BET 430

Coursework examines the need to understand buyers, the use of prospecting and marketing in sales, the sales process, and assessing the value of  a sale from both the perspective of customer impact and achievement of the financial/strategic goals for the organization.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
introduce the key sales concepts that are required for a sales career or business success
develop knowledge of the key elements of the sales process, the basic components of customer relationships, and the need for a defined sales methodology to create repeatable and scalable businesses
understand how the sales relationship can drive customer buying decisions built on strong value cases
understand how customer experience facilitates the development and maintenance of these customer relationships
develop specific sales and communication skills that will immediately help you in your career

All assignments are subject to the following late penalty: Any submission more than 72 hours past the due date receives a 20% for being late plus an additional 4% per day. No submissions accepted after 10 days from the due date.

Quizzes

Reading the posted Course Readings will prepare you for the related weekly Quizzes. Each Quiz consist of randomized multiple-choice questions designed to evaluate your learning on a week-to-week basis. The time limit will be up to ten for completion of 5 to 10 MC questions. You can only attempt each quiz once and the grade is final. 

You are responsible for completing these graded components in the time frame allotted so please be sure you have access to a stable Internet connection and a properly configured computer or device.
 

Term Test

An in-class term test consisting of up to 25 randomized multiple-choice questions will be held during the 2nd last class of the course. The time limit will be up to 25 minutes. You can only attempt the term test once and the grade is final.

You are responsible for completing these graded components in the time frame allotted so please be sure you have access to a stable Internet connection and a properly configured computer or device.

 

Pitch Video

You will create a three-minute video pitch selling anything that you are passionate about with the objective of sparking the interest of  the audience and move them towards a specific action. You will be marked on the clarity of the value proposition, your confidence, credibility, and the abilityto close your pitch. You must be visible in your pitch video.

All needed instructions will be provided on Learn and in class.

Why Should You Be Hired

Choose a company that sells Business to Business (B2B) that you would like to work for in a sales role. Createa 2-page document and a 3-minute recorded submission as to why they should hire you. You will be marked on your understanding of that business along with your confidence, credibility, and ability to close your pitch. You must be visible in the video.

All needed instructions will be provided on Learn and in class.

Group Project: Sales Playbook 

Sales Playbooks are created to document the approach to finding and securing sales on a consistent basis. It is a powerful training tool for new Sales Representatives and is unbelievably valuable to early-stage companies to organize their sales efforts. Your group will be randomly generated and then assigned to work with  a current start-up company to produce a Sales Playbook.

Your requirement will be to get input from the founders, research their business and then, using key learnings from this course, to develop a Sales Playbook for this company. You will have learned all the required key    elements of a Sales Playbook such as Company Value Proposition, Target Markets, Lead Generation, Sales Process and New Client Onboarding.

Students will have the option of doing group evaluations for this project and individual student marks can be adjusted based on these evaluations.

All needed instructions will be provided on Learn and in class. 

Group Project: Sales Playbook Presentation

Every member must participate in the delivery of a maximum 10-minute recorded Sales Playbook presentation. This presentation will be an instructional video on how to use and understand the Sales Playbook for a new Sales Representative. Use PowerPoint or any other presentation tools to effectively convey the key elements of your written submission. Each group member must be visible during the presentation.

Students will have the option of doing group evaluations for this project and individual student marks can be adjusted based on these evaluations.

All needed instructions will be provided on Learn and in class 

Participation 

This course is designed to examine topics in human decision making, communication and behaviour and requires regular and ongoing participation in the class by each student. This class is highly interactive therefore attendance and participation in class is expected and will be assessed by the instructor.  As such, not participating in class or missing more than 4 classes, without appropriate VIF's, can result in a grade of zero for participation.

Where assessment of participation is not deemed possible by the instructor due to the cancellation of in class teaching as required by the University, the marks allocated to Participation will be added to the marks allocated to the Pitch Video and Why Should You Be Hired Assignments.

Per Adjusted Marks on Group Projects

Each assigned group will create and submit a group contract at the beginning of the term, which each group member agrees to, which will define the objectives, communication methods and standards of the group, and the conflict resolution process the group will follow. If requested by the group, the Professor will provide a peer evaluation process and will adjust individual marks based on the evaluations received from the team members.

Failure to participate in a Group Project can result is a grade of zero on a group assignment.
