Course Description

BET 400:

This course develops the theoretical knowledge and practical skills needed to develop a marketing strategy for early-stage ventures. Topics include managing product development, marketing and brand development, segmentation and targeting, competitor analysis, and positioning.

View requirements for BET 400

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Attain a keen grasp of key marketing concepts and strategies (e.g., customer insight, market segmentation, targeting, positioning, and customer adoption of innovations) with focus on the challenges faced by new ventures.
Gain proficiency in marketing mix strategies and tactics and understand how to use them to achieve strategic objectives.
Create a comprehensive and actionable marketing plan for a startup or entrepreneurial idea based on thoughtful market analysis.
Hone presentation and pitching skills to persuasively articulate customer value, engage stakeholders, and secure support for new venture initiatives.

In-Class Participation                                                                        

Active participation in class discussions is essential to the learning process and to the success of the course. You will be graded on your individual participation including the quality of your contributions to the discussion. Absenteeism (except when due to illness) will negatively affect your contribution grade. You should come to each class well prepared, which means reading any assigned cases/articles before class, developing answers/opinions to any discussion questions, participating in class discussions, and sharing your solutions/thoughts on in-class exercises and case studies. You are also encouraged to bring in examples, articles, and questions for class discussion. 

To ensure that you receive credit for in-class participation, use your name tag in every class. Remember it is your responsibility to demonstrate your preparation and insight to your instructor. While regularity of contribution to discussion will be important, quality of contribution will be more important. 

As part of your participation grade, you will be asked to complete two reflective self-assessments during the semester.

 

Peer Feedback

Collaborative learning is an important part of this class. The marketing plan presentations provide an opportunity for the class to see different applications of the concepts and ideas discussed in class. They also give the presenting groups the opportunity to hear from the class, with thoughts, suggestions, questions about the presentation. Thus, the feedback you provide is valuable both for your peers and for your own learning.

 

Marketing Plan 

Working on real ventures: Throughout this course, the context for what you are learning will be a venture developing a product or service to serve a real market need. Ideally you will work in groups of 2-5, but you have the option of working individually. The important consideration is that the venture should be real and in the process of being created by one or more members of the group. The venture can be in the planning stages or already launched.

Proposal Pitch                                                                                                          

The proposal should provide the situational analysis of the market in which your new venture will compete and explain what gap in the market your venture will serve. Detailed instructions for the proposal will be posted. The proposal should address, among other things:

  • A description of the existing ‘industry’
  • The nature of customers, their buying behaviour and the segments that exist, including those segments that are not well-served, i.e., what are the gaps in the market?
  • The dominant players within the ‘industry’ and their market positions
  • The competitive challenges within the ‘industry’
  • How you would position your market offering

Note: the term ‘industry’ refers to the customers, competitors, suppliers, etc. that are addressing the same general need/problem that your new venture will target. The purpose of the proposal is to provide the background understanding and rationale necessary to develop your marketing plan. You should only look at the ‘industry’ that is relevant to your new venture.

Depending on the number of groups in the class, the proposal pitch will either be conducted as an in-person presentation during class time (provided that class time is sufficient for all groups to pitch) or recorded by your group and submitted via Learn.

Final Pitch                                                                                                                  

The pitch of your final marketing plan will consist of a short summary of the situational analysis (which you initially addressed in the proposal), a more developed marketing strategy (segment(s) to be targeted, positioning statement), and an appropriate marketing mix for the new product or service. You also need to include anticipated results over a 3-year timeframe, including market share and financial analysis (projected revenues, expenses, profits, break-even).

Depending on the number of groups in the class, the final pitch will either be conducted as an in-person presentation during class time (provided that class time is sufficient for all groups to pitch) or recorded by your group and submitted via Learn.

 

In-Class Assessments                                                                                                                       

There will be two (2) in-class assessments (approx. 60 minutes each) based on applications of marketing concepts. You will be given a number of short case scenarios about situations and/or decisions facing new businesses and then asked to use marketing concepts discussed in the course to provide recommendations or analysis for the business.

Generative AI:

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions.

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
