Course Description

BET 320:

Once your "big idea" has been formulated, what is the best way to realize its potential? With whom should you partner? How do you protect your intellectual property? What is the optimal business model? The objective of this course is to provide an understanding of the commercialization process, with a specific focus on assessment of opportunities and development of an entrepreneurial business plan. Course content includes the importance of strategic partnerships, understanding the bases of competition, conducting industry and market level analyses, evaluating innovative ideas for commercial potential, business planning in a corporate context, intellectual property strategy, and strategic tools needed for launching new ideas.

View requirements for BET 320

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
understand the importance of innovation in established firms.
evaluate innovative ideas to determine potential for creating opportunities.
analyze business situations and apply knowledge and logic to provide viable innovative solutions.
apply creativity to identifying core business problems and developing effective solutions.
understand the unique challenges of successfully leading change and launching new ideas in a corporate environmnt.

Student Assessment

There is no defined grading scheme for this course.

 

Class Contribution (individual)                                                                                                  20%

The final grade for class contribution will be based on 1) an assessment of your contribution to discussions in the first four classes (5%), 2) a similar assessment for classes six to nine (5%) and 3) your feedback and comments on the final group presentations (10%).

Class Contribution Self-Assessments

Active participation in class discussions is essential to the learning process and to the success of this course.  You will be graded on your individual participation including the quality of your contributions to class discussion and your understanding of assigned reading materials.  

Absenteeism will negatively affect your contribution grade. You should come to class well prepared, which means reading assigned articles before class, completing assignments and developing answers/opinions to any discussion questions, and being willing to participate in class discussions. You are also encouraged to bring in examples, articles, questions for class discussion. 

To ensure that you receive credit for in-class contribution, use your name tag in every class. Remember, it is your responsibility to demonstrate your preparedness and insight.

There are also a number of ways that you can engage with your classmates, outside of the classroom. For example, contributing comments and questions to Learn discussion topics, suggesting interesting news articles, organizing small group discussions with selected classmates, or other ways that you feel add value. Given the variety of ways you can contribute to class learning, you will have an opportunity to evaluate your contributions using the provided Class Contribution Self-Assessment forms. 

Self-assessments are to be completed on the forms provided in Learn>Content and submitted to the appropriate Learn dropboxes following the classes on January 29 and March 12. 

Feedback and Comments about final group presentations:

The final group assignment presentations provide an opportunity for the class to see different applications of the concepts and ideas discussed in class. They also give the presenting groups the opportunity to hear from the class, with thoughts, suggestions, questions about the presentation. After the group project presentations have been submitted, they will be posted for everyone in class to see and provide feedback.

I encourage you to watch all of the presentations but for Class Contribution credit I ask that you select four of them to review. Put all four reviews in a single WORD document. For each review, provide the topic of the presentation along with your questions and comments.

Reviews will be evaluated based on your understanding of the presentations and your application of BET 320 course concepts.

 

Guest Speaker Reflection                                                                                                            5%

On March 26, we will be inviting an entrepreneur from the community to share their story with our class and other Conrad School classes. This is a chance to hear how others are building their companies, their challenges, and tips, and an opportunity to engage with other students interested in entrepreneurship. To further encourage engagement and learning, you will be submitting a 400–500-word reflection on this session. This can include your thoughts on a topic the guest speaker touched on, something they may have said that surprised you or resonated, a connection to any of the course concepts, reflections on the readings for that week, or anything you want to talk about that is DIRECTLY related to the talk. This reflection will be marked as follows:

  • 0/5 – No reflection submitted
  • 2.5/5 – Reflection submitted but shows little effort or suggests student was either not present or paying attention to the speaker(s)
  • 5/5/ - Thoughtful reflection, suggesting student was paying attention and spent some time reflecting on what the speaker(s) discussed.

The reflection is to be submitted to the Learn Dropbox by April 2.

Quiz                                                                                                                                                      25%

On February 5 there will be a 90 minute in-class quiz based on understanding and application of BET 320 course concepts. The quiz will include multiple choice and short answer questions, requiring an understanding and application of BET 320 course concepts.

Individual Assignment                                                                                                                  25%

 

This is an individual evaluation and you are not to discuss the questions or your answers with anyone until after the due date. 

You may refer to readings from the class and your notes. You can also refer to other sources, but not another person. Your answers must be based on content from BET 320 readings and class discussions. Answers that are based on concepts not discussed in BET 320 will receive no credit.

Submissions should be two to three pages in length, plus exhibits. 

The assignment is due at the beginning of class on March 12. Reports are to be submitted to the LEARN dropbox. 

 

Group Assignment                                                                                                                          25%

In groups of three or four, you will investigate the application of BET 320 course concepts to a topic of interest to you. For example, the topic can be about the future opportunities in an industry, the future prospects for a new technology, or it can be one of the topics discussed in the course. The important point is that you must answer a question about the topic, using BET 320 concepts and frameworks for your analysis. 

 Students will form their own groups.

A topic proposal is due February 12. Proposals are to be submitted to the LEARN dropbox. Only one group member needs to submit the proposal.

The only deliverable for this assignment is a narrated presentation, no longer than 10 minutes, which is due before the beginning of class on March 26. The preferred format is a narrated powerpoint presentation and/or mp4 video. The presentation should outline the topic, why it was chosen, the research that was conducted, the course concepts that were useful/relevant, and a conclusion or recommendation that answers the question you are addressing. A report is not required.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as understanding business concepts and applying them to real life situations. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.).
