Course Description

AMATH 353:

Second order linear partial differential equations - the diffusion equation, wave equation, and Laplace's equation. Methods of solution - separation of variables and eigenfunction expansions, the Fourier transform. Physical interpretation of solutions in terms of diffusion, waves, and steady states. First order non-linear partial differential equations and the method of characteristics. Applications are emphasized throughout.

View requirements for AMATH 353

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Develop a mathematical model to explain a scientific principle in the language of Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)
Determine what analytical method is appropriate to solve a given PDE
Apply analytical methods to solve PDEs
Physically interpret solutions of PDEs

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assignments 20%
Midterm 30%
Final Exam 50%

Assignments: There will be 5 assignments. Assignments are an important element of this course. They will help you to work through, practice, and apply the material discussed in the lectures. See schedule for assignment due dates. Assignments will be submitted and returned on Crowdmark. 

Collaboration: I encourage you to discuss the assignments with your classmates. However, since these are individually marked assessments, I expect you to write up your work independently. If you consult or use any references or resources other than the textbook and lecture notes, please cite them in your work. Keep in mind that the purpose of the assignments is to help you learn the course material and prepare for the midterm and final exam. You will not have access to your classmates or extra resources during the midterm and final exam. 

Policy on late assignments: Unless prior arrangements are made with the instructor, late assignments will receive a penalty of 1% per minute starting immediately after the deadline. Note about Crowdmark: although you can make as many submissions as you wish before the deadline, Crowdmark will only accept one submission after the deadline has passed (and this is only if you have not submitted anything at all before the deadline).  If you do have to submit an assignment late, you must make sure that your submission is complete: upload all of your files and then click the "submit" button. 

Policy on missed assignments and midterm: Missed assignments and tests will be given a mark of zero. An accommodation may be given if there is a self-declared absence, VIF, or other extenuating circumstances. For assignments, the accommodation may be an extension or re-weighting of the missed assignment to the other assignments. For midterm, the grades for the missed midterm will be shifted to the final exam. 

Midterm:  There will one 80-minute midterm to be held in the tutorial on Feb 25. Marked midterms will be returned on Crowdmark.

Crowdmark tips: You will submit assignments using Crowdmark. Here are some general instructions for using Crowdmark:

  • Answer each question on a separate page. Different parts of the same question (e.g. parts a, b, c) may be answered on the same page. It is OK if your answer to a question extends to more than one page.
  • If all your answers are in a single pdf file, you can submit the entire document for question 1 and then drag pages to the appropriate questions.
  • Assignments may be hand-written or typed. For hand-written assignments, scan your answers using a scanner or a smartphone scanning app.
  • A few days before the assignment due date, you will receive a Crowdmark link by email. Submit your work by following the instructions in the email. The email will be sent to your UWaterloo email address, so please remember to check it regularly. This is a personalized link, so do not share it with anyone else.
  • After submitting, close the Crowdmark browser window. Using a laptop or desktop (not a phone), click on the Crowdmark link again to view your submission. Be sure to confirm that your submission was successful, your pages are linked to the right questions and are in order, each page is oriented correctly, and your answers are legible.
  • If you notice any errors after submitting, you can submit again before the deadline. Resubmission will remove previous submissions.

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as solving PDEs. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

Students are encouraged to reach out to campus supports if they need help with their coursework including: 

  • Student Success Office for help with skills like notetaking and time management
  • Writing and Communication Centre for assignments with writing or presentations
  • AccessAbility Services for documented accommodations
  • Library for research-based assignments
