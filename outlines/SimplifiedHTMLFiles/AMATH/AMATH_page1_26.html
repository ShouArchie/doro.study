Course Description

AMATH 451:

A unified view of linear and nonlinear systems of ordinary differential equations in Rn. Flow operators and their classification: contractions, expansions, hyperbolic flows. Stable and unstable manifolds. Phase-space analysis. Nonlinear systems, stability of equilibria, and Lyapunov functions. The special case of flows in the plane, Poincare-Bendixson theorem, and limit cycles. Applications to physical problems will be a motivating influence.

AMATH 651:

their classification: contractions, expansions, hyperbolic flows. Stable and unstable manifolds. Phase-space
analysis. Nonlinear systems, stability of equilibria and Lyapunov functions. The special case of flows in the

View requirements for AMATH 651

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
State sufficient conditions for existence and uniqueness of solutions of initial value problems
Understand the general form for solutions of linear systems with constant coefficients
Sketch phase portrait diagrams for dynamical systems
Describe the significance of stable and unstable manifolds and find them for particular systems
Use Lyapunov's Method for determining stability
Distinguish different types of critical points, limit sets, and periodic orbits

Assignments

There will be four equally weighted assignments, due at 6:00 p.m. on Wednesdays of the weeks indicated in the course schedule. Topics covered in each assignment will be those of roughly the two weeks up to and including Monday of the week before the deadline. The problems will be posted in LEARN at least a week before they are due and submissions should be made in Crowdmark. Late submissions will be accepted with a penalty of 1% per hour, up to 24 hours after the deadline. 

You may request exemptions for assignments; if granted, the total weight of assignments will be reduced proportionally.

Midterm

The midterm test is scheduled for Monday, 10 February 2025, 1:30 p.m. - 2:20 p.m. (during the tutorial time) in QNC 1502 & 1507.

If you miss the midterm for a valid (e.g., medical) reason, the weight will be redistributed (+15% to assignments and +10% to the final exam).

Final Exam

The final exam will be registrar-scheduled and in-person, if possible, with a duration of 150 minutes.

Graduate Project

Students taking the graduate version of the course (AMATH 651) must submit a written project report by the last day of class, April 4.

Re-mark Request Policy

If you believe there was an error in how an assignment or midterm test was graded, please send an email to the instructor or TA describing the issue. Re-mark requests must be submitted within one week of receiving your graded work.
