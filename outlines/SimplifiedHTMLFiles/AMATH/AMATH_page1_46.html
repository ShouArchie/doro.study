Course Description

AMATH 753:

The main themes are well-posedness of problems, Hilbert space methods, variational principles and integral equation methods. Topics include: first-order nonlinear partial differential equations, quasilinear hyperbolic systems, potential theory, eigenfunctions and eigenvalues, semi-groups, and power series solutions. Applications from various areas will be used to motivate and illustrate the theory. A previous course in partial differential equations at the undergraduate level is strongly recommended.

View requirements for AMATH 753

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Analyze second-order elliptic and parabolic equations, in particular, prove when there exist solutions to these problems.

Assignments:

You can take one (no questions asked) three day extension on assignments (except for the final assignment which must be handed in on time). You will upload your assignments to Crowdmark.

Midterm and Final:

The midterm and final exams are both in-person. The course schedule states the date and location of the midterm. The final exam schedule will state the date and location of the final exam. Information on what will be covered on the midterm and final exams will be announced on LEARN during the term.

Late assignments/make-up exams:

Other than the above mentioned extension on one assignment (except the final assignment), there will be no other extensions on assignments, except under special circumstances. A late assignment will count as 0. Regarding the midterm: there will be no make-up midterm. If you are ill, please be prepared to provide a note from your doctor. If your instructor decides that your circumstances warrant an excuse, your final grade will be calculated based on your performance on the assignments and final exam. When possible, an advance notice must be given.
