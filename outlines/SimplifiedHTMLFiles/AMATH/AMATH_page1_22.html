Course Description

AMATH 373:

Critical experiments and old quantum theory. Basic concepts of quantum mechanics: observables, wavefunctions, Hamiltonians, and the Schroedinger equation. Uncertainty, correspondence, and superposition principles. Simple applications to finite and extended one-dimensional systems, harmonic oscillator, rigid rotor, and hydrogen atom.

View requirements for AMATH 373

Additional advanced topics, time-permitting, could include an introduction to entanglement, non-locality and Bell's theorem, introductory applications in quantum computing.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain how the mathematical formalism of quantum theory an be applied to describe elementary quantum systems.

Tentative Course Schedule
