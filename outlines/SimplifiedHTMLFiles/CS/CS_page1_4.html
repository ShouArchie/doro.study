Course Description

CS 105:

An introduction to the fundamentals of computer programming through media computation. Students will learn to write interactive graphical programs. Fundamental language concepts such as variables, conditionals, loops, functions, and arrays. Programming concepts such as coding style, modular design, testing, and debugging. Media concepts such as 2D graphics drawing, input, animation, and image processing.

View requirements for CS 105

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
You will learn to write interactive graphical programs that introduce fundamental language concepts such as variables, conditionals, loops, functions, and arrays.
You will learn programming concepts such as coding style, modular design, testing, and debugging.
The course also covers media concepts such as 2D graphics drawing, input, animation, and image processing.

Additional Notes

  • You must pass the weighted average of the weekly quizzes and final exam.
  • You must get at least 40% on the final exam in order to pass the course.

Module Questions and Exercises

By default, programming questions all allow unlimited attempts. Other questions such as multiple choice, multiple select, and short answer questions typically allow one or two. There may be some exceptions, so be sure to verify the number of attempts permitted before answering a question.

All multiple choice, short answer, and multiple select module questions are equally weighted and are worth 1 point each. All coding exercises are equally weighted and are worth 2 points each. We will drop 5% of your lowest scoring answers of each type. This means it is possible to get the full 20% for the Module Questions and Exercises component of your final grade without answering every question correctly.

 

Note the "Progress" tab at the top of every page of the course. You can use that page to track your completion of concept checks and quiz questions. Make sure you don't accidentally skip anything!

Assignments

There will be eight assignments during the term. Assignments will consist of programming questions.

You will submit assignments using Learn, which allows TAs to view and mark the assignment and provide you with feedback. Always read the feedback from TAs. They may tell you about errors that could cause you to lose marks in later assignments.

Weekly Quizzes

There will be 11 weekly quizzes during the term. They will be timed and completed on Learn. Weekly quizzes are equally weighted. We will drop the lowest scoring result of the weekly quizzes and calculate the grade based on the remaining quiz scores.

Final Project

There will be a final project for the course. The details of the project will be posted later in the term. It is expected to be approximately the scope of between two and three assignments. There will be ample time to complete the project after the last weekly assignment is due.

Final Exam

There will be a final exam at the end of the term, after the end of lectures. The time will be scheduled by the registrar. The exam will be proctored and in-person, and it will be written on paper.

Assignment Screening

Assignments and project will be scanned using MOSS (Measure Of Software Similarity).

Administrative Policy

Assignment deadlines

It is your responsibility to ensure that you submit your assignment correctly before the deadline. The deadline is based on the time of day in Waterloo, Ontario (Eastern Standard Time or Eastern Daylight Time, depending on the time of year). The current date and time in Waterloo are always shown at the top-right of course web pages.

Late assignment submissions will never be accepted under any circumstances. We will not grant an extension because you attempted to submit at the last minute, or because your internet connection failed at the crucial moment of submission. We will not accept an assignment sent via email. We will not accept a revision after the deadline that fixes a typo that prevented your assignment from working.

Start early, submit early, and submit often. Give yourself (more than) enough time to submit.

Missed assignments/Tests or Quizzes

If you are unable to complete an assignment because of illness, or other extenuating circumstances, submit appropriate documentation to Quest. There is no need to contact course staff. We are notified daily of absences and at the discretion of the course staff, you will be excused from completing the missed assignment (or other components) and your other assignments will be reweighted to compensate. If you miss too many assignments, you may be asked to withdraw from the course.

Re-marking

If you feel that there was an error or oversight in the marking of your assignment, please fill out the Remark Request form for the particular assignment on Learn. The forms will be in a Survey on Learn for each assignment for two weeks. The remark will be done following the two week period. After submitting a remark request ISAs will review and remark your entire assignment; if there are any major concerns then these will be communicated to the instructors if needed. Note that your assignment grade may increase or decrease after a remark.
