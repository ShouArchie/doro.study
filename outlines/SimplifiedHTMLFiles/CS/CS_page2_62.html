Course Description

CS 454:

An introduction to distributed systems, emphasizing the multiple levels of software in such systems. Specific topics include fundamentals of data communications, network architecture and protocols, local-area networks, concurrency control in distributed systems, recovery in distributed systems, and clock synchronization.

Assignments

There will be 4 assignments, a combination of answering questions and implementation work.

Assignments are to be completed individually. Late assignments will not be accepted.

Assignments when released will be available from LEARN and will be accompanied by a Piazza announcement to inform you of the assignment’s availability as well as submission procedure for each of the assignments.

Assignments must be typeset. Choose your favorite word processor to typeset your assignment. Handwritten assignment will not be accepted.

For assignments, do not use any public or web-based source code repository hosting service other than https://git.uwaterloo.ca.

Tentative assignment release dates:

  • Assignment 1: early February
  • Assignments 2 and 3: mid-February
  • Assignment 4: mid-March

Note that assignment release dates are subject to change and can shift depending on coverage of course material.

Exam

The final exam will be scheduled by the Registrar’s Office.

Administrative Policy

TA Office Hours

TAs are available to answer assignment related questions only during office hours. TAs will only answer questions related to implementation-based assignments. Questions for other assignments and the course material should be sent to the instructor. A schedule of office hours will be announced in the first two weeks of the term. TAs will only answer questions during office hours, they will not answer questions on Piazza.

Piazza

Students can use Piazza posts to ask questions about assignments. Questions related to the material or to assignments with written answers will be answered by the course instructor.

The TAs and course instructor will not answer Piazza questions related to the implementation assignments. For questions related to implementation assignments, please use the TA office hours.

For general questions about implementation assignments you can still ask them on Piazza and ask for help from other students in the class. Collaboration on solving common problems with implementation assignments is encouraged. Questions that are specific to a student’s assignment solution including a student’s assignment implementation details (such as code particulars) must be asked in TA office hours and not shared on Piazza. A breach of this guideline will be considered a violation of academic integrity and it will be dealt with according to the university procedures described in Policy 71, Student Discipline: https://uwaterloo.ca/secretariat/policies-procedures-guidelines/policy-71

Plagiarism

Plagiarism is representing the work of others as your own. Plagiarism on an exam includes communicating in any way with others over the duration of the exam. Plagiarism on assignments includes copying another student’s solution and submitting it as your own, allowing another student to copy your solution, collaborating excessively with another student, or obtaining solutions from any other source. The standard penalty for plagiarism is a grade of zero for that piece of work, with a minimum deduction of 5% from the final course grade. In addition, you would be placed on disciplinary probation for the remainder of your undergraduate studies at the University of Waterloo.

All academic offenses are reported to the Associate Dean for Undergraduate Studies and are recorded in the student’s file. Subsequent academic offenses in the same course or in other courses will lead to more severe penalties, up to and including suspension and expulsion.

You may discuss general concepts and problems with classmates, tutors, TAs, and instructors. However, the solution that you submit must be worked through by yourself and written in your own words. It is not acceptable to work on an assignment or exam with somebody else and write it up individually. When discussing course matters, do not take notes, and do not look at another person’s partial solutions, or show them yours. When trying to deal with difficulties, do not consult books other than the textbook, or any Web sources, unless explicitly authorized to do so.
