Course Description

CIVE 507:

The building process. Loadings; gravity, wind, thermal, moisture, and fire. Enclosure design; walls, windows, and roof. Subgrade construction. Energy related considerations.

View requirements for CIVE 507

This is an introduction to building science, enclosure (envelope) and indoor environmental quality. Topics include heat flow, including conduction,. Convection (natural and forced) and radiation with an emphasis on building applications (ie often steady-state simplified approached), predicting gradients and building system energy. Solar gains and control. Air-flow, air barriers, and the role of airflow in condensation and heat flow. Psychrometrics, condensation. vapour pressure, and vapor transport and control. Rainwater control.  Capillarity, surface tension. Thermal comfort. Indoor air quality. Case studies will be used through out. 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
explain the importance of buildings to society, their impact on the environment, and the context in which professions operate in the building industry.
describe the build up of some common enclosure assemblies and what the layers do
understand some of the factors that influence the design of the buildings and their envelope elements, especially energy, moisture & durability, risk, reliability, constructability, sustainability, and circularity.
explain and thereby help avoid common building design and construction errors that result in poor performance, litigation, expensive repairs, etc.
demonstrate the design skills developed in the course applying the knowledge and analysis methods
CEAB Attributes
Formulate a problem statement; develop models to solve engineering problems including identifying approximations, assumptions and constraints; critically evaluate solutions of engineering problems; select appropriate engineering tools, considering their limitations; describe the importance of codes, standards, best practices, laws, and regulations within engineering.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
