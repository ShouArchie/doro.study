Course Description

CIVE 497:

A special course on advanced topics in civil engineering is offered from time to time, when resources are available. For the current offering, inquire at the Department.

View requirements for CIVE 497

This course introduces the analysis and design of structures exposed to fire. It will cover the fundamentals of fire safety, behavior, heat transfer, the effects of fire loading on materials and structural systems, and the principles and design methods under fire loads. Applications of fire resistance design for steel and timber buildings will also be presented.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand history of structural fire engineering
Understand fire resistance concept and mechanics of fire development
Utilize different fire models and thermal actions on structural members
Understand material behavior and structural mechanics at high temperature
Understand structural design of steel and timber elements for fire resistance
Explore advanced computational methods for thermal-structural analysis

Tentative Course Schedule

  • Introduction and history of structural fire engineering
  • Fire behaviour in buildings
  • Temperature-time relationship
  • Fire models and thermal actions on structural members
  • Fire resistance concept
  • Material behavior at high temperature
  • Structural mechanics at high temperature
  • Structural design of steel and timber elements for fire resistance
  • Examples of structural fire engineering studies
  • Advanced computational methods for thermal-structural analysis

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assignments 25%
Midterm Exam 25%
Final Exam 50%
  • Approximately 4 assignments will be given over the course of the semester. Each assignment will consist of several problems related to topical content from lectures.  
  • Due dates will be indicated on the assigned problem sets (typically providing 1 to 2 weeks for completion after posting). No late homework submissions will be allowed unless prior permission is given. 
  • There will be one in-person tests during the term worth 25% of the course grade.
  • These tests will be administered as synchronous in-person tests and will be written during the lecture period. If restrictions arise relating to in-person meetings/gatherings, the tests will be administered virtually, using the course website. The time and date will be set through in class discussion.
  • The final exam will be held during the final exam period.
