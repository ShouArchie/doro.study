Course Description

CIVE 512:

This course deals with the assessment, rehabilitation and/or strengthening of building and bridge infrastructure. Topics include damage mechanisms, instrumentation and non-destructive test methods, conventional repair techniques, innovative repair and strengthening techniques with composites. Case studies provide students with the opportunity to learn from field applications. The laboratory portion involves test methods used to evaluate repair and strengthening measures.

View requirements for CIVE 512

Topics:

  • Assessment and Rehabilitation Process
  • Limit States Design (Review)
  • Monte Carlo Simulation
  • Bridge Assessment and Inspection
  • Bridge Management Systems / LCCA
  • Instruments for Monitoring Structures
  • Metal Corrosion and Fatigue
  • Evaluation and Inspection of Steel Structures
  • Rehabilitation of Steel Structures
  • Concrete Deterioration
  • Evaluation and Inspection of Reinforced Concrete Structures
  • Concrete Repair
  • Concrete Strengthening with FRP
  • Concrete Anchors
  • Masonry and Wood Rehabilitation

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Have a deeper understanding of various means by which structures can be damaged or deteriorate over time.
Have a methodology that they can follow for approaching new structural assessment and rehabilitation problems.
Be familiar with state-of-the-art techniques that can be used for assessment, rehabilitation, and strengthening of structures.
Be able to perform some basic analysis and design calculations related to the assessment and strengthening of structures.
Understand how structural rehabilitation fits within the broader topic of infrastructure management.

Lab Information:

The laboratory component of the course will include four labs. Two of these labs will cover “rehabilitation of reinforced concrete beams with FRP sheets”. For these labs, the class will be divided into ~six groups. Working in the CEE structural testing laboratory in ~1-hour sessions during the lab weeks, students will repair and strengthen several reinforced concrete beams. The laboratory work will involve surface preparation and application of the repair system (Lab 2) and testing the specimens to failure (Lab 3). The topics for the other labs will include an instrumentation demo (Lab 1) and concrete anchor installation demo by guests from Hilti Canada (Lab 4). Short worksheets will be submitted at the end of each lab session for grading. 

Deliverable Submission and Late Submission Policy:

The quizzes and labs will be in-person. Assignments shall be submitted using Crowdmark. Late assignments will be penalized 20% per day, until either 5 days have passed, or a solution has been posted, after which they will receive a grade of zero.

Group Work Policy:

Generative AI

The use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
