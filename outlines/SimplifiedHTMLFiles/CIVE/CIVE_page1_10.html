Course Description

CIVE 303:

Analysis of statically indeterminate structures using force and displacement methods. Influence lines for indeterminate structures. Introduction to the matrix stiffness method. Computer applications using commercial structural analysis software.

View requirements for CIVE 303

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Sketch the deflected shape of determinate and indeterminate structures, and calculate deflections due to various loads.
Identify and quantify structural redundancy and stability.
Analyze complex indeterminate structures using both flexibility(displacement) and stiffness methods
Determine where to place a moveable load to produce the maximum effect at a point in a structure (Influence Lines)
Develop competency in the use of MATLAB to perform structural analysis using the Stiffness Matrix Method
Develop competency in the use of a commercial structural analysis program.

Tentative Course Schedule

Assignments:

  • There will be regular assignments throughout the course.
  • The purpose of assignments is to help you engage and stay up to date with the course lecture material.
  • You will have at least 6 days to complete each assignment.
  • All assignments will be completed in groups of 4. The same group will be used for all assignments and for the class project.
  • Assignments will be graded partially for completeness and partially in detail.
  • It is expected that each member of the group will make a full effort to complete and contribute to all parts of each assignment. As such, the inability of any single member to contribute to the completion and on-time submission of an assignment is an insufficient reason for a group not to complete an assignment.
  • Only if all members of a group are unable to complete an assignment for a documented valid reason will accommodation be granted.
  • Accommodation will involve moving the relevant weight of the item to the final exam, so as not to delay the timely release of assignment solutions.
  • Assignments will be submitted via Crowdmark and Learn

 

Mini-project:

  • To be completed in groups of 4. The same groups will be used for the assignments.
  • The focus of the project is on the application and use of commercial structural analysis software, in this case SAP2000.
  • It is expected that each member of the group will make an effort to complete and contribute to the project over several weeks. As such, the inability of any single member to fully contribute to the completion and on-time submission of a project is an insufficient reason for a group not to submit the project on time.
  • Only if all members of a group are unable to complete the project on time for a documented valid reason will accommodation be granted. Accommodation may involve moving the weight to the final exam.
  • More information to follow.

 

Group Work:

  • Professional practice in architecture, design, or engineering takes place through close collaborations between teams of people. Identifying effective ways to communicate, assign responsibilities, identify milestones, and achieve objectives are essential skills to succeed in professional practice. For these reasons, the assignments and the mini-project will require students to work in teams throughout the term. This collaboration should allow for intensive work and iteration to take place in parallel and for learning to be shared. All members of each group will be graded equally.
  • Collaboration between groups (or members of different groups) are not permitted on assignments and the mini-project.
  • Students shall register their groups with TA Mi Zhou by email by 4pm on Thursday January 9th. One email should be sent, with all 4 group members copied on the message. The message should include a list of the names and student number of each group member. Do not send Mi a group with fewer or more than 4 students.
  • If you struggle to find a complete group, there will be an opportunity at the end of class on Thursday Jan 9th.
  • Any student who is has not be registered in a group by 4pm Jan 9th, will be assigned to a group by the instructor.

Generative AI

Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
