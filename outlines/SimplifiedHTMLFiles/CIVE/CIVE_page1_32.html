Course Description

CIVE 542:

Pavement design, soil identification, subgrade design, base courses, flexible pavement design, design and testing of asphaltic concrete mixes, surface treatments.

View requirements for CIVE 542

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the major pavement types, their main functions, and importance
Be familiar with the general history of pavements and evolution of structural design methods
Understand the basics of pavements mechanics and critical structural responses
Understand the key concepts of pavement materials and their characterization
Apply a variety of analytical, and numerical/mathematical methods to solve problems about stress, strain, and displacement distribution within pavement layers
Apply load equivalency theories to compare damage from different vehicles
Be able to apply AASHTO 1993 design method for both flexible and rigid pavements
Be able to use RTAC and Asphalt Institute method to design flexible pavements
Apply proper analytical methods to determine Traffic Loading on pavement structures
Have a working knowledge of KENPAVE and AASHTOWare software packages
Understand the Mechanistic-Empirical Pavement Design (MEPDG) framework
Understand the major failure mechanisms for flexible and rigid pavements and distresses
Be able to apply the basic principles of engineering economics to evaluate the Life Cycle Cost Analysis (LCCA) and compare different paving scenarios.
Have the means to think critically about a pavement engineering problem and evaluate the accuracy and validity of results from the design methods

Assignments

This course has three assignments that will be submitted to Crowdmark, and some questions may require submission of other file formats to LEARN. The assignment deadlines are listed on the Course Schedule. 

Assignment late penalties are 10% for <24 hours late, and 30% for > 24 and <48 hours late. Assignments over 48 hours late will not be accepted. Exceptions to this late policy are (1) an approved accommodation provided by the course instructor, or (2) students claiming their grace days. 

Each student has two grace days to claim on assignment deadlines throughout the term. The student must send a short email to the instructor before the due date, copying the TA in the email, and expressing their intention to use their Grace Day. Each claimed grace day removes the late penalty for that day, but each assignment is still required to be submitted by the 48 hours after the deadline. Assignment solutions must be laid out properly, with all steps shown, answers clearly identified, and diagrams properly labeled. Uploads to Crowdmark must be of good quality, legible and properly organized. 

Solutions using KENPAVE, AASHTOWare or a spreadsheet may require submission of the files or spreadsheet (this will be specified on the assignment). Teaching team assignment solutions will be posted on LEARN on the Mon morning after the due date (unless otherwise deemed necessary). 

Crowdmark and DropBox (via Learn) will be mainly used to distribute and collect assignments. It is your responsibility to make sure that your work is properly uploaded by the deadline. Assignments are due by 5:00PM of the date indicated for each assignment. 

Term Project Case Study

As part of the course work, a case study focusing on pavement design using AASHTOWare software and other tools will be defined as the term project to be done individually. A description of the term project will be provided separately on LEARN. The term project will be used as a means for assessment, to be done individually or small group of students (Maximum of two people). These assessments will be delivered by and submitted to Dropbox. 

Final Exam

   This course has a 2.5‐hour final closed book exam scheduled through the Office of Registrar's (TBA). As with assignments, uploaded solutions must be of good quality, legible and properly organized.   The final exam will be cumulative, including topics covered in the assignments and lectures. . You must pass the final exam to pass the course. As a results, a student who receives a grade lower than 50% in the final exam will get an overall grade of 49 or lower.  More details on the topics covered, structure and allowable aids will be provided before the assessments.

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as critical thinking, engineering judgment, engineering design, problem solving, and professional engineering practices. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
