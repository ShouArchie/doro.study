Course Description

PMATH 445 / PMATH 745:

Basic definitions and examples: subrepresentations and irreducible representations, tensor products of representations. Character theory. Representations as modules over the group ring, Artin-Wedderburn structure theorem for semisimple rings. Induced representations, Frobenius reciprocity, Mackey's irreducibility criterion.

PMATH 445: View requirements for PMATH 445

PMATH 745: View requirements for PMATH 745

Learning Outcomes

In this course, you will explore:
The classical representation and character theory of finite groups
Various topics in multilinear algebra, including tensor, symmetric and exterior products
The theory of modules and algebras
The Artin-Weddernburn theory of semisimple rings

Tentative Course Schedule

Basics of representation theory and character theory (~6 weeks)
Module theory (~2 weeks)
Artin-Wedderburn theory (~3 weeks)
Further topics (~1 week)

Administrative Policy

Group Work Policy: You are encouraged to discuss assignments problems with your classmates. The discussion should be at a high level and you should not take notes during such discussions. Do not share or copy complete solutions. All submitted work must be written up individually. Collaborators and any other outside sources of help must be properly acknowledged.

Late Assignment Policy: -1% per minute late. No extensions (beyond the best-6-of-7 policy).

Missed Assignment Policy: With instructor-approved excuse (e.g. documented illness), weight will be shifted to the other assignments. No extensions or make-up assignments.

Missed Test Policy: With instructor-approved excuse (e.g. documented illness), weight will be shifted to final exam. No make-up tests.
