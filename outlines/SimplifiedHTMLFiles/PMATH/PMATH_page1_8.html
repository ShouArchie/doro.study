Course Description

PMATH 334:

Rings, ideals, factor rings, homomorphisms, finite and infinite fields, polynomials and roots, field extensions, algebraic numbers, and applications, for example, to Latin squares, finite geometries, geometrical constructions, error-correcting codes.

View requirements for PMATH 334

This course provides an introduction to the fundamental algebraic structures of rings and fields, with an emphasis on their properties and applications. Topics include polynomial rings, field extensions, and their connections to areas such as number theory and geometry.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Memerize fundamental concepts of algebraic structures, including groups, rings, integral domains, fields, vector space, etc. By the end of the semester, student should be able to identity whether a given set is a ring or field.
Analyze structures of rings and fields using homomorphisms. Prove two structures are isomorphic.
Analyze and identify reducibility of a polynomial over a field
Understand field extension and be able to construct extension fields
Understand some standard algebra proof techniques. Be able to produce (or reproduce) the proof of certain propositions.

Assignments

There will be a total of 6 graded assignments, that must be scanned and submitted electronically via Crowdmark. Only the 5 highest assignment scores will be counted, regardless of whether a student missed an assignment. Detailed instructions are available on LEARN, and students must follow them carefully. 

Assignments will be posted biweekly, and students will typically have about two weeks to complete each one. Solutions will be posted within 3 days after the submission deadline. Our goal is to return grades and feedback within one week of the submission deadline.

While collaboration in understanding the material is encouraged, the work submitted must be your own, written independently. Do not seek out solutions from the Internet or printed sources. Refer to the Academic Integrity FAQ on LEARN for more details.

Late Submission Policy:
Late submissions are accepted with a penalty of 1% per minute for the entire assignment, starting immediately after the deadline. For example, submitting 60 minutes late will result in a maximum possible score of 40%, assuming a perfect score. Only one submission is allowed after the deadline, and this is only possible if no submissions were made before the deadline. Ensure your submission is complete by uploading all files and clicking the "submit" button.

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as reading and writing proofs. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
