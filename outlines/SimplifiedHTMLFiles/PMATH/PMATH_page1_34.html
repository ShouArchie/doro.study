Course Description

PMATH 441 / PMATH 741:

An introduction to algebraic number theory; unique factorization, Dedekind domains, class numbers, Dirichlet's unit theorem, solutions of Diophantine equations.

PMATH 441: View requirements for PMATH 441

PMATH 741: View requirements for PMATH 741

Learning Outcomes

No explicit learning outcomes defined for this course.

Tentative Course Schedule

  • Rings of integers, Dedekind domains, and unique factorization of ideals
  • The ideal class group
  • Computing the ring of integers in number field
  • Kummer’s theorem on factoring ideals
  • The Splitting of primes
  • Cyclotomic Fields
  • Minkowski’s geometry of numbers
  • Dirichlet’s Unit Theorem

Student Assessment

PMATH 441
Component Value
Written Assignments 70%
Midterm Exam 15%
Final Exam 15%
PMATH 741
Component Value
Written Assignments 70%
Midterm Exam 10%
Final Exam 10%
Presentation 10%
  • Exams: Both Midterm exam and Final exam are held in classes. Tentatively, the midterm exam will be held on February 19, 2025 and the Final exam will be held on April 2, 2025. I plan to have both exams are open book. However, all electronic devices and calculators are prohibited.
  • Assignments: There will be roughly 10 written assignments. Written assignments will be submitted through Crowdmark which will be typically due on Friday. Late submission is not accepted. If you missed the deadline or you are not able to complete the assignments within due date, you must contact me immediately. This process must be done before a suggested solution will be posted on Learn site. In addition to written assignments, I will also have extra practice problems which I will regularly add to a file on Learn throughout the term. These practice problems will not be required to hand in.
  • Presentation: Students who take PMATH741 are asked to choose one question from practice problems to give a short presentation at some point towards the end of the term.
  • Regrade request: If you feel there has been an error in the marking of your written assignment or exam, you have one week from the time you receive your graded work to request a regrade. Your regrade request should be directed to the instructor, and should include the question(s) you would like regraded as well as a brief justification as to why you believe a regrade is necessary. Regrade requests received after one week has elapsed will not be considered.
