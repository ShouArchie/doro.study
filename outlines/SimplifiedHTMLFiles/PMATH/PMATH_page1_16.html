Course Description

PMATH 352:

Analytic functions, Cauchy-Riemann equations, Goursat's theorem, Cauchy's theorems, Morera's theorem, Liouville's theorem, maximum modulus principle, harmonic functions, Schwarz's lemma, isolated singularities, Laurent series, residue theorem.

View requirements for PMATH 352

Complex numbers provide the natural setting in which to study differentiable functions. Cauchy's theorem plays a central role in this subject, and many important results follow from it: Cauchy's integral formula, the theory of Taylor and Laurent series, Cauchy's residue theorem, the argument principle, the fundamental theorem of algebra, the maximum modulus theorem, Mittag-Lefler expansions, and Weierstrass products. We’ll start the course with a basic review of complex numbers, and the complex exponential and logarithm, complex analytic functions, and make our way towards Cauchy's theorem and its wonderful consequences.

My lectures will draw on a number of books and sources, but I recommend Knopp's Elements of the Theory of Functions, and his Theory of Functions Parts I and II. Another reference is Marsden and Hoffman's Basic Complex Analysis. Murray R. Spiegel’s Complex Analysis (Schaum’s Outline Series) is a good source for seeing the theory summarized with lots of worked out problems.

Tentative Course Schedule

We will develop topics in approximately this order:

1) review: complex numbers, properties, sequences, limits, continuity.

2) complex exponential, sine, cosine, logarithm, powers

3) analytic functions, Cauchy Riemann equations

4) complex differentiation

5) level curves

6) complex integration

7) Cauchy's Theorem

8) Cauchy's Integral Formulas

9) Path Independence, Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

10) Liouville's Theorem, Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

11) Gauss Mean Value Theorem, Max/Min Modulus Theorems

12) Morera's Theorem

13) Sequences and series of analytic functions, Taylor series

14) Laurent series

15) Isolated singularities

16) Residue Theorem

17) Argument principle

18) Rouche's Thorem

Possible additional topics chosen from: Linear Fractional Transformations, Schwarz's lemma, Gamma function, Mittag-Leffler expansions, Weierstrass Products
