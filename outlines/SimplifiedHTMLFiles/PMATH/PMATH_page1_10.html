Course Description

PMATH 340:

An elementary approach to the theory of numbers; the Euclidean algorithm, congruence equations, multiplicative functions, solutions to Diophantine equations, continued fractions, and rational approximations to real numbers.

View requirements for PMATH 340

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Solve linear diophantine equations
Find rational approximations to irrational numbers
Compute continued fraction expansions of rational numbers
Convince their friends and family that number theory is excellent

Tentative Course Schedule

The following is a rough guide of what topics will be uncovered as the course progresses. It is by no means set in stone, and will very likely change as the term progresses.

Divisibility, the Euclidean algorithm, solving linear diophantine equations, continued fractions, rational approximations to real numbers, primes, modular arithmetic, groups of units, sums of squares.

Grading Schemes:

Grading Scheme 2 is only available as an option for you if you take all three quizzes and score above 75% in each quiz.

If you qualify for Scheme 2, you may still choose to sit the final exam and use Scheme 1 as your grading scheme. However, if you choose to sit the final, then you do not have an option to use Scheme 2 (even if Scheme 2 ends up giving you a higher mark).

Assignments:

You may complete each assignment in a group of up to three people. Your groups must be declared before submitting each assignment. You do not have to stay with the same group from assignment to assignment, and you may choose to complete an assignment alone. (See LEARN and attend the lectures for instructions on how to do this).

Each Assignment will be worth 10% and is to be submitted through Crowdmark by 6:00pm on the due date (which is always a Friday). The due dates for the assignments are:

A1 - 24th Jan
A2 - 7th Feb
A3 - 28th Feb
A4 - 14th Mar
A5 - 28th Mar

Quizzes:

The Quizzes will be worth 5% each. They will be 45 minutes long and take place during the lecture time on the following dates (always Friday):

Q1 - 31st Jan
Q2 - 7th Mar
Q3 - 4th Apr

There will be no midterms! Hooray!

Final:

The final exam (for those who take the exam, see the "Grading Schemes" section above), will be scheduled during exam period.

Missed Assessments and Late Submission Policy:

In case of a missed assessment, you should notify your instructor via email within 24 hours of the assessment's completion/due date in order to receive an accommodation. To be eligible for an accommodation, you must either use the self-declaration form on Quest or, if the assessment was missed due to illness, you must submit a Verification of Illness form (https://uwaterloo.ca/math/vif-submission). If you do not send an email informing the instructor of your absence, your grade will be automatically recorded as zero. Properly documented absences will result in the following accommodations:

  • Quizzes: The weight will be shifted to the other Quizzes.
  • Assignments: A 24-48 hour extension will be provided, as appropriate.

If you miss a quiz you will not be eligible for grading scheme 2 (even if an accommodation is provided).
