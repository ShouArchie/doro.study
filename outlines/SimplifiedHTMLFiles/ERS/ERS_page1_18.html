ERS 375

Sustainable Procurement and the Circular Economy: A Guide for Sustainability Professionals

Sustainable Procurement is the means by which organizations – corporations, SMEs, NGOs, public sector institutions – manage their supply chains to reduce emissions, waste, pollution and ecological footprint, whilst expanding the circular economy. Requiring close collaboration and long-term partnerships, Sustainable Procurement is one of the fastest growing areas of corporate governance. Addressing both legal requirements and competitive pressures relating to ESG (Environmental and Social Governance), large organisations competing with each other to hire trained procurement specialists. Based on a UK-based and world-leading IEMA (Institute for Environmental Management and Assessment short course aimed at industry professionals, this module will provide expanded version with more background material including ecological economic understanding of supply chain metabolism, circular economy and Canadian context and examples. Offering one of the most complete overviews of the field in Canada, the module will provide an excellent foundation for aspiring sustainability professionals. 

 

The course text is Sustainable Procurement by Jonathan O'Brien

 

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain what Sustainable Procurement
Understand its relation to the broader frameworks of ecological economics and the circular economy
Define key Sustainable Procurement terms and definitions
Identify the sustainability impacts and considerations across the different aspects of procurement activity
Support the implementation and auditing of sustainable procurement
Conduct internal audits against the requirements of ISO 20400.

Tentative Course Schedule

The course will be delivered in 12 sessions

 

PART 1: Course introduction

  • Key terms and definitions

PART 2: What is sustainability and why is it important? (O’Brien CH 1-3)

  • Biosphere: The state of the earth

PART 3: What is sustainability and why is it important? Society: 

  • The condition of people and communities 
  • Scenarios

PART 4: Ecological economics and circularity

  • Key terms and definitions (ISO20400: 2017 Clause 3)
  • Ecological economics and the supply chain
  • Sustainable Development and Circular Economy

PART 5: Overview of Sustainable Procurement 

  • Overview (O’Brien Ch 4)
  • Business case (O’Brien Ch 5)
  • ISO20400: 2017 Clause 4

PART 6: Setting the Direction  

  • Frameworks and targets (O’Brien Ch 6)
  • ISO20400: 2017 Clause 4

PART 7: Integrating sustainability considerations into procurement strategy (Clause 5)

  • Supply base evaluation (O’Brien Ch7)
  • Prioritization (O’Brien Ch 8) 
  • ISO20400 Clause 5

PART 8: Organisational conditions and management techniques needed to implement and improve sustainable procurement (Clause 6) 

  • Project evaluation (O’Brien Ch 9)
  • ISO20400: 2017 Clause 6

PART 9:  Sustainable Purchasing (O'Brien Chapter 10)

PART 10: Integrating sustainability considerations into procurement operations (Clause 7)

  • Driving sustainability in suppliers (O’Brien Ch11)
  • Auditing suppliers (O’Brien Ch 12)
  • ISO20400: 2017, Clause 7

PART 11:  Making it Happen

  • Driving sustainability in the supply chain (O’Brien Ch 13)
  • Measuring outcomes and success (O’Brien Ch 14)

PART 12: Sustainable procurement and Environmental Assessment in Canada

Administrative Policy

You are limited to one of these per term, and can only use it during the formal lecture period. Note that you cannot use this absence for consideration in FLD, LAB, or STU components of your course.
