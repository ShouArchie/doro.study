Course Description

ENVS 278:

This course examines further techniques for collecting, evaluating, and using data-based evidence in environmental research. It builds upon ENVS 178, with a focus on inferential statistics, including sampling distributions, confidence intervals, parametric and nonparametric hypothesis tests, and linear regression models. It further develops skills in using statistical software for data analysis and modeling of environmental data.

Calendar Description for ENVS 278

This course examines further techniques for collecting, evaluating, and using data-based evidence in environmental research. It builds upon ENVS 178, with a focus on inferential statistics, including sampling distributions, confidence intervals, parametric and nonparametric hypothesis tests, and linear regression models. It further develops skills in using statistical software for data analysis and modeling of environmental data.

Prereq: ENVS 178; Environment students only. Antireq: ARTS 280, ECON 221, SDS 250R, PSCI 314, PSYCH 292, REC 371, SOC 280, STAT 202, 206, 211, 221, 231, 241

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Define and understand key terminology and concepts.
Select and apply suitable statistical tools for a variety of data analysis problems.
Interpret and critically evaluate the results of statistical analyses.
Use statistical software to perform data selected analysis tasks.

How to succeed in ENVS 278:

Each week the course covers a different statistical method for a different type of application. To be successful, students are expected to spend 5-7 hours each week completing the following tasks:

  1. Attend the two weekly lectures. Lecture 1 (Monday) is a content lecture where new concepts are introduced and explained. Lecture 2 (Wednesday) helps solidify foundational understanding of the content for each module, through a series of examples and demonstrations. Participate in class using Vevox (accessed through LEARN).
  2. Complete the MyLab homework to solve a series of practice problems and solidify learning. Multiple attempts are allowed on each assessment.
  3. Attend and participate in a Problem-Solving Session (Tuesday; guided by a TA) that focuses on interpreting and communicating the output from a statistical analysis.
  4. Post additional questions on the Discussion Forum on LEARN for additional support, review the relevant textbook chapters where needed.

The course content is incremental: one week’s material builds on the previous week’s. This makes it more difficult to catch up after missing a class or two, and so it makes good sense to keep on top of the workload each week. This course has a reputation for being “difficult”, but I think that means it forces students out of their comfort zone. History has shown that all students have the potential to do well in this class, regardless of their background in math and statistics, if they commit to applying themselves consistently throughout the term. While ENVS278 requires more math than other courses in the Faculty, the math is Grade 9-10 level in Ontario high schools, and the Instructor and TAs are here to assist you. If you complete all the activities each week, you will gain confidence by seeing that solving statistical problems is actually not that difficult at all.

 

More about the tests:

Two non-cumulative tests will assess student progress on material covered up to and including the week before the test. The tests will be conducted in-person during the Thursday class time. It may include calculation and short-answer questions, and will assess student’s ability to interpret the output from statistical analyses. Some calculations may be required during the tests, and students may use a calculator to complete them. 

As part of the test booklet, students will be provided all relevant statistical tables and equations. 

More about the final exam:

A compulsory cumulative final examination (date to be announced in the middle of term) will examine students’ knowledge of all concepts covered in the class. 

Students may bring one DOUBLE-SIDED handwritten page of study notes into the final exam room (US letter paper 8.5x11in, DOUBLE-sided, no flaps, no photocopies). The study notes may contain any materials that the student chooses. Note that students are not expected to include statistical tables as part of their study notes (the tables take up too much space). If any statistical tables are required for the exam, they will be provided to students on the question booklet.

 

What happens if I miss a class or an assessment item?

Life happens. Life is important. This course employs a flexible grading scheme to help alleviate some of the stress and anxiety associated with school. Sometimes we all need time away from school or work for a multitude of reasons: because we get sick, due to a family bereavement, or perhaps a friend or relative needs our support. I hope your term goes smoothly, and that you don’t need to take any time off. But if you do, for whatever reason, then you shouldn’t need to worry about school; it’s just not as important as those other things. So, if you need to take time off during term, then please just take it. You don’t need to contact me (unless you want to), and I don’t need to see any doctor’s notes, or other documentation.

The following apply equally to all students:

  • If you need to miss a MyLab quiz, then just miss it: the three lowest quiz grades (including zeros) will be automatically dropped for all students.
  • If you need to miss a Problem-Solving Session, then just miss it: the three lowest grades (including zeros) will be automatically dropped for all students.
  • If you need to miss one test, then just miss it: the weight of each student’s lowest test will automatically be redistributed to the other course assessment items proportional to each item's contribution to the final grade. The reweighting is a complicated calculation, so it's not easy to predict in advance what any student's final grade will be. However, a student's grade will never go down as a result of reweighting, and most students will see a modest increase in their grade. The reweighting is applied to all students' marks at the end of term, regardless of whether they wrote the test, or not.

Due to the size of the class no extensions will be offered for any assessment item, and there are no alternative (make-up) dates to sit the tests or final exam. On rare occasions, students may require more than the default accommodation provided through the flexible grading scheme. Any student requesting additional accommodation must contact the Instructor at their earliest convenience, and in most cases a voice or video meeting will be required to work out the details of the accommodation.

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as [fill this in with your discipline-specific skills]. You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence

Policy on regrading assignments: If you notice an error in the assessment of your work please follow these steps:

  • Wait 48 hours after the assignment was returned before contacting a member of the instruction team.

  • The entire assignment will be regraded, not the just the errors indicated in the written request.

The resulting grade may increase or decrease depending on the result of the regrading.

Absences

There are three types of absences which entitle students to academic considerations.

Short term (2 day) self-declared absences: undergraduate students are entitled to one two-day absence per term during the formal lecture period only. If students need academic considerations for tests or other assessments due during this period, they must contact you within 24 hours of declaring the absence. Students declare short-term absences via Quest. Students requesting extensions for personal reasons should be directed to this absence.

Covid self-declared absences: for students with symptoms of Covid-19, one absence up to ten days is available per term, including the examination period. Students can only use this for known or suspected Covid; all other illnesses requiring considerations must be documented. Students need to contact you for academic consideration as soon as possible. It is possible for a student to declare for fewer than 10 days, and then extend the absence if symptoms have not improved during the shorter absence. While this absence is available for Fall 2024, it will be discontinued at some point in the future.

Documented absences, including Verification of Illness: for illnesses other than Covid which require consideration, as well as other documented circumstances (e.g. funerals). Students submit documentation to vif.uwaterloo.ca, and absences are approved/denied on a case by case basis. Documented absences can occur during both the lecture and exam period, and there is no formal limit on the number of days a student can miss. If a student misses enough time that you are concerned that they cannot feasibly achieve the learning outcomes, please contact the associate dean, undergraduate studies. Students should not submit documentation to course instructors, please do not accept this. Students need to contact instructors for academic considerations as soon as feasible under their individual circumstances.

All absences for students in your class can be viewed at vif.uwaterloo.ca
