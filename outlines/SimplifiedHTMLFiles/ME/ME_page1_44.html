Course Description

ME 557:

Combustion thermodynamics, introduction to chemical kinetics of combustion, combustion properties of fuels, flammability of combustible mixtures. Flame propagation mechanisms, pre-mixed and diffusional; stability of flames; introduction to combustion aerodynamics, jet flames; atomization; droplet and spray combustion. Elementary ignition concepts and theory. Basic detonation theory.

View requirements for ME 557

 

Course Objectives:

  • to provide the basic concepts of combustion
  • to apply these concepts to solve practical problems
  • to develop a systematic methodology for the solution of practical problems

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
• Calculate the temperature and compositions (for both major and minor species) in the combustion gas mixture, the amount of energy released during combustion process, and the amount of harmful emissions formed/released during/from combustion process
• Analyze the rate of fuel consumption and rate of heat release during combustion process
• Describe the different modes of combustion (detonation, deflagration, premixed flames and diffusion flames), ignition and extinction phenomena
• Understand how pollutant emissions are formed and could be controlled in a combustion process/device/system
