Course Description

ME 599:

Various courses dealing with selected topics at the undergraduate level in automation and control, solid mechanics and machine design, materials engineering and processing, fluid mechanics, and thermal engineering. Courses offered when resources permit.

View requirements for ME 599

Become knowledgeable about the automotive industry's vehicle design process following the automotive industry’s hybrid, autonomous and connected, vehicle design.  Such design includes but is not limited to simulation, hands on implementation, industry grade equipment use, application of rapid prototyping processes, and reviews by industry experts.  Students will be exposed to controls, mechanical systems, electrical systems, human-machine interface using the experiential design process (very different from fourth year design project design processes), connected autonomous/automated vehicle technologies (CAVs), emissions, software development, and consumer acceptability considerations.  Learning mode is Project Based.  Students will focus on and implement one or more UWAFT sub-projects selected to match students interest.

The scope of the project is significantly open. The idea is to allow students to pursue projects of their interest, while gaining experience in the automotive industry and furthering the team. The projects will need to follow the EcoCAR EV Challenge timeline and not inhibit the completion of other deliverables.  Teamwork and integration will be incorporated into the grades for the project. Students must be self-driven and comfortable with self-learning to successfully complete their project.

The project is in the area of improvement and innovation. The competition goal is to re-engineer a 2023 Cadillac Lyriq to reduce emissions, drive autonomously, maintain performance and customer acceptability. 

There are two parts to ME 599, those who have completed ME 599-1 in the previous term will do ME 599-2. The timeline and grade breakdown outline the difference between the two sections. The exact due dates will be discussed in class and through Slack.

ME599- Part 1 students will have to attend a 1 hour lecture every week. ME 599- Part 2 students do not have lectures however the weight of the course is more on their project presentation and project report with higher expectations set for ME599- Part 2 projects.

Non-disclosure agreements (NDA) must be signed and submitted to get access to confidential information such as CAD, component information etc. ME 599-2 students do not need to re submit it if they have already done so in ME 599-1. The process for this can be retrieved by downloading the UWAFT Guidebook pinned to the uwaftecocar.slack.com General channel.

 

Project Proposal

  1. Students will define their project details based on the topic given.
  2. Timeline for the project must be established, including milestones and deliverables. 
    Note: It is imperative to be realistic with these milestones and deliverables as they will be checked at each Review Meeting (Scrum Meeting) for progress. Project scope moving forward can be re-evaluated and adjusted only with permission. Inability to deliver on time will result in mark penalties for the review meeting. No exceptions, late is late, just as in any other technical elective.
  3. The team will review the proposal to ensure that it is within the scope of the term and that the student will be capable of completing the project. It should be no more than 5 pages of text (figures do not count towards this page limit), see the marking scheme below.

Review Meetings

UWAFT follows an agile development workflow. Review meetings will occur 3 times over the term (3 week sprints). Procedure is as follows:

  1. Prepare slide deck presenting work that has occurred in previous sprint. Aesthetics of this presentation will NOT be graded, only the content. This content may include screenshots, embedded demo videos of simulations etc. Anything to demonstrate your work done to date is helpful.
  2. Submit slide deck at 11:59pm on Learn dropbox the day prior to your scheduled review meeting.
  3. Attend meeting to answer questions about slide deck and discuss progress in achieving milestones
  4. Reaffirm (and potentially set new) goals for the next sprint.

Attendance/Discretion

Students are expected to be spending at least 8h a week on the course in-person in order to get more fully immersed in team operations. If you wish to interact with a TA reach out to them individually about their in-person availability. Attendance/discretion grade will be based on the participation in the team meetings and staying in touch with the TAs, and ensuring milestones are being met as per projects outlined in the project proposal at the start of the term. If there is schedule conflict with the biweekly subteam meetings, it is student’s responsibility to report their project status to their team lead.

Project Presentation

The student is to present their work to the class near the end of the term similar to the presentation for the final year design project (FYDP).

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used for assignments in this class with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Recommendations for how to cite GenAI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
