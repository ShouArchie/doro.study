Course Description

ME 563:

Classification of turbomachines, performance parameters and laws of modelling. Basic equation of flow in turbomachines, compressible flow. Energy transfer in radial and axial turbomachines, performance characteristics, losses, and efficiencies. Blade and cascade design, 3-dimensional effects.

View requirements for ME 563

This course will examine and evaluate various types of rotating fluid machinery such as pumps, fans, compressors, turbines, hydraulic turbines and wind turbines. The course will progress from specifying a machine for a system through dimensionless parameters, the Euler turbomachine equations, 1 D flow paths to 2D and complex flow analysis. There is a mixture of analysis and design throughout the course.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the terminology, general flow paths, and describe the physical layout of common turbomachinery Determine system behavior for an incompressible system (pipes or ducts) and specify machine requirements
Describe important dimensionless groups common in turbomachinery and utilize them to characterize machines Apply dimensional analysis techniques to determine performance parameters
Comprehend concepts of 1st and 2nd law of thermodynamics, Angular Momentum, moment of momentum, and efficiency to analyze flow paths for turbomachines
Develop 1D Velocity diagrams, and apply the Euler turbomachine equation to performance analysis
Utilize tools developed above to analyze turbomachinery including fans, compressors, turbines, and pumps Describe the terminology and physical layout of wind turbines
Comprehend some basic aerodynamics to understand wind turbine energy extraction
Utilize the conservation laws (continuity, momentum, energy) to analyze wind turbines
Develop a blade element momentum(BEM) procedure to analyze wind turbine performance

Assignments

The assignments will involve the use of lecture material and allow further understanding of the topics covered. Due dates for assignments will be given in LEARN. The assignments must be done individually. You may not communicate directly or indirectly with any person except the course instructor. Some online material as references may be allowed but use of online tutoring or solution sites is prohibited (obviously).

Project 1 and Project 2

The projects will involve the use of lecture material and other references to complete a study of a turbomachine of your choice (see Project outline). Due dates for individual components of the project will be given and a formal final report will be submitted containing all the individual components. This projects must be done individually. You may not communicate directly or indirectly with any person except the course instructor.

Mid Term Test

The mid term test will be an examination of the material covered up to the test date (further information on LEARN). The test will be timed release and will be completed individually. You may not communicate directly or indirectly with any person except the course instructor. The use of on-line resources of any kind is strictly prohibited with the exception of material on LEARN for this course. Test date will be scheduled during week of  February 10-14, 2025

Final Assessment

The Final exam will be an examination of the material covered in the course. The exam will be scheduled by the registrar's office. This exam must be done individually. You may not communicate directly or indirectly with any person except the course instructor. The use of on-line resources of any kind is strictly prohibited with the exception of material on LEARN for this course.

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
