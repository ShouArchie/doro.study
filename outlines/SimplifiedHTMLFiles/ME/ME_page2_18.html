Course Description

ME 739:

View requirements for ME 739

  1. Basic of additive manufacturing, classification, trends and opportunities
  2. Basics of lasers used in additive manufacturing and material processing
  3. Interaction of the laser beam with materials 
  4. Modeling of laser additive manufacturing
  5. Laser-based metal additive manufacturing 
  6. Laser microscale and nanoscale additive manufacturing technologies  
  7. Sensing, monitoring, real-time control and quality assurance for additive manufacturing
  8. Case studies: Design and Topology Optimization, Biomedical and industrial applications of additive manufacturing

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain foundational concepts of additive manufacturing
Describe the basics of lasers and their types
Analyze laser-material interactions in additive manufacturing
Conduct basic modeling of laser-based additive manufacturing processes
Understand applications of additive manufacturing across industries
Understand and select appropriate sensors for additive manufacturing

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assiignment 25%
Project 60%
Presentation related to the project 15%

Grading Policy:

  1. 25% assignment: One assignment will be given in early Feb, due in two weeks. The assignment will be mainly concerned with conceptual questions and analytical modeling. The assignment must be done individually. The assignment solution should electronically be uploaded to Dropbox of LEARN. 

 

  1. 60% project:  Two students may select a topic for their project. Individual student projects are also allowed. Five preferable topics must be selected by January 30, 2025, from the list of available topics provided. To prevent duplication, I will choose your topic from the list and let you know the topic before February 7, 2025. The projects are concerned with a thorough literature review of the selected topics. At least 25 relevant articles must be summarized, critically analyzed, and compared. New tables and figures may be developed for the comparison purpose. The project report must be uploaded to Dropbox on LEARN no later than April 11, 2025. The report format can be in any standard reporting template; however, the number of characters (excluding table of content, references and figures/tables’ captions) must not be less than 45000 (without space) for group reports. Use Arial or Times Roman font at 12 points with 1.5 line spacing. If one student decides to do his/her project individually, the number of characters must not be less than 22500 (without space). 

 

  1. 15% presentation related to the project topic: Each group will have ~25 minutes presentation on their project topic. If the project team includes two students, both must equally contribute to the presentation. The presentation will be held during the last three or four lectures depending on the number of students enrolled (e.g., March 20th, March 27th, and April 3rd).  The presentation slides must be uploaded to Dropbox on LEARN, before your presentation time slot. The presentations will be scored by your classmates and myself, who will evaluate your presentation for 1) Organization of presentation (20%); 2) Effective information/data related to the process background (30%); 3) Effective information/data related to the associated theory and applications (30%); and 4) Clarity and timing (20%). Your mark will be an average of all evaluation points.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as the development of scholarly work and presentation. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

Students are encouraged to reach out to campus supports if they need help with their coursework including: 

  • Student Success Office for help with skills like notetaking and time management
  • Writing and Communication Centre for assignments with writing or presentations
  • AccessAbility Services for documented accommodations
  • Library for research-based assignments
