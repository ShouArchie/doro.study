Course Description

ME 380:

In this course, students study the design process, including needs analysis, problem definition; design criteria and critical parameter identification, generation of alternative solutions; conceptual design, detailed design, optimization; and implementation. Most of the term is devoted to a significant design project in which student groups work independently and competitively, applying the design process to a project goal set by the faculty co-ordinator. The design project typically includes construction of a prototype, and part of the course grade may depend on the performance of the prototype in a competitive test. In exceptional circumstances, the requirement for a prototype may be replaced by a computer simulation, or may be waived. Other mechanical engineering faculty members, particularly those teaching 3B courses, are available to provide advice and supervision to ME380 students.

View requirements for ME 380

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Identify and formulate a practical mechanical engineering problem
Design a mechanical engineering system, product, or process
Implement and verify a design solution
Apply project management skills to develop and follow a project plan and budget
Work effectively as a member of a team, with responsibility and accountability
Communicate accurately and effectively

Design Logs and Weekly Meetings:

Design Logs: Groups will be required to submit three brief design logs at defined points throughout the term detailing the group’s design activities and project management. Each design log is submitted as a group (not individually). The purpose of the design logs is to track and assess progress of the design work completed by the group members throughout the term and receive feedback and guidance from the instructional team.  

The content of design logs should reflect evidence of the on-going design work using the Engineering Design Process. In evaluating the group’s capacity to reach their design goals, the design logs must provide project management updates related to the questions below (use of tables or charts is encouraged):

1. What individual or group project related tasks or activities did each team member do? How long did you spend on each activity? Is the activity on schedule and when will it be completed by?

2. Is the project on budget? Are resources being sourced and used appropriately?  

3. Are short and long-term project risks being considered? Have mitigation strategies been identified?

Design logs should be a concise summary over ~2-5 pages; use of descriptive bullet points, photos and figures is highly encouraged. Design logs will be evaluated based on project management and evidenced of design progress. The course grade component (15%) will be equally split over the three design logs; failed design logs will receive a grade of 0.  

Design logs must be submitted to Learn by the deadline (see Schedule).  

Weekly Meetings: Starting in Week 2, groups are required to meet with their assigned TA to provide and receive project feedback. Meetings will be scheduled in 10-minute blocks on Fridays between 2:30-4:30pm. Attendance at the weekly meeting is required for all group members each week (as part of the teamwork evaluation). You can miss up to one meeting without penalty (applied individually against the teamwork grade). Previous feedback on the weekly meetings has been very positive.

(3) Design Verification Presentation (25%)

Acknowledgement is typically stated in a separate section and/or referenced with a citation. To acknowledge individual and team contributions, all presentations must contain the following statement:

“We are the sole authors of this presentation and, unless otherwise stated and properly referenced, the entire content of this presentation is our original work. We have all contributed to the presentation and are aware of the content. The content of this presentation has not received credit in any other course that we have taken in the past or are currently taking at this time.”

The portfolio should provide a visual summary that contextualizes the contributions and technical details of their work. Each design portfolio must be signed by another group member as confirmation of the described contributions.

Project Description:

Each team is required to design, build, test, and verify the performance of a powered mechanism to move multiple objects from a storage area onto a raised conveyor belt to deliver to a drop-off area.

Project Requirements:

  1. Move 2 objects (one from red, one from blue) from the storage area to the drop-off area of the corresponding colour
    • Baseline objects to be moved should be a 3DBenchy (https://www.3dbenchy.com/3d-print/). Other objects can be used to demonstrate certain design objectives have been achieved 
  2. Demonstrate how your design meets your selected Design Objectives and Engineering Specifications

Project Constraints:

  • Energy must be supplied to the system using provided electrical power source(s) or a separate used console
  • Users cannot touch or make direct physical contact with the objects to assist with moving
  •  Pick-up and drop-off areas cannot be manipulated to assist with object pick-up or placement* 
  • No flying/launching/throwing designs 

Possible Design Objectives:

To encourage unique designs, each group must select two design objectives from the following list. You can suggest alternate design objectives for approval by the instructional team. Design objectives should be verified or demonstrated at the end of the term in addition to the project criteria/constraints listed above.

Possible Design Objectives:

  1. Design for speed to move 2+ objects 
  2. Design for accuracy/precision of object placement or storage in drop-off area
  3. Design for strength-weight ratio to lift and move heavier objects
  4. Design for efficiency to minimize the amount of energy or work required 
  5. Design for range of motion to pick up objects from multiple storage areas
  6. Design for dexterity to place objects on the conveyor in different orientations 
  7. Design for compliance to pick up difficult to grasp objects (beyond the baseline 3DBenchy)
  8. Design for smooth operation (e.g. minimize jerkiness)
  9. Design for adaptability to handle variable storage area conditions (e.g. mixed objects)

 

Project Scope: The individual design criterion and demonstration methods for the selected design objective are intended to be open-ended and up to the team to propose to the “client” (instructor and TAs). Teams are encouraged to draw inspiration from existing ideas/examples, as the idea does not have to be entirely original. All ideas should be properly referenced with specific rationale why a new design or advancement is needed and how the designed improvement adds value. Team projects will be guided and advised by the instructional team to assist with defining a suitable project scope. In general, the overall structure of the project should follow:

  • Identify the design need, define design requirements, and develop design specifications
  • Generate and assess design concepts
  • Embodiment and detailed design
  • Verify that the design meets the requirements

Administrative Policy

Recommendations for how to cite GenAI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. 

Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
