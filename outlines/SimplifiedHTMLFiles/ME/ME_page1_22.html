Course Description

ME 360:

Open loop and feedback control. Laws governing mechanical, electrical, fluid and thermal control components. Analogies. Analysis of some engineering control systems using block diagram algebra, transient and steady-state operation. Different modes of control. Review of Laplace Transform methods. Concepts of stability. Principles of analog computer simulation. Brief treatment of linear flow graphs and bondgraphs.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Be able to model linear and nonlinear dynamic systems using differential equations, Laplace Transforms, Laplace Transfer Functions, and block diagrams.
Be able to determine the poles and zeros of a system and understand how the poles and zeros determine the behaviour of the system.
Be able to determine the damping ratio, natural frequency, open-loop gain, closed-loop gain, steady-state error, rise time, and overshoot.
Be able to analyze the stability of a system.
Be able to design PID controllers based on the desired time response of a dynamic system.
Be able to design PID controllers based on the desired frequency response of a dynamic system.
Be able to apply root locus plots and bode plots to design PID Controllers

Midterm

If you do not write the midterm exam, then you will receive a mark of zero. If there are extenuating circumstances, submit appropriate documentation (VIF) to MME’s undergraduate office and then a deferred exam may be possible. For a self-declared short-term absence (two days) the weight of the midterm will be shifted to the final.

The midterm exam is two hours and is closed book. You will be provided with a formula sheet. Other allowed materials are a pencil, pen, eraser, pencil sharpener, and scientific calculator (nonprogrammable, non-graphical). Assignments, class notes, textbook, external resources, websites, electronic devices, laptops, phones, etc. are not permitted.

Final Exam

If you do not write the final exam then you will receive a grade of DNW (Did Not write = 32%). 

The final exam is 2.5 hours and is closed book. You will be provided with a formula sheet. Other allowed materials are a pencil, pen, eraser, pencil sharpener, and scientific calculator (nonprogrammable, non-graphical). Assignments, class notes, textbook, external resources, websites, electronic devices, laptops, phones, etc. are not permitted.
