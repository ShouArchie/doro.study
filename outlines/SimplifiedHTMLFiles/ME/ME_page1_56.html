Course Description

ME 598:

Various courses dealing with selected topics at the undergraduate level in automation and control, solid mechanics and machine design, materials engineering and processing, fluid mechanics, and thermal engineering. Courses offered when resources permit.

View requirements for ME 598

This course will introduce students to high performance fiber-reinforced plastic (FRP) composite materials from the perspective of material science, manufacturing processes, and mechanics, with an underlying emphasis on design of composite structures. The course is suitable for students without any prior knowledge of the field or that have some previous exposure (e.g., through a student design team).  Three important aspects of FRP composite materials will be covered. Part I of the course will introduce students to FRPs starting with an overview of the composition of different classes of these materials, while also focusing on different constituent phases that make up these materials and how they are manufactured. Part II of the course involves mechanics of FRPs, with focus on mechanical property assessment, strength prediction and theory of laminates. Part III of the course will focus on design of FRP laminated composite materials and structures where theory from Parts I and II will be applied.

No prerequisites. However, background knowledge of mechanics of deformable bodies, materials science, and manufacturing processes is required.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Identify the different types of FRP composite materials and their constituents
Understand the role/characteristics of the constituents and their influence on properties of composites
Understand various manufacturing processes for FRP composite materials and the influence of processing cycle parameters on potential defects
Predict the mechanical properties of FRP composites
Apply classical lamination theory to determine stresses and deformations of laminates
Design laminated FRP composite materials and corresponding components/structures to perform an intended function

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as [fill this in with your discipline-specific skills]. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
