Course Description

ME 351:

Physical properties of fluids and fundamental concepts in fluid mechanics. Hydrostatics. Conservation laws for mass, momentum and energy. Flow similarity and dimensional analysis as applied to engineering problems in fluid mechanics. Laminar and turbulent flow. Engineering applications such as flow measurement, flow in pipes, and fluid forces on moving bodies.

View requirements for ME 351

This course is an introduction to Fluid Mechanics.  It covers basic definitions and static fluids and then develops an analytical approach to analyze fluids in motion using the conservation equations.  Practical applications are evaluated using the conservation equations and correlations where appropriate.  Scaling and modeling are presented using a variable based approach.  Flow over external objects is examined and conservation equations and correlations are used to analyze these flows.  There is a mixture of analysis and design throughout the course. 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand and classify fluid flow regimes and discuss flow phenomena using appropriate vocabulary
Understand static fluids and manometry and determine forces in typical applications
Develop the Reynolds Transport Theorem and apply with vector relations to conservation laws
Apply the Continuity, Momentum and Energy equations to control volumes and systems
Determine system behavior for an incompressible fluid system (pipes or ducts)
Measure flow parameters and analyze experimental data
Describe important dimensionless groups and utilize them appropriately
Describe external flow and the associated dimensionless groups and determine forces in typical applications

Midterm Assessment

The midterm assessment will be an examination of the material covered in the previous weeks.  Exact content coverage will be provided.  The assessment will be scheduled by the department. This assessment must be done individually.  You may not communicate directly or indirectly with any person except the teaching team (course instructor and TA). Online: The use of on-line resources of any kind is strictly prohibited with the exception of material on LEARN for this course and the course textbook. In Person: Only a  non-programmable calculator will be allowed for the assessment.

Laboratory

The laboratory will be completed in a small group (3-4 students) and involves the collection and analysis of data related to fluid mechanics measurement.  Attendance at the laboratory is mandatory.  The lab times will be scheduled by the TAs and required information for completing the lab will be provided on LEARN. The standard operating procedure (SOP) for the lab is available on the MME SOP website under the name Air Board.  The use of on-line resources of any kind is strictly prohibited with the exception of material on LEARN for this course and the course textbook.  Required results and discussion will be submitted online through Crowdmark, or equivalent platform. A full lab report is not required. 

Regarding Self-Declared Absences: The University provides instructors with discretion to determine appropriate accommodations for cases of student absences in their courses. Where a student misses a term test (e.g. midterm exam) due to a self-declared absence, appropriate accommodation could include administration of a make-up test (written, oral, or hybrid).

Final Assessment

The Final exam will be an examination of the material covered in the course.  The exam will be scheduled by the registrar's office. This exam must be done individually.  You may not communicate directly or indirectly with any person except the teaching team (course instructor and TA).  Online: The use of on-line resources of any kind is strictly prohibited with the exception of material on LEARN for this course and the course textbook. In Person: Only a non-programmable calculator will be allowed for the assessment.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
