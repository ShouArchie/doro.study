Course Description

ME 597:

Various courses dealing with selected topics at the undergraduate level in automation and control, solid mechanics and machine design, materials engineering and processing, fluid mechanics, and thermal engineering. Courses offered when resources permit.

View requirements for ME 597

Course Overview

This course covers four fundamental topics of Autonomous Mobile Robotics; 1) modeling, 2) sensors and perception, 3) estimation (localization and mapping), 4) planning and navigation. Modeling covers kinematic (and/or dynamic) models of various types of wheeled mobile robots. Sensors include various types and algorithms (for vision and range sensors). Estimation includes Bayesian estimation theory and its application to localization (and/or mapping). Topics in Planning include odometry motion models and graph-based and probabilistic motion planning as well as obstacle avoidance (reaction-based) methods.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Develop fundamental understanding of essential components for autonomous mobile robotics
Understand motion and measurement models for a large variety of vehicles and sensors.
Understand localization/mapping algorithms for perception within environments.
Plan collision free paths through environments based on maps.
Automate the operation of a ground robot to enable navigating its way through an environment

Labs  

M/Th 5:30 ~ 8:00 pm @ E3-3178

Main lab activities will be administered using Turtlebot 4 and ROS2 (Robot Operating System 2). There will be several prelabs which will mainly be held through videos (with short homeworks). The in-person prelab will be held in Week 3 (a week before the first lab) to go over system setup. Labs will be conducted in groups of 3. Go to Learn => Connect => Available Groups to choose your lab group. Each group will be allowed to use one Turtlebot. The central portal for the lab is https://github.com/ME597c/ME597c-Students. The followings are tentative lab schedules:

① Online Prelabs (Weeks1,2,3 through video tutorials) – Introduction to Ubuntu, Python, and ROS 2, configuration settings, etc.

②  In-person Prelab (Week3) – System setup to be ready for Lab#1

③ Lab#1 (Week 4) – Sensor data processing

④ Lab#2 (Week 6) – Closed-loop controller

⑤  Lab#3 (Week 10) – Estimation (localization) and mapping: estimate vehicle motion from sensor data.

⑥ Lab#4 (Week 13) – Planning: using indoor positioning and a known map, plan a route from one point to another and execute the path with navigation (On-site evaluation)

Assignments

Analytical problems and coding (mostly with Matlab). About 4 exercises will be assigned.

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used for assignments in this class with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Recommendations for how to cite GenAI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
