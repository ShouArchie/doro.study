Course Description

MNS 331:

Mechanical, chemical and biological properties of biomaterials; relationships between biomaterials and their properties, functions, manufacture, and applications; applications of biomaterials in technologies, such as tissue engineering, prosthesis manufacture, drug delivery systems, bioreactors.

View requirements for MNS 331

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
recognize the various classes of commonly used biomaterials
understand their properties
understand their applications and practical aspects of their use

Administrative Policy

Public health Measures: Due to the current public health situation, the course will be conducted as synchronous online lectures via WebEX. The lectures will be available as recording afterwards. The lectures will switch to in-person, on campus lectures at the scheduled times as soon as the public health rules allow. All assignments will be available online. The term test is intended to be in-person, on campus. If required by public health measures, the test will be administered as synchronous online exam. 

Rules for Group Work in Assignments: All work for the course needs to be performed by each student on their own.

Late Submissions: Late submissions of the Research Paper Presentation will be accepted. All late submissions will have 20% of the mark deducted for each day or part of a day that the assignment is late. Assignments submitted after the last day of classes will receive a mark of 0%.
