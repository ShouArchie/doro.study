Course Description

MNS 431:

After a review of the essential concepts in nano-biomaterials, in-depth discussion includes two or three of the following topics: biomaterials on atomic and nanometre scales in which DNA, RNA, and/or polypeptides are incorporated as components; physical, chemical, and biological processes; nanoscale forces, adhesion, indentation; applications in nanomotors, nano-biomimetic materials, nanocomposite materials, and nano-biosensors.

View requirements for MNS 431

The focus of this course is on DNA-based biosensors and nanomaterials. General introduction to other soft materials including proteins, polymers and lipids may also be added if time allows. After going over the fundamentals about DNA chemistry, we will cover the design principle of various functional DNA-based biological sensors and materials. Topics for lectures are listed below. More detailed outlines will be provided on a lecture-by-lecture basis.

Topic 1: DNA/RNA properties and chemical synthesis
Topic 2: Combinatorial selection of functional DNA
Topic 3: Examples of DNA-based biosensors
Topic 4: DNA-assembled nanomaterials
Topic 5: DNA-based materials
Topic 6: Immunoassays and lateral flow devices

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
read and understand related scientific papers and technical reports. Furthermore, students will be able to perform related engineering calculations to design practical sensors and materials.

The objective of the course is to provide students a broad view designing biosensors and biomaterials with biomacromolecules. DNA will be the central topic of the class, and other biopolymers and polymers will also be covered. Students are also required to perform literature search and study the current state-of-the-art sensor and biomaterials. Students will learn the basic nucleic acid chemistry and biotechnology. After successful completion of the course, students are expected to be able to read and understand related scientific papers and technical reports. Furthermore, students will be able to perform related engineering calculations to design practical sensors and materials.

Administrative Policy

Missed course elements. We have already mentioned penalties for late submissions of homework assignments. For the two exams and presentations, you should make every effort to attend. If a student has to miss an exam with a valid reason, for example, by providing a Verification of Illness Forms (VIF), it might be possible to be accommodated. This will be handled on a case-by-case basis by the instructor. If the degree of incapacitation indicated on the note does not seem to be a valid reason for missing an exam or assignment, or there is something else about the documentation that does not warrant giving special consideration, a DNW (final examination not written) will be given for a missed final exam, or zero for an assignment. VIFs need to be specific and they will be handled to the Associate Dean for Undergraduate Studies. Students are encouraged to bring their VIFs to the Engineering Undergraduate Office for verification and filing. 

If a student misses a homework assignment with a valid VIF, the portion of the grade will be added to his final exam. For example, instead of 30%, the final exam will be 35% of his final grade. If a final exam is missed with a valid VIF, the course assessment will be based on other elements such as homework, presentation, and final paper.
