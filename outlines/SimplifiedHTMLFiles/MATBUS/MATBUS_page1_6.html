Course Description

MATBUS 471:

Analysis of fixed income securities. Duration, convexity. Structured products. Market characteristics. Valuation of bonds with embedded options. Accounting and taxation issues.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Mobius Assignments 10%
Written Assignments 5%
Video Assignments 5%
In class activities 5%
Midterm 20%
Final Exam 55% (see below)

Details and policies for all the assessments can be found on LEARN.  

Written assignments can be completed in groups of up to three students. You do not need to be in the same group for all written assignments.

The assignment deadlines are all scheduled approximately one week after the related content has been covered in class (with the exception of Mobius Assignment #1). Effectively, each assignment deadline already has a short extension "built in" based on the course schedule. Students are advised to finish assignments ahead of the deadline, thus making extensions for last-minute short-term illness unnecessary. Consequently, no extensions or grade adjustments will be made to any weekly assignment or any other assessment worth 1% or less of the course grade.  Exceptions can only be made for students who have documented disabilities and have registered with AAS. In those cases, accommodation can be made depending on individual circumstances.  Such students should contact the instructor to discuss a personalized accommodation plan. More information on the application and rationale for this policy can be found on LEARN.

The in-class activities consist mainly of Vevox polls and quizzes. Half the marks will be for correctness and half will be for participation. 

Students who miss both the midterm and the final exam will not be eligible for an INC grade. Instead, you will be awarded a DNW grade. Students must pass the final exam in order to pass the course.
