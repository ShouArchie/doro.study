Course Description

MATBUS 371:

Cash flow analysis. Cost of capital, investment decision rules, capital budgeting, depreciation. CAPM. Description and valuation of financial instruments including stocks, swaps, options, and bonds.

View requirements for MATBUS 371

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Grasp corporate finance objectives and understand how financial systems function.
Understand corporation formation, board functions, and governance impact on ethics and compliance.
Interpret financial statements to evaluate a firm's financial status.
Analyze the risk-return trade-off and its implications for investment.
Calculate expected return for single asset and portfolio of assets.
Understand the mathematics of portfolio risk and diversification.
Explain the importance of the market portfolio and know how to construct a value-weighted portfolio.
Evaluate the impacts of debt on a firm's risk and profitability.
Understand the capital asset pricing model (CAPM) and other asset pricing theories, and their application in the investment decision process.
Calculate and apply WACC in investment and valuation contexts.
Employ valuation techniques to determine stock intrinsic value.
Master techniques for evaluating capital investment decisions.
Assess the drivers and financial evaluations of M&As and their impact on shareholder value.
Understand various types of derivatives: Futures, Options, Forwards, and Swaps

By the end of this course, you will be able to gain Bloomberg Market Concepts Certificate by completing the required modules and CFI Corporate Finance Foundations Professional Certificate.

Participation Bonus: These are in-class examples to solve, the number and availability will vary in each session. You will earn half mark for just attempting and half mark for getting it right. Total will add to 2% of bonus to your grade. 

Mobius Assignments: These are 3 Mobius assignments (pay extra attention on the format of inputting answers in Mobius). You will have 2 attempts and grade will be of the highest. The content of the Mobius assignments covers the instructor-lead topics onlys.

   Mobius Assignment 1: Intro. to Corporate Finance, Corporate Governance, Financial Statements

   Mobius Assignment 2: Risk & Return, Cost of Capital

  Mobius Assignment 3: Capital Structure, Valuation, Capital Budgeting, Mergers and Acquisitions, Derivatives

Bloomberg Cases: Cases are applications and learning content for course concepts. Through your Bloomberg for Education, you have access to those cases which have reading article, video on how to apply the concept in Bloomberg and questions to answer. Questions can be answered by applying the video tutorial in each case. Some questions are qualitative analysis that you would need to answer by analyzing the finding. Some other questions are quantitative applications of data retrieved from Bloomberg.

You will need access to Bloomberg Terminals to complete those.

Each case is worth 5%, due dates are set to have 2 cases per month to be submitted.

Bloomberg Market Concepts (certificate): These are seven tutorial videos (Terminal Basics 1h:55m, Economic Indicators 1h:30m, Fixed Income 2h10min, Equity 3h20min, Currencies 3h:15m, Commodities 1h:30m, and equity options 1h:31m) that you will need to complete. These videos are part of the Bloomberg Market Concepts certificate that you can complete to add to your CV. Your grade will be that of the quizzes in the module. You can work ahead at your flexible times with the modules but due dates are to be completed by the due date. You don't have to complete each module in one sit, it can be paused. This has to be completed at the Bloomberg Terminals on Campus only. Bring your ear/headphones to the lab.

LinkedIn Learning (CFI Corporate Finance Foundations Professional Certificate): This is a series of five video modules. You have due dates to complete each of them. To gain access to the LinkedIn Learning, you will have to login with UWaterloo SSO using your uwaterloo email. You will need to pass the exam and gain the certificate. To gain the marks for the completion of these modules, you will need to submit evidence of completing each module on time in Learn and the certificate you got by March 31, 2025. 

Group Final Project: You will be divided into groups randomly by January 27, 2025. Assigned case will be posted through library reserves. You will need to prepare a report and video presentation to submit along with any supporting graphs and data analysis. This final project is in lieu of a final exam so the due date for submission of the report and the video will be April 20, 2025 midnight. Late submissions will not be accepted.
