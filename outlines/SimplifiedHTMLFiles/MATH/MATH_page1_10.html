Course Description

MATH 118:

Methods of integration: by parts, trigonometric substitutions, partial fractions; engineering applications, approximation of integrals, improper integrals. Linear and separable first order differential equations, applications. Parametric curves and polar coordinates, arc length and area. Infinite sequences and series, convergence tests, power series and applications. Taylor polynomials and series, Taylor's Remainder Theorem, applications.

View requirements for MATH 118

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Get experience in writing well-organized and readable mathematical solutions, so that you will be able to communicate mathematical or technical ideas.
Select an appropriate integration technique and apply it to solve indefinite or definite integrals.
Select an appropriate series test to determine if an infinite series is convergent or divergent.
Find a power series representation of a function and find a closed form for a function represented by a power series.
Apply error bounds to various approximations.
Recognize and apply ordinary differential equations to solve a problem.
Recognize and apply parametric and polar coordinates to solve a problem.
Understand the formal definition of the limit of a sequence and use it to prove basic limits.
Understand the principle of mathematical induction and use it to prove some mathematical statements.

Tutorial Assignments:

Each tutorial will feature a graded problem set designed to get you thinking at a deeper level about the material. You may also see some new content that augments what was discussed in class. It is strongly recommended that you prepare for the tutorials in advance by attempting the practice problems on LEARN and reviewing your notes from the previous week of class.  Additional practice problems can be accessed using the Mobius link on the LEARN homepage. Mobius problems are purely optional.

Tutorial assignments need to be completed within the timeslot of the tutorial, in your assigned tutorial room.  The tutorial assignment questions will be given to you at the beginning of your tutorial, and your completed solutions must be submitted (on paper; electronic submissions will not be accepted) by the end of the tutorial session.
 

  • Group Work on Tutorial Assignments:  You may solve the problems in a group of up to 3 people, or individually if you prefer. If working in a group, you must indicate on your assignment who you worked with. Each group member must write and submit their own solutions, in their own words. 

    The objective in allowing you to work with other students is to help with the learning process; so long as you are discussing the concepts and teaching each other math, it is permitted.  It is NOT permitted to copy solutions that you didn’t write or do not understand, in whole or in part, including solutions written by other students or somebody online. Exchanging ideas is encouraged, but the final submission must be your own work.
     
  • Permitted Resources: In the tutorial you are permitted to use your personal notes, any resources on the MATH 118 LEARN page, and a scientific (non-graphing) calculator. No other resources are permitted.  You may not use any online resources other than the MATH 118 LEARN site.
     
  • Tutorial Bonus: Students who attend all tutorials for their section and effortfully attempt all tutorial assignments will receive a 1% bonus to their final course grade. Verified absences reported through Quest or approved by your instructor will not prevent you from earning the tutorial bonus.

Regrade Requests:

A regrade on an assignment or exam may result in an increase, decrease, or no change in grade. You must be specific when asking for reconsideration of your work: requests for more marks without justification or reference to a specific question will not be considered. 

  • Tutorials: Please direct tutorial regrades to the attention of your tutorial TA. After receiving a graded tutorial assignment, any concerns about the grading should be brought to the attention of your TA by the end of the next tutorial session. (e.g., if a graded assignment is returned to you during Tutorial 2, please bring any grading concerns to your TA by the end of Tutorial 3.)
     
  • Exams: Please direct exam regrade requests to the attention of your instructor.

 

Declaring absences: 

Self-declared absences (for COVID-19 and short-term absences up to 2 days) and religious accommodation requests must be submitted through Quest. Absences requiring documentation (e.g., Verification of Illness Form, bereavement, etc.) are to be uploaded by completing the form on the VIF System. The UWaterloo Verification of Illness form, completed by a health professional, is the only acceptable documentation for an absence due to illness. Do not send documentation to your advisor, course instructor, teaching assistant, or lab coordinator. Submission through the VIF System, once approved, will notify your instructors of your absence.

 

An approved absence from a tutorial will typically result in its weight distributed amongst the other tutorials. An approved absence from the midterm will typically result in its weight shifted to the final exam. There will be no make up midterm. 

 

Rescheduling Co-op Interviews: 

As best as possible, coop interviews should be scheduled around your academic commitments. Unavoidable tutorial conflicts with coop interviews should be communicated to your instructor by email. Attendance at coop interviews is not considered to be a valid reason to miss an exam, hence you will be expected to reschedule any co-op interviews that conflict with midterms or finals.
