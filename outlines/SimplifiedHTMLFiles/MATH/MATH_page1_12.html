Course Description

MATH 119:

Elementary approximation methods: interpolation; Taylor polynomials and remainder; Newton's method, Landau order symbol, applications. Infinite series: Taylor series and Taylor's Remainder Theorem, geometric series, convergence test, power series, applications. Functions of several variables: partial derivatives, linear approximation and differential, gradient and directional derivative, optimization and Lagrange multipliers. Vector-valued functions: parametric representation of curves, tangent and normal vectors, line integrals and applications.

View requirements for MATH 119

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Calculate derivatives of multivariate functions (including use of the general chain rule)
Find maxima and minima of functions of two variables, with or without constraints
Calculate integrals of multivariate functions over a variety of domains (the focus will be on two-variable problems)
Estimate roots of functions using Newtonâs Method
Estimate values of integrals using Taylor Polynomials and Taylorâs Inequality
Calculate Taylor Series efficiently for a variety of functions
Test the convergence of infinite series
Evaluate limits using Taylor Series

Student Assessment

Component Value
Tutorial Assignments (Weekly, 10 graded, lowest two dropped) 20%
Midterm Exam 30%
Final Exam 50%

Tutorial Assignments - Distributed, submitted, and returned during weekly tutorials.

Each tutorial will feature a graded problem set designed to get you thinking at a deeper level about the material. You may also see some new content that augments what was discussed in class. It is recommended that you prepare for the tutorials in advance by reviewing your notes from the previous week of class and attempting the practice problems posted on LEARN and in the textbook.

You may solve the problems in groups, or individually if you prefer. If working in a group, each group member must write and submit their own solutions in their own words. Exchanging ideas is encouraged, but the final submission must be your own work. You are permitted to use your personal notes, any resources on the MATH 119 LEARN page, and a scientific (non-graphing) calculator. No other resources are permitted. The Tutorial Assignments must be handed in by the end of the tutorial; no extension on submission is allowed. We will take the best 8 out of 10 assignments when computing your final grade. If a student misses a tutorial (due to illness, etc.), then that missed assignment will be counted as one of the dropped assignments. Students who miss more than 2 assignments will have the weight(s) of those assignments transferred to the final exam.
