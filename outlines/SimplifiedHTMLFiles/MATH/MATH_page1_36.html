Course Description

MATH 138:

Introduction to the Riemann integral and approximations. Antiderivatives and the fundamental theorem of calculus. Change of variables, methods of integration. Applications of the integral. Improper integrals. Linear and separable differential equations and applications. Tests for convergence for series. Binomial series, functions defined as power series and Taylor series. Vector (parametric) curves in R2. Suitable topics are illustrated using computer software.

View requirements for MATH 138

This section (081) is fully online (except for the final exam). Please check the course site in LEARN for announcements and course material. Interactive communication will primarily be through the Piazza online discussion forum. 

There will also be an optional weekly live Q&A sessions held online. The Q&A sessions will be held at Wednesdays 12:30-2pm and Fridays 11am-12pm.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Write well-structured mathematical proofs
Precisely define partitions of an interval, Riemann sums, definite integrals, and indefinite integrals
Be familiar with common classes of functions that are integrable and some that are not
Appreciate the meaning and significance of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
Evaluate definite and indefinite integrals using various techniques
Use calculus to solve problems about lengths, areas, and volumes of shapes
Solve some special classes of differential equations
Describe some applications of differential equations to real-world problems
Define absolute and conditional convergence of an infinite series of numbers
Determine whether series converge using various convergence tests
Recall the special properties of power series and Taylor series

Mobius and Written Assignments

There will be 12 equally weighted Mobius Assignments and 11 equally weighted written assignments. The topics covered in assignment n are those of Week n. 

Mobius Assignments (accessed through LEARN) will be available from 8:30 a.m. on Monday of Week n to 8:30 a.m. on Monday of Week n+1 (exception: 12:00 a.m. Jan 7 to 5 p.m. Jan 16 for Week 1). There is no time limit but you must complete and submit your Mobius Assignments within these periods. Mobius assignments are graded for completion, rather than accuracy. No extensions will be granted except in the event of technical issues affecting the whole class.

Written Assignment questions will be posted in LEARN by 8:30 a.m. on Thursday of Week n and should be submitted in Crowdmark by 5:00 p.m. on Thursday of Week n+1. Late submissions will be accepted with a penalty of 2% per hour up to 24 hours after the deadline. The penalty will be waived for the first 10 minutes.

N.B. There is no Written Assignment for topics from Week 12 but these topics will be covered in the Final Exam.

Your lowest Written Assignment score will be dropped from the final grade calculation. If you miss more than one due to illness or other circumstances, please discuss this with the instructor. The weighting for your overall grade may be adjusted if you are given exemptions. 

You are permitted to work together on assignment problems, with certain limitations. Rules for collaborative work can be found on the LEARN course site.

You may post questions and answers in Piazza about assignments but this should be limited to clarifications about the problems and brief hints.

Re-mark Request Policy

If you believe there is a problem with how your Mobius or written assignment was graded, you may request a re-mark on that assignment. Re-mark requests are sent to your instructor and then directed to the TA, who marks the relevant question/s.

Re-mark requests must be submitted within one week of receiving your graded work.

Your request must include a detailed description of the issues with the marking, referring to your solutions and those provided by the teaching team. If you submit a re-mark request without a description or explanation, you will be asked to provide details. An email template for re-mark requests is provided in LEARN.

Final Exam

The final exam will be registrar-scheduled and in-person, with a duration of 150 minutes. It will also be closed-book with no calculators permitted.

If you are taking on-campus courses, you will be scheduled to write the exam on-campus. Otherwise, you will need to make arrangements to write the exam at an exam centre or with a proctor. Please do this as soon as possible and be aware of the deadline for this. The CEL webpage has more details about exam arrangements.

You must earn a grade of at least 50% in the Final Exam to pass the course. If your Final Exam grade is below 50%, then it will be used as your Final Grade.

Assignment Screening

No assignment screening will be used in this course. TAs will be looking out for written assignment solutions that are too similar to each other or to other sources.

Generative AI: Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
