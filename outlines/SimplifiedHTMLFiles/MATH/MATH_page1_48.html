Course Description

MATH 217:

Curves and surfaces in R3. Multivariable functions, partial derivatives, the chain rule, gradients. Optimization, Lagrange Multipliers. Double and triple integrals, change of variable. Vector fields, divergence and curl. Vector integral calculus: Green's theorem, the Divergence theorem and Stokes' theorem. Applications in engineering are emphasized.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Written Assignments 10%
Quizzes 10%
Midterm 30%
Final Exam 50%

Written work must be legible, neatly presented, and organized. If the grader cannot read or make sense of your work, your mark may be reduced as a result. You are encouraged to include text explanations of your mathematical work. This practice will:

  • help you solidify your own understanding,
  • help you study for exams because the explanations are in your own words,
  • demonstrate your understanding to the grader, and,
  • develop technical communication skills that will serve you in your future roles as a professional engineer.

Late assignments and quizzes: Late assignments and quizzes will receive a grade of 0 (except due to extenuating circumstances/illness).

Lowest grade dropped: The lowest Written Assignment grade will not be counted toward the final course grade. The lowest Quiz grade will not be counted toward the final course grade. (Note: this policy includes Written Assignments and Quizzes that received a grade of 0 due to lateness.)

Absence declarations: Please consult the Absence Declarations policy of the Faculty of Mathematics. 

  • If you miss a Written Assignment or Quiz due to a properly documented Absence, the weight of the missed item will be transferred to the final exam. (Note: the remaining Written Assignments and Quizzes will still be eligible for the "Lowest grade dropped" policy stated above.)
  • If you miss the Midterm due to a properly documented Absence, the weight of the midterm will be transferred to the final exam.
  • If you miss the Final Exam due to a properly documented Absence, please consult the Incomplete (INC) Grade Process. A grade of INC will be considered on a case-by-case basis. A grade of INC may be granted based on the likelihood that a student will pass the course as determined by: (1) completion of the Midterm; and, (2) the student's overall performance on course work submitted before the Incomplete grade was requested.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLMs) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like ChatGPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used for Written Assignments in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will be discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite GenAI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Be aware that GenAI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of GenAI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using content from AI sources. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

GenAI is not a substitute for your own learning. As a mature individual learning to navigate a world in which GenAI is increasingly ubiquitous, you have a corresponding responsibility to take ownership of any consequences—positive or negative—of the use of GenAI in your academic journey, should you choose to use it. You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by GenAI.
