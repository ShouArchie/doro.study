Course Description

MATH 237:

Calculus of functions of several variables. Limits, continuity, differentiability, the chain rule. The gradient vector and the directional derivative. Taylor's formula. Optimization problems. Mappings and the Jacobian. Multiple integrals in various co-ordinate systems.

View requirements for MATH 237

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Analyze functions of two or more variables: compute their limits, determine their continuity and differentiability, and discuss their linear approximations.
Choose appropriate techniques from differential multivariable calculus to solve optimization problems both with and without constraints.
Choose appropriate coordinate systems, build non-linear mappings, and apply the Change of Variable Theorem to evaluate double and triple integrals.

Missed Assessments and Late Submission Policy

In case of a missed assessment, you should notify your instructor via email within 24 hours of the assessment's completion/due date in order to receive an accommodation. To be eligible for an accommodation, you must either use the self-declaration form on Quest or, if the assessment was missed due to illness, you must submit a Verification of Illness form (https://uwaterloo.ca/math/vif-submission). If you do not send an email informing the instructor of your absence, your grade will be automatically recorded as zero. Properly documented absences will result in the following accommodations:

  • Quizzes: The weight will be shifted to the other Quizzes.
  • Written Assignments: A 24-48 hour extension will be provided, as appropriate.

Assessments missed without instructor approval will receive a grade of 0.
