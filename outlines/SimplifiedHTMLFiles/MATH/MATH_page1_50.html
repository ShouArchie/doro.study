Course Description

MATH 225:

Vector spaces. Linear transformations and matrices. Inner products. Eigenvalues and eigenvectors. Diagonalization. Applications.

View requirements for MATH 225

In this course, we will learn certain advanced topics in linear algebra. It turns out that the term “vector space” applies not only to the n-dimensional real vector space Rn, but also to collections of m-by-n matrices with real coefficients Mmxn(R) and polynomials of bounded degree PnR. We will see that such familiar terms as the dot product, norm, distance, orthogonality, and projection can be generalized in a natural way to these vector spaces. We will also extend the theory of matrix diagonalization to these new settings. The course will also touch upon some topics that find many real-world applications, such as the Method of Least Squares, Singular Value Decomposition and Linear Programming.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Compute bases for, and the dimensions of, subspaces of R^n, M_mxn (R), and P_n R.
Determine whether a linear mapping between vector spaces is an isomorphism.
Diagonalize a linear mapping.
Compute an orthogonal basis of a subspace of an inner product space with the Gram-Schmidt Algorithm.
Use the Fundamental Theorem of Linear Algebra to prove that the rowspace and the columnspace of a matrix are orthogonal to each other.
Determine the best fitting polynomial for a collection of data points using the Method of Least Squares.
Orthogonally diagonalize a symmetric matrix.
Compute a Singular Value Decomposition of a matrix.
Solve constrained optimization problems related to quadratic forms and linear mappings.
Solve constrained optimization problems using linear programming.

Administrative Policy

Course Policies

Calculators

For the final exam, only calculators approved by the Faculty of Mathematics will be permitted. Please refer to the Calculator Regulation to see a list of approved calculators.

If you are unable to acquire one of these calculators, you must contact your instructor in the first week or two of the course so an equivalent alternative can be identified and added to the exam proctors' list of permitted aids.

Avoiding Academic Offences

The Möbius Assignments, Written Assignments, and Final Examination are individual exercises. They must not be completed in pairs or groups. Other resources may be used to supplement the material in lessons, but looking up solutions to assignment questions is prohibited. You are capable of meeting the expectations for this course. If you are concerned about how well you are doing in this course, please contact your instructor for a discussion instead of considering academic misconduct.

Course Conduct

In this course, diverse perspectives are recognized and valued as a source of strength. Your instructor will work with you to create a class culture based on open communication, mutual respect, and inclusion. All discussions should be approached with respect and civility. Disagreements and debates in academic discourse are expected and welcome, but personal attacks are never OK, and will not be tolerated. Your instructor strives to ensure an open and welcoming classroom for all students.

Caregiver Responsibilities Policy

Some of you may be balancing your pursuit of education with the responsibilities of caring for children or other family members. If you run into challenges or if your caregiving responsibilities are interfering with your ability to engage in online learning, please contact your instructor.

School-Life Conflict

Many students face obstacles to their education as a result of work or family obligations or unforeseen personal difficulties. If you are experiencing challenges throughout the term that are impacting your ability to succeed in this course, or in your undergraduate career more broadly, please reach out to your instructor to work together to form a plan for your academic success. If you are unable to attend student drop-in hours, please email your instructor to set up a time that works for you or arrange a meeting.
