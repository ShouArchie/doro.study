Course Description

MATH 136:

Systems of linear equations, matrix algebra, elementary matrices, computational issues. Real n-space, vector spaces and subspaces, basis and dimension, rank of a matrix, linear transformations, and matrix representations. Determinants, eigenvalues and diagonalization, applications.

View requirements for MATH 136

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand several important concepts, methods, and techniques in linear algebra.
Use the definitions, theorems, methods, and techniques from memory to construct solutions to problems and/or proofs.
Improve your ability to read and write mathematical proofs.
Improve your ability to communicate mathematics.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Möbius Assignments (10) 10%
Written Assignments (10) 10%
Midterm (see note below) 20%
Final Examination 60%

The Mobius assignments will be due weekly and deadlines are posted on LEARN. Students have unlimited attempts at each assignment prior to each deadline, and only your highest grade will count.

Written assignments will be submitted to Crowdmark.  Late Crowdmark submissions will be accepted at a penalty of 5% per hour. 

The plan for the midterm is tentative. If we are unable to hold the midterm for technical or other reasons, the weight of the midterm will be transferred to the final exam.  Specific details on the proposed midterm will be posted on LEARN. Students will need to make midterm writing selection preferences within the first 2 weeks of class.

You must pass the final examination (a minimum score of 50%) to pass the course. The final exam will be an in-person proctored examination held during the usual final exam window.  Final exam writing arrangements will be made in collaboration with the Centre for Extended Learning.

Administrative Policy

Regrade requests should be made by email to the instructor (c2mintah@uwaterloo.ca) within 5 business days of the assessment's return. Requests should state the assessment, the question(s), and a specific reason for the request, with reference to the grader's comments and solution where applicable.

If you request a regrade, your entire assignment will be reviewed. Each individual question may be re-marked if issues are found in the original marking, including but not limited to the issues you outlined in your regrade request. This could result in your overall mark being adjusted higher, lower, or not at all.
