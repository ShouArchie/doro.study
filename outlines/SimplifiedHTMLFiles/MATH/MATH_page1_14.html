Course Description

MATH 127:

Functions of a real variable: powers, rational functions, trigonometric, exponential and logarithmic functions, their properties and inverses. Intuitive discussion of limits and continuity. Definition and interpretation of the derivative, derivatives of elementary functions, derivative rules and applications. Riemann sums and other approximations to the definite integral. Fundamental theorems and antiderivatives; change of variable. Applications to area, rates, average value.

View requirements for MATH 127

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
understand the essential concepts of calculus, including the limit, derivative, and integral.
be able to explain the relationship between quantities and their rates of change with precise mathematical statements.
have extensive familiarity with a number of basic but fundamental models that showcase the role of calculus in their respective disciplines.
have experience describing and interpreting mathematical procedures in precise language (i.e., in complete sentences) within the context of short but formal project reports.
