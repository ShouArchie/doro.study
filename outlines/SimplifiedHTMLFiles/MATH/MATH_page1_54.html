Course Description

MATH 228:

First-order equations, second-order linear equations with constant coefficients, series solutions and special functions, the Laplace transform method. Applications in physics and chemistry are emphasized.

View requirements for MATH 228

The purpose of this course is to introduce students in physics, chemistry, and other fields to differential equations. Differential equations capture how some variable of interest, such as the position of a planet, changes as a function of some independent variable, such as time. Differential equations are very widely used in the sciences because of the broad range of real-world phenomena they can describe in a mathematical framework. Therefore, a basic familiarity with how to characterize them, how to solve them, and how they behave is very valuable. In this course, students will be introduced to the universe of differential equations, including first-order equations, second-order linear equations, series solutions, the Laplace transform method, and both exact and numerical methods for solving differential equations. Applications in physics and chemistry will be emphasized.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
understand how differential equations can be used to model physical systems,
know how to classify differential equations,
know how to solve various classes of differential equations using numerical methods and exact methods, and
be able to understand and visualize solutions of differential equations.

Tentative Course Schedule

This course is divided into eight units as follows:

  • Unit 1 - Defining, Classifying, and Solving Differential Equations (Week 1)
  • Unit 2 - First-Order Differential Equations (Weeks 2 - 3)
  • Unit 3 - Second-Order Linear Equations (Weeks 4 - 5)
  • Unit 4 - Mechanical and Electrical Vibrations (Week 6)
  • Unit 5 - Series Solutions of Second-Order Linear Equations (Weeks 7 - 8)
  • Unit 6 - The Laplace Transform (Week 9)
  • Unit 7 - Numerical Methods (Week 10)
  • Unit 8 - Systems of Differential Equations (Weeks 11 - 12)
