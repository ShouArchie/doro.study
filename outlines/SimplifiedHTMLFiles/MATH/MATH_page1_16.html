Course Description

MATH 127:

Functions of a real variable: powers, rational functions, trigonometric, exponential and logarithmic functions, their properties and inverses. Intuitive discussion of limits and continuity. Definition and interpretation of the derivative, derivatives of elementary functions, derivative rules and applications. Riemann sums and other approximations to the definite integral. Fundamental theorems and antiderivatives; change of variable. Applications to area, rates, average value.

View requirements for MATH 127

This course builds upon single-variable calculus that students saw in high-school. The course will focus on mathematical modelling and analytical thinking in addition to calculus concepts that are important in science. There will also be an emphasis on communicating mathematics.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
demonstrate an understanding of several essential calculus concepts.
create and analyze simple mathematical models.
communicate your knowledge of the course concepts using precise mathematical language.

Additionally, this course is designed to improve your:

  • critical and analytical thinking skills.
  • ability to create solutions to unfamiliar problems.
  • ability to express mathematical ideas clearly in writing.

QUIZZES
Quizzes will be held during the weekly tutorial (TUT) time slot on Mondays from 5:30-6:20 PM. There will be 9 quizzes throughout the term (see schedule above). All quizzes will use assigned seating. You can find your assigned seat here: https://odyssey.uwaterloo.ca.  Seat assignments will vary from week to week.

The questions on the quizzes (both multiple choice and full answer) can be anything covered in the course up to and including the section listed in the Assessment Schedule (posted on LEARN).  The quizzes will reflect the expectations for the midterm and final exam. To receive full marks on long answer questions, your solutions must be stated in a clear and logical form, and you must show all required steps. Marks may be deducted if your work is difficult to read or understand. The course notes contain mid-section exercises and end-of-section problems to help you prepare for the quizzes. There are also sample quizzes posted on LEARN. Complete solutions for the quizzes will be posted on LEARN shortly after they have been written.

QUIZ POLICIES
(1) Instructions for what to do if you miss a quiz due to illness or other extenuating circumstances can be found here.
(2) There are no make up quizzes. If you are excused from a quiz, the weight will be shifted to other assessments.
(3) Once your graded quiz is returned you should review the solutions (posted on LEARN) and your own graded quiz. If you notice an error in the grading, you should contact your instructor. Requests to have your grade changed must be specific (indicating the relevant question(s) and what error was made) or they will be ignored. Requests to change quiz grades will only be accepted for seven (7) days following the date when the graded quizzes are returned to you. NOTE: regrade requests may result in your grade being lowered.

OPTIONAL MIDTERM REWRITE
This is an opportunity to improve on your midterm grade!
NOTE: You must have written the midterm on Thursday February 27th and register for the rewrite to be allowed to do the rewrite. Information on how to register will be given once the midterm grades are released.
When: Monday March 10th from 5:30 - 6:50 PM in your tutorial (TUT) time slot (there is no quiz that day).
Location: To be determined.
Information: The questions on the rewrite will be very similar to those on the midterm. The idea is that you can demonstrate that you have improved your understanding and corrected the mistakes you made on the original midterm. To facilitate that, you will receive your marked midterm several days before the optional midterm rewrite to give you time to do further study and to decide whether you want to write the optional midterm rewrite.

How it affects your grade: If you write the optional midterm rewrite, then your midterm grade will be recalculated as:
maximum [ original midterm score, (original midterm score + optional rewrite score)/2 ]
For example:
- If you get 50% on the midterm and get 80% on the rewrite, then your midterm grade would be recalculated as (50 + 80)/2 = 65%.
- If you get 60% on the midterm and get 50% on the rewrite, then your midterm grade will be 60%.

Writing the optional midterm rewrite cannot lower your midterm grade.

OPTIONAL MIDTERM REWRITE POLICIES
(1) You must bring your Watcard to the optional midterm rewrite.
(2) You must have written the midterm on February 27th to be allowed to do the rewrite.
(3) Due to the nature of the optional midterm rewrite, there are no accommodations if you are unable to write it at the scheduled time.
