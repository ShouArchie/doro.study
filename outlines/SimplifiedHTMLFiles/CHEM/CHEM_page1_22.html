Course Description

CHEM 262L:

Selected experiments for engineering students taking CHEM262.

View requirements for CHEM 262L

The laboratory course is meant to supplement the CHEM 262 lecture, and it is composed of five experiments demonstrating basic organic syntheses and laboratory techniques. Topics include acid-base chemistry, substitution reactions, liquid-liquid extraction, product isolation and purification.  All laboratory materials are available on LEARN. The experiments are conducted in person, every other week.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Be proficient with basic organic laboratory techniques including selecting proper glassware, measuring small and large quantities of solids and liquids, designing the setup required for simple synthetic reactions, filtration, and basic characterization.
Understand and adhere to proper laboratory protocol and safety recommendations, including appropriate use of personal protective equipment (PPE), safe handling of chemicals and disposal of waste in accordance to guidelines.
Understand and be able to design and execute a liquid-liquid extraction processes for the separation of ionizable acidic, ionizable basic, and non-ionizable (neutral) substances.
Purify solid substances by recrystallization and determine an experimental melting point.
Purify liquid substances by distillation and determine an experimental boiling point.
Apply the theory from CHEM 262 to the reactions executed in CHEM 262L.
Complete laboratory reports ethically by using data generated by you or your lab partner, crediting all sources of information and demonstrating independent thinking by answering questions in your own words.
Demonstrate independent thinking by answering questions in your own words, showing critical reflection beyond the initial description of observations and facts.

Pre-laboratory Quizzes (Möbius)

There is a Möbius quiz associated with each of the 5 experiments and the introductory module (Introduction to CHEM 262L). Each of these quizzes is meant to assess your understanding of the background information and procedure and in some cases provide you with the quantities used for the experiment. Quizzes are answered individually and independently. You have a maximum of two attempts for the assignments that are automatically graded online. Möbius will keep the best of the two grades. Each pre-lab assignment must be completed before your experiment; late pre-lab assignments will not be accepted and you will not be allowed to complete the lab without completing your pre-lab.

Laboratory Performance

Laboratory performance marks will be given by the Teaching Assistants and Lab Instructor and averaged for each in-person lab period. The performance grade includes: coming to the lab prepared and on time, observing all lab regulations and safety practices, being organized and neat while completing the experiment, using proper techniques and good time management allowing to complete the experiment in the allotted time, being able to independently perform the tasks in the laboratory, proper waste segregation as well as leaving your workstation in good standing.

In early experiments, there is often a perception that asking too many questions lead to performance penalties. This is not directly the case; we do expect students to need a lot of clarifications when working in the lab for the first time. It is less “costly” on the marks to ask these questions early on rather than making mistakes that lead to not successfully completing the experiment or that endanger you. It is normal to be less independent at the start of the term, but your confidence will improve, and your performance mark will increase faster if you learn from the many questions you ask your TA.

The grid used to evaluate your performance in each experiment will be available on LEARN. 

Experimental Outlines

An experimental outline summarizes what you expect to do in the laboratory during an experiment. The document should be a brief, flow-chart or point-form, personally handwritten summary of what you will do during the experiment. The document should not contain excessive detail like the procedure does to be easy to follow while you perform the experiment. The purpose of the experimental outline is to help you organize your time efficiently by listing tasks in the approximate order in which you expect to accomplish them. For example, whenever a reflux (boiling) period is specified in a procedure, you will have some free time to set up the apparatus for the next step, reorganize your work area, clean glassware or take a melting point. Note: Your outline should include what glassware you need, the quantities of reagents/solvents required and what data you should record. A template is provided on yellow-coloured paper at the end of every experiment for you to complete. If you need more room, you can attach more pages but make sure to let your TA know as they will expect to only mark the yellow page!

Laboratory Worksheets and Reports

Each experiment has an associated worksheet (Experiments 1 and 2) or report (Experiments 3, 4 and 5). The worksheets are completed and submitted during your laboratory session (6.25% each) while reports are submitted online using the Crowdmark Grading System one week after your experiment (12.5% each). There are no formal reports, you only need to answer each of the questions in the report templates and submit to Crowdmark. 

The worksheets are included in this manual, you will share only raw data with your partner and complete the rest of the worksheet individually. Report templates to complete are found on LEARN and Crowdmark, lab report submission can either be individual or with your partner. If submitting with your partner, add them on Crowdmark and submit a single report.

Crowdmark assignments must be submitted by the assigned due-date. Late assignments will not be accepted (grade of zero automatically applied). Any inquiries regarding the grading of post-lab assignments must be directed to your instructor within one week of receiving your graded feedback.

Final Lab Exam

The final exam is a 1.5-hour exam scheduled on Friday March 28 at 9:30 AM and will test your understanding of the CHEM 262L laboratory course content. Questions similar to the pre-laboratory quizzes and reports will be used, but may be modified to be multiple choice. More information regarding the final lab exam will be available towards the end of the semester. 

Obtaining a grade lower than 40% on the final lab exam will result in a reassessment of your grade and may result in a final course grade no higher than 50%.

Laboratory Attendance

  • Laboratory work can be conducted in pairs or individually. All in-lab worksheets are completed individually but reports submitted on Crowdmark can be completed with your partner.
  • Late arrival to the lab is not permitted. If you arrive before the pre-lab talk has finished, you will lose performance marks. No lab admission is permitted after the pre-lab talk has finished.
  • You should always attend the laboratory section that you are assigned to on your term schedule. If for any valid reason you cannot show up to your regularly scheduled session, you should contact the lab instructor and make alternative arrangements prior to the lab period.
  • If you are ill, contact the instructor before the lab period to have the experiment rescheduled or exempted. With an exemption, your lab mark will be the average of the other experiments/reports.
  • You cannot write a lab report for an experiment you have not completed yourself unless given explicit permission. This is generally reserved for incidents occurring while in lab and not for absences.
  • To obtain the lab credit you must complete at least four experiments and submit four post-lab assignments (missing one only for a valid reason). Valid reasons are supported by a verification of illness form. Submitting a VIF does not mean you are automatically rescheduled or exempted from the lab. If you must miss your lab, please email the instructor (Leanne Racicot) with the following information:
  • Your name and student ID
  • Your lab section

There are only two sections of CHEM 262L this term, so rescheduling is a challenge. Please be mindful of this and avoid missing your lab for unapproved reasons.

  • Students who miss laboratory sessions without a valid excuse will not be permitted to complete the experiment in another section and will be assigned a mark of zero for the worksheet/report, performance grade and experimental outline grade for that experiment.
  • You must arrive prepared and ready for your experiment. This means you must have completed all pre-laboratory quizzes, you must bring a lab coat and safety glasses and your experimental outline must be prepared as stated in your experimental procedure. Coming to the lab unprepared will likely result in poor performance grade as your independence in the lab is evaluated. If your lack of preparation poses a danger to yourself and your peers, you will be asked to leave the lab and will receive a grade of zero for the experiment. Working in a group does not mean you are excused from your individual preparation!
  • For each laboratory session, you must bring:
  • Proper attire – you must wear long pants and closed-toe shoes to all labs.
  • Lab coat and safety glasses – this is mandatory for all labs.
  • Lab manual – strongly recommended to be able to refer to the full procedure as needed!
  • Completed experimental outline – A brief summary of the steps of the experiment, written in your own words as either a flowchart or point form list. More details on the outline are included with each experiment and a template is provided in the lab manual (yellow pages).

Grades and Assignments

  • Insufficient preparation for your experiment will lead to performance marks deduction. While you will be working in pairs in the laboratory, performance marks are individual, and your TA will notice if you are relying on your partners to know what to do in the lab to complete your experiment. Completing the pre-lab quizzes and writing your own experimental outline will help you prepare, and don’t hesitate to reach out to the lab instructor if you have questions.
  • All in-lab worksheets are individual. You can submit your reports on Crowdmark in pairs or individually. If you are collaborating with your partner, make sure you add them to assignment prior to submission.
  • Late assignments are not accepted, and a grade of zero is automatically applied to late assignments.
  • When you receive your marked lab report on Crowdmark, look it over. If you have any questions, please ask for clarifications via email to the instructor.
  • The lab instructor will ONLY re-grade the entire report, except in cases of obvious misgrade (ie. addition error or answer not seen). Sections of the lab report will not be re-graded without reviewing the rest of the report. If you choose to have your report re-graded by the instructor, the mark you receive from the instructor will be your final mark.
  • Discussions regarding grading issues must be done within one week (seven (7) days) of receiving the graded reports.
