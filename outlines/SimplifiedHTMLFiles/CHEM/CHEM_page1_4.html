Instructor & TA (Teaching Assistant) Information

CHEM 123 is divided into several sections, taught by a team of instructors.  The Course Coordinator deals with general inquiries and policies concerning the course and tries to make sure that this multi-sectioned course runs as smoothly as possible.  For detailed instructor and teaching assisting information, please see the course LEARN site.  

Course Description

CHEM 123:

Properties of liquids and solutions. Introduction to chemical equilibria. Principles of acid-base equilibria, solubility and electrochemical processes. Chemical kinetics.

View requirements for CHEM 123

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Describe the energy and entropy changes that accompany physical and chemical changes.
Calculate the equilibrium constant for a reaction using the standard thermodynamic properties of reactants and products.
Predict the direction of spontaneous change for a given set of initial conditions for a variety of chemical systems.
Determine the rate law for a chemical reaction and explain how the rate of reaction depends on time, concentration, temperature, and the use of a catalyst.
Explain how the rate law for a reaction is related to the reaction mechanism.
Apply equilibrium principles to a variety of chemical reaction types including gas-phase reactions, acid-base reactions, precipitation reactions, complexation reactions and redox reactions.
Analyze oxidation-reduction reactions and identify species being oxidized and reduced.
Explain the basic construction of, uses of, and the differences between galvanic and electrolytic cells.

CHEM 120 and CHEM 123 comprise a full-year course in introductory chemistry. These courses lay the foundation for more advanced chemistry courses including organic chemistry (e.g. CHEM 266), inorganic chemistry (e.g. CHEM 212), analytical chemistry (e.g. CHEM 220), physical chemistry (e.g. CHEM 254) and biochemistry (e.g. CHEM 237).

Course Structure and Continuity Plans

Classes:  Classes are scheduled each Monday, Wednesday, and Friday in STC 1012.  CHEM 123 is designed as a blended course. Before each class, all students are expected to prepare by watching assigned video content and reviewing the course notes.  During each class, we will focus on content review and application of knowledge through problem-solving activities.  You must do the pre-class work to effectively use our synchronous in-person classes.

Course Continuity Plans:  CHEM 123 is a synchronous, in-person course.  Attending all classes in person during your quest-scheduled section is strongly recommended.

If you cannot come to class: We understand that at any point during the term, you may become symptomatic and unable to join the in-person class. If you cannot attend the in-person class, we will record and post one section of CHEM 123 for asynchronous viewing. You do not need to notify your instructor if you miss regularly scheduled classes unless a prolonged absence is necessary.

In the event of campus closure: If in-person activities are suspended, or in the event of a campus closure, all in-person classes and tutorials will occur as online synchronous meetings using the appropriate platform.  Assessments will run online in this situation.

Learning Methods

Content Videos:  Throughout the content videos, the instructors will focus on selected topics from the course notes. Prepare for each video by reading the relevant sections of the course notes or text in advance.  You are expected to learn some of the course material on your own. 

Synchronous classes:  In your synchronous classes and tutorials your instructors and teaching assistants will review content and guide you through various problem-solving activities to help you apply your knowledge and skills to new problem-solving situations.

Chemistry Study Hall:  Starting the 2nd week of classes, there will be a first-year study hall on Tuesdays and Thursdays at 5:30 pm - 6:50 pm in STC 1012.  The study hall will be staffed with CHEM Teaching assistants and volunteer tutors for you to ask questions and get help for the course.  Note:  Even though your quest schedule indicates "CHEM 123 Tutorial", it is a placeholder for the Chem Study Hall.  You can attend as many Study Hall Sessions as you would like - grab your friends and connect with your TAs!

Online Assignments:  The assignments are provided online so that you can obtain frequent and immediate feedback on your understanding of the concepts.  Each online assignment can be repeated up to three times, so you can correct your mistakes or get help.  If you earn a grade of 80.0% or higher on an online assignment it will be treated as 100% when calculating the assignment grades at the end of the term.  Historically, students who do the online assignments as scheduled earn higher grades than students who do not.

Instructor Student Hours/Q&A sessions:  During virtual or in-person drop-in Q&A sessions, the instructors or teaching assistants will be available for “drop-in” question-and-answer interaction.  These Q&A sessions may be held in person or online.  Further instructions will be given on LEARN.  

Piazza Q&A:  We use Piazza for questions for help from instructors, teaching assistants and classmates.  Using Piazza, you can post anonymously, publicly to the class, or privately to the instructors.  All course content questions are to be posted on the course Piazza boards (not sent by email) to ensure we can help you quickly and efficiently.  Instructors will recommend that all students post questions on the Piazza discussion boards instead of emailing instructors directly.

Extra Practice Problems:  For each module, we have assigned problems for you to try (posted on LEARN).  You are not required to hand in solutions to these problems; the responsibility to do them is yours.

Participation (10%): There are 9 participation components in CHEM 123.  We will count 7 of the 9 components, dropping the lowest two.

  • Eight Participation Challenges:  These are timed, single-attempt online quizzes scheduled approximately weekly. The quizzes are graded 25% for completion and 75% based on your earned grade. Submission policy: Participation Challenges must be completed by the indicated deadline. No extensions are permitted.
  • One combined clicker grade:   Points are awarded for answering in-class clicker questions, which are not graded for correctness.   To earn full credit for your iClicker grade, you need to answer 75% of the total clicker questions.  Clicker grades over 75% will be adjusted to 100% at the end of the term.

If you do not complete the participation components, or if your participation grades do not improve your overall grade, the 10% weight for participation will be transferred: 5% to your term test grades and 5% to your final exam grade. We calculate all grades with and without the participation component and only include the grade if it helps your overall grade. Please note: Because every student can drop two participation components, VIFs are not required or accepted for participation challenges.

Online Assignments (10%): Online assignments are posted on LEARN and deployed using the Mobius platform. We will count 8 of the 9 online assignments. You get three attempts for each assignment, and the highest earned grade is recorded. If you earn a grade of 80% or higher on an assignment, the mark will be treated as 100% when calculating your assignment average. This adjustment is done at the end of the term. If you choose not to do the assignments, the 10% weight for the assignments is transferred: 5% to your term test grades and 5% to your final exam grade. We calculate all grades with and without the assignment component and only include the grade if it helps your overall grade. Submission policy: Assignments must be completed by the due date. Each student may use up to 3 “grace days” for each assignment, after which no further submissions or extensions will be permitted.  CHEM 123 has flexible grading where one assignment is dropped for all students, and the weight of the assignments is shifted to the final exam if it does not help the overall grade.   For this reason, VIFs are not needed or accepted for missed online assignments.  

Term Tests (40%):  There will be two tests in CHEM 123. Test 1 is on Wednesday, February 5th  and counts for 20% of the course grade.  Test 2 is on Wednesday, March 12th  and counts for 20% of the course grade.  Details for each test will be posted on LEARN approximately one week in advance. Note: If you write both term tests, the weight of your lowest written test can be transferred to your final exam if it increases your overall grade.  This will be calculated automatically for you at the end of the term.  If you miss a test for a valid reason, the weight of the missed test will be transferred to the final exam.

Final Exam (40%): There will be a cumulative final exam held during the exam period (April 9-25). Check the schedule posted by the Registrar’s Office:  https://uwaterloo.ca/registrar/final-examinations/exam-schedule

Important Note:  The grade calculation described above applies only if the weighted average of your tests and exam is at least 50%.  If the weighted average of your tests and exam is less than 50%, then that weighted average is also your course grade.

Why do we place extra emphasis on the weighted average of your tests and exam? Our experience, compiled over many years, indicates that low marks on the tests and exam are a very strong indicator that a student has not mastered the material.  The assignments are important because they will help you prepare for the tests and exam, so it is in your best interests to do them. Learn from your mistakes on the assignments before writing the tests or exam.

Policy for Requests for Extra Credit or Grade Increases:  Please do not request extra marks at the end of the term from your course instructor either in person or by email.  Providing “extra credit” opportunities or arbitrarily increasing your grade is unethical and unfair to the rest of the class.  These requests will not be responded to by any of the course instructors.

Administrative Policy

Submission of Participation Challenges:  Participation challenges are timed, single-attempt quizzes that must be completed by the indicated due date.  No extensions are granted for participation challenges.  Because we drop two participation components for all students, VIFs are not required or accepted for participation components.

Submission of Online Assignments:  Assignments should be completed by the recommended due date, but all students will be granted up to 3 grace days after the due date to complete each assignment.  Once the grace period has passed, no further assignments will be accepted for grades, and a practice version of the assignment will be available for study and review purposes.  You do not need to contact your instructor if you need to use a grace day for assignment submission.  CHEM 123 has flexible grading where one assignment is dropped for all students, and the weight of the assignments is shifted to the final exam if it does not help the overall grade.   For this reason, VIFs are not needed or accepted for missed online assignments.  

Conflicts with Scheduled Term Tests:  If you have a valid conflict with a term test contact your instructor at least two weeks prior to the term test (or sooner, if possible) to explain the nature of the conflict and to discuss possible accommodations.  For religious accommodation requests, please contact your instructor and Submit a Request for Religious Accommodation on Quest (Information at this link).

Missing a Test:  A grade of zero will be assigned to any missed test or exam, unless you submit proper documentation.  If proper documentation is submitted, the weight of the missed test is transferred to the final exam.  There are no make up term tests for CHEM 123.

Missing the Final Exam: If the exam is missed and not documentation is sbumitted, a DNW (did not write) will be assigned.   If proper documentation is submitted and approved, a makeup exam may be arranged.  The arrangements will be recorded within an INC agreement form and sent to the Registrar’s office.  INC agreements will only be arranged if at least one term test was written, and the student is in good academic standing in CHEM 123.  It is the student’s responsibility to contact their instructor to discuss the arrangements for writing a makeup exam.

How to arrange documentation for a missed test or final exam:

Important link: Please see the Accommodations for Illness or Extenuating Circumstances for the Faculty of Science.

You can avoid a zero grade by completing the three-part process described below.  Failure to carry out steps 1 – 3 below will result in a grade of zero on the missed test. 

1. Contact your instructor within 24 hours of the missed term test. 

2. Get documentation to explain your absence.  You have three options for documentation, indicated below.

  • Self-declare Covid-19 illnesses on Quest (Information at this link)
  • Self-declare a short-term absence on Quest – Not applicable to the Final Exam Period (Information at this link)
  • Submit a Verification of Illness Form to the Science Undergraduate Office. (Information at this link)

Please see the Faculty of Science Guidelines to decide on appropriate documentation.    

3. Wait for notification from your instructor. Your instructor will review the documentation, decide, and then notify you as to whether it will be accepted or if you will be assigned a grade of zero.  Note that filing documentation with the Science Undergraduate Office or on Quest does NOT automatically excuse the missing of a term test. The information provided in the documentation will be evaluated when deciding whether a student should be excused. 

IMPORTANT:  Misuse of the absence protocols or False claims constitute an academic offence that will result in disciplinary action under Policy 71 (Student Discipline).

Failure to carry out steps 1-3 above will result in a grade of zero on a term test or a DNW on a final exam.
