Course Description

CHEM 313L:

Introduction to synthetic inorganic chemistry.

View requirements for CHEM 313L

CHEM 313L is an introductory Inorganic chemistry laboratory. CHEM 313L is designed to emphasize the principles of inorganic chemistry, techniques of measurement, and analysis and interpretation of experimental data. Students will be introduced to techniques and instruments used in modern chemical research.  Selected experiments include topics such as molecular orbital calculations of fullerenes and the syntheses and characterization of quantum dots, high-temperature superconductors, and silicon compounds.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Provide the student with the fundamental knowledge and practical experience in the area of inorganic chemistry
Gain an appreciation and understanding of the more complex problems in which they may encounter further in their future chemistry careers
Illustrate the principles and theories of structure and bonding of inorganic compounds

Tentative Course Schedule

Students will be assigned into groups that will rotate between the different experiments - not all students will be performing the same experiment at any given time. The experiments are:

Ex1.        Syntheses of Silicon Compounds

OM1.     Molecular Orbital and Infrared Spectra Calculations of a Lewis Acid:Base Adduct BF3:NH3

Ex2.        Molecular Orbital Calculation and Electrochemistry of Fullerenes

OM2.     Preparation of Ferrocene, (C5H5)2Fe 

Ex3.        Syntheses and UV-Vis of ZnS Quantum Dots

Ex4.        Solid State Synthesis of High-Temperature YBa2Cu3O7-d (0 < d < 0.5) Superconductor

Student Assessment

Component Value
Lab Modules 60%
Final Exam 40%

Lab Modules consist of prelabs, in-lab performance, in-lab live marking (oral quiz), and, in some cases, lab reports. Lab reports are to be submitted online on LEARN in assigned dropboxes. The final exam is accessible on LEARN under the Quizzes section and is scheduled to be done on April 3rd.
