Course Description

CHEM 262:

Bonding, structure, and nomenclature in organic chemistry. Physical properties and simple reactions associated with the important functional groups.

View requirements for CHEM 262

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Be familiar with the language of organic chemistry including pushing electrons, conformational analysis and stereochemical nomenclature
Have a good understanding of reactivity in organic chemistry with emphasis on structure-reactivity relationships
Understand and predict specific reactivity patterns of some classes of organic compounds
To appreciate the inherent logic in organic chemistry

Administrative Policy

Important:

  1. The grading schemes described above apply only if you obtain a minimum weighted average of 50% on your tests and final exam. Otherwise,your course grade will be the weighted average of your tests and exam.
  2. You must pass the final exam to pass the course. 

 

  • There are no make-up examinations for missed quizzes or tests.
  • Online quizzes are accessed via LEARN. Extensions will not be granted due to computer or internet related problems. Avoid this situation by working on your quizzes well in advance of the deadline.
  • Students missing both term tests will not be allowed to write the final exam and will be asked to withdraw from the course.
  • If missing a test or the final exam for medical reasons, a verified Verification of Illness Form (VIF) should be provided within 48 hours of the test or exam date. Only VIFs issued by the University of Waterloo's Health Services will be acceptable documentation when this service is available.
  • If a student is sick on a weekend,during off-hours, while out of town or receiving ongoing care from a family physician or specialist, it is acceptable to provide documentation from other health service providers. Information should include (1) date of the physician assessment, (2) dates of illness,(3) level of incapacitation and (4) whether the diagnosis was made by the physician or based on description by the student
  • You must bring your VIF to the Science Undergraduate Office (STC 2031) for verification and filing. For other extenuating circumstances that are not illness related, for which the VIF is not appropriate, official documented proof that something has occurred will be required (e.g., plane tickets, court documents, death certificates, counselor letters).
  • A VIF does not necessarily excuse a student from missing an exam. Being excused from an exam is NOT automatic upon the presentation of a VIF. The VIF will be used to decide whether a student had a legitimate reason for missing the exam.
  • If you miss the final exam, and provide a verified VIF (or other official documented proof with a valid reason) within 48 hours of the exam date, you may be allowed to write the final exam the next time the course is offered provided it is the only examination missed (otherwise, your case will be presented to the corresponding Associate Dean, Undergraduate Studies).
  • Date and location for the Comprehensive Final Exam (2.5 hours) will be scheduled by the Registrar’s Office. Travel plans are not considered acceptable grounds for granting an alternative examination time.
