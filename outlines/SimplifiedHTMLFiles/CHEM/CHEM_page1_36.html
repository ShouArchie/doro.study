Course Description

CHEM 335L:

Selected experiments for students having completed or concurrently taking CHEM331. Topics to be covered include NMR, allostery, enzymology, electrophoresis, carbohydrates, lipids, photosynthesis, and respiration.

View requirements for CHEM 335L

This laboratory course is designed to complement the content of lecture courses CHEM 331 and CHEM 357 through a series of selected experiments focusing on proteins and enzymes as the major areas of study. The set of individual labs comprising the course explore the following themes with the aim of reinforcing concept understanding of (i) protein purification, (ii) protein research, and (iii) optimizing protein characterization, with an overall focus on experimental design, data analysis, and interpretation. 

In this laboratory course,  students will gain exposure to and experience fundamental biochemistry lab techniques and equipment including (but not restricted to) (i) the making and use of buffers; (ii) different chromatography resins/columns; (iii) micro and high-speed centrifuges; (iv) standard and microplate UV-Vis spectrophotometer and accessories; (v) electrophoresis methods (SDS-PAGE gel); (vi) methods for accurate volume transfer; and (vii) enzyme activity characterization.

Finally, this course aims to improve the student’s scientific communication skills through the practice of (i) protocol development and writing; (ii) the understanding and analysis of various kinds of experimental data; (iii) scientific writing with both formal and informal laboratory submissions; and (iv) various data fitting models. There is no text book for this course. An on-line laboratory manual outlining all experimental procedures, equipment operation and theory, safety practices, report formats and data treatment is provided on the LEARN course website.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Apply different protein purification methods: sonication/osmotic shock, chromatography, dialysis, BCA assay/UV/Vis spectrophotometry and SDS-PAGE
Conduct protein research: structure, stability, enzyme activity, substrate(s) and affinity, inhibitors and activators
Optimize protein characterization: protein concentration, substrate concentration, inhibitor/activator concentration, ideal buffer conditions (pH, salt concentration, buffer concentration), instrument limitations

Assignment Screening

Text matching software (Turnitin) will be used to screen assignments in this course. This is being done to verify that use of all material and sources in assignments is documented. In the first week of the term and in the outline below, details will be provided about the arrangements for the use of Turnitin and alternatives in this course. See Administrative Policy below for more information and links.

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as report writing, biochemistry fundamentals, and procedure development. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.).

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your OWN rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean.

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence

Administrative Policy

COVID-19 Accommodations

It is possible that another wave of COVID-19  can cause school to shut down. It is also possible that a student becomes ill and is unable to complete all course assessments/requirements. The following policy address both these circumstances. If by chance, a circumstance occurs that is not mentioned in the policy below, an alternative sensible arrangement will be made between the instructor and student/s. Any concerns you have with the policy below should be addressed to the instructor, Harmeen Deol.

In the event that the UW campus is shut down and on-campus activities cease: 

  • The in-lab performance grade will reflect the performance achieved up until the last day on campus.
  • The practical exam will be cancelled and its grade contribution removed. The final tallied grade will be calculated from a denominator of 92 instead of 100.
  • The final written exam may or may not be conducted remotely.
  • The pre-lab quizzes and assignments will continue as is
  • The post-lab reports will continue with data that is provided to you by the instructor via LEARN or email.
  • Group projects: In-lab portion will be cancelled and an alternative assignment will be proposed

In the event that a student becomes ill: 

  • If you are sick and feel that you should not attend the lab in person, you must obtain a verification of illness form or declare your illness according to UWaterloo guidelines.
  • Please follow the instructions found on the Science Undergraduate Office page for SUO illness accommodations.
  • If you are missing a component of the course due to valid reasoning, you should always inform the instructor at the earliest time possible. An alternate arrangement or schedule will be discussed between you and the instructor.
  • The in-lab performance grade will reflect your performance achieved on only the days present in the lab. This only applies for up to one missed lab day. If you miss more than one lab day an incomplete (INC) or in-progress (IP) grade may be assigned.
  • If your absence due to illness coincides with the practical exam date, you may be asked to complete it on another day or the respective grade will be removed from the grading scheme. The final tallied grade will be calculated from a denominator of 92 instead of 100.
  • If your absence due to illness coincides with the written exam date, an alternative date will be arranged between you and the instructor (in most cases the student will write during the next offering of the course). An INC or IP grade may be assigned.
  • If your illness causes the inability to complete one online quiz, the quiz grade will be redistributed to another course component (the final exam). If more than one quiz is missed, the respective grade allocations for the missed quizzes will be removed when tallying the final grade denominator. Note: this only applies if you are ill.
  • If your illness causes your inability to complete one in-lab experiment, you may be asked to complete it on another day or lab results/data will be sent to you via email. You will be responsible for completing the post-lab report by the original due date. If you miss more than one lab day, an incomplete (INC) or in-progress (IP) grade may be assigned.
  • If a prolonged illness causes your inability to submit either more than one pre-lab assignment or more than one post-lab report an INC or IP grade may be assigned.

 

All graded reports/assessments should be retained until the final course marks are released and reviewed. For this term, all lab submissions will be ONLINE- on LEARN.

If not able to perform/write the lab exam during the term, a make-up lab exams will be performed/written with the next course offering (an IP or INC grade will be documented in the interim). CHEM 335L is offered every Fall and Winter term. Student travel plans are not considered acceptable grounds for granting an alternative examination time. The final written exam for CHEM 335L will take place during the last laboratory period for the term.

University Policy

Academic integrity: In order to maintain a culture of academic integrity, members of the University of Waterloo community are expected to promote honesty, trust, fairness, respect and responsibility. [Check the Office of Academic Integrity for more information.]

Grievance: A student who believes that a decision affecting some aspect of their university life has been unfair or unreasonable may have grounds for initiating a grievance. Read Policy 70, Student Petitions and Grievances, Section 4. When in doubt, please be certain to contact the department’s administrative assistant who will provide further assistance.

Discipline: A student is expected to know what constitutes academic integrity to avoid committing an academic offence, and to take responsibility for their actions. [Check the Office of Academic Integrity for more information.] A student who is unsure whether an action constitutes an offence, or who needs help in learning how to avoid offences (e.g., plagiarism, cheating) or about “rules” for group work/collaboration should seek guidance from the course instructor, academic advisor, or the undergraduate associate dean. For information on categories of offences and types of penalties, students should refer to Policy 71, Student Discipline. For typical penalties, check Guidelines for the Assessment of Penalties.

Appeals: A decision made or penalty imposed under Policy 70, Student Petitions and Grievances (other than a petition) or Policy 71, Student Discipline may be appealed if there is a ground. A student who believes they have a ground for an appeal should refer to Policy 72, Student Appeals.

Note for students with disabilities: AccessAbility Services, located in Needles Hall, Room 1401, collaborates with all academic departments to arrange appropriate accommodations for students with disabilities without compromising the academic integrity of the curriculum. If you require academic accommodations to lessen the impact of your disability, please register with AccessAbility Services at the beginning of each academic term.

Turnitin.com: Text matching software (Turnitin®) may be used to screen assignments in this course. Turnitin® is used to verify that all materials and sources in assignments are documented. Students' submissions are stored on a U.S. server, therefore students must be given an alternative (e.g., scaffolded assignment or annotated bibliography), if they are concerned about their privacy and/or security. Students will be given due notice, in the first week of the term and/or at the time assignment details are provided, about arrangements and alternatives for the use of Turnitin in this course.
