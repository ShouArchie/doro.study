Course Description

CHEM 340:

This course introduces students to molecular modelling theories and commercially available molecular modelling software packages. This course incorporates weekly lectures and hands-on computational sessions (labs). Examples are drawn from many areas of chemistry to illustrate the power and utility of molecular modelling.

View requirements for CHEM 340

An introduction to the theory and practice of computational methods used in chemistry, through the use of molecular modeling software.  The investigation of the structure and behavior of various chemical systems, including topics such as electronic structure, geometry of molecules, calculation of potential energy surfaces for reactions, will be explored.

The course introduces the students to molecular modeling tools by working through a series of exercises using the molecular modeling software, Gaussian (from Gaussian Inc.).  The course incorporates lectures where the underlying theory and program instructions will be discussed as well as hands-on computational sessions. Examples are drawn from many areas of chemistry, illustrating the kinds of information obtainable from molecular mechanics and molecular orbital computational techniques.

The modeling concepts that will be acquired through the course include the following:

  • creation of Z-matrices
  • creation of images of various molecules on the screen.
  • single point energy calculations versus geometry optimization.
  • energy minimization of structures.
  • comparison of structures.
  • examination of potential energy surfaces.

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
To introduce students to common molecular modeling software that they can use throughout their chemistry studies and more advance molecular modeling courses
To engage the students in molecular modeling exercises that will complement and enhance their understanding of concepts being discussed in other classes
To allow the student to explore new and interesting chemistry on the computer.

Administrative Policy

Participation:  All students enrolled in CHEM 340 are expected to attempt and complete all CHEM 340 practice exercises, practice exercise quizzes and hand-in exercises.

If a student is unable to complete and/or submit a practice exercise quiz and/or hand-in exercise due to illness lasting more than seven (7) consecutive days, it is the student's responsibility to contact the course instructor, by e-mail, preferably within 48 hours, but no more than 72 hours.  Have a medical practitioner complete a Verification of Illness Form (VIF).  Email a scanned copy of the VIF to the course instructor.  In your email to the course instructor, provide your name UW student ID number, and exactly what course activity you missed.  A copy of the completed VIF must be submitted to the Science Undergraduate Office.  The dates of illness on the VIF must include the date the student was not able to complete the course activity, otherwise the VIF will be considered invalid and will not be accepted.  It is imperative that you contact the course instructor and Science Undergraduate Office as soon as you are well enough to do so.  Any student failing to provide a VIF within a reasonable time line, will receive a mark of zero for their practice exercise quiz and/or hand-in exercise. The policy above applies to missing the computational project deadlines.

Any other reasons other than illness, such as travel plans, are not considered acceptable grounds for not completing a CHEM 340 practice exercise quiz, hand-in exercise, and/or any portion of the computational chemistry project.  Students are expected to make plans such that do not conflict with the CHEM 340 Course Schedule.  Situations where alternative plans cannot be made, students must inform the course instructor within one week of the start of the academic term, so that alternative arrangements can be made.  Students are required to submit a copy of any official supporting documentation before any special accommodations will be considered.  Students should refer to the CHEM 340 Course Schedule, found on the CHEM 340 LEARN site, before scheduling any plans.

For cases involving extenuating circumstances, the student must provide supporting documentation as to why they are unable to complete a CHEM 340 activity, such as practice exercise quizzes, hand-in exercises and/or any portion of the computational chemistry project.  Supporting documentation will be considered on a case by case basis. Official documentation is necessary before any action can be taken. Examples of supporting documentation include plane/train/bus tickets, court documents, police reports, death certificates, obituaries, letters from funeral directors, or counselor letters.  Student should contact the course instructor via email, as soon as possible.  The email must include your name, UW student ID number, what course activity you missed, the dates you will not be able to participate in CHEM 340 due to the extenuating circumstances, and a legible copy of the supporting documentation.  Students requiring more than two weeks to be excused from any Chem 340 activities will be required to submit additional supporting documentation, such as a counselor letter.

Practice Exercises and Practice Exercise Quizzes:  Students must attempt and complete all CHEM 340 practice exercises, associated with each session.  After completing a practice exercise, students must successfully complete the corresponding practice exercise quiz, by achieving a grade of 100%.  Students are allowed 3 attempts to successfully complete the practice exercise quiz.  If after 3 attempts the student fails to successfully complete a practice exercise quiz all input files must be submitted to the course instructor and/or teaching assistant for further inspection.  Each practice exercise quiz consists of 3 randomly chosen questions pertaining to the associated practice exercise.

Submitting Hand-in Exercises for Grading:  All CHEM 340 hand-in exercises will be submitted using Crowdmark.  Crowdmark is an online marking system.  Due dates for the hand-in exercises can be found on the CHEM 340 Course Schedule post on LEARN.

 All answers must be clearly legible.  Any answers that cannot be read will not be graded.  All text must be typed.  Equations should be created using the Equation Editor found in MS-Word or a comparable equation editor.  Text written by hand will NOT be graded. It is recommended all answers to be uploaded using a PDF format

Re-grading Hand-in Exercises:  If there a concern related as to how a hand-in exercise was graded, the student should first consult the teaching assistant responsible for marking the hand-in exercise.  If a resolution between the student and teaching assistant cannot be found, the course instructor will re-grade the entire hand-in exercise. The mark assigned by course instructor will be final.
