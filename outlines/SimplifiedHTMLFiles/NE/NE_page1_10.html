Course Description

NE 452:

Topics in this theme area may include: an overview of modern computational methods and algorithms in nanoscale materials, such as steered molecular dynamics, ab initio molecular dynamics, multiscale modelling, dissipative particle dynamics, transition path sampling, phase-field modelling, quantum simulations using Feynman path integral techniques, condensed-phase spectroscopy, linking of simulations to experiment, simulations and their practical applications.

View requirements for NE 452

*********************************************************************************************************************

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to molecular simulations, emphasizing their application in bionanotechnology. Students will explore the fundamental concepts of biomolecular structure, gaining insights into proteins, lipid membranes, and other biomolecules relevant to nanoengineering. The course delves into molecular simulation techniques, focusing on molecular dynamics (MD) simulations as a core method for studying biomolecular interactions, dynamics, and properties.

Advanced topics include enhanced sampling techniques such as umbrella sampling and metadynamics, which address challenges in exploring complex energy landscapes and rare events. Real-world examples from bionanotechnology applications will highlight the role of molecular simulations in solving engineering challenges, such as understanding nanoparticle-biomolecule interactions, studying transport phenomena through lipid membranes, and designing nanostructures for drug delivery and biosensing.

Through lectures, hands-on practicals, and case studies, students will develop the skills to apply molecular simulations to analyze and innovate within the field of bionanotechnology.

************************************************************************************************************************

 

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Describe the structural and functional properties of biomolecules such as proteins, lipid membranes, and polymers, emphasizing their relevance in nanoengineering applications.
Gain hands-on experience on different software programs such as Visual Molecular Dynamics, Chimera, Pymol that can be used for structure and simulation analyses
Perform molecular dynamics simulations of different types of molecules and investigate complex free energy landscapes and rare molecular events
Examine molecular transport through lipid membranes and nanopores, applying simulations to understand key properties and mechanisms.
Model and interpret the interactions between nanoparticles and biomolecules, understanding their implications for bionanotechnology applications such as drug delivery
