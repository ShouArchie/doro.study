Course Description

OPTOM 152:

Clinical techniques for the primary care examination of the optical properties and ocular health of the eye. Case history taking. Medical emergency responses. Professional boundaries.

View requirements for OPTOM 152

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1) Explain how measuring visual acuity works, accurately and quickly measure aided and unaided visual acuity at distance and near and record each accurately
2) Explain how ametropia affects vision, how to measure spherical and astigmatic ametropia, and accurately and quickly perform subjective and objective refraction (with a trial frame and phoropter).
3) Explain how direct and indirect ophthalmoscopy and fundus imaging works.
4) Explain ocular accommodation and presbyopia, and accurately and quickly measure amplitude of accommodation and record it properly.
5) Explain the principles and applications of keratometers and corneal topographers.
6) Demonstrate an understanding of the indications, contraindications and procedures of cycloplegic refractions.
7) Describe what spatial contrast sensitivity is, and show how it is measured.
8) Explain the procedures for prescribing a near addition.
9) Demonstrate an understanding of the importance of record keeping, legal issues and responsibilities as they relate to the eye examination.
10) Demonstrate an understanding of the components of an eye examination and the indications for the tests covered in this course as well as appropriate test sequencing.
