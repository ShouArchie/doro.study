Course Description

OPTOM 255L:

Selected clinical techniques for students taking OPTOM255.

View requirements for OPTOM 255L

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. Select appropriate tests to assess the retina.
2. Perform a confrontation visual field and an automated perimetry on a young adult without visual field abnormalities.
3. Perform comprehensive binocular indirect ophthalmoscopy technique on a young adult with a healthy retina and correctly document findings with VisualEyes EMR system.
4. Perform comprehensive fundus biomicroscopy technique of the posterior pole on a young adult with a healthy retina and correctly document findings with VisualEyes EMR system.
5. Repeat from demonstration: Amsler grid assessment, photostress recovery, red cap, carotid auscultation, and direct ophthalmoscopy.

The following lists identify the specific learning objectives for each of the lab sessions.
The laboratory sessions will enable the student optometrist to:
 

Lab 1- VR Magic Information Session

1. Gain access to use of VR Lab BIO simulators.

2. Recall the proper activity and equipment use within the SIM Lab.
 

Lab 2 – Binocular Indirect Ophthalmoscopy
1. Use the concepts of infection control in the clinical setting while performing fundus
examinations.
2. Recall the basics of pupil dilation using mydriatic drugs.
3. Integrate the clinical application of mydriatics, including types of drugs, indications
for use, contraindications, and adverse effects.
4. Recall the basic concepts associated with BIO including advantages and
disadvantages, indications, and optical properties
5. Carry out binocular indirect ophthalmoscopy procedure of the posterior pole on a
young adult with a healthy retina.
a. Set up and adjust the binocular indirect ophthalmoscope
b. Position the patient correctly (reclined)
c. Obtain a clear image of the ocular fundus and fill condensing lens with the
image of the posterior pole
d. Obtain a stereoscopic view
 

Lab 3 – Binocular Indirect Ophthalmoscopy (continued)
1. Review lab # 1
2. Perform a systematic examination of the entire fundus.
3. Identify anatomical landmarks
– Disc
– Vascular arcades
– Vortex vein ampullae
– Long posterior ciliary nerve/artery
– Short posterior ciliary nerves
4. Document fundus observations

Lab 4 – Fundus Biomicroscopy
1. Conduct a thorough and systematic fundus biomicroscopy examination of the
posterior pole, including the disc, retinal vasculature, and macula
2. Assess the optic nerve head and cup/disc ratios
3. Examine the retinal periphery
4. Examine the posterior vitreous
5. Document fundus observations
6. Initiate comprehensive fundus drawing with fundus biomicroscopy

Lab 5- Fundus Biomicroscopy Continued

Lab 6 - Visual Field Testing
1. Recall the basic concepts of visual field and visual field assessment
2. Conduct a confrontation visual field assessment on a young adult with no visual field
abnormalities
3. Conduct an Amsler grid examination on a young adult with no visual field abnormalities.
4. Review a 30-2 and/or 24-2 automoated threshold visual field on a young adult with no
visual field abnormalities.
a. Set up of the Humphrey Visual Field Analyser
b. Printing test results.
c. Analyse Humphrey visual field results.
 

Lab 7 – Direct Ophthalmoscopy, Carotid Auscultation, Auxiliary Testing
1. View ocular structures using the direct ophthalmoscope on a young adult with a healthy
retina.
2. Repeat carotid artery flow by means of stethoscope examination.
3. Repeat and interpret the photostress recovery test on a young adult.
4. Repeat and interpret the brightness comparison test on a young adult.
5. Repeat and interpret the “red cap” test for colour saturation on a young adult.

Lab 8 -Review and Practice

Optometry 255L is graded on a credit basis (i.e. CR or NCR). To obtain credit in OPT 255L the
following must be completed in addition to attending labs:


1. Watch the video tutorials (~15-30min each) on LEARN prior to each lab session

2. Complete the online quiz on LEARN by the dates specified above.
a. Two attempts permitted. Highest score accepted.
b. 10 minutes given to complete each quiz.
c.  Achieve cumulative average of 60% on all quizzes.

3. Complete posterior pole fundus drawing. Due date: Feb. 24, end of lab

4. Complete visual field analysis assignment. Due date: Feb.10, end of lab

4. Pass (50%) VR Magic BIO Courses (A1-A3, A5-A7, B1, B2) by April 1.

5. Pass (60%) all sections of the clinical practical examinations
(I) Fundus Biomicroscopy
(II) Binocular Indirect Ophthalmoscopy
(III) Confrontation Fields
 

Formal clinical practical examinations will be held during the laboratory sessions at the end of
term (Mar. 10 & 17). Specific details regarding the practical examination will be provided.


SUPPLEMENTAL EXAMS:

In the event that the practical exam was not successfully completed, March 24 is set aside for remediation and the exam will be readministered the last day of the lab course (March 31). Failure to pass the retake exam will result in failure of the course.

COMMITMENT EXPECTATIONS:
Mandatory activities in this course are:
All laboratory sessions
Quizzes, Fundus drawing, Perimetry assignment, VR modules, Final laboratory examination
Attendance in the laboratory and examination is required. You will be learning clinical
procedures that are routinely used in clinical practice. You must be proficient with all these
techniques before entering clinic. Additional practice outside of lab sessions (including
