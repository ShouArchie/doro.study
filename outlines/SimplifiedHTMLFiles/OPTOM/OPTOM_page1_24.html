Course Description

OPTOM 246:

Multifocal spectacle lenses. Aberrations of thin lenses and spectacle lens design. Prescribing and fitting of spectacles. Environmental and occupational prescribing. Prescription analysis. Ophthalmic and visual standards. Computer vision syndrome. Ophthalmic laboratory procedures: measurement of complex spectacle lenses, and spectacle frame adjustment and repairs.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Accurately recall the nomenclature for fitting parameters of multifocal lenses and describe their measurement/placement.
Discriminate between multifocal lens types, and propose appropriate designs for patient’s vocational/avocational requirements.
Understand and apply the limits of tolerance for quality control on prescription finished blanks and finished spectacles.
Apply a theoretical understanding of interocular prescription differences to determine appropriate prismatic/aniseikonic corrections.
Explore occupational and/or hazardous activities to propose appropriate lens designs for occupational or eye protection.
Accurately classify and analyse prescriptions, and use to make appropriate recommendations for lens designs.
Distinguish between basic magnifier and telescope types, and apply advantages/disadvantages of each to match an appropriate aid to patient requirements.
By the end of the laboratory section students should be able to:
Identify the lens design and measure the prescription power, base curve and addition power (where indicated) of an unknown lens.
Measure the lens thickness to within 0.1mm of an unknown lens.
Identify the presence/absence of slab off prism and neutralise/verify it in an unknown lens.
Measure monocular pupillary distances for distance and near with a ruler and pupilometer.
Complete the required measurements to accurately position bifocal, trifocal, and PAL lens designs.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Midterm Assessment 25%
Laboratory Assessments* 30%
Final (Comprehensive from all of terms material) 45%

An overall grade of 60% or better is required to pass the course overall. This is obtained by the weighted sum of the grades achieved in each of the course components. 

*In the event that 60% or better is not achieved in the final laboratory practical assessment and the student is otherwise in good standing, an INC will be filed and a re-assessment of the practical assessment will be scheduled. If 60% is not achieved at the re-assessment, an overall grade of 56% will be filed for the course as a whole. 

Laboratory

Laboratory notes and any pre-laboratory exercises will be posted on Learn. 

40% of grade – weekly assignments & attendance

60% of grade - final mechanical lab practical assessment

The final mechanical laboratory assessment is taken to be a measure of clinical competency in practical dispensing skills (accuracy & efficiency). Students must achieve a passing grade (60%) in the final mechanical laboratory assessment in order to successfully complete this component.

Assessment and Final Exam Format

The format of the midterm and final examinations will be announced in class meetings and on Learn prior to the examination. 

The final examination is comprehensive (i.e. it may include any content covered in the course, in either the labs or lectures). The assessments will also expect content covered in Ophthalmics Optics 1 relevant to multifocal lenses to be recalled/applied (lens materials, tints & coatings, frame selection/materials etc).
