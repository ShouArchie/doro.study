Course Description

OPTOM 342B:

A continuation of OPTOM342A. Emphasis is placed on the differential diagnostic method of analyzing clinical data with special emphasis on refractive and binocular vision conditions.

View requirements for OPTOM 342B

This course is taught with lectures and team-based learning (TBL) sessions. 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
At the end of this course, students will be able to:
1. Read eye movements
2. Differentially diagnose common and critical ocular motor problems.
3. Know appropriate management strategies for common and critical ocular motor problems.
4. Use critical thinking skills to solve common and critical clinical.
5. Appreciate analytical and non-analytical models of problem solving.
6. Follow the five steps of evidence-based practice.
7. Demonstrate an ability to use life long, self-directed assessment and learning skills.
8. Use effective teamwork skills.

 

Team-based learning: 

  • TBL was originally developed by Larry Michaelsen for instructing business students in the early 1990s. TBL is an active learning method that is learner-centred but instructor led. It fosters individual and group accountability as groups of 6-8 students work together to solve clinical problems. 
  • This method is used for large classes that are divided into smaller groups (referred to as teams) that have maximal diversity within the teams but relative evenness between the teams. This is accomplished by deciding what the key characteristics of individual members are that would promote success of a team and then distributing people with these qualities evenly between the teams.  In that way, each team has a maximal diversity in the knowledge and experience of the members that can be drawn upon when solving the analysis problems. 
  • The instructor sets the learning objectives and purposefully designs the course into modules that will address those learning objectives. The modules consist of three repeating phases. The first phase involves a prior learning assignment where the students study material in advance of the learning session that has been assigned by the instructor.
  • The second phase focuses on readiness assurance and has four steps.  Initially the students take an individual readiness assurance test (IRAT). This is a multiple choice test that assesses concepts from the prior learning assignment. The focus of the test is on recall of factual material rather than application. After the students individually complete the test, the team then takes the same test as a group. This is referred to as the group readiness assurance test (GRAT) Using an Immediate Feedback Assessment Technique (IF-AT) the group members work together on the answers until all of the answers are correct. The IF-AT is a scratch card that contains the correct answer for each question. The groups will normally perform better than the highest score of any individual member. The students are next allowed to appeal in writing any questions that they do not feel are fair. Each challenge is considered by the instructor. In the final step, any outstanding misconceptions around the content are addressed by the instructor.
  • In the third phase of the process the students work in the same teams to solve problems that are based on the material that has been learned. These are called application exercises. The problems are designed to have the following characteristics; they are the same for all of the groups, the problems are significant for the learners, the groups must make a specific choice for the correct answer (e.g., a multiple choice question) and the results are simultaneously reported by all groups. The problems have multiple solutions that allow for debate of the correct answer. The teams then defend their answer to other teams, who have chosen a different answer. The instructor facilitates the discussion between teams.
  • An important component of TBL is the group evaluations. Team members give feedback to the other group members on team performance once during the course. This allows the students to practice giving and receiving feedback to members about their ability to work in productive team.
