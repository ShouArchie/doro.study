Course Description

OPTOM 139:

An introduction to colour perception, colourimetry and colour discrimination. Characteristics of congenital and acquired colour vision deficiencies, colour vision test design and patient management.

View requirements for OPTOM 139

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand basic colour specification systems; basic theories of normal and abnormal colour vision; colour vision test design
Know basic colour discrimination for normal and abnormal colour vision; several testing and management strategies for individuals with abnormal colour vision
Distinguish between acquired and congenital colour vision defects
Experience colour vision matching and mixing experiments; colour vision testing

OVERALL GRADE:

An overall grade of 60% or better is required to pass the course. A grade of 50% or better must be achieved in the final assessment. Students who do not achieve 50% or better in the final assessment will be assigned an overall grade in the course that is the lesser of: the earned grade or 55%.

MIDTERM & FINAL ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:

Format. These may include multiple-choice and short-answer questions. The exact format will be described nearer the scheduled time of the assessment. The final assessment is a comprehensive examination and may include questions relating to the concepts and procedures learned in the laboratory sessions. 

Equipment. Please bring to the assessment a pen, pencil, ruler and calculator. Ensure that your calculator is approved by the instructor and operating prior to the examination.

Final Assessment Date. The final assessment date will be released by the Registrar's Office.

IN-CLASS, TAKE-HOME AND ONLINE ACTIVITIES INFORMATION:

A variety of interactive methods will be used to enhance learning and assess comprehension. These may include in-class or online quizzes, peer grading, assignments, and group work. Each activity will be weighted equally. Assignment of zero marks for an activity will be assigned in the event of an absence. The lowest activity grade will be dropped from the total assessment.

LABORATORY ASSESSMENT INFORMATION:

Lab Reports: This is a group activity required for each laboratory assessment.  Submit one assignment as a group for grading.  Assignment of zero marks for a lab report will occur if the group fails to submit it by the deadline.

Laboratory Exercises

  • Scrubs are mandatory. 
  • Everyone in the group is responsible for completing the lab exercise. TA will monitor and encourage group activity.  If a student doesn't involve in the group activity, TA may opt to reduce grade of the individual who was not involved in completing the lab exercise.  
  • The lab schedule will be posted in Learn.
  • The lab assignments will be due 1 week after they are assigned.   Upload them in the appropriate Dropbox when you are finished.
    • Your grade for the individual labs will be reduced by 15% for each day that it is late (i.e., a reduction of 15% for the first 24 hrs and another 15% for the next 24 hours)

Administrative Policy

To promote knowledge acquisition and application, you are encouraged to participate in all aspects of this course. The following activities are mandatory: 

  • final assessment
  • midterm
  • all laboratory sessions and reports

Each student is provided with the ability to drop the lowest scoring in-class, take-home, or online activity. Therefore, if only one is missed, then the grade will be based on the ones you have completed. If multiple activities are missed with an approved absence or verification of illness, then contact the instructor directly within 1 week of your return to class to make alternative arrangements.
