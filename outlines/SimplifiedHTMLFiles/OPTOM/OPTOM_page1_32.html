Course Description

OPTOM 272:

Detection and evaluation of sensory and motor characteristics of vision in aniseikonic, strabismic and nonstrabismic patients. Classifications, diagnoses, prognoses, and modes of therapy for aniseikonic, nonstrabismic, and strabismic patients.

View requirements for OPTOM 272

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Define and understand the critical terms and concepts central to the assessment, diagnosis and management of anomalies of binocular vision in particular strabismus and aniseikonia
Be proficient in the clinical testing for binocular sensory and motor problems
Understand the evidence which is needed in the management of strabismic disorders
Be proficient in the design and delivery of appropriate therapies for specific binocular motor problems using ophthalmic appliances and/or vision training
Define and understand the role of refractive and strabismic causes of amblyopia
Be able to treat and manage amblyopia in all ages of patients based upon current evidence
Have a working knowledge of aniseikonia as it pertains to anisometropia
Be proficient to provide appropriate assessments, diagnoses and treatments in binocular vision as needed in 4th year clinic rotations in primary care, binocular vision and pediatrics

Consequences of a missed graded element

Where a student requests a planned absence, or notifies an unplanned absence from a mandatory activity, attempts will be made to accommodate the absence. Missed midterms cannot be accommodated. In the event that the request cannot be accommodated, the missed graded element will be added to the weight of the final examination. Unplanned absences without notification will be given a grade of 0.
