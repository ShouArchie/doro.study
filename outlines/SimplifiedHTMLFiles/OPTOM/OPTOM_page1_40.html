Course Description

OPTOM 468:

Supervised optometric patient care in one, or more, external clinical settings. This course is complementary to OPTOM458 (Clinical Clerkship 1) and includes optometric assessment, diagnosis, and management of refractive errors, disorders, and diseases of the eye and visual system, along with associated systemic conditions and practice management.

View requirements for OPTOM 468

OPTOM 468 (Clerkship 1) is a 4th year, 15-week clinical training course performed in a variety of approved clinics within Canada.

All students assigned to OPTOM 468 must be in good academic standing and have fees arranged with the University of Waterloo three business days prior to the start of the clerkship term.  

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Enhance their skills in diagnosing, treating, and managing refractive, oculomotor sensory and ocular health problems.
Develop an understanding of the importance of collegial interprofessional relationships and learn to appreciate the role of optometric services in different settings.
Experience and understand clinical philosophies different than those experienced at the University of Waterloo School of Optometry and Vision Science (UWOVS).

ASSESSMENT

Minimum Patient Requirements:

Students will be directly involved in a minimum of 25 full or partial oculovisual assessments* per week.  In addition to patient care, students are expected to engage in all areas of the practice which may include but are not limited to: pre-testing, contact lens teaching, spectacle dispensing, reception duties, meeting industry representatives, and attending office meetings. 

*all oculovisual assessments should include patient testing, data interpretation, and development of a management plan.

Minimum Time Requirements:

Students are expected to attend clinic the equivalent of 5 days per week for at least 8 hours each day in order to receive the assigned number of credits for the course. Please note that actual hours and days of office and patient care activity are determined at the discretion of the clerkship supervisor.

Course Grade Requirements:

The final course grade will include all submitted evaluations. Successful completion of OPTOM 468 depends on meeting the following standard:

  • Overall passing grade of 70% or higher (CR)*
  • Completion of patient logs and clerkship site evaluation

* Credit (CR) for OPTOM 468 is granted if the student achieves an overall grade of 70% or higher. If a student receives an overall grade of less than 70% this is recorded as a failure and the student will be assigned a non-credit (NCR) for the course and follow the policies outlined by the University of Waterloo. The overall grade for OPTOM 468 is determined by the clinical skills and clinic behaviour evaluations submitted throughout the term.

Calculation of Course Grade:

Principal clerkship supervisors complete three written evaluations of the student for site durations of 15 weeks. The first evaluation considers performance over the first 5 weeks, the second evaluation considers the second 5-week block and the third evaluation considers performance over the final 5 weeks. For site durations of 7 or 8 weeks, the first evaluation considers performance during the first half and the final evaluation considers the last half. When more than one supervisor is involved, input from all clerkship supervisors contribute towards the evaluation submitted by the primary supervisor. Evaluations should include written comments in addition to the grading scale indicators.  Clerkship supervisors are encouraged to offer comprehensive and constructive feedback for improvement and should share the first and second evaluation with the student before submitting it to the RedCap Grading System. A student conference is not required for the final evaluation.

In all cases, written/oral forms of feedback to the student are expected from the clerkship supervisor.  The clerkship supervisor uses behavioural descriptors provided on the evaluation form as a guide in grading the student. Additionally, an academic penalty of 5% reduction in the overall grade will be applied.  If the number of ‘no’ responses for the clinical behaviour evaluation is five or more, the students final grade will automatically revert to a 32%.

Student Patient Log/ Final Summary:

Students are required to maintain a comprehensive log and final summary of all patient encounters and enter them on ESES weekly throughout the term. Keeping a complete and accurate summary of all patient care activity on a weekly (preferably daily) basis, is a mandatory course requirement for OPTOM 468. Patient logs will be checked weekly, and random checks will occur throughout the term for accuracy.  In the case where patient logs are found to be inaccurate, the student will be contacted, and the Associate Director for Clinical Education will be notified. 

Student Site Evaluations:

Students are required to complete a site evaluation on ESES no later than 2 business days after completing OPTOM 468.  Failure to submit a final site evaluation will result in a NCR for Optometry 468. 

Expectations for Students:

Students are expected to dress appropriately and to always act in a professional manner. All students are required to conform to the professional dress code determined by the individual clerkship facility. Failure to comply with this code may result in the student being excused to modify her/his dress. If the site has no established dress code, the student will be expected to comply with the dress code of the University of Waterloo Optometry Clinic.

Students should consider themselves to be representatives of UWOVS, as well as of the profession of Optometry, and should behave accordingly. To avoid any potential for misunderstanding the student role at a clerkship site, students are required to wear name badges that clearly identify them as an “Clinical Intern”. Students should be willing to accept responsibility for patients assigned to them, and they should act courteously and responsibly.

Administrative Policy

ABSENCES:

Student attendance in considered mandatory for all clinical assignments and is a requirement even if there are no pre-booked appointments. This course follows the principles outlined in the undergraduate optometry students Attendance Policy. 

Student Attendance:

Attendance is mandatory at all activities associated with the site to which they are assigned. The hours and days of office and patient care activity are determined at the discretion of the clerkship supervisor.  

Students should report to their clerkship supervisor at the beginning of each clinic day. Any student reporting later than 30 minutes after the start of a clinic day will be considered unexcused and will be required to submit an Absence Request Form.  

Habitual tardiness or unexcused absences is considered inappropriate clinical behaviour and will be graded accordingly. 

Student Authorized, Anticipated Absences from Assigned Clerkship Clinic:

Students should assume their obligation to the clinical program and to patients is paramount, and that permission to be absent from clerkship rotations will be given only when clearly appropriate. Absences from the clerkship rotation may be approved and endorsed, such that no make-up time is required.  

Students are allowed a maximum of 3 clinic days of planned and endorsed absence from their clerkship site (including travel*) without make-up time. If a student exceeds 3 days of endorsed absences, make-up time will be automatically required. Alternatively, an absence may also be approved but not endorsed, such that make-up time is required.

*Travel days are only endorsed when the clerkship site is located at least120km from the event location. 

Examples of planned absences that are approved and endorsed  (supporting documentation required):

  • Academic conferences for:
    • Research presentations (first author only*)
      • Copy of abstract acceptance or invitation to present is required
        • *If the student is not the first author of a research presentation, then a letter from the research supervisor and/or first author must explain why the student is being asked to present. 
    • Student certification
      • Proof of student certification application and completion required
    • Leadership
      • Poof of relevant student leadership position and their role/activities at the conference required
  • Optometry related certification examinations (letter from examining board):
    • Optometry Examining Board of Canada (OEBC)
    • Jurisprudence examination for any province
    • National Board Examiners in Optometry (NBEO)

In addition to the guidelines listed above, planned absences may not be approved during the orientation week of clinic, or during the final week of the rotation to ensure completion of all patient care activities.  

In cases of bereavement or chronic illness (with doctors note), a student may be entitled to exceed the 3 day maximum. In these instances, cases will be dealt with on an individual basis.

Examples of planned absences that are approved and not endorsed (supporting documentation required):  

  • Alcon Academy for Optometry students (confirmation of registration)
  • Any Optometric meeting as an attendee, not as a presenter, volunteer etc. (confirmation of registration)
  • Residency interviews (letter from institution)
  • School committee membership (committee and role)
  • Family emergency, funeral or memorial service (relationship)
  • Health care appointment (note from health care provider, information on medical condition is not required)
  • UW-recognized religious observance (specific observance, including date(s)/time)
  • Major personal celebration event (event, participation, role, and travel days)
  • Organized athletic event or other competition (invited participation; correspondence of invitation)

Unplanned Absences:  

An absence or lateness may arise due to unforeseen and often emergent circumstances. In such cases, UWOVS recognizes the right of students to determine the best course of action based on their unique knowledge of, and perspective on, the situation. This course of action may include a decision to miss an assigned clinic. Students are expected to immediately notify the clerkship site to which they are assigned if this occurs. UWOVS is committed to fostering a supportive, compassionate environment at the heart of which is the conviction that student well-being is intimately connected to student success.  

Nevertheless, it must be recognized that a student’s right to decide to miss clinic is accompanied by a responsibility to accept and address any academic consequences of the decision. Students who decide to be absent from clinic are still responsible for submitting notification of their absence as soon as possible, after attending to the immediate needs arising from the situation. Upon return, an Absence Request Form MUST be completed and signed by the clerkship supervisor. The form is then to be emailed to the Clerkship Coordinator.  

Absences are considered “unauthorized” whenever a student has not notified the clerkship site or the UWOVS, and is found to be absent from an assigned location or activity. Leaving a site, center, or clinic without permission constitutes an “unauthorized absence”. Unauthorized absences is considered inappropriate clinical behaviour and will be graded accordingly. Make-up protocols may be developed at the discretion of the Course Instructor.

Absence Request Form:

An Absence Request Form must be completed and submitted for both planned and unplanned absences. If the absence request is for a planned absence, it must be submitted at least 4 weeks in advance of any mandatory activity or as soon as the special circumstance is known. If the absence is unplanned (due to emergent situation, illness etc.), the absence must be reported no later than one week after the first day of absence. The site supervisor must initially authorize the absence before submitting the form and the clerkship head will determine the amount of make-up time required. All absence request forms must be submitted to the Clerkship Coordinator.

Students should anticipate that each approved absence is generally contingent upon making up that time at a later date within the same clinical rotation. If the absence is unable to be made up during the clerkship, the absence will carry-forward, and the student will be required to make-up that time at the UWOVS Optometry Clinics prior to completing their fourth year. Students may not make-up clinic time prior to the absence date.

Clerkship Supervisor Absences:

Clerkship supervisors have been instructed to provide alternate clinic activities if direct patient care cannot be provided due to a planned or unplanned absence. In cases when alternate activities have not been arranged, students are required to inform the Course Instructor and Clerkship Coordinator as soon as possible.

Other Clerkship Absences:

All absences and absence requests not specifically addressed above will require expressed permission from the clerkship supervisor and the course instructor.

Unforeseen Clerkship Site Closure:

If for any reason the clerkship site is not able to continue supervision for the term, the Course Instructor and Clerkship Coordinator will attempt to arrange for immediate placement at an alternate site and time missed will be re-scheduled accordingly.

 

PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT OF STUDENTS:

UW Doctor of Optometry Program Mission: “to graduate competent, ethical Doctors of Optometry by providing them with a comprehensive and contemporary optometric education”

Consistent with this mission, the UW Doctor of Optometry program has identified goals for the program. One such goal is to “provide high quality optometric education which will prepare graduates to provide independent contemporary optometric services in an ethical and professional manner”. OPT 468 contributes greatly to the UWOVS' ability to reach its goal of preparing graduates to meet these goals.

Consistent with University Policies 33 Ethical Behaviour and 71 Academic Student Discipline, students are expected to:

  • conduct themselves with honesty and integrity at all times;
  • act professionally and respectfully toward clinic instructors, staff members, patients and colleagues;
  • put, in most cases, the needs of the patients before their own;
  • make themselves aware of, and adhere to, clinic policies;
  • be available for clinic assignment, complete all assigned responsibilities and follow the directives of their clerkship supervisors;
  • demonstrate an interest in self-improvement;
  • familiarize themselves with, and adhere to, the professional conduct sections as outlined by the provincial College of their clerkship site

Students who anticipate problems in adhering to policies as required are encouraged to speak to the course instructor.

Complaints:

If the course instructor receives a complaint about professional misconduct by the student, the student will be notified of the complaint and provided with an opportunity to respond.  If there is concern for patient safety or clinic personnel, or a breakdown of professional or patient trust, the student may be withdrawn from providing patient care until such time as the matter can be adjudicated.
