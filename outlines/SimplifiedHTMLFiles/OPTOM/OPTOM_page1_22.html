Course Description

OPTOM 219:

Physical space and visual space. Fundamental perceptual processes, binocular vision, stereopsis, binocular space perception. Systems of analysing binocular vision. Theory of aniseikonia. Perceptual aspects of aniseikonia.

View requirements for OPTOM 219

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Identify and describe the components of normal binocular vision, and describe how they are interrelated.
Describe the key psychophysical and physiological components and characteristics of classical and contemporary theories of binocular vision.
Identify and describe the physical stimuli of each component of binocular vision, and the physiological and perceptual response to each stimulus.
Describe how the various components of physical space influence the final perception within normal binocular visual space.
Evaluate binocular vision and apply or devise appropriate diagnostic tests to identify anomalous binocular vision.
