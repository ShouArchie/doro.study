Course Description

OPTOM 262:

Differential diagnosis and problem specific testing using a systems examination approach. Minimum database expectations. Record keeping, case presentations, counselling, referrals and report writing. Preparation for entry into Optometry Clinic including policy review and standards of practice. Observation and preliminary testing in the Optometry Clinic.

View requirements for OPTOM 262

This course is taught with lectures, practical skills laboratories, role plays, small group problem solving and active learning sessions within the classroom (when possible). The laboratory component will also simulate 3rd year WOVS clinic activities in order to prepare students for Term 3A of clinic.

Our main campus is situated on the Haldimand Tract, the land granted to the Six Nations that includes six miles on each side of the Grand River. Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Conduct a database examination including recording on a "new" adult patient in 1hr45 minutes using Visual Eyes
Accurately record clinical data including the diagnosis and management plan in an EMR system
Modify the database examination for patients with presbyopia or those with findings outside of norms
Apply verbal, nonverbal and written communication strategies
Recognize analytic and non-analytic clinical reasoning processes
Recognize sources of error in clinical reasoning
Use test selection principles in test and treatment thresholds for a simple case
Recall how an optometrist is positioned within and relates to the health care system in Canada.
Recognize professional behaviour and accept responsibility for any breach
Plan for own development of clinical competence

MANDATORY COMPONENTS OF THIS COURSE are weekly labs, weekly lab quizzes (these will be open for 4.5 days after 530pm), in-lab reflection, the final lab exam, and the final course assessment (Faculty of Science-approved calculators permitted). Please follow the School's absence policy (described in the Administrative Policy section) for any planned or unplanned absences from mandatory components. 

Consequences of a missed mandatory element:

  • Where a student requests a planned absence, or notifies of an unplanned absence from a mandatory course assessment, attempts will be made to accommodate the absence. In the event that the request cannot be accommodated, the missed graded element will be added to the weight of the final examination. If the missed mandatory is a final assessment you will be given an INC (incomplete) until such time as the assessment component is completed. Missed mandatory lab components in Optom 262 will have to be made up outside of lab time and most likely in the student's own time. In most instances quizzes will not be reopened if missed.

COMMITMENT EXPECTATIONS: 

Class attendance: not mandatory but expected. Students are responsible for all material presented in class including content not included in the handouts, and changes and additions to laboratories not included in the course description. Some classes will have an assigned reading and/or video to review in the student's own time prior to class. These activities will be posted/indicated in advance.

Labs: Prep time will be required before each lab. The time required will vary from student to student, but each student should sufficiently plan to read the lab instructions ahead of time (posted to LEARN weekly), and to complete any assigned readings and/or videos. For the database examination labs, examining students are required to set a goal in advance of arrival. Time outside of lab is likely required for all students to practice clinical techniques and to complete the weekly lab assignments. 

Lab assignments: Recognize that each student has 3 in-lab opportunities to practice running through a full OVA in the allotted time before the final lab examination. Your preparedness, practice outside of lab time and personal plan for efficiency and self-development will play important roles in maximizing these opportunities.

During labs 2 and 4 (or 3 and 5), you will do a full oculo-visual assessment (OVA) on an assigned classmate (partner assignments will be posted on LEARN). Students will be required to set a goal ahead of each of these labs, and complete a reflection at the end of the session regarding a plan for improvement. All database tests will be performed including ones you are concurrently learning in Optom 255L. Examining students are advised to start the lab as close to the scheduled time as possible - for the 130pm session in particular this may mean arriving early to set up the exam lane; students acting as patients are similarly expected to arrive no later than the lab start time. Results should be documented in VisualEyes (under the examining student's name), the diagnosis and plan list should be completed, and the chart should be signed. Any tests not completed during the lab should be completed outside laboratory time. 

During lab 8 (or 9), you will complete as much of an OVA as possible on a classmate in 1 hour and 45 minutes. Any tests not completed will need to be completed outside of lab time. The completed examination record will then be printed using the screen dump function in Visual Eyes, and handed in to Dr. Khan for review and feedback. Feedback will be given in preparation for the lab examination which will be graded for completeness, clarity, test selection etc.  

The lab examination will be conducted during labs 10 through 12. You will have 1 hour and 45 minutes to do a full examination including dilation, fundus biomicroscopy and BIO on an assigned classmate, and then complete, sign and print the exam record. The test selection, identification of abnormal test results and correct record keeping will be graded for the lab examination.  

Lab quizzes: There are 8 graded quizzes. Quizzes will be undertaken through LEARN and will remain open for 4.5 days, starting at 530pm each Wednesday. These should take 20-30 minutes each. Quizzes that are not submitted by the deadline will receive a mark of zero and will not be reopened for completion. The final grade will be the average of all the quizzes, which will be given equal weighting.  You can repeat the quizzes twice, but the mark for each quiz will be based on an average of the percentage correct answers. The quizzes are primarily a learning (formative) exercise and you will gain the most benefit by working on them by yourself.

GENERATIVE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE POLICY:

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as writing personal reflections regarding improvement as well as a letter-writing activity. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean.

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence

Students are encouraged to reach out to campus supports if they need help with their coursework including:

  • Student Success Office for help with skills like notetaking and time management
  • Writing and Communication Centre for assignments with writing or presentations
  • AccessAbility Services for documented accommodations
  • Library for research-based assignments
