Course Description

OPTOM 150:

Legal aspects of practising optometry in Canada. Fundamentals of Canadian law. Negligence. Informed consent. Legislation and regulations affecting optometric practice.

View requirements for OPTOM 150

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Describe and discuss the structures for the regulation of health professions in Ontario and other provinces with specific emphasis on optometry
Explain the respective roles, responsibilities and rights of regulated health professionals (i.e., optometrists), patients, and regulatory bodies
Describe and discuss the many ways regulatory authorities (e.g., Colleges) act to protect the public interest
Examine the roles and responsibilities of various optometric organisations (e.g., Colleges, Associations) in the regulation of the profession
Compare and contrast the regulatory structure in Ontario and other Canadian jurisdictions
Examine and appraise how case law applies to the practice of optometry

An overall grade of 60% or better is required to pass the course.

In Person Quizzes:

Quiz 2 and 4 will take place in-person during a portion of our regular scheduled class time. Students will require a pen or pencil to complete the quizzes during class time. The quiz will consist of multiple choice and/or true & false and/or short answer questions. You will be given 30 minutes to complete the quiz. The quizzes are cumulative. It is a closed book exam, which means NO additional materials are permitted to be used during the exam. Examples of additional materials include: internet searches, your course notes, the textbook, or discussions with your classmates (texts, instant messaging, email) during the exam period. 

Online Quiz Instructions:

Quiz 1 & 3 will be completed individually on LEARN during a portion of the regular scheduled class time. It is a closed book exam, which means NO additional materials are permitted to be used during the exam. Examples of additional materials include: internet searches, your course notes, the textbook, or discussions with your classmates (texts, instant messaging, email) during the exam period. 

As the quiz will be available during a portion of the regular class time, I respectfully ask that you do not discuss the exam with any of your classmates until after the exam window has closed. You will be asked to complete an academic integrity declaration in order to submit each quiz. Once you start the quiz, you will have 30 minutes to complete it. The quiz will consist of multiple choice and/or true & false and/or short answer questions.  The quizzes are cumulative.
