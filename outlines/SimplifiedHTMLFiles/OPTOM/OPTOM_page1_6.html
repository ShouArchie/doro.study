Course Description

OPTOM 126:

Refraction and reflection of light by the surfaces of the eye. Optical properties of emmetropic and ametropic eyes as a function of growth and pupil size and their measurement. Properties of the retinal image including size, blur and quality and their relationship to imperfections of the optics. Light scattered in the eye. Relationships between the retinal image and visual perception. The retinal image and accommodation.

View requirements for OPTOM 126

 

Must have completed the following:

OPTOM106 - Geometrical, Physical and Visual Optics (0.50)

Enrolled in Optometry

Learning Outcomes

No explicit learning outcomes defined for this course.

At the end of the course the student should be able to understand the basic optics of the eye and the optical components,

1.Understand entoptic phenomena

2.Understand  emmetropia and ametropia, and correction of refractive error, 

3.Mechansims of accommodation, and its effect on vision quality

4.Know aberrations and their effect on retinal image quality. 

 5.The student should be able to do calculations using a simple model eye.

6.Understand principles of Intraocular lenses,LASIK, etc.
