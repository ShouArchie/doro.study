Course Description

OPTOM 134:

An introduction to the vertebrate immune response; the cells and tissues of the lymphoid system; humoral and cell-mediated immunity; initiation and regulation of the immune response; the immune system and disease, techniques used in immunology. Unique features of the immunobiology of the eye are included.

View requirements for OPTOM 134

The main goal of the course is to provide a comprehensive overview of the fundamental concepts in immunology, to present recent advances in our understanding of the immune system, and to address its role in health and disease. The students will get the opportunity to acquire clinically relevant knowledge regarding important immunological processes and diagnostic tests based on tissue and serum antibody determination. The course will place emphasis on the indications, clinical implementation, and interpretation of immunological test results and phenomena, integrating both basic and clinical immunology. It is expected that students completing this course will have mastered the terminology and knowledge needed to understand not only the current literature in immunology and the principles of clinical immunology, but also the impact and application of immunology in other medical disciplines.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the fundamental concepts in immunology and the components and processes involved in the immune respose
Acquire clinically relevant knowledge regarding important immunological processes and diagnostic tests
Understand indications, clinical implementation, and interpretation of immunological test results and phenomena
Understand the immunopathology behind antitumor immunity, immunotherapy, hypersensitivity, transplant rejection, autoimmunity, and immunodefficiency

COURSE CONTENT AND OUTLINE - LECTURE AND LAB TOPICS:

 

1. Basic Concepts in Immunology. Immunity - Pathogenesis and Clinical Implications

2. Cells and Tissues of the Immune System

3. Innate and Adaptive Immunity. Innate Immunity Initiation Mechanisms

4. Innate immunity. Inflammatory Responses. Complement System

5. MHC/HLA (Main Histocompatibility Complex)

6. Transplantation of Tissues and Organs 

7. Anti –Tumor Immunity. Immunotherapy

8. Virtual LAB 1. Diagnosis of Disease Based on Immune Response and Antibody Detection in Serum. Virtual Laboratory Exercise - Enzyme Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA). Results Interpretation and Significance                   

9. Virtual LAB 2. Acquired Immunity. Vaccines. Types of vaccines. Indications and contraindications for immunization.  Advantages and disadvantages of different types of vaccines.

10. Virtual LAB 3. Using Immunological Response and Parameters in the Diagnostics of Acute and Chronic Infectious Diseases or Previous Exposure to Infectious Diseases. Rubella Radial Haemolysis Test Lab Exercise

11. T-cell Mediated Immunity, Mechanisms, Clinical Significance

12. Humoral Immunity, Mechanisms, Clinical Significance

13. Hypersensitivity Reactions part I

14. Autoimmunity and Autoimmune Diseases (Hypersensitivity part II, III)

15. Failure of Host Defences. Immunodeficiency Syndromes, Clinical Significance

University Policy
