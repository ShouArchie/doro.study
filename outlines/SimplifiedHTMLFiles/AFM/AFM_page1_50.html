Instructor & TA (Teaching Assistant) Information

 

Course Description

AFM 345:

This course reinforces the data analytics process, introducing applications that leverage tools such as text analytics and sentiment analysis to analyze content from social networks to address business problems.

View requirements for AFM 345

Social media have become vital sources of information that influence decision making procedure in industries. Social media platforms and web-based communities offer invaluable insights into customer sentiment and organizational dynamics.

This course introduces social media analytics and its applications to extract insights in accounting and finance. It is designed to provide students with practical knowledge to harness the power of social network data. We will cover various analytics methods to extract useful and useable information from publicly available dataset, including but not limited to text analytics, network analysis and unsupervised learning. 

By the end of the course, students will be equipped with coding skills and analytical tools necessary to navigate and make data-driven decisions in the complex landscape of social networks, unlocking new opportunities for business growth and innovation.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand social media analytics and data analytics process from data collection to data analysis.
Lean how to extract meaningful information from social media data and use those data for business decision making.
Equip with data analytics techniques such as network analysis, unsupervised learning, natural language processing, text analytics etc.
Enhance Python programming skill and conduct exploratory data analysis for complex data projects.
Analyze, discuss, and summarize critical issues related to social media analytics.

Late Submission Policy

All assignments and projects are expected to be submitted on time. The LEARN platform will accept submissions during the period posted for each deliverable. Exceptions may be permitted only by arrangement with the instructor at least three days (72 hours) before a submission deadline, or in case of documented, extenuating circumstances (such as illness, bereavement, and so on). The penalty is 10% of your assignment grade for each day delayed.

Group Work Policy

All assignments are graded group work. Groups can consult with other groups, but each group must submit their own version of the working results.
