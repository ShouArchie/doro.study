Course Description

AFM 102:

This course is an introduction to the preparation and use of accounting information for management decision-making and reporting. Cost behaviour, cost accumulation systems, and short- and long-term decision models are discussed.

View requirements for AFM 102

In this course, we are going to be learning managerial accounting, which relates to performing analysis for internal decision making. We will be learning how to perform internal analysis including cost-volume-profit analysis, profitability analysis, performance analysis, and capital budgeting analysis.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of Unit 1, students should be able to:
Distinguish between various cost classifications and analyze a company’s cost structure to facilitate internal decision-making.
Apply cost-volume-profit (CVP) analysis to recommend the best course of action given different “what-if” scenarios.
By the end of Unit 2, students should be able to:
Utilize job-order costing to determine gross margin by job.
Reconcile the difference between variable and absorption costing and identify the advantages and disadvantages of each.
Apply activity-based costing to determine profitability by product and customer.
By the end of Unit 3, students should be able to:
Explain the budgeting process and prepare components of master and flexible budgets.
Prepare and interpret internal analysis used to evaluate company performance, such as variance analysis, segmented reporting, return on investment, residual income, and balance scorecards.
By the end of Unit 4, students should be able to:
Evaluate the acceptability of investment projects using different analytical tools such as net present value, internal rate of return, and payback period.

Administrative Policy

Course Policies

Late Submissions

The weekly assignments and unit tests must be uploaded by the date and time listed in the Course Schedule. These days and times are those of the Eastern Time Zone.

Academic Integrity

The weekly assignments and unit tests are both individual exercises. They must not be completed in pairs or groups.

Re-Grading

Tests will only be regraded if there is an error in the question. There will be no regrading to provide part marks for partially correct solutions.

Dropping Weekly Assignments and/or Unit Tests

The course is designed with flexibility to automatically drop the lowest weekly assignment AND lowest unit test without submitting documentation. Additional weekly assignments or unit tests will typically NOT be dropped. Please see the University Policy section for missed assignments/tests/quizzes due to illness.
