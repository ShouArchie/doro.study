Course Description

MTE 352:

Physical properties of fluids and fundamental concepts in fluid mechanics. Hydrostatics. Conservation laws for mass, momentum, and energy. Flow similarity and dimensional analysis as applied to engineering problems in fluid mechanics. Laminar and turbulent flow. Engineering applications such as flow measurement, flow in pipes, and fluid forces on moving bodies.

View requirements for MTE 352

This is an introductory course in fluid mechanics aimed at introducing concepts and strategies to solve engineering problems involving fluids. A fluid in the engineering sense is an interesting state of matter that can exhibit odd and counter intuitive behaviours that can surprise even experts in the  field. This course will introduce foundational principles, including forces in fluids, conservation laws applied to fluids, and qualitative tools, including flow visualization for improving intuition.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
classify fluid flow regimes and discuss flow phenomena using appropriate vocabulary
determine critical flow processes and estimate critical parameters of a fluid device
estimate pressure changes and flow rates in a fluid flow
estimate the forces on bodies surrounded by fluids

• classify fluid flow regimes and discuss flow phenomena using appropriate vocabulary (2a,2b) 

• determine critical flow processes and estimate critical parameters of a fluid device (1c,2b,2c) 

• estimate pressure changes and flow rates in a fluid flow (1a, 1b,1c) 

• estimate the forces on bodies surrounded by fluids (1a, 1b,1c, 2b, 2c)

Exams: We plan to have in-person midterm and final exams. They will be closed-book exams. In the eventuality that the exams must be completed online, different rules may apply.

Short quizzes: Four short, multiple choice and/or short answer quizzes will take place through-out the term. You will have 24 h window to complete the quizzes on Learn. No deferrals or makeup quizzes will be permitted.

The best 3 out of 4 quizzes will count towards the final grade. 

Homework: Problem sets will be posted to Learn approximately once a week. Complete solutions will be posted the week after the problems are assigned. Completing these assignment is essential for developing a good understanding of the course concepts. Thus, it is strongly recommended that you do the assigned problems before referring to the solutions.

Project: A project will be assigned after the midterm. The project will put into practice many of the theoretical concepts presented in class and during the tutorials. The project can be completed in a group of up to 4 students. No late submissions.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as mathematics, problem solving, developing engineering judgement. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
