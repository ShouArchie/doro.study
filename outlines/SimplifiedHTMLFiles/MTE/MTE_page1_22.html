Course Description

MTE 321:

Principles of the geometry and motion in linkages and mechanisms. Computer-aided kinematic and kinetic analysis of mechanisms. Synthesis of mechanisms. Static failure and yield criteria in ductile and brittle materials. Fatigue failure criteria due to fluctuating stresses. Shaft design under static and fluctuating loads. Shaft components, including shoulders, keys, and keyways. Deflections in shafts.

View requirements for MTE 321

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Identify different types of mechanisms and their degrees of freedom
Analyze and obtain displacements, velocities, accelerations, and forces at different points of a mechanism using analytical methods
Design and fabricate mechanisms with three precision points to do specific tasks
Design and analyze 1DoF mechanims
Write codes in MATLAB
Analyze the stress distribution in mechanical members under combined loading
Design ductile and brittle parts to prevent yield failure under static loading
Design mechanical parts to prevent fatigue failure under fluctuating loading
Simplify complicated mechanical design problems in order to predict failure under static and fluctuating loads
Identify, represent and communicate a mechanical/mechanism design problem
Work effectively as a member of a team
Apply project management skills and techniques

Exam Rules

  • The exams are closed book/note.
  • Some of the required equations, tables, plots, and figures will be provided along with the exam questions.
  • You are allowed to bring one letter size single-sided formula sheet as well.
    • You are allowed to write ONLY formulas on your formula sheet.
    • No figures, plots, and drawings are allowed.
    • Short labels or texts are fine.
    • You cannot write problem solutions or solutions procedures on your formula sheet.
    • Your formula sheets will be checked during the exam.
    • Your formula sheet can be handwritten or typed/printed.
    • It is highly recommended to make your own formula sheet.
  • If you have any problem with the exam questions, make reasonable assumptions and state your assumptions in your solution.
    • To ensure fairness to all, we refrain from answering questions during the exam. However, we are available to address inquiries related to the clarity of the questions.
  • You need a calculator, and you are not allowed to bring other electronic devices to the exam.
  • You may need drawing tools like ruler, triangle, protractor, and compass.
  • Please do not forget to write your name and student ID on the exam booklet. No-name exams won’t be marked.
  • If you missed the midterm exam for any reason, the weight of the midterm exam will be added to that of the final exam.
    • If you missed the exam due to sickness you need to provide Verification of Illness Form.
    • If you want to use the short-term absence, read the rules on UW website carefully. Links are given at the end of this outline.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
