Course Description

MTE 140:

This course emphasizes the following topics: structured software design data structures, abstract data types, recursive algorithms, algorithm analysis and design, sorting and searching, hashing, and problem-solving strategies.

View requirements for MTE 140

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. Performing rigorous complexity analyses of simple algorithms and data structures, (Attributes: 1 & 2)
2. Comparing different data-structuring techniques from the point of view of time and memory requirements, (Attributes: 1 & 2)
3. Determining a suitable data structure to solve a specific algorithmic problem, (Attributes: 1 & 2 & 3)
4. Implementing data structures correctly and efficiently in one or more high-level programming languages, such as C/C++ (Attributes: 3 & 5).

To successfully complete this course students are required to demonstrate their ability to: 

  1. Retrieve, recognize, and recall relevant knowledge,
  2. construct meaning from oral, written, and graphic communication through interpreting, exemplifying,
  3. classifying, summarizing, inferring, comparing, and explaining the subject matter covered in the course, 
  4. carry out or use a procedure or methodology that is part of the course material through using, a detailed pseudo-code for every problem.
  5. Carry out modelling and analysis and use it to reach valid conclusions, 
  6. Perform professionally and ethically.

Course Evaluation 

 

Comprehensive Checks:

Comprehensive Checks will be conducted on Learn multiple times throughout the term. It will be open-book, meaning that students can access their learning materials. However, collaboration is not allowed, meaning that students must complete it on their own without communicating with others. A time window will be set up for students to choose the best time to start the comprehensive check. Once started, each comprehensive check must be completed within the given time. This limited time requires students to be familiar with the learning materials and complete all questions quickly before the time runs out.

 

Participation Activities:

Weekly attendance activities starting from week 2. Every week the instructor will have a 5 min participation activity in the form of a game/quiz (Kahoot style) in class. This will be unannounced and could be during any lecture day and any time during the lecture (beginning, middle or end). It is every student’s responsibility to attend the class. Those will be marked for completion/participation not correctness. The split of the grade is to reward attending the activities. Missing those will be penalized as follows:

 

 

Valid reasons (medical note, short-absence, interviews, any other occasions will not be accepted) approved by the instructor can result in waiving any missed session. 

 

Any student registered with AccessAbility Services who has concerns about accommodations for these deliverables to talk to their Accommodation Consultant and the course instructor by week 2 of the term.

 

Sharing the code of the session with other classmates that are not present is an academic integrity breach and will be subject to the consequences that could extend to failing the course. In the case of a breach that is unknown the whole class will loose 1 point on their final mark for every breach up to a maximum of 9 points. 

 

Lab Assignments:

Assignments will be managed using GitLab at UWaterloo. You will have the chance to work on your assignments during the weekly “Lab” time slot (Tuesdays). However, collaboration is limited, meaning that students are encouraged to discuss the problems with the instructor, TA, and other students, but directly copying from others’ code is not permitted. Sharing own assignment work with other students is also not permitted. Students should use original variable names and comment their code thoroughly with explanation to demonstrate originality. Plagiarism detection tools will be used. Individual interview may be conducted, where a student is asked to explain the completed work to the instructor and TA.

Late penalty: work submitted after the due time may be penalized 0-100% of the points scored, depending on the cause of late submission. A student must save a copy of the submitted work on his or her personal computer or personal file system in case of any website problem. The student must not make any change to the saved copy of the submitted work. If the student is not able to provide a saved copy when requested by the instructor or if she/he makes any change to it after submission, that work may receive 0 credit.

 

Midterm and Final Exams:

Each student must complete the exam solely by her or his own efforts. Learning materials and notes prepared before an exam are allowed in an open-book exam. Access to calculators and programming environment is allowed.

Any missed mid-term exam will receive a mark of 0, unless there is a valid documented reason approved by the instructor. If such a reason is provided and accepted by the instructor, the mid-term weight may be applied to the final exam. If a student is unable to write the final exam, they need to notify the instructor at least 24 hours before the exam (as earlier as possible).

 

Grade Disputes:

All grade disputes must be returned with a written (email) explanation to the instructor within one week from the date when the score is released. The reasons must be clearly explained using a logical argument and supported using materials from the class (e.g., textbook and lecture notes) when necessary. Re-grade requests without written justifications will not be considered.
