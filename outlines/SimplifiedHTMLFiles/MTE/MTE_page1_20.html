Course Description

MTE 320:

Review of circuit analysis and basic electromagnetic theory. Power electronics: power electronics circuits, H bridges, PWM control, interfacing, power amplifiers. DC servo and stepper motors, AC synchronous and induction motors. Transformers. Introduction to typical speed and torque control techniques of motors.

View requirements for MTE 320

This course provides an in-depth study of the principles and applications of actuators and power electronics in modern engineering systems. It combines foundational knowledge in circuit analysis and electromagnetic theory with practical skills for analyzing and operating electromechanical systems. Topics include the fundamentals of AC circuits, magnetic circuits, transformers, DC machines, and power electronics, with a focus on their use in motor control and power conversion.

The course also includes a comprehensive laboratory component that reinforces theoretical concepts through hands-on experiments. Students will work in teams to enhance their understanding of actuators and power electronic systems.

By the end of the course, students will have developed the skills necessary to analyze electromechanical systems in diverse engineering contexts.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understanding AC circuit concepts, including phasor notation, power factor correction, and magnetic circuits with losses, to analyze electric systems.
Understand the operation, characteristics, and control techniques of single-phase transformers and DC machines, including efficiency, regulation, and speed control.
Analyze power electronic converters, including semiconductor devices and DC/DC converters, for various applications.
Implement speed control techniques for DC motors using power electronic converters to optimize performance.
Develop strong problem-solving and innovative thinking skills to address the evolving needs of actuators' operation and control
Acquire a solid foundation in the theoretical and practical aspects of electric drives and control, preparing for a successful career in electrical engineering.

LABORATORY WORK:

Detailed instructions for the laboratory sessions are included in lab-manual which can be downloaded from the course account on LEARN. Each student must choose a group and sign up for the lab schedule.

You will save much time if you thoroughly study the relevant sections of the lab- handout before attempting the experiments in the lab. Come to the lab. with a clear understanding of what you are meant to accomplish, or with clear questions, so that the Teaching Fellows can help you.

Lab marks and mark adjustments if you miss a lab session or a deadline:

  • There are 5 lab modules, which together constitute 20% of your final grade. 
  • All submissions are to be done electronically, through the LEARN system 
  • Prelabs and lab reports are due as specified in the Lab Calendar posted on LEARN.  Please verify that you have uploaded your submission on time, the document is complete, and it is under the group account of the correct course number.
  • Pre-labs will not be graded, but they are mandatory to attend the lab. Late pre-labs are not permitted. 
  • Late lab reports will incur a penalty of 20% in the first 24 hours, and 100% thereafter, unless prior arrangements are made or a valid reason presented within a week from the missed deadline.  In no case will a lab report be accepted more than a week past the deadline; if a valid reason exists for being unable to hand in the lab within the week following the deadline, then the lab will be assigned a weight of zero and the remaining labs will be reweighted accordingly.
  • Lab attendance is mandatory for each student. Missing a significant portion of a lab session without a valid reason will result in a reduction in the lab report grade (for that student only). The grade reduction will be proportional to the fraction of the lab session missed, where missing an entire lab session (without a valid reason) results in a 100% reduction in the lab report grade. In cases where a student misses part of a lab session, the lab instructor is the one who determines the mark reduction. 

 

Note:  A Student must attend all lab’s. If the student fails to attend any of the five labs, they will automatically be receiving an NCR (no credit generated) for his final grade.
