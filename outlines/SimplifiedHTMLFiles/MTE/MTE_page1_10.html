Course Description

MTE 182:

Kinematic of particles, rectilinear and curvilinear motion. Kinetics of particles. Energy and momentum methods. Systems of particles. Kinematics and kinetics of rigid bodies; planar motion.

View requirements for MTE 182

Dynamics is the study of motion, its patterns and causes. This is a first course in dynamics limited to planar motion of particles and rigid bodies. It covers:

• planar kinematics: the patterns of motion of particles and rigid bodies in a plane

• planar kinetics: unbalanced forces and their relationship with motion.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Relate the displacement, velocity, and acceleration of an object
Determine the displacement, velocity, and acceleration of an object from the forces acting to it
Plan a motion trajectory and determine the forces required to obtain it
Evaluate the kinetic and potential energy of an object
Evaluate the power and efficiency of a machine
Analyze events involving impacts and explosive forces

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assignments 15%
Project Report 10%
Midterm 25%
Final 50%

- There will be a total of 8 assignments. The lowest two will be dropped and the rest will be weighted equally.

- We will use WileyPlus via Learn for assignments. Problem sets will be assigned on Tuesdays as listed in the class calendar. They are due by the following Sunday at 11:59 PM.

- Please let the TAs know by 5:00PM Tuesday via the class channel on Teams, if you would like them to address a particular problem during Friday's tutorial. You are also welcome to ask questions on this channel outside office hours.

- The project will be carried out in groups of 4. Each group should elect a lead to report the group composition (members) on Crowdmark and act as their point of contact. The project will be assigned on Monday, March 3. The project report is due by Friday, April 4.

- Exams are closed book - open formula sheet. Problems are a mixture of “solve” and “explain”. Your answer sheet should reflect the thought process and analysis you went through to arrive at your answers not just the final result. I am interested in how you think about and analyze a problem more than whether you ‘got’ the right answer.

Class Policies:
