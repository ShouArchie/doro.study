Course Description

MTE 325:

Synchronization and data flow; interfacing to sensors and actuators;
parallel, serial, and analog interfacing; buses; direct memory access (DMA); interfacing considerations; privacy and security considerations. [Offered: W,S]

View requirements for MTE 325

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Summarize the flow of data between software and hardware elements in an embedded system
Explain simplified models of the hardware required to implement digital (parallel, serial) and analog interfaces
Describe the trade-offs between implementation alternatives for both hardware and software
Outline the ethical implications of embedded system design choices
Use industry-standard tools to implement and debug embedded systems
Design an embedded system to perform a given task

Tentative Course Schedule

In this course, lectures, tutorials, and projects are used to instruct you in the fundamentals of embedded microprocessor systems. Lectures introduce course material. Tutorials review select concepts. Projects provide students with an opportunity to explore selected concepts in a real-world context. Lectures and tutorials are held in person and attendance is expected at all sessions for learning and skill development.

Videos covering lecture content will be made available when possible as an alternative for those who are unable to attend a lecture due to illness or scheduling conflicts. They are not intended to replace lecture attendance and participation.

Tutorials are not recorded. When appropriate, notes may be posted after the session.

Course Deliverables 

The graded deliverables in this course are the projects, midterm and the final exam.

Projects                                                                                                                                            

Students, participating in teams of 3 they select from their lab day, must complete a project that requires students to implement and extend course concepts to a 2-axis machine. Students will need to make design decisions and use the resources that are typically available for a particular embedded system application. The purpose of this project is to provide practice with applications of course concepts and experience tackling an open-ended interfacing problem in preparation for their 3rd and 4th year design projects. To complete the project, students must submit the deliverables required by the project description and complete the required demonstrations. All group members are expected be present for demonstrations. Any extenuating circumstances should be discussed with the instructor. The full details of the project will be posted on LEARN in the Projects module.

The project will be completed in stages, spread over 6 lab sessions throughout the term. You are expected to attend all scheduled lab sessions. You are expected to complete roughly 3 hours of work outside the lab for every lab session. If you are unable to attend your session as scheduled for any reason, please contact Prof. Giannikouris as soon as possible to discuss.

Students are required to review safety information related to the lab. This information can be found in the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for the 2-axis machines, which is included in the project documentation which will be available on the course LEARN site. QR codes are also attached to each machine that link to SOPs. You are required to pass the safety quiz before lab 1. You will not be allowed to participate until it is completed.

Students are welcome to assist each other, but no code or submitted work (plots, screen shots, scope traces etc) should be shared. Helping another group debug by discussing possible root causes, sharing knowledge on using tools such as the debugger etc. is acceptable, showing them or providing copies of your group's code is not.

Late Submission Policy

Each student group is provided with 3 grace days total, useable in one day increments, for all submission-based deliverables throughout the term. Weekends, holidays and other breaks are counted as regular days for the purpose of calculating grace days used. If you submit deliverables with more than 3 late days total, late penalties will apply. The late penalty is 20% of the deliverable’s maximum value per late day. For example, submitting a deliverable due Friday at 8:00 pm on the following Monday anytime before 8:00 pm would use all 3 of the available grace days for the term. Grace days must be used by all group members to extend deadlines.

University of Waterloo Verification of Illness forms and self-declared absences will be taken into consideration only for the student to whom it applies, if appropriate. If an illness or absence has had a significant impact on a student's ability to contribute to a group deliverable, the instructor reserves the right to adjust deliverable weightings or provide an alternate deliverable to the affected student. The affected group and/or student should contact the instructor for guidance as soon as possible. The self-declared absence does not automatically extend the deadline for other group members.

If you are unable to attend a session or meet a deliverable deadline, please let Prof. Giannikouris know immediately. If you are facing challenges that affect more than one course, please contact Prof. Wright (Mechatronics Program Director) as soon as possible. He will review your case and coordinate a reasonable and fair plan in consultation with others (for example: instructors, department chair, AccessAbility Services, Engineering Counselling services, the Registrar’s Office etc.) as appropriate for your situation.

Example scenario: Student1 completes a 48-hour absence declaration in Quest that starts the day the synchronization report is due. The group can decide to submit their report by the deadline or delay it for one or more days by using some or all of their 3 grace days available for the term. Whether the weight of the deliverable is shifted to other project components or not for the absent student will be determined by the instructor after consultation with the absent student and the rest of the group to determine what contributions were made to the submitted deliverable.

Student groups are not provided with grace days for lab demonstrations. Groups failing to demonstrate by the end of their last scheduled lab session will be awarded a grade of 0 on the lab demonstration. Groups will be permitted to demonstrate without all members in cases where a self-declared absence/illness form has been submitted to Quest or a University of Waterloo Verification of Illness form has been submitted. If other extenuating circumstances arise, please speak to the instructor as soon as possible.

Exams                                                                                                                                                

Exams are the primary form of assessment used in this course. There will a midterm and a final exam. The midterm will be held during midterm week, and a final exam will be held during the final exam period.

Midterm Exam

The midterm exam is a 1.25 hour, closed-book exam written in an exam booklet. No additional materials are allowed. During the exam, no questions will be answered by the proctors to ensure fairness for all students. Further details on the midterm exam will be provided on Learn at least one week in advance of the exam.

Missing the Midterm Exam

Missing the midterm exam due to illness or a declared absence will result in the weight of the midterm exam being shifted to the final exam provided that an acceptable University of Waterloo Verification of Illness form is submitted within 48 hours or the appropriate declaration is completed in Quest. If the illness cannot be verified or if the explanation is not deemed acceptable, a grade of 0 will be assigned as the midterm grade.

Final Exam

The final exam is a cumulative 2.5 hour, closed-book exam written in an exam booklet. No additional materials are allowed. During the exam, no questions will be answered by the proctors to ensure fairness for all students. Further details on the final exam will be provided on Learn at least one week in advance of the exam.

Missing the Final Exam

Missing the final exam due to illness will result in an assigned grade of INC (Incomplete) provided that an acceptable University of Waterloo Verification of Illness form is presented to your academic adviser within 48 hours. The grade of INC (Incomplete) will be resolved by a deferred exam within 4 months of the end of the course. If no deferred exam is written within that timeframe, the grade will be automatically converted to FTC (Failure to Complete). If the illness cannot be verified or if the explanation is not deemed acceptable, a grade of DNW will be submitted for the student.

Every effort will be made to provide a makeup exam on the last day of exams for students that are verifiably ill during the scheduled final exam slot. This term, the last day of exams for students is August 17th. If you are ill and unable to write the final exam as scheduled, you are expected to be available for the makeup exam. The timing of this makeup exam will be determined once it is known which other exams on that date are to be written by the students who were ill.
