Course Description

MTE 351:

Introduction to systems modelling and analysis. Graph theoretic models and formulation of system equations. State space formulation and solution. Time and frequency domain solution. Application to engineering systems.

View requirements for MTE 351

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, learn to analyze, implement, and use various circuits techniques in a variety of applications. This includes:
Derive the mathematical equations describing dynamic engineering systems (mechanical, electrical, electromechanical, fluid, and/or thermal), (Attributes: 1c, 2a, 2b)
Generate state-space models of dynamic engineering systems using newton’s 2nd law and graph theory, (Attributes: 1c, 2a, 2b, 2c)
Generate block diagrams of dynamic engineering systems, (Attributes: 1c, 2c)
Perform time domain and frequency domain analysis of dynamic engineering systems (Attributes: 2a, 2b, 2c)
Communicate design of digital systems effectively in written documentation, (Attributes: 7a)
Use proper engineering tools/software to simulate engineering systems. (Attributes: 5c)

Lectures, regular laboratory work and periodic assignments are used to pursue these objectives.

To successfully complete this course students are required to demonstrate their ability to:

  1. Retrieve, recognize, and recall relevant knowledge,
  2. construct meaning from oral, written, and graphic communication through interpreting, exemplifying,
  3. classifying, summarizing, inferring, comparing, and explaining the subject matter covered in the course,
  4. carry out or use a procedure or methodology that is part of the course material through using a detailed method of attack for every problem.
  5. Carry out modelling and analysis and use it to reach valid conclusions,
  6. Perform professionally and ethically.

Participation Activities:

Weekly attendance activities starting from week 2. Every week the instructor will have a 5 min participation activity in the form of a game/quiz (Kahoot style) in class. This will be unannounced and could be during any lecture day and any time during the lecture (beginning, middle or end). It is every student’s responsibility to attend the class. Those will be marked for completion not for correctness. However, the top 3 students who get the most correct answers at the end of the course will get a reward. Valid reasons (medical note, short-absence, etc.) approved by the instructor can result in waiving any missed session. 

 

Assignments:

3 take-home assignments that need to be done individually (may change based on the instructor’s discretion). Each of these assignments may include analytical, numerical (using MATLAB/Simulink/Simscape) and explanatory components exploring fundamental aspects of the material covered in the course. The assignments are for us to assess your knowledge and provide you with constructive feedback. Plagiarism detection tools will be used. Individual interview may be conducted, where a student is asked to explain the completed work to the instructor and TA.

 

Project:

Students will work in teams to create, analyse and solve a project applying the course’s content to real-life systems applications. The project must be done using SimulationX. Teams should self-enroll in Learn->Connection->Groups by the due date (set later on during the term). If you face any problems for self enrolment, please send an email to cazzi@uwaterloo.ca (all team members cc’d; subject: SYDE 351 team composition). After that due date, undecided students will be grouped randomly into teams of 4. 

The project and its tasks are described in a separate document. The projects will help students to explore practical aspects of the fundamentals covered in lectures. Schedule of project deliverables are listed in Deliverables schedule.

 

Midterm and Final Exams:

Each student must complete the exam solely by her or his own efforts. Learning materials and notes prepared before an exam may be allowed and will be communicated via LEARN by the instructor. 

Any missed mid-term exam will receive a mark of 0, unless there is a valid documented reason approved by the instructor. Using short-absence on a midterm exam will shift its weight to other course components – no makeup will be offered. If a student is unable to write the final exam, they need to notify the instructor at least 24 hours before the exam (as earlier as possible)
