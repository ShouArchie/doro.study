Course Description

MTE 201:

Measurement errors, calculations with unknown quantities, and error propagation. Sensitivity analysis. Techniques of sampling and statistical estimation. Introduction to sensors and data acquisition tools. Frequency distributions. Probability. Binomial, Poisson, normal distributions. Tests of hypotheses. Significance. The t-test and chi-squared test. Curve fitting by least squares. Correlation and regression. Design of experiments.

View requirements for MTE 201

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the basic concepts of probability, random variables, probability distributions, sampling distribution and central limit theorem
Use statistical models and concepts including probability, hypothesis testing and confidence interval to solve engineering problems
Demonstrate working knowledge of experimental measurements, data collection and uncertainty analysis through open-ended discovery exercises and projects

Student Assessment

Component Value
Midterm 30%
Labs 5%
Project 15%
Final exam 50%

Exams: Exams are closed book with an equation sheet permitted. 

Labs: Lab exercise #1 will be assigned during the second week of the term to demonstrate data collection and analysis concepts from the course material. The lab will be completed on your own without supervision. Students will work in groups of two, collecting data and performing a statistical analysis for a physical quantity of their choosing. Measurement resources and assistance are available at WATiMake, the MME Clinic in DWE 3508/3509. The lab will include a written report completed as a group and a penalty of 10% per day will be deducted from the final grade for late submissions. Lab exercise #2 will be conducted during Weeks 12 and 13. The lab will be completed on your own without supervision. The recommended location for the lab is the 3rd to 2nd floor stairs in E5 or E7, but the measurements can be performed from any elevated location. Students must observe safety rules and regulations while working in labs and fabrication spaces, in accordance with departmental health and safety rules and regulations.  https://uwaterloo.ca/mechanical-mechatronics-engineering/undergraduate-students/health-and-safety-training 

Project: A group project will be assigned during Week 6 to design, build, calibrate and demonstrate a device to perform a simple measurement, such as distance, pressure, or temperature. WATiMake, the MME Clinic in DWE 3508/3509 is available during weeks 9 – 10 for students to work on their projects. The project will include a written report and short presentation / demonstration, both of which will be completed as a group.  The demonstrations will be held during Week 11, March 18 - 21 from 8:30 – 11:30am. A penalty of 10% per day will be deducted from the final grade for late submissions.

Problems: Problem sets and solutions are posted on the course website. Students are expected to work independently on these problem sets according to the course schedule and check their work using the solutions provided.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used for assignments in this class with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Recommendations for how to cite GenAI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
