Course Description

MTE 322:

Integrated design of mechanical motion transmission systems: gearing, couplings, bearings, power screws, fasteners, and their integration; sensing and measurement of mechanical motion; specification and selection of motors and electromechanical actuators; analysis and design of controllers for motion transmission systems; case studies.

View requirements for MTE 322

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Getting familiar with specifications of different machine elements and their usages.
Exercising formal design process of electromechanical machines through practical design examples.
To be able to use analytical methods in designing basic components of electromechanical machines.
Understanding different types of electric drives and their usages in operating machines.

Design Projects: 

There will be 2 projects with 2 reports

1. Project 1 (individual project): load analysis and understanding solid models/drawings of machines

2. Project 2 (in groups of two): a comprehensive design project

Guidelines on project report will be provided in as much detail as possible. However, design is an open problem (i.e. no unique solution). Therefore, other factors will affect your mark such as formality (professionalism) / efficiency (e.g. achieving the same goal with simpler design) / organization / cleanliness /aesthetics / writing quality of your designs & reports (, which will be solely at the discretion of your instructor and TA’s). You will need to use Solidworks for projects. There is a student license available from University. Please check the announcement on course website for instruction to use Solidworks. To help those who are not familiar with Solidworks, two tutorial videos will be posted. Two Solidworks live sessions will also occur, with pointers on best practices for Project 1 and Project 2 respectively.

1. Solidworks 1 (Assembly): week 3 (tentative)

2. Solidworks 2 (Drawing): week 9 (tentative)
