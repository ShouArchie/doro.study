Course Description

ENVE 280:

An introduction to fluid mechanics. Fluid properties. Review of fluid statics. Buoyancy. Bernoulli equation. The momentum equation and applications. Laminar and turbulent flow. Dimensionless numbers. Closed conduit flow including friction losses. Pipe network analysis including energy losses and efficiencies. Four lab sessions.

View requirements for ENVE 280

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. Characterize fluids and fluid systems based on their properties and system variables
2. Analyze and compute static fluid pressures, forces, and moments
3. Evaluate mathematical models of basic dynamic fluid systems using principles of mass conservation, energy conservation, and momentum conservation
4. Design basic pipe and pumped systems for viscous liquids
5. Communicate laboratory findings through clear and concise reporting

Midterm Exam

The course includes a 1.5-hour midterm exam scheduled for Week 6 (February 11). The exam will take place in the classroom, RCH 103. It will follow a closed-book format, with an equation sheet provided as part of the exam booklet.

Final Exam

The course will conclude with a 2.5 hour final exam during the formal final exam period. The date and

time of the exam will be scheduled by the Registrar’s Office (RO). The final exam will be a closed book format with an equation sheet provided with the exam booklet.

The final exam will be a higher level of assessment than the quizzes. Room and seat assignments will be administered though Odyssey.

 

Quizzes

This course has two quizzes (closed book format). The quizzes will be 60 minutes in length and will be conducted during the Wednesday 8:30am - 9:30am tutorial time slot (RCH 305). The material assessed by the quizzes will come from the lecture notes, practice problems and tutorials. 

 

Labs

The course will provide opportunities to conduct live experiments, measure and later analyze collected data. The labs will be conducted/supervised by a lab technician and a course TA/CA. For each lab,

technical briefs will be submitted. The lab submissions will be graded by the course TA/CA. Labs will be conducted in groups of four. Lab submissions will be through LEARN drop boxes. The lab component of

the course represents 20% of the course. The labs will be conducted in E3-2103. The labs are scheduled for the following weeks:

  1. Lab attendance and participation are mandatory to obtain credit.
  2. Group members are expected to collaborate as they complete the lab requirements and submission.
  3. Each group member will take the role of group leader for one of the three labs.
  4. The lab material and all associated calculations will be assessed on the final exam.
  5. Students missing a lab (due to a VIF) will have the weight of the lab shifted to their final exam.
  6. Students absent from a lab will be awarded a grade of zero.
  7. Students arriving late to a lab will receive a -25% penalty.

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
