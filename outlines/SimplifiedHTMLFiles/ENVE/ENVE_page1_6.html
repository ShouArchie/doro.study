Course Description

ENVE 275:

Overview of risk, biosphere compartments and contaminant fate. Composition of water. Electroneutrality and activity. Reactions and speciation including reaction kinetics, mass transfer, vapor pressure, equilibrium, and chemical thermodynamics. Equilibrium chemistry including Log-concentration diagrams, titration and buffering intensity, dissolution/precipitation, carbonate system, hardness, and complex formation. Classification, nomenclature, physical/chemical parameters and partitioning of organic compounds. Basic redox chemistry including: half cell reactions, Faraday and Nernst equations, and pE-pH diagrams. Four labs.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Appreciate the role of chemistry in environmental engineering.
Be able to express concentration using a variety of methods
Develop the capabilities to solve problems by combining several chemistry concepts.
Appreciate simple methods to determine dissolved ionic species in solution.
Understand chemical kinetics, reaction rates, and the relationship to equilibrium.
Develop conceptual knowledge of equilibrium, equilibrium constant, and their applications to systems at equilibrium.
Use chemical thermodynamics to estimate equilibrium.
Calculate acid-base equilibria, buffering capacity and the role they play in environmental systems.
Understand metal speciation and its relationship to pH.
Estimate hardness and alkalinity.
Develop skills for balancing oxidation-reduction reactions, and understand pE-pH diagrams.
Calculate the partitioning of organic compounds based on physical/chemical properties.
Write a simple laboratory report including data and analysis.
Work as part of a team.

Tentative Course Schedule

Week 1-2: Introduction

Application of chemistry in environmental engineering; natural water and its composition; methods of expressing concentration: concentration units, electroneutrality; ionic strength. 

Week 3-4: Chemical reaction in aqueous solution

Activity and activity coefficient; Equilibrium, Thermodynamics, Temperature Dependence 

Week 5-6: Equilibrium Chemistry

PBE/ENE/MBE, Acids/Bases, Log-concentration diagrams, Titration and Buffering Intensity; Alkalinity 

Week 7-8: Equilibrium Chemistry (cont.)

Metal Speciation, Dissolution/Precipitation, Carbonate System, Hardness. 

Week 9-11: Reduction and oxidation (RedOx) reactions

Half Cell reactions, Faraday and Nernst Equations, pE-pH diagrams

Week 11-12: Organic contaminants

Classification/nomenclature, physical/chemical properties, partitioning, transformation reactions
