Course Description

ENVE 224:

Role of probability in engineering and decision-making under uncertainty. Basic probability concepts. Probability distributions. Functions of random variables. Data analysis. Confidence intervals and hypothesis testing. Introduction to regression analysis. Introduction to design of experiments and statistical quality control.

View requirements for ENVE 224

Engineering designs and decisions are often made “under uncertainty”; i.e., engineers do not have the time and/or money to obtain all the data of interest regarding the project. Probability concepts and statistics are used to quantify this “uncertainty” and hopefully reduce the “risk” in our design and decision-making. This course aims to introduce students to the basic concepts of probability and statistics.  The specific topics covered by this course include:

  • General Treatment of Data
  • Probability
  • Discrete Probability Distributions
  • Continuous Probability Distributions
  • Mathematical Expectation
  • Statistical Estimation
  • Hypothesis Testing
  • Regression Analysis

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to understand and use the following concepts:
Probability
Discrete probability distributions
Continuous probability distributions
Statistical estimation
Hypothesis testing
Regression analysis

The overall course mark is based on 4 (four) equally weighted tests. There is no final exam for this course. Each test will be 75 min and held during regular class on the above dates.

Problem Sets:

There will be 6-7 problem sets throughout the term. These problem sets are ungraded and meant for you to test and apply the concepts learned in the lectures. The problem sets may be reviewed during the tutorial. 

Please work out the problems before looking at the solutions, as the answers are obvious and will not aid with learning the course concepts.
