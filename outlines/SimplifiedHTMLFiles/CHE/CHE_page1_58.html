Course Description

CHE 562:

Application of process engineering principles to the design and operation of fermentation reactors which are widely used in the pharmaceutical, food, brewing, and waste treatment industries. Aspects of mass transfer, heat transfer, mixing, and rheology with biochemical and biological constraints.

View requirements for CHE 562

•This course covers advanced bioprocess (in particular, bioreaction) concepts, theories, and applications.

•Bioreaction topics: enzyme kinetics; growth kinetics; homogeneous (i.e., enzymatic reaction and cell cultivation) vs. heterogeneous (immobilized) biocatalysts; bioreaction vs. mass transfer in immobilized biocatalysts; bioreactor modeling and design (BR, CSTR, PFR)

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand how immobilized biocatalysts are designed and made
Model bioreactor systems with bioreactions catalyzed by both non-porous or porous immibilized enzymes
Model bioreactor systems with bioreactions catalyzed by both active and passive immibilized cells

Tentative Course Schedule

•Week 1: Bioprocess and biological background review: Immobilized biocatalyst introduction

•Week 2: Non-porous immobilized enzyme (IE) system introduction

•Week 3: Non-porous IE system modeling

•Week 4: Porous IE system introduction

•Week 5: Porous IE system modeling

•Week 6: Porous IE system and bioreactor modeling

•Week 7: Porous IE system/bioreactor modeling and immobilized cell (IC) system introduction

•Week 8: Active IC system modeling

•Week 9: Passive IC/slab system modeling

•Week 10: Passive IC/slab system and bioreactor modeling

•Week 11: Passive IC/spheric system modeling

•Week 12: Passive IC/spheric system and bioreactor modeling
