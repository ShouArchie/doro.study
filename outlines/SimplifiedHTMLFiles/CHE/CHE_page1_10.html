Course Description

CHE 181:

Development of chemical engineering analysis, investigation, design, and communication skills. Introduction to engineering measurement. Instrument performance characteristics. Electric circuits, current, voltage and resistance, measurements, and signal transmission. Electrical safety. Measurement uncertainty, types of errors, calibration, instrument uncertainty analysis, and error propagation. Measurement of force and load, chemical composition, temperature, pressure and flow. Sensor interfacing and data acquisition using software. Process safety management and inherently safer design. Prototyping, testing, and verification. Communication of technical information, written reports, and oral presentations. Teamwork. Students undertake a term-long design project under the supervision of the instructor.

LECTURES:

RCH-110

The lecture portion of this course is officially set for 2 hours per week, which is slightly less than a ‘normal’ lecture course. This is to accommodate the fact that a large portion of the learning occurs in the hands-on studio component. Nonetheless, there is still some technical material to be covered, so lectures are needed. The course consists of 9 chapters, which are covered in a set of PDF notes to be distributed electronically by the professor.

 

SEMINARS:

CPH-1346

The seminar time slot is dedicated to the AutoCAD material.

The seminar portion of this course (officially set to 1 hour per week). We will be dedicating this hour to a suite of technical drawing tutorials. Approximately 10 hours of tutorial videos will be assigned to guide you through the steps of learning AutoCAD and Inventor and/or SolidWorks, 2D and 3D drawing programs widely used in industry. Details of gaining access to this software will be provided.

STUDIO:

Ideas Clinic-E7-2409 / Your kitchen.

Studio

The official course calendar list 2 hours per week of studio time. These sessions are largely self-guided, with the professor and technical instructor available offer help. The entire activity will still be very self-guided which is actually the intention anyway.

The course kit:

• An Arduino Uno

• A USB2.0 A to B plug 3’ cable (to connect a computer to the Arduino uno)

• A breadboard

• A component holder (capable to pinch two wires)

• A 1 kilo ohm resistor

• Eight male-to-male 6” jumper wires

• Two male-to-female 6” jumper wires

• A platinum resistance temperature detector (RTD)

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Author and format professional quality documents. [7b]
Produce and interpret technical drawings and figures. [5c]
Identify components of and select appropriate measurement systems and sensors. [1c, 5a]
Define performance characteristics and related terminology. [1c]
Calculate uncertainty arising from instrument error. [1a]
Design electronic circuits to measure response of electronic sensors. [4a, 4b, 4c & 5c]
Describe the operating principles behind common sensors. [1c]
Calculate sensor response for common systems. [1c]
Troubleshoot and diagnose technical problems. [2a. 2b, 2c]
Approach open-ended problems where the quality of the final product depends only on your creativity, vision, work ethic, motivation and time available. [12a]
Create and accomplish things that are beyond your current skillset. [12b]

The numbers in brackets refer to program indicators defined by the Faculty of Engineering to assess the twelve CEAB defined graduate attributes ( https://uwaterloo.ca/engineering/graduate-attributes).
