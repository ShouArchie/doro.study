Course Description

CHE 101:

Development of chemical process analysis skills. Introduction to single- and multi-phase physical equilibria. Material and energy balances in reactive and non-reactive systems. Introduction to investigation and technical communication through laboratory experiments illustrating the physical principles discussed. In the winter term only: brief review of co-op fundamentals.

View requirements for CHE 101

The laboratory course for Chemical Engineering Concepts 2 uses introductory experimental methods and instrumentation for the measurement of various process quantities relevant to chemical engineering. The primary course objective are for students to reinforce their knowledge base of chemical engineering theories and concepts in a hands-on laboratory experience.  Students will draw specifically on information from Chemical Engineering Concepts courses (ChE 100 and ChE 101). Upon completion of the course students will have developed skills in experimentation, data analysis, result interpretation, and engineering report writing. 

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. Perform experiments related to the following topics: (i) phase equilibria, (ii) heat transfer, (iii) reaction kinetics, (iv) energy balance correctly and safely. (1b, 1c, 7c, 3a, 3b, 5c)
2. Analyze experimental lab results in the following topics: (i) phase equilibria, (ii) heat transfer, (iii) reaction kinetics, (iv) energy balance. (1b, 1c, 3c, 5c)
3. Apply uncertainty and propagation of uncertainty techniques to experimental data (1c, 5a)
4. Contribute in a team to laboratory tasks. (6a, 6b)
5. Communicate complex engineering concepts in technical lab reports. (7b)
6. Identify risks and hazards associated with laboratory protocols and take appropriate safety measures. (5c)

The numbers in brackets refer to program indicators defined by the Faculty of Engineering to assess the twelve CEAB defined graduate attributes. More information is provided on the department website: https://uwaterloo.ca/chemical-engineering/undergraduate-students/current-undergraduate-students/academic-information.

Teamwork Module

Students are responsible for completing the online teamwork module posted on LEARN, submitting the pre-class activity (individual), and participating in the in-class activity with your lab group.

Students are responsible for attending the laboratory period and reading announcements made on UW-Learn. Students must act professionally in the laboratory period, be aware of the safety for each experiment and hazards in the laboratory, and wear proper protective equipment in the laboratory.  Lab Performance is assessed individually and details for assessment are in the lab manual posted on UW-Learn.

The lab quiz for the lab you are performing must be completed by 11:59 PM the day of your scheduled lab.  Review the lab manual, virtual lab (Lab 3 and Lab 4), lab procedure, chemical safety data sheets (SDS) for the lab, and general lab safety information.  See the links on UW-Learn. Other Course Notes

Late Work 

Extensions are available for lab reports upon request to the course instructor (cheryl.newton@uwaterloo.ca) before the report due date. Available no penalty extensions are two business days. Late penalty is 10 % a week (2 % each business day). Weekends, reading days, midterm week, and holidays do not accumulate late penalties. For example, if you miss the an extended deadline of Friday and hand in before Monday at 11:59 PM it is a 2 % deduction. All late penalties are at the discretion of the course instructor. Dropboxes do not close past the due date and close the last scheduled day of classes.  Late work is not accepted to after the last day of classes without instructor permission.

Regrading

A request for lab report regrading must be received by the TA or instructor no more than 3 days after the grade was issued.  Please speak to the TA first regarding grade clarification. All regrading requests are at the discretion of the course instructor.

A note about group work

Unacceptable collaboration includes not completing the data analysis for your individual report and any word for word or figure/graph duplication of material from another report, lab manual, or any other reference. Word for word duplication in your lab report is flagged by Turnitin® and investigated by the course instructor. Use your own data for your analysis and always complete your own calculations for the individual report.  It is expected that group reports are worked on collaboratively and as a result all group members are responsible for the material in the report.  For example, if one group member commits an academic misconduct offence in part of the report they prepared all the group members are also responsible for the academic offence. 

Individual lab quizzes are completed by accessing course content available on UW-Learn and information from the safety orientation.  Collaboration on the lab quizzes is acceptable with classmates, TAs and the course instructor.

Examples of student academic misconduct include:

  • Obtaining, distributing, or receiving unauthorized academic material without the express consent of the course instructor.  Unauthorized materials include lab manuals, quizzes and other course material available in-class and on UW-Learn.
  • Sharing unauthorized course-related materials via hard-copy, email, social media or UW-Learn.
  • Using laboratory data, calculation workbooks, lab reports from other groups or students.
  • Using images or schematics from another source without proper referencing.
  • Not preventing your work from being used by others (facilitating plagiarism)

Attendance Policy

Lab attendance in the lab session is required of all students in each group. If there is a reason you cannot make it to the lab session (for example illness, co-op interview, self-declared absence), you must contact the course instructor prior to the lab period so that an alternative arrangement may be made.  Any missed labs due to Quest self-declaration will need to be made up in the next lab session if available or at an agreed upon time with the course instructor.  Lab attendance is a prerequisite to lab report. If you do not attend the laboratory session you will receive zero on the corresponding lab report.

Generative AI

  • The laboratory course permits the use of generative AI (GenAI) such as ChatGPT, MS CoPilot, GitHub Copilot, etc. as a tool to enhance laboratory learning, particularly in report writing, Python programming.
  • Lab submissions, whether aided with GenAI or not, will be strictly assessed based on the relevance of the contents, required deliverables, and lab report format stipulated in the lab manual and marking rubric.
  • The use of GenAI needs to be acknowledged in terms of where and how the GenAI is used as a learning tool in the lab submissions.

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
