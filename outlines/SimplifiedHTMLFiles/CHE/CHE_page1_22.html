Course Description

CHE 313:

Convective heat transfer. Analysis of convective heat transfer in external flows using the boundary layer approach. Analysis of convective heat transfer in internal flows. Empirical correlations for convective heat transfer. Heat transfer with phase change: condensation and boiling. Heat exchanger design. Convective mass transfer. Empirical correlations for convective mass transfer. Mass transfer at fluid-fluid interfaces. Design of continuous differential contactors for absorption/stripping, distillation and liquid-liquid extraction. Analogy between heat, mass and momentum transfer. Dimensional analysis. Simultaneous heat and mass transfer operations.

View requirements for CHE 313

1.         Conductive, Convective, and Radiative Modes of Heat Transfer  

2.         Review of Conductive Heat Transfer

  • Fourier’s Law of Heat Conduction
  • Heat Diffusion Equation
  • Conductive Thermal Resistance

3.         Introduction to Convective Heat Transfer

  • Rate Equation for Convective Heat Transfer
  • Convective Thermal Resistance

  • Fick’s Law of Diffusion

  • Rate Equation for Convective Mass Transfer
  • Convective Mass Transfer Resistance

8.         Analysis of Convective Mass Transfer

  • Basic Concepts
  • Analysis of Convective Mass Transfer in External Flows; Equations for Mass Transfer Coefficient
  • Analysis of Convective Mass Transfer in Internal Flows; Equations for Mass Transfer Coefficient

Learning Outcomes

No explicit learning outcomes defined for this course.

After successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Understand and apply the principles of conductive, convective, and radiative heat transfers [1c,1d]
  • Develop insulation systems to minimize heat losses [2a,2b]
  • Analyze and solve convective heat transfer problems dealing with internal and external flows [2a,2b]
  • Select and apply appropriate convective heat transfer coefficient correlations to internal and external flows [2b,2c]
  • Understand and apply the principles of mass transfer in a stationary medium and in a moving fluid [1c,1d]
  • Analyze and solve convective mass transfer problems dealing with internal and external flows [2a,2b]
  • Select and apply appropriate convective mass transfer coefficient correlations to internal and external flows [2b,2c]
  • Analyze and solve problems dealing with interphase mass transfer [2a,2b]
  • Design double pipe heat exchangers [2a,2b,4a]
  • Design shell-and-tube heat exchangers [2a,2b,4a]
  • Design packed bed columns for mass transfer – estimate height and diameter of columns [2a,2b,4a]
  • Estimate heat transfer in fluids undergoing phase change – Boiling and Condensation [2a,2b]
  • Understand and apply the concepts of radiative heat transfer to relevant problems [1c,1d]
  • Appreciate analogy between heat, mass, and momentum transfer [1c,1d]

The numbers in brackets refer to program indicators defined by the Faculty of Engineering to assess the twelve CEAB defined graduate attributes (https://uwaterloo/engineering/graduate-attributes)
