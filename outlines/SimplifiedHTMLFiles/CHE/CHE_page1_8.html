Course Description

CHE 161:

Introduction to basic concepts of biochemistry and cell biology. Overview of the chemistry of amino acids, carbohydrates, lipids, and nucleic acids. Properties and functions of biopolymers. Elements of cell structure and diversity, and relationship of biochemistry with cell metabolism. A focus on biotechnologically relevant examples such as biomimetic engineering design, proteomics, system biology, and high throughput biology.

View requirements for CHE 161

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1) Demonstrate a basic understanding of biochemistry, cell biology and molecular biology (1b)
2) Converse at a fundamental level with scientist and engineers that use biology and biotechnology (1c)
3) Demonstrate an understanding of bioengineering approaches, including enzyme engineering, metabolic engineering, and genetic engineering. (1b, 1d)
4) Apply biochemistry, cell biology, molecular engineering and bioengineering to solve problems. (2a, 2b, 2c)
5) Identify societal, safety and ethical concerns surrounding biotechnology. (9a, 10b)

The numbers in brackets refer to program indicators defined by the Faculty of Engineering to assess the twelve CEAB defined graduate attributes ( https://uwaterloo.ca/engineering/graduate-attributes).
