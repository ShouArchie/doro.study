Course Description

CHE 200:

Equilibrium between phases; the equilibrium stage concept. Cascades of stages with and without reflux; group methods and stage-by-stage approaches; graphical solutions. Applications in the separation of components by distillation, absorption, stripping, extraction, and leaching.

View requirements for CHE 200

 

    1.    Introduction to Equilibrium Stage Operations (Chapter 1)

  • Importance or Relevance of Equilibrium Stage Operations
  • Concept of an Equilibrium Stage and Different Types of Equilibrium Stages
  • Cascading of Equilibrium Stages - Multistage contacting of two phases
  • Industrial Applications of Equilibrium Staged Separation Processes
  • Phase Equilibrium, Raoult’s Law, Henry’s Law

    2.  Gas Absorption and Liquid Stripping (Chapters 2 to 5)

  • Single-Stage Gas Absorption (Chapter 2)
  • Single-Stage Liquid Stripping (Chapter 3)
  • Multi-Stage Gas Absorption (Chapter 4)
  • Multi-Stage Liquid Stripping (Chapter 5)

     3.  Distillation- Definitions and Basic Concepts (Chapter 6)

  • Vapor Pressure
  • Raoult’s Law
  • Volatility and Relative Volatility
  • Bubble Point and Dew Point
  • Construction of P-x-y, T-x-y, and y-x Diagrams

     4.   Mass and Energy Balances for Open (Flow) Systems (Chapter 7)

     5.   Single-Stage Flash Distillation (Chapter 8)

     6.   Multi-Stage Distillation (Chapter 9) 

  • Cascading of Stages
  • Multi-Stage Distillation Column
  • Analysis and Design of Multi-stage Distillation Columns

    7.   Approximate Analytical Method of Designing Distillation Columns (Chapter 10)

  • FUG method of estimating the number of equilibrium stages
  • Tray efficiency, estimation of actual number of stages, height of column, diameter of column

    8.   Batch Distillation-Without and With Reflux (Chapter 12)

    9.   Multicomponent Distillation (Chapter 13)

    10.   Liquid-Liquid Extraction (Chapter 14)

  • Single-stage Extraction
  • Multi-Stage Extraction

    11.   Solid-Liquid Leaching (Chapter 15)

  • Single-Stage Leaching
  • Multi-Stage Leaching

 

Learning Outcomes

No explicit learning outcomes defined for this course.

After successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • explain the concepts of equilibrium and equilibrium stage [1b,1d]
  • carry out steady state mass and energy balances around an equilibrium stage using different composition variables, such as mole fractions and mole ratios [1b,1d]
  • explain the concept of cascading of equilibrium stages [1d]
  • analyze multistage countercurrent cascades for absorption and stripping-graphically and analytically (Kremser equations); determine the number of equilibrium stages needed for a given separation [2a,2b]
  • prepare isobaric equilibrium diagrams such as T-x-y and y-x diagrams [1b]
  • perform bubble point and dew point calculations [1b]
  • analyze flash distillation graphically and analytically [2a,2b]
  • apply McCabe-Thiele method to analyze multi-stage distillation columns; determine the number of equilibrium stages needed for a given separation; estimate the height and diameter of distillation columns [2a,2b]
  • use approximate method of designing multi-component distillation columns [2a,2b]
  • estimate overall and tray efficiencies of a distillation column [2a,2b]
  • analyze batch distillation graphically and analytically (Rayleigh equation) without and with reflux [2a,2b]
  • analyze multistage liquid-liquid extraction using triangular diagrams; determine the number of equilibrium stages needed for a given separation [2a,2b]
  • analyze and design single-stage and multi-stage solid-liquid leaching processes [2a,2b]

The numbers in brackets refer to program indicators defined by the Faculty of Engineering to assess the twelve CEAB defined graduate attributes (https://uwaterloo.ca/engineering/graduate-attributes)
