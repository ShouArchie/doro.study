Course Description

CHE 102:

Chemical principles with applications in engineering. Stoichiometric calculations, properties of gases, properties of liquids and solutions, gas phase chemical equilibrium, ionic equilibrium in aqueous solution, oxidation-reduction reactions, chemical kinetics.

View requirements for CHE 102

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain the behavior of ideal systems (including different states of matter, physical and chemical equilibrium and reaction kinetics) in terms of the molecular nature of matter. [1b]
Write balanced chemical reactions and perform stoichiometric calculations. [1b]
Apply the Ideal Gas Law while appreciating the limitations of this law. [1b, 2b]
Identify which principles of physical chemistry are relevant to the analysis of problems involving physical and chemical equilibrium and rates of reaction. [1b]
Select and apply an appropriate law to solve problems involving physical and chemical equilibrium and rates of reaction. [1b, 2b]
Apply course concepts to identify and analyze concerns regarding the safety and/or sustainability of a chemical process. [9a, 9b]

The numbers in brackets refer to program indicators defined by the Faculty of Engineering to assess the twelve CEAB defined graduate attributes ( https://uwaterloo.ca/engineering/graduate-attributes).

Student Assessment

Component Value
Mobius Lesson Participation 5%
Assignments 20%

Final Exam 50%

Note: You must pass the final examination to pass the course.

Mobius Lesson Participation

The lessons in this course contains interactive content including questions. Your answers to these questions will contribute to your lesson participation grade. Lesson participation grades for each lesson are evenly weighted in the total lesson participation grade. Lesson completion due dates are indicated on the course schedule above. You can view your lesson participation grades at any point in the Mobius gradebook and after the due date for each unit in the LEARN gradebook.

Mobius Assignments

Assignments are designed to reinforce course content. These assignments will be administered approximately biweekly using Mobius and can be accessed through LEARN.  The assignments are evenly weighted in the total assignment grade. You can view your assignment grades in the LEARN gradebook and Mobius gradebook. Assignment due dates are indicated on the course schedule above.

Correct and Continue Assignments

After completing Unit 1 - 5, and the associated assignment, you will be asked to dig a little deeper. You will receive a single, multi-part assignment question that has been started for you. However, there will be some errors in the provided solution. Your job is to identify the issues with the partial solution, correct them, and then finish the rest of the question. These assignments will be submitted through Crowdmark.

Late Assignments

No late submissions will be accepted. If you are unable to complete work due to illness or other extenuating circumstances the weight will be shifted to the remaining grade items in that category.

 

Assignment Screening

No assignment screening will be used in this course.
