Course Description

CHE 230:

Thermodynamics: work and heat as forms of energy. First law, internal energy and enthalpy. Heats of chemical and physical changes. Cycles and the second law, entropy. Spontaneity and equilibrium, free energies. Systems of variable composition, chemical equilibrium. Phase equilibrium and the phase rule. Ideal solutions, colligative properties.

View requirements for CHE 230

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the first and second laws of thermodynamics and apply them to solve problems involving state changes of systems undergoing different processes. [1b, 2b. 2c]
Understand the concepts of internal energy, enthalpy, entropy and free energy, and know how to determine system state property changes for given processes. [1b, 2b, 2c]
Know the important features of heat effects, and know how to determine reaction heat and other thermodynamic property changes of reactions. [1b, 2b, 2c]
Understand the concepts of phase equilibrium and phase rule, and know how to apply them to material equilibrium. [1b, 2b]
Understand reaction equilibrium, and determine the composition of reaction mixtures at reaction equilibrium. [1b, 2b, 2c]
Understand the concepts of ideal and ideally dilute solutions, and apply them to determine colligative properties. [1b, 2b, 2c]

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assignments 20%
Midterm Exam 30%
Final Exam 50%

There will be two to three assignments designed to complement your understanding of the course materials. You may work with peers to help you understand the assignments but the final work must be your own and submitted individually. Identical or paraphrased final works or final works submitted after the deadline will receive a grade of zero. 

The final exam will be comprehensive (i.e. covering the entire course material).

Note: you must pass the combined examinations to pass the course. If your midterm and final grade are less than 40 out 80, you will receive this grade as final grade for the course.
