Course Description

CHE 290:

A selection of computer and laboratory exercises refreshing and reinforcing material covered in the previous term. Topics may include basic microbiology and biotechnology, introductory physical chemistry, mass and energy balances.

View requirements for CHE 290

The experiments in this laboratory course are designed to reinforce theoretical concepts introduced in your first year of study (e.g. biology, energy balances, electrochemistry, thermodynamics) while providing an introduction to chemical process modeling using ASPEN Plus.  This course provides opportunities to study some lab techniques/equipment (e.g. aseptic technique, bioassays, potentiostats, calorimeters, etc.) along with further practice in report writing and teamwork. 

ChE290 consists of 6 “labs” (4 experiment-related/2 ASPEN-based software modeling tutorials), each requiring some prelab, in-lab component, and final report submission.  Attendance in a lab is mandatory to qualify to submit the corresponding report.

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Apply investigative strategies (i.e. scientific method) to derive conclusions from measured data specifically in topics of control of microbial growth, protein separation, bioassays, electrochemistry/reactions (2b,2c,3b,3c)
Understand the theoretical basis of the operation of a variety of lab and analytical equipment including bioreactor, gel electrophoresis, potentiostats and/or calorimeters (5a,5b,5c)
Apply RAMP principles (Recognize, Assess, Minimize, Prepare) for working safely with hand tools, solids/liquid/gaseous chemicals and Biosafety Level 1 microorganisms (5a, 5b, 5c).
Model a real-world industrial process using ASPEN PLUS simulation software (5a, 5b, 5c)
Develop behaviours to work successful in a team and effectively communicate findings in technical reports (3a,3b,6a,6b,7b,7c,12a,12b)
Identify sources of error and apply error propagation for chemical engineering experimental and process situations (2c)

LAB REPORTS:  three different formats of reports required in this lab course: Data Analysis and Memo styles and a ASPEN Tutorial report.  Guidelines for expected content in DA and Memo type of reports can be found in the lab manual.  A breakdown of the report marking schemes are posted on LEARN Content General Materials.  Guidance is provided in the lab manual.  Tutorial reports address questions posed in tutorial assignments.  

The goals of reports are:

  • to assess the fit of the data to applicable theory,
  • to evaluate the quality of the data
  • to provide possible explanations of errors based on a critical review of the procedures
  • to report and interpret significant trends observed in the data.

Content of INDIVIDUAL REPORTS must be your own unique work.  Some data analysis may be shared with your lab groupmates but analysis and interpretation of results must be your own work (i.e. not paraphrased from classmate or copied from online resources).

MEMO REPORTS for Lab#1 Intro to Biolab (ind) and Lab#3 Bomb Calorimetry (group) (worth 20 and 16%, resp.):

A MEMO report is a full account of your observations and interpretations of experimental data in a proper technical report format that is both clear and concise.  It includes an abstract to summarize the report content and key findings of the undertaken study.  Memo reports also include an introduction to entice the reader, objectives to explain the purpose of the study, background theory to justify the data analysis presented and of course, the actual analysis of your empirical results with associated errors that tie into the concluding remarks and recommendations for how to improve/extend the study in future work.  The marking scheme for full memo reports is posted on LEARN Content -- General Materials folder. 

DATA ANALYSIS REPORT for Lab#2 Protein Extraction (group) and #4 Battery Studies (ind)(worth 12 and 18%, resp.):

A DA report is an abbreviated version of a memo report which focuses on the calculations and discussion of results.  Objectives should be outlined and procedures summarized with a reference to lab manual for the full step-by-step procedure.  Any modifications made to the experimental procedure must be highlighted.  The report should include a detailed summary of the calculations performed, discussion of observed results (inc. validity of assumptions and error analysis), conclusions and recommendations based on your findings.  Report format is similar to formal memo reports (i.e. numbered sections, tables, figures and proper referencing, etc.). Refer to marking scheme for expected content and lengths of each section in DA reports posted on LEARN Content General Materials folder (are similar to ChE101L schemes).

Note that some data analysis may be shared between group members in Lab 4 but these individual reports should be written independently (i.e. no paraphrasing of colleague’s work).

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
