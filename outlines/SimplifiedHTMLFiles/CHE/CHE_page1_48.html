Course Description

CHE 520:

An integrated view of process flowsheet synthesis, analysis and optimization, emphasizing the use of computer aids to perform steady-state mass and energy balancing on chemical processes. Application of relevant chemical engineering concepts, including mass and energy balances, thermodynamics of mixtures and unit operation models, to process simulation. Process simulation modes, numerical solution of relevant non-linear equation systems, recycle partitioning, and tearing. Applications including heat exchanger networks, multistage separation processes, and chemical reactor networks. Practical skills will be developed through the application of available process simulation software.

View requirements for CHE 520

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Describe, formulate, and develop nonlinear mass and energy balance models for standard chemical process units.
Develop process-level models using individual nonlinear unit models, given process topologies and flowsheets.
Formulate and solve process-level models using simplified linear models for individual units and ideal thermodynamic methods.
Develop and solve process-level models using nonlinear unit models and thermodynamic methods via process simulation software.
Describe and select appropriate thermodynamic methods given process conditions and available empirical data.
Apply process simulation software to solve, analyze, and optimize staged-separation units with multicomponent feeds.
Describe and select appropriate numerical methods for the solution of nonlinear unit and process models.
Interpret and communicate process-level performance and analysis to peers in written format.
