Course Description

CHE 330:

Review of fundamentals, including 2nd law and concepts of equilibrium, phase and reaction equilibria, fugacity, exergy. Thermodynamics applied to practical situations. Examples chosen from: fluid flow; power generation; refrigeration; air conditioning and water cooling; liquefaction of gases; equilibria in complex chemical reactions and separation processes; surface phenomena; electrochemical reactions; biological processes.

View requirements for CHE 330

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the P-V-T relations of pure substances, and know how to determine volumetric properties of pure fluids using equations of state and generalized correlations. [1b, 2b]
Understand the concepts of sensible heat, latent heat and standard heat of reaction, and know how to apply them to determining the heat effects of industrially important reactions. [1b, 1d, 2b, 2c]
Understand residual properties and their fundamental property relations, know how to use thermodynamic diagrams and tables to determine thermodynamic properties of fluids. [1b, 2b]
Understand flow process thermodynamics, and know how to apply it to flow processes (e.g., compressors, pumps, turbine/expanders, nozzles, throttles). [1b, 1d, 2b]
Understand the concepts of power generation and refrigeration/liquifaction, and apply them to various operating cycles. [1b, 1d, 2b, 2c]
Understand solution thermodynamics, and apply it to vapor-liquid equilibrium problems. [1b, 2b, 2c]
Know how the reaction equilibrium constant is related to standard Gibbs energy of the reaction and be able to determine the composition of the reaction mixture at reaction equilibrium. [1b, 2b]

Tentative Course Schedule

  2. Review of basic concepts
  3. Volumetric properties of pure fluids
  4. Heat effects
  5. First, second and third laws of thermodynamics

  7. Thermodynamics of flow processes 
  8. Production of power from heat 
  9. Refrigeration and liquefaction 
  10. Solution thermodynamics
  11. Vapor-liquid equilibria 
  12. Chemical reaction equilibria
