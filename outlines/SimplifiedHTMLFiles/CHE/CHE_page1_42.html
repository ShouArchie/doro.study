Course Description

CHE 491:

Project-based experimental investigation of complex systems in main areas of application of chemical engineering. Topics selected from reaction engineering, separation processes, bioprocess engineering, electrochemical engineering, materials engineering, and process control.

View requirements for CHE 491

ChE 491 Chemical Engineering Laboratory 5 involves the experimental investigation of chemical engineering unit operations through open-ended project laboratories, focusing on solving practical chemical engineering experimental, design, and simulation problems through the integration of fundamental unit operation concepts and pilot-scale laboratory experiments. The primary objectives of this course are (1) to enhance the in-depth understanding and applications of chemical engineering theories to the performance of authentic unit operation processes through hands-on experience, and (2) to integrate the unit operation theories and experimental observation into practical chemical engineering design and simulation. Some other objectives can be found on Page 13 and 14 of the lab manual.

Course Contents

  • The laboratory course consists of two project-based laboratories.
  • Each lab group is recommended to select one laboratory project from each of the two lab series (G and H) listed below, but having both laboratory projects from the same lab series is entirely possible if lab scheduling for the class is permitted.
  • A project statement outlining the scope and design specifications of each lab project is available in the lab manual, along with the recommended references and relevant lab equipment information.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
The learning outcomes of the course are listed below with the number in brackets being the corresponding CEAB number.

Upon the completion of the course, you should be able to:

  1.  Identify, formulate, and solve practical chemical engineering unit operation problems by applying core principles of chemical engineering [1, 2].
  2. Design and conduct experimental investigations of ChE unit operations by appropriately selecting experimental methods and procedures, critically analyzing and interpreting experimental results, and synthesizing all into valid conclusions [1, 2, 3].
  3. Apply modern engineering tools (online data acquisition and control, numerical computation software) and problem-solving skills to solve practical ChE experimental, design, and simulation problems. [2, 3, 4, 5]. 
  4. Locate and evaluate relevant standards and technical literature, and seek out and use the well-established information to validate experimental, design, or simulation results and conclusions [1, 3, 12], and further implement open-ended design or simulation project [4, 9, 11, 12].
  5. Communicate clearly and effectively in oral, written, and/or visual form through working in a lab team and writing technical reports [6, 7],
  6. Contribute to and manage laboratory assignments and experimental activities including following all lab safety protocol and academic policy as a member and/or a leader of an engineering team [6, 7, 8, 10].

Student Assessment

Lab Reports

  • Group partial report for the prelab proposal and group full lab reports for the project lab reports (see detailed lab report requirements and marking scheme on Pages 6 and 14~15 of the lab manual).
  • The prelab project proposal is due on the scheduled lab day and the final project report due three weeks after the lab day by 11:59 pm.

Assessment and Grade Distribution

  • Overall grade distribution:
  • Project labs: 88% (each project 44%).
  • Presentation of the first project lab: 12%
  • 1% bonus mark for accessing/participating the course survey at the end of term.
  • Grading scheme for the individual project labs:
  • Project consultation: 10%
  • Prelab project proposal: 15% 
  • Lab attendance and performance: 5%
  • Lab report: 70%

Administrative Policy

Academic Integrity and Discipline: UW Policy # 71 

  • Inappropriate academic behaviors and misconducts such as plagiarism, cheating, copying, excessive collaboration, and sharing are strongly prohibited for this laboratory course, and can result in serious consequences of the academic offences. For detailed information, see Procedures Related to Academic Offences and UW Policy 71 at:

        https://uwaterloo.ca/engineering/procedures-related-academic-offences-and-policy-71

 

Generative AI in ChE 491

  • The laboratory course permits the use of generative AI (GenAI) such as ChatGPT, MS CoPilot, GitHub Copilot, etc. as a tool to enhance laboratory learning, particularly in report writing, Python programming.
  • Lab submissions, whether aided with GenAI or not, will be strictly assessed based on the relevance of the contents, required deliverables, and lab report format stipulated in the lab manual and marking rubric.
  • The use of GenAI needs to be acknowledged in terms of where and how the GenAI is used as a learning tool in the lab submissions.

Lab Attendance, Safety, and Performance

  • The scheduled lab session attendance is mandatory for all the labs, and attendance is also required of all students for the project-based lab consultation. 
  • When attending in-person labs, you must obey the general lab safety rules summarized in the lab manual (Pages 3~4) and any lab specific safety instructions. Lab safety is part of the lab attendance mark, and failing to obey all the lab safety rules can result in an expulsion from the in-person lab. 
  • As a deep understanding of lab equipment, process, and experimental observation is paramount for data analysis and design for the unit operation labs, active participation of all the lab activities through hands-on operation as well as active observation and interaction is strongly encouraged.
  • Lab participation is also a prerequisite for lab report.
