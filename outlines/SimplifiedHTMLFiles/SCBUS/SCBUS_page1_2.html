Course Description

SCBUS 122:

This course uses case studies, project management, team/group dynamics, and strategies to explore the essential parameters that comprise the management of business organizations: planning, organization, leadership, control, and ethics. The course considers the importance of diversity/globalization, human resource management, a comparison of traditional and entrepreneurial leadership, and management as well as business ethics.

View requirements for SCBUS 122

This course introduces and applies common tools used by managers such as the Business Model Canvas and frameworks like Decision Making and Project Management models.  Managerial skills such as communication (oral and written), working in and leading teams and critical thinking will be a focus, using current events and real life scenarios in business and organizations to learn from.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Analyze an organization (roles, organization, control) using a Business Model Canvas
Identify the roles leaders and managers play within an organization, in particular the important role of Science and Innovation in organizations
Apply a decision making framework taking into account ethical concerns and other criteria
Describe and apply the principles of project management
Demonstrate professional skills important throughout your career (e.g. time management, project management, presentation skills, working in a team, conflict management, critical thinking, problem solving and report writing)

General Information

  • Contributions to the class will be a combined mark reflecting attendance, in class participation, contributions to the online discussions in Perusall and a Peer evaluation for group work.
  • Reflections, Current Events and Project Management Plans will use Drop Box feature in LEARN.  Quizzes will also be done in LEARN
  • Personal Reflections, Quizzes, Midterm and Final Exams must be the exclusive work of the individual students.
  • There will be no additional accommodations (i.e., other than dropping the lowest 2 of the 10 quizzes), no extensions or re-grade requests of any kind for quizzes. If you miss a week’s quiz for any reason, including illness, you will receive a grade of zero, no exceptions.
  • Additional information on assignment requirements and rubrics will be provided in Learn.

Working in Teams (Current Event,  Project Management Document)

  • Teams will be required to complete a one page team contract which will outline their intended code of conduct, how the team will interact, what online collaboration tool(s) they will use, when they will meet each week online, and how they will discuss and assess team performance and resolve team issues.
  • It is important that teams work to include all team members and to resolve any individual or team issues that arise.  The team contract should help set the stage for this process and for any issue resolution. Teams should escalate quickly to Faculty if they need help resolving critical team issues.   It will be considered a failure of the team if underperforming team members are excluded from contributing without working to resolve the issue according to the team contract and engaging Faculty for help if required.  In some cases, this could result in Faculty imposing a 5-10% downwards adjustment of all team grades for all team members.

Assignment Screening

Text matching software (Turnitin®) may be used to screen assignments and examinations in this course. Turnitin® is used to verify that all materials and sources in assignments are documented and that academic integrity exist on both examination and assignment submissions. Students will be given an option if they do not want to have their assignment screened by Turnitin®. If you have concerns about using Turnitin or would like to opt out of using it, please contact your Instructor by the end of Week 1 to discuss alternate arrangements to submit your assignment. Please note that direct use and copying from AI tools such as ChatGPT will also be considered an academic integrity offence.

This course will be delivered with in person lectures. In such circumstances courses will not automatically record lectures. Permission from the instructor is required prior to recording of lectures. Accommodations

Students who experience an extenuating circumstance (e.g. illness, injury, varsity tournaments, academic conferences) that is sufficiently significant to temporarily render them unable to meet academic requirements must contact their instructor as soon as possible and be prepared to submit verification. The weighting of the missed evaluation may be added to the final examination weighting.

You can self-declare an absence on Quest in certain situations. For other illnesses, obtain a verification of illness form (VIF) and submit it electronically to the Science Undergraduate Office (SUO).

When an evaluation is missed due to illness, the student should be prepared to provide a verification of illness. This information will be used to determine if the student is eligible for consideration. The dates affected and degree of incapacitation will be used to determine follow-up action. Students should ensure that their health care provider indicates this type of information on the medical certificate. For example, “was seen” or “attended emergency Dept on...” are not acceptable. The University of Waterloo verification of illness form is available online. Any University of Waterloo student can go to University of Waterloo Health Services, but the VIF form can also be filled out by non-UWaterloo healthcare professionals.

Although illness may constitute a valid reason for missing an exam, it does not automatically entitle a student to an alternate exam. If an alternate exam time is provided, it is expected that it will as soon as possible after a student returns to class. In some instances, students may be asked to write the exam the next time the course is offered.

The University acknowledges that, due to the pluralistic nature of the University community, some students may seek accommodations on religious grounds. Accordingly, students must consult with their instructor(s) within two weeks of the announcement of the due date for which accommodation is being sought. Failure to provide a timely request will decrease the likelihood of providing an accommodation.

Regulations for Academic Discipline

What is an Academic Offense?

Offenses include the following:

  • Plagiarism on written assignments.
  • Inappropriate citations on written assignments.
  • Cheating on exams or tests.
  • Double submission of material.
  • Fraudulent use of medical certificates.
  • Sharing of assignment answers (excessive collaboration) will result in cheating penalties for both individuals, regardless of intent.

Avoidance of Academic Offenses

All students are expected to know what constitutes an academic offense and thereby avoid committing them by doing the following: 

  • Thoroughly familiarize yourself with Policy 71.
  • Understand that any work submitted will be assumed to be the work of the individual student (other types of submission, i.e., group or teams, must be clearly indicated by the Instructor or Teaching Assistant for the course).
  • Only permitted material will be allowed on your desk during the examination period (pen, pencil, exam paper, and approved, designated calculator, if needed).

Resolution of Academic Offenses

Any activity that contravenes Policy 71 is deemed an academic offense and will be followed up with the Instructor for the course in the following manner: 

  • Teaching Assistants will bring all cases (suspected or otherwise) of academic dishonesty to the attention of the Instructor.
  • For written assignments, the material will not be handed back.
  • The students will be required to meet with the Instructor to discuss the infraction.
  • Instructors must notify the Associate Dean of the infraction in writing with all supporting evidence.

First offenses, depending on the severity of the infraction, will be handled as outlined below:

  • A mark of 0 will be given for the assignment by the Instructor.
  • A letter of offense and grade change, signed by the Instructor and student, will be forwarded to the Associate Dean of Undergraduate Studies, Faculty of Science.
  • Copies of this letter will be placed in the student's file.

More serious offenses or second academic infractions will be handled on an individual basis by the Associate Dean of Undergraduate Studies, Faculty of Science.

Faculty Policy

Intellectual Property

Students should be aware that this course contains the intellectual property of their instructor, TA, and/or the University of Waterloo. 

Intellectual property includes items such as:

  • Lecture content, spoken and written (and any audio/video recording thereof);
  • Lecture handouts, presentations, and other materials prepared for the course (e.g., PowerPoint slides);
  • Questions or solution sets from various types of assessments (e.g., assignments, quizzes, tests, final exams); and
  • Work protected by copyright (e.g., any work authored by the instructor or TA or used by the instructor or TA with permission of the copyright owner).

Course materials and the intellectual property contained therein, are used to enhance a student’s educational experience. However, sharing this intellectual property without the intellectual property owner’s permission is a violation of intellectual property rights.  For this reason, it is necessary to ask the instructor, TA and/or the University of Waterloo for permission before uploading and sharing the intellectual property of others online (e.g., to an online repository).

Permission from an instructor, TA or the University is also necessary before sharing the intellectual property of others from completed courses with students taking the same/similar courses in subsequent terms/years.  In many cases, instructors might be happy to allow distribution of certain materials. However, doing so without expressed permission is considered a violation of intellectual property rights.

Please alert the instructor if you become aware of intellectual property belonging to others (past or present) circulating, either through the student body or online. The intellectual property rights owner deserves to know (and may have already given their consent).
