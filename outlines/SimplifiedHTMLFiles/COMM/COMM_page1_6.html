Course Description

COMM 400:

A study of the spirit of entrepreneurship in the technology industry, opportunities emerging in the new information economy, and the implementation issues associated with starting an entrepreneurial venture in today's rapidly changing environment. Many of the concepts covered will also be applicable to careers in the technology industry or in information-based companies, where "intrapreneurship" is an emerging theme. Approximately one-half of the class time will involve guest lectures by entrepreneurs actively involved in the business community.

View requirements for COMM 400

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Recognize the “unstoppable forces” that shape the entrepreneurial opportunity for high tech businesses, including:
The Changing Technology Environment – How is it disrupting business models and entire companies and industries? What opportunities does it create for entrepreneurs?
Globalization – How is the structure of the world’s economies changing the nature of opportunities around the globe?
Understand Entrepreneurship and Entrepreneurial Characteristics -- What does it take to be an Entrepreneur, and is it right for me?
Understand the Entrepreneurial Process -- What issues are involved in starting a business and managing it through the various stages of its life cycle?
Better understand Corporate Innovation -- How are entrepreneurial skills and approaches increasingly important to all careers?

Student Teams

You will be randomly assigned to teams of 4-6 students in order to complete the team assigned portions of the course deliverables. These team assignments will be made online at the end of the first week of classes.  Your team will be assigned to one of the guest entrepreneur presentations and will be required to lead the class discussions that night and to provide a written reflection on the guest entrepreneur’s presentation (see below). Your team will also have a final group presentation on a topic of interest related to entrepreneurship that will be due at the end of the term (see below).

Guest Entrepreneurs 

A primary component of this course will be Guest Entrepreneur presentations.  These will be conducted in class or via Teams during the scheduled Thursday 5PM sessions, as per the course schedule.  Since this is a major component of the learning process you are required to attend these sessions as they occur.  

The presentations will not be recorded. If we are hosting the speaker via Teams, the link will be posted in the Speaker's section of the LEARN page.

Each group will be assigned a Guest Entrepreneur. These groups have to prepare ahead of their session questions to ask. They will be preparing a report on that Guest Entrepreneur (Guest Entrepreneur Reflection). Major participation points will be marked for asking questions during these sessions with greater weight for the assigned group. Students whose groups were not assigned the Guest Entrepreneur will also be graded for their participation.

Peer Evaluations

Because 60% of your term mark will be rewarded in teams, you will be asked to provide a peer evaluation at the end of the course.  This will take the form of MS Forms announced by the end of the term.

If you are having challenges in your team, you MUST engage the instructors early to try to resolve those issues.  This is required if there are to be any reductions to individual team member grades.  Failure to do so may result in a penalty of 5-10% to the entire team.

Guest Entrepreneur Group Reflection

Your team will be responsible for submitting a reflection on your assigned guest entrepreneur.  This will be worth 30% of your mark for the course.  Additional details for the required reflection rubric will be provided separately on LEARN.

TopHat Engagement 

You will receive an invite to the TopHat textbook for the course to your uwaterloo email address.  Please check your spam folder first, and if you are either new to registering for the course or you have not received the invite please email your instructors.

Throughout the term you will be assigned the fifteen (15) TopHat chapters for completion based on the online due dates in the TopHat platform which are also reflected in the detailed course schedule.  It will mainly be two chapters per week. Chapters are easy to follow and will take 1-3 hours/chapter on average. Each chapter typically includes five (5) or more assigned multiple choice questions and some occasional free form reflection questions.  There are unlimited attempts per question for the multiple choice (50% per participation and 50% for correctness) and the reflection questions are free form and are marked for completion only.  These must be submitted by the due dates on TopHat to get credit.  Your total TopHat engagement score across the 15 chapters will count for 15% of your term mark.

There is a separate set of free form questions asking for your feedback at the end of each chapter.  These are entirely voluntary and anonymous … and do NOT count towards your TopHat engagement score.  THERE IS NO MIDTERM or FINAL EXAMS FOR THE COURSE.

Group Capstone Entrepreneurship Presentation 

Your team will be responsible for submitting a 30 minute presentation in class. This will be worth 30% of your term mark and will be scheduled later during the course. Additional details will be provided on LEARN.    THERE IS NO FINAL EXAM FOR THE COURSE.

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
