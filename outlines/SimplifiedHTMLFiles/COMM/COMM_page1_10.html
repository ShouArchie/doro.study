Course Description

COMM 432:

This course will introduce students to approaches, techniques, and terminology used in electronic business. Students will also study issues in disciplines related to electronic business. They will review a number of sites and identify efficient e-commerce analysis, design and development techniques. Students will be introduced to current electronic business tools and standards, and will construct their own simple electronic business site.

View requirements for COMM 432

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
conduct feasibility study of e-commmerce for a given business;
develop e-commerce strategies for a given busines, including revenue models and digital marketing plan;
design e-commerce web sites;
perform accessbility test and usability test on web sites and apps.

Group Project

The group project is a running business case study that provides an opportunity to apply the concepts learned in the course to a “real” situation. There will be several interim progress reports and one final report, as well as a presentation due throughout the term. Late project reports will not be accepted. All reports are to be typed. All diagrams are to have a professional appearance, and all hand drawn diagrams must be drawn using rulers and stencils. Project reports are to be submitted to the online drop box. 

Each group will consist of no more than 4 students. You may choose your own group partners and your should register your group on the class Google Doc (its URL will be posted on D2L) no later than Jan 16th. Students not in a group will be randomly assigned to a group by the instructor. Tasks related to this project must be divided evenly and fairly among the group members. All students in a group are expected to contribute equally. Peer evaluation will be conducted at the end of the term to determine individual grade for the project.

Use Collaboration Software

Since you must work on a group project, it is strongly recommended that you use some software to coordinate team work and communications. One option is Microsoft Office 36, for which you have free access as a UW student: https://uwaterloo.ca/office-365/. It is a powerful collaboration tool for both shared documents as well as communications.

Students should be aware that this course contains the intellectual property of their instructor, TA, and/or the University of Waterloo.  Intellectual property includes items such as:

Lecture content, spoken and written (and any audio/video recording thereof);

  • Lecture content, spoken and written (and any audio/video recording thereof);
  • Lecture handouts, presentations, and other materials prepared for the course (e.g., PowerPoint slides);
  • Questions or solution sets from various types of assessments (e.g., assignments, quizzes, tests, final exams); and
  • Work protected by copyright (e.g., any work authored by the instructor or TA or used by the instructor or TA with permission of the copyright owner).

Course materials and the intellectual property contained therein, are used to enhance a student’s educational experience. However, sharing this intellectual property without the intellectual property owner’s permission is a violation of intellectual property rights.  For this reason, it is necessary to ask the instructor, TA and/or the University of Waterloo for permission before uploading and sharing the intellectual property of others online (e.g., to an online repository).

Permission from an instructor, TA or the University is also necessary before sharing the intellectual property of others from completed courses with students taking the same/similar courses in subsequent terms/years.  In many cases, instructors might be happy to allow distribution of certain materials.  However, doing so without expressed permission is considered a violation of intellectual property rights.

Please alert the instructor if you become aware of intellectual property belonging to others (past or present) circulating, either through the student body or online.  The intellectual property rights owner deserves to know (and may have already given their consent).
