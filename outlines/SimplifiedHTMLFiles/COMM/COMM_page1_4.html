Course Description

COMM 321:

This intermediate level accounting course will focus on the usage of financial information from a management perspective.

View requirements for COMM 321

The objectives of this course are for students to: become more familiar with the structure and content of financial statements, understand the environment in which financial reporting choices are made, know the options available under the current reporting models, develop skills in applying complex accounting standards, and understand how to use accounting information to make informed decisions. The course continues the study started in AFM 101 but treats subjects in considerably more depth 

 

Topics covered include each of the 4 financial statements, most individual items commonly found in a typical set of financial statements, as well as some supplementary information generally contained in the notes to the financial statements. This course will emphasize not only accounting knowledge but also skills and attitudes necessary for the success of financial professionals, such as problem-solving skills, communication skills, and professional and ethical conduct.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Know the North American format of, and be able to prepare any of the four financial statements from a given list of accounts and balances
Explain the objective of financial reporting, the conceptual framework, warning signs that financial statements might be fraudulent and the agency problem
Prepare background journal entries for the major balance sheet items and understand reporting issues associated with each
Calculate basic and diluted earnings per share from a given data set
Know disclosure requirements for segmented reporting, subsequent events and related party transactions, as well as where to locate typical analyst data requirements in a typical annual report
