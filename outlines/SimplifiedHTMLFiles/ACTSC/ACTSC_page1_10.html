Course Description

ACTSC 363:

Introduction to the collective risk model; models for loss frequency: (a, b, 0) and (a, b, 1) classes of distributions, compound distributions and mixtures; models for loss severity: exponential, gamma, lognormal, Pareto, Weibull, and mixtures; measures of distribution tails; impact of policy adjustments on loss frequency and severity; estimation of frequency and severity models.

View requirements for ACTSC 363

You need at least 60% in ACTSC 363 to fulfill ACTSC Honours requirements.

60% in this course is a prerequisite for enrollment in ACTSC 431, which is also a required course. ACTSC 363 is an introductory property and casualty (P&C) course and a prerequisite for more advanced and technical courses in the P&C sequence: ACTSC 431, ACTSC 432, and ACTSC 463. 

This course helps fulfill FSA, FCAS, and FCIA requirements

This course covers many learning objectives for the SOA FAM(-S) and ASTAM Exams, and CAS MAS-1 Exam. The course may treat topics differently than the SOA/CAS exams' syllabus, but can still help you prepare for the exams.

This course is accredited under the CIA accreditation program. More information on this program can be found on this CIA website and this UW website.

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
identify classes of severity and frequency distributions for modelling loss outcomes and construct new families of distributions;
compute and explain the uses and limitations of risk measures like VaR and TVaR;
calculate various measures of tail weight and interpret the results to compare the tail weights;
evaluate the impacts of coverage modifications like deductibles, coinsurance, and policy limits on severity and frequency distributions;
estimate loss and payment distributions using frequency and severity data.

Assessment schedule

If you have a time conflict with the assessment schedule, email me as soon as possible to discuss possible accommodations. 

I highly recommend you to keep up with all practice problems and ask questions often.

Practice problems and solutions will be posted on LEARN with each chapter. While they will not be graded, completely them independently is critical for reinforcing your understanding of the underlying concepts. I highly recommend you to keep up with each chapter, and attempt each problem without looking at the provided solutions since there can often be multiple ways to solve a given problem.

Concepts build quickly on top of each other in this course, so it is essential to ask for clarification as soon as possible. You can ask questions before/during/after each lecture, or during instructor/TA office hours.

I also recommend Dan Wolczuk's Learning How to Learn Series and these Student Success Office resources related to time management, study skills, and stress management, among other topics.

Assignments will be released on Crowdmark two weeks before the due dates.

Your solutions to assignments also need to be submitted on Crowdmark. Detailed instructions for assignments will be provided when they are released. Since the intent of the assignments is to have you practice applying the material (and supplement the practice problem sets), they will be graded based on a mix of completion and accuracy.

Midterm coverage and extra office hours will be announced one week in advance.

The material covered in each exam will be announced on LEARN one week before each assessment. The final exam covers all topics introduced in the course.

Your exam seats will be assigned.

Your seat location will be assigned a few days before each exam and can be found on Odyssey.

You have 3 days to request an assessment regrade.

Assessments will be graded and returned within one week using Crowdmark, and the solutions will be posted on LEARN. You will have 3 days to file a regrading request. Instructions on how to file a request will be posted on LEARN with the return of each assessment. The instructor/TA reserves the right to review the grading of your entire assessment, and after the review, your resulting grade can be higher, lower, or stay the same.

Follow these policies for accommodation due to illness or extenuating circumstances.

If you experience a significant illness, ongoing medical condition, or other circumstances that prevent you from meeting academic obligations, please refer to the Faculty of Mathematics' Absence Declarations webpage for guidance on faculty policies. The weight of missed assessments due to approved declared absences will be shifted to the final exam. 

Students who have been unable to engage with their courses for two weeks or longer, whether by illness or extenuating circumstances, should follow up with their individual instructors and their academic advisor to assess whether maintaining their current course load is still viable.

For illness during the final exam, please refer to the Math Faculty’s INC policy. If you miss the final exam for a valid reason, you can request a grade of INC and write the exam the next time the course is taught. Typically, a grade of INC is only awarded to a student who has done coursework well enough that they could reasonably be expected to pass the course. INCs will not be granted if you miss both midterms.
