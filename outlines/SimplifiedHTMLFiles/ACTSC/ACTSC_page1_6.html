How to get support

We’re here to help you succeed! There are several ways to get assistance:

1. Piazza discussion board

The Piazza discussion board is your online resource for course-related questions.

Who responds: The instructor/TA will monitor the board and respond to questions. We aim to respond within 24 hours on weekdays or by the next business day on weekends and holidays.

When to use Piazza vs. instructor office hours: Piazza is ideal for mild to moderate confusion about specific problems. If you're stuck on several problems (more than three per week) or have complex questions (e.g., "Can someone walk me through Problem 4.3?"), I suggest attending instructor office hours for more personalized support, allowing for follow-up questions and more comprehensive explanations. 

Piazza posting guidelines:

  • You can post anonymously to classmates by selecting "Show up as Anonymous," but instructors and TAs will see your name.
  • Use a clear subject line (e.g., "Problem Set 1 Q3(b)").
  • Indicate what you've tried and where you're stuck (e.g., "I thought the solution should be ... but my answer differs from the provided solution. What went wrong?").
  • If our response doesn’t fully address your question, reply and explain what you’re still confused about—don’t hesitate to ask follow-up questions. If you remain unclear after the back-and-forth exchange, attend instructor office hours for face-to-face support, which can often provide more nuanced explanations.
  • We encourage you to respond to classmates' questions. Teaching others is one of the best ways to learn and helps you build written communication skills valued by employers.

2. Instructor office hours

I encourage you to join my weekly office hours. You can ask detailed questions about lecture content, seek help with specific problems, and discuss learning strategies.

If the first midterm doesn’t go as planned, don’t worry—office hours can be valuable for refining your learning strategies to improve in the rest of the course.

3. Instructor email for personal matters

For questions about personal circumstances (e.g., accommodations, scheduling conflicts) or to provide course feedback, please email your instructor directly.

4. Other resources

I highly recommend Dan Wolczuk's How to Learn Series, along with resources from the Student Success Office on time management, study skills, and stress management.

If you are managing personal or academic challenges, AccessAbility Services and academic advisors are available to help with accommodations and course planning.

Course Description

ACTSC 232:

The future lifetime random variable: probability and survival functions; force of mortality; complete and curtate expectation of life; Makeham and Gompertz mortality laws. Life tables: characteristics of population and insurance life tables; selection; fractional age assumptions. Life insurance payments and annuity payments: present value random variables; expected present values; higher moments; actuarial notation. Annual, 1/mthly, and continuous cases. Relationships between insurance and annuity functions. Premiums: expense loadings. Present value of future loss random variables and distribution, net and gross cases. Equivalence principle. Portfolio percentile principle. Extra risks.

View requirements for ACTSC 232

This course introduces the foundational concepts and mathematics of life contingencies which are essential to calculating life insurance benefits, pension schemes, and other actuarial applications.

The course builds on your understanding of probability from STAT 230, financial mathematics from ACTSC 231, and some concepts from MTHEL 131.

By the end of the course, you'll have a strong grasp of key life contingencies techniques, laying the foundation for further study (ACTSC 331, 453, 454, 455) and practical applications in actuarial science.

To fulfill the ACTSC Honours requirements, you need at least 60% in ACTSC 232

You can find the Actuarial Science program and plan requirements in the Undergraduate Academic Calendar. To connect with an academic advisor, visit the department’s advising webpage.

This course meets several requirements for SOA, CAS, and CIA designations

This course covers most SOA FAM-L Exam learning objectives, but will not address those related to stochastic processes and multi-life contingencies. It is accredited under the CIA accreditation program (more details can be found on the CIA actuarial education webpage and the UW actuarial accreditation and exemptions webpage).

Strategies for success

Attend all lectures: Regular attendance helps you grasp key concepts and stay on track with the material.

Complete practice problems: Work through the assigned textbook problems (posted on LEARN) multiple times. Independent practice is essential for developing and self-assessing your understanding. Compare your solutions critically with the provided answers to identify gaps in knowledge. I highly recommend watching Dan Wolczuk's video on effective practice.

Solve problems promptly: As new concepts are introduced, solve related textbook problems to reinforce your understanding while the material is fresh.

Ask questions early: Don’t wait if something is unclear. Ask questions during or after lectures, on Piazza, or during instructor/TA office hours to get timely clarification.

Assessments vary in difficulty based on their purpose.

Tutorial problem sets: Designed as learning tools to help you gauge your understanding of lecture material and stay current with content. 

Midterms: Serve as both learning and diagnostic tools. They assess whether you have a weak, satisfactory, or strong grasp of course content. Midterms include a few challenging questions to test your ability to apply concepts to more complex, unfamiliar scenarios, making them more difficult than quizzes. Reviewing your graded midterms offers a valuable learning opportunity.

Final Exam: Primarily diagnostic, slightly more challenging than midterms, and designed to evaluate your overall understanding of the course material.

You must use a nonprogrammable calculator. 

A financial calculator is not required, and all questions are designed so that full marks can be achieved with a standard non-financial calculator.

Assessment coverage will be announced one week before each assessment.

The material covered in each assessment will be announced on LEARN one week before each assessment. The final exam covers all topics introduced in the course.

Your seat will be assigned for the midterms and final exam, but not for tutorials.

Your seat location will be assigned a few days before the assessment and can be found here.

You have one week to request a regrade.

All assessments will be graded within one week, and the solutions will be posted on LEARN. You will have one week to file a regrading request. Instructions on how to file a request will be posted on LEARN with the return of each assessment. The instructor/TA reserves the right to review the grading of your entire assessment, and after the review, your resulting grade can be higher, lower, or stay the same.
