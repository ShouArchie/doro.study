Course Description

ACTSC 455 / ACTSC 855:

Profit testing for traditional and non-traditional life insurance. Pricing and valuation of embedded options in life insurance products. Defined benefit and defined contribution pension plan design. Theory and practice of unit credit methods for pension plan funding and valuation for final average salary, career average earnings, and career average revalued earnings pension plans; post-retirement health benefits.

ACTSC 455: View requirements for ACTSC 455

ACTSC 855: View requirements for ACTSC 855

Course Delivery - Flipped Classroom: 

  • Pre-recorded videos and notes for each week will be posted on Wednesdays at 4:00 pm. You can watch them any time you like.
  • Synchronous meetings will occur once per week, on Wednesdays from 2:30-3:50 in QNC 1507. In these sessions we will start with a review quiz on the video material from the week and discuss any confusing points, and then complete graded activities in small groups with help from the instructor/TA.

Tips for Success:

  • When watching videos, pause and think about questions asked - try your best to answer before unpausing and finding out the answer. Add your own comments to the notes I post as you watch.
  • Complete optional Knowledge Check and practice problems (in textbook or on Learn) to self-assess your level of understanding.
  • Participate in tutorial activities actively, ask and answer questions on Piazza - learn from each other.
  • Reach out to me if you are having any trouble. I want you to succeed and I will do my best to support you in any way I can.

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Identify diversifiable and non-diversifiable risks
Calculate pension obligations for DC and DB pension plans
Use profit testing to price and reserve both traditional and modern insurance products
Quantify and manage risk associated with investment guarantees
Construct and document spreadsheets to solve actuarial problems
Communicate actuarial results effectively to technical and non-technical audiences

ACTSC 455 vs 855: If you are taking ACTSC 855, you may have additional or slightly different questions on the assignments, midterm, and final exam. You will also be responsible for doing a project. More details about this will follow near the end of term.

Tutorials: Class sessions on Wednesdays will be used for interactive activities to both enhance and assess your comprehension of the course material, each worth 1% (lowest 2 dropped.)

Assignments:  There will be 5 written assignments throughout the term. Assignments are tentatively due at 10 pm on: Jan 31, Feb 14, Mar 7, Mar 21, and Apr 4. Assignments will include significant writing and/or computing components, and clear communication and documentation of work is important. Assignments may be completed in pairs or groups of three.

Midterm: There will be one midterm test on Fri Feb 28 from 6:30 – 8:00 pm in MC 1056.  It will cover the first half of the course, up to the end of profit testing.

Oral Exam: There will be a short (15-minute) one-on-one exam scheduled individually at a time that works for you. It will be open-book and you can bring in any materials you like. This is an opportunity for you to demonstrate your knowledge (and more importantly, your ability to communicate that knowledge) of the course material. More details about this will follow.

Final Exam: There will be a usual 2.5-hour final exam during the exam period (Apr 9 – 25). 

You must have a passing weighted average for the Exam component (Midterm, Oral, Final) to pass the course.

Administrative Policy

Regrading requests: You can ask for an assessment to be re-graded within 7 days of receiving the marked assessment, by submitting a written note with your concerns to the TA.

Missed Assessments: If you miss an Assignment deadline due to illness or emergency, a 48-hour extension will be provided (there is no need to use a 2-day or self-declared absence for assignment deadlines.) If you miss a Tutorial activity, depending on the week you may be offered the chance to complete the activity on your own outside of class time and submit for full credit. The lowest two Tutorial activities are dropped. If you miss the Midterm due to illness or emergency, please contact Diana for an arrangement. The most likely accommodation will be an individual oral exam on the assessment material, rather than shifting weight to other assessments. If you miss your Oral Exam time, we will reschedule.
