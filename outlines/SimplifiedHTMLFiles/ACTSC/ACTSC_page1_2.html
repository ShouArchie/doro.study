Course Description

ACTSC 221:

The theory of rates of interest and discount; annuities and sinking funds with practical applications to mortgage and bond questions. Yield rates.

View requirements for ACTSC 221

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Be able to understand how a mortgage works and how refinancing a mortgage may be able to save money.
Be able to price a bond on and between coupon dates.
Be able to understand the basics of what drives the price of a stock using the Dividend Discount Model (DDM).
Be able to do calculations to find out how much needs to be invested periodically to reach retirement goals.

Student Assessment

MOBIUS ASSIGNMENTS: Mobius assignments are auto-generated (algorithmic) questions that are test you on each section of the material. 

As you complete the section, you will do a Mobius assignment.  It is marked by the system and you are provided with feedback.  You may do these as many times as you wish, and you get the highest mark attained. 

On the class schedule (see the end of the outline), there are recommended due dates for each Mobius assignment.  These dates largely correspond to when you should be done a given week’s work.  I feel that you should attempt them after you finish the week’s studying to see how you are grasping the materials.  If you do not get to do the Mobius assignment by that date, it will remain open until the last day of lectures.  This class and learning the materials is iterative in nature. You may completely understand the week 3 material after doing the week 6 sections.  You may go back and redo the week 3 Mobius assignment and improve your mark. 

Be careful, however, that you do not allow this freedom to neglect the timely study of the materials.  You still have assignments and tests to write, and those dates are set and will not be moved.

TESTS: There will be five (5) tests and the lowest mark will be dropped for average calculations.  Tests are done in lecture time and in the lecture room, unless you are told otherwise.  No online test alternative will be made available.

ASSIGNMENTS: There will be five (5) assignments and all marks are included in the final grade calculation.   

These written assignments will be distributed through Crowdmark.  They will be distributed a minimum of 5 days before they are due, and they will be submitted via Crowdmark.  If for some reason an assignment is not distributed on time, the due date will be adjusted accordingly,

FINAL EXAMINATION: The final exam is cumulative. You must receive a passing mark on the final examination to pass the class. It will be written on campus in a time slot determined by the Registrar’s Office.  I have no control over when that happens.

If you do not pass the final exam, then the grade you will receive will be the lower of the calculated mark or your final examination mark.

POLICY REGARDING MISSED QUIZZES OR ASSIGNMENTS

I have a very liberal missed quiz or assignment policy.  Here it is simply put:

  1. If you wrote all 5 quizzes, I drop your lowest quiz mark when calculating your final grade.
  2. If you missed one quiz for any reason, I drop that no show mark when calculating your final grade since a missed quiz is a mark of zero.  I don’t care why you missed it.  Illness.  Home for Easter and can’t get back to campus on time…whatever.  It doesn’t matter to me.  It is the dropped quiz.
  3. If you missed more than one quiz for any reason, I drop one of the no shows when calculating your final grade.  The other no shows go on the final.  Again, I don’t care why you missed them.  In all cases, you will only get 1 dropped quiz maximum.

So, if you think you will get your lowest mark of the quizzes you wrote dropped, that is not how it works.  If you wrote 3, for example, and got 30%, 70% and 80%, then those marks are used for your grade.  One missed quiz is dropped and one missed is moved to the final exam.

In more detail:

  1. Wrote 5 = lowest dropped
  2. Wrote 4 = no show dropped
  3. Wrote 3 = one no show dropped, other no show on the final
  4. Wrote 2 = one no show dropped, other no shows on the final
  5. Wrote 1 = one no show dropped, other no shows on the final
  6. Wrote 0 = one no show dropped, other no shows on the final

That’s how it works.  If you have questions, please let me know.
