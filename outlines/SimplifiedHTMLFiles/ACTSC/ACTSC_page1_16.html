Course Description

ACTSC 431:

Aggregate loss and payment models: properties, analytic results, convolution-type methods, recursive methods, inversion-type methods, and simulation; advanced aggregate claims models; reinsurance; applications to insurance pricing; insurance pricing using generalized linear models.

View requirements for ACTSC 431

This course is a continuation of the loss modelling topics covered in ACTSC 363. Aggregate loss and aggregate payment models will be considered  for applications in insurance contexts. The course content is structured into the following set of chapters:

Chapter 0: Brief review of ACTSC 363

Chapter 1: Individual risk model

Chapter 2: Collective risk model

Chapter 3: Simulations in loss modelling applications

Chapter 4: Reinsurance

Chapter 5: Loss Modelling using GLMs

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
propose various probability models for the aggregate claim of an insurance portfolio
calculate distributional quantities of interest (mean, variance, VaR, ....) for these aggregate claim models
apply simulation methods in actuarial loss modelling
understand the basic principles of reinsurance to help insurers mitigate the risks of a portfolio of insurance
apply generalized linear models to complete a tariff analysis for a portfolio of non-life insurance policies

Administrative Policy

  1. Your final mark must be at least 50% to pass the course.
  2. It is strongly recommended that you follow the Weekly Schedule plan posted on Learn in the folder Course Information. The Weekly Schedule plan was designed to keep learning at a manageable pace throughout the semester.
  3. Requests for re-grading an examination must be made within 1 week after the papers are returned to you. No request will be accepted after this deadline.
  4. In the event that you are unable to write an examination, you should inform your instructor by email as soon as it is possible for you to do so. In your email, please provide the reason(s) why you are unable to write the examination. Additionally, you must follow the entire procedure as outlined by the Faculty of Mathematics at https://uwaterloo.ca/math/accommodations. You will not be eligible for an accommodation until all requirements are met.
  5. The final decision to grant or not a special accommodation rests with your instructor. In the event you miss a test and your instructor accepts to provide accommodation for the missed test, its test weight will be transferred to the final exam. Only in exceptional circumstances will a student be allowed to transfer both test weights to the final exam. Finally, in the event you miss the final exam and your instructor accepts to provide accommodation for the missed final exam, the final exam will be an oral exam that will take place during the final exam period at a time that does not conflict with your other final exams. More details will be shared with you in the event this situation applies.
