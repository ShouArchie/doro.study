Course Description

ACTSC 623:

Generalized linear models: multiple linear regression and normal linear model; exponential family; link functions; linear predicators; estimation; testing. Time series: Univariate ARIMA; multivariate AR; ARCH and other non-linear models.

View requirements for ACTSC 623

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Recognize and identify situations and data for which regression modelling might be an appropriate method of statistical analysis
Develop and fit appropriate generalized linear models (including normal, logistic, and Poisson models) to datasets using the R programming language
Use graphical and numerical analyses to determine whether the fitted model is appropriate
Interpret model output and results, draw conclusions in the context of the study, and communicate these conclusions to disparate interest groups
Explain the mathematical theory behind the results and output from a regression analysis, including the derivation of the distributions of model parameter estimators
Apply linear regression models to time series data to model seasonal and trend components
Explain the concept of stationarity as it relates to time series data
Use graphical and numerical analyses to understand and investigate autocorrelation in time series data
Fit ARIMA models to time series data using R, and interpret model output and results
Explain the concept of differencing in the fitting of ARIMA models to time series data
Use model selection methods to arrive at a suitable regression model
Fit multivariate AR models using R and interpret model output and results
Fit ARCH and GARCH models to time series data using R, and interpret model output and results

Texts / Materials

Note: Any prices provided in course outlines are best estimates based on recent online prices and do not include shipping or taxes. Prices may vary between retailers.

No materials required.

There is no textbook required for this course. The lectures, course notes, and supplementary learning material, along with the assignments and concept quizzes, are sufficient resources to enable a thorough understanding of course concepts. Students are encouraged to seek their own supplementary resource material (web-based or otherwise) if they so desire.

If you wish to use a published textbook as an additional reference resource on linear regression (as well as on some logistic regression content), one such text I could recommend is Introduction to Regression Modelling, by B. Abraham and J. Ledolter (Brooks/Cole pub., any edition). The text uses the same notation and terminology as that used in the lectures and lecture notes, and for the most part roughly follows the same content order as presented in lectures.

Contingencies for Emergency Remote Teaching: This course outline presents the instructor’s intentions for course delivery, assessments, weights, and due dates. To provide contingency for unforeseen circumstances, the instructor reserves the right to modify course topics and/or assessments and/or weight and/or deadlines if/when necessary.   • in-person lectures will be substituted with (asynchronous) online lesson delivery (narrated Mobius slides)
  • In-person concept quizzes will be replaced with online quizzes
  • the midterm exam will be replaced with an online version to be written at the same scheduled time

R Programming Language: We will use R for assignments, and R output in the lesson content. Both R and the more user-friendly environment, RStudio, are available as a free download. An installation guide for R and RStudio, as well as an introduction to RStudio and the R language are posted on the class website (LEARN). You will be responsible for understanding and interpreting R output on the concept quizzes, midterm, and final exam. The midterm may also include a component in which you are expected to fit and analysis regression models using R. 

Assignments: Assignments are to be submitted via Crowdmark by 11:59 pm (midnight) of the deadline date. See the schedule above or the LEARN calendar for deadline dates. You are welcome to work with classmates, but you must each submit your own assignment and create/provide your own output. Answers to questions requiring verbal explanations must be in your own words. Any individuals from whom you have received assistance or to whom you have provided assistance must be acknowledged on the cover page of your assignment.

Concept Quizzes: Quizzes will consist of two parts. Part 1, worth 1%, consists of an individual, closed book, proctored component. After Part 1 is submitted, students will write Part 2 of the quiz, worth 1.5%, for which they are encouraged to discuss the questions with classmates and ask questions of the instructor. Some of the questions may be the same for both parts. For Part 2, students may use their lecture notes and any ACTSC 623 W25 LEARN content. Electronic devices (e.g., laptops, smartphones, etc.) may be used only to access their lecture notes and/or ACTSC 623 W25 LEARN content. Varied format (multiple choice/select, true/false, short answer, …). Additional information will be posted on LEARN prior to each quiz.

Midterm Exam: To be held in person on Friday, March 7. Time TBD. Proctored, short answer. Additional information on content coverage and format will be posted well prior to the examination date.

Final Exam: Held in-person during final exam examination period. Proctored, comprehensive, short answer, closed book. Additional information on content coverage and format will be posted well prior to the examination date.
