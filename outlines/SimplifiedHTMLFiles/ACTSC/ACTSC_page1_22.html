Course Description

ACTSC 489:

Advanced topics course as announced by the department. Topics and term offerings will vary.

View requirements for ACTSC 489

The course will centre on a case study in which students will develop a pricing model (rating structure) on insurance data. The insurance data provided is reserved for educational purposes and cannot be used outside the classroom.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Learn the fundamentals of predictive modelling and its applications to loss modelling and pricing in the property & casualty insurance field
Gain hands-on experience with data cleansing and analysis techniques on a realistic large dataset
Build and validate three generalized linear models
Develop oral and written communication skills through reports and presentations

Assessment Policies

Late/Missed Assignments: Late or missed assignments/presentations can only shift their portion to the Assessment Test. 

Late/missed Assessment Test: Late or missed assessment tests cannot be rescheduled to other portions of the course. An in-person make-up test will be offered, with the date determined by the instructor. Failure to attend the make-up test will result in a score of 0 for the assessment test portion. 

Acceptable rules for group work: Specific rules on groupwork will be provided with assignments and projects. For general guidelines, please refer to https://uwaterloo.ca/academic-integrity/integrity-students/group-work
