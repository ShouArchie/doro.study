Course Description

ENBUS 314:

Development of alternative business models has important implications for sustainability. This course will explore the emergence of entirely new sustainable business models along with the revitalization of 'old' alternative business models from the past.

View requirements for ENBUS 314

People are the foundation of any economy. Organizations of all types – for-profit, public, non-profit, co-operative, and others – are all embedded within a variety of communities. The value generated by these organizations can be shared amongst those within these communities but is often extracted from them. Imagining our local economic future involves envisioning and creating economic systems where communities have direct influence and control over their economic destiny. This includes exploring diverse ownership and governance models such as community land trusts, cooperatives, and municipal ownership that aim to address not only wealth inequalities but also power imbalances in our communities.

Imagining our local economic future is a broad challenge with many potential solutions. Engaging in this work can raise even more questions than it answers. Who gets to decide which communities' futures are prioritized? When does one work with, in parallel to, or in opposition to existing power structures? How can resilience be enhanced at the individual, organizational, community, and ecosystem levels? What are the social, economic, and ecological forces that shape our local economic futures? Where do creativity, joy, and play fit into our conceptions of where we live?

By working through these and many other questions, you and your classmates will identify and map these complex systems to pinpoint key leverage points where you believe there is potential for transformative change. With this knowledge, you will develop prototype interventions that you can further refine alongside key community stakeholders after completing this course.

We would like to thank United College – GreenHouse, Waterloo Cooperative Residences Incorporated (WCRI) and all our community participants in advance for their collaboration and active participation in our course

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Act in response to complexity, ambiguity and conflict
Analyze elements of a “wicked problem” and use them to develop an analytical framework
Communicate with a variety of audiences
Integrate different perspectives into design
Design a system intervention
Assess and re-evaluate your role as an agent of change

Generative Artificial Intelligence

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will be discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions.

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo maybe found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgbt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases.

In addition, you should be aware that the legal-copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions for content from AI sources, including images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence. This includes the uploading of course material as an input into AI systems – the uploading of course materials (presentations, syllabi, assignment descriptions, fellow students’ work, etc.) to AI systems is not allowed in this course and may represent a copyright violation.

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
