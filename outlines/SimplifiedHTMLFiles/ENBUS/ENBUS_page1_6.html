Course Description

ENBUS 302:

A review of the successes and failures of businesses which have attempted to adopt sustainability principles into their strategies and practices. Case studies will be reviewed to identify barriers to adopting environmental priorities and the processes which proved successful in overcoming these obstacles.

View requirements for ENBUS 302

This course focuses on managerial decisions and actions related to strategic management with emphasis on sustainable enterprises. Following a typical strategic management cycle (or process), we review managerial decisions related to environmental scanning, strategy formulation, strategy implementation, and evaluation and control. We use recent business reports and case studies to apply these concepts. We will illustrate where relevant using the successes and failures of businesses which have attempted to adopt sustainability principles in their strategies and practices.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Describe and assess the connections between fundamental strategic motivations of business and sustainability
Challenge the traditional economic-based perspective to an alternative, more integrated approach to business
Understand and explain how competitive advantage(s), in an era where sustainability is increasingly important, and can be achieved
Craft environmental sustainability strategies for businesses using the strategic management process

Policy on Late Submissions

Late assignments will be penalized 10% of the total possible grade per day, starting 30 mins after the due date and time. After 96 hours (4 days), the assignment will receive a grade of zero. Exceptions will be made in (very) extraordinary circumstances (usually related to medical emergencies supported by documentation). Students anticipating that their assignment will be submitted late are encouraged to contact me earlier rather than later. 

For individual submissions (i.e., case responses), each of you will receive a total of 2 'grace days'. A grade day provides a 24-hour extension on the individual submission. You can use both grace days on one submissions or on two separate submissions. If you're electing to use one or both of your grace days, please note it in your submission while submitting on LEARN. Grace days can not be retroactively re-allocated. 

It is the student’s responsibility that digital submissions are not corrupt partially or fully (i.e., submissions can be opened and viewed by the teaching team). Re-submissions because of corrupt files will be graded as late. It is strongly recommended that after submitting to the LEARN dropbox, open the file on LEARN to ensure that the submission is not corrupt.

Group-work

This course will have students working in small groups in the Strategy Assignment. Thereby, group-work is required. To encourage reasonable fair distribution of work among groups, a group contract, peer-review, and group contribution form (for each submission) will be required. 

No group work will be permitted during midterms and the final exam. 

Protocol for Contesting Graded Assignments

Upon receipt of your graded assignment, should you wish to contest the grading of your assignment (outside of simple errors in the addition of marks) the following protocol should be followed:

  1. Read comments provided thoroughly.
  2. After 24 hours, submit an email to me detailing why your assignment warrants a review. Please be sure to provide specific examples and justification as to why you feel your grade should be reconsidered. Be sure to include a copy of your graded assignment in the email.
  3. I will then review the stated reasons and decide whether re-grading of the full assignment is warranted.
  4. Should I deem that re-grading is warranted, I will then ask you whether you would like your assignment re-graded, recognizing that your grade could go up or down depending on the my re-assessment.
  5. You can choose to either have the entire submission re-assessed or keep the original grade.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. In submitted assignments or parts of the group project, EVERY reference will be checked to ensure it has not been falsified. 

GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
