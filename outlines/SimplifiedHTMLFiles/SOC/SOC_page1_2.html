Course Description

SOC 101:

An introduction to the basic concepts and frames of reference of sociological investigation and interpretation. Topics for analysis will include communities, associations and institutions, classes and status groups, crowds and publics, social processes, and social change. Special attention is given to Canadian society.

View requirements for SOC 101

Learning Outcomes

Learning Outcomes appear within each Module.

By the end of the course, you will be able to distinguish sociology as an academic discipline. You will be able to describe and explain the theoretical and methodological tools sociologists use to examine the social world. We will apply the tricks of the sociological trade – sociological theories, concepts, and methods – to challenge conventional wisdom.

Administrative Policy

Quizzes

Quizzes are ‘open’ allowing students to potentially complete them in advance; however, each week’s quiz will close on Friday at 11:59 PM of that week. Please see the due dates specified in the Course Schedule.

Late Assignments

Assignments are ‘open’, allowing students to potentially complete them in advance. 

Any assignments submitted to the dropbox after the deadline specified in the Course Schedule will be considered late. (Assignment submissions are time-stamped, so there are no inaccuracies.)

To avoid late penalties, students wishing to apply Grace Days to the assignments should indicate the number of grace days on the cover page of the assignment (see "Grace Days Policy" below). 

Once the grace days are used up, assignments will be considered late. Late assignments will result in a penalty of 5% for each 24-hour period.

Grace Days Policy

Each student has a total 3 ‘Grace Days’ they can apply to the assignments. This will allow students to push back an assignment deadline without penalty. Students can apply these 3 days any way they wish (e.g., using 1 Grace Day for Assignment 1 and 2 Grace Days for Assignment 2; using 0 grace days for Assignment 1 and 3 grace days for Assignment 2). 

To avoid late penalties, students wishing to apply their grace days to an an assignment should indicate the number of grace days on the title page of the assignment. 

Once the Grace Days are passed or used up, assignments will be considered late. Late assignments will result in a penalty of 5% for each 24-hour period

*Please note that confusion over submitting to the dropbox is not a valid reason for a late assignment.
