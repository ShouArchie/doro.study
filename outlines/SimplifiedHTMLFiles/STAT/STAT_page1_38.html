Course Description

STAT 435 / STAT 835:

Statistical methods for improving processes based on observational data. Assessment of measurement systems. Strategies for variation reduction. Process monitoring, control, and adjustment. Clue generation techniques for determining the sources of variability. Variation transmission.

STAT 435: View requirements for STAT 435

STAT 835: View requirements for STAT 835

Goals: Reducing the variation in process outputs is a key part of process improvement. Excess variation has dire consequences. It can lead to scrap, rework, the need for added inspection, customer returns, impairment of function and a reduction in reliability and durability. Reducing variation can lower overall cost and simultaneously improve customer satisfaction. This course is structured around a systematic algorithm for reducing process variation and thus improving existing manufacturing and assembly processes.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
understand the Statistical Engineering variation reduction algorithm.
understand the seven generic variation reduction approaches and have some experience in choosing an appropriate approach for a given application.
be able to plan and analyze investigations (such as, stratification, multi-vari, variation transmission, component swap, group comparison, verification) needed in the search for a dominant cause.
be able to plan and analyze investigations (such as, designed experiments, prediction) needed to assess the feasibility of a particular variation reduction approach.
be able to draw appropriate conclusions – graphically and analytically – from data arising from the above-mentioned investigations and be able to present these conclusions to a “non-statistical” audience.
appreciate the sequential (or iterative) nature of process improvement and understand how current process knowledge influences the choice and design of subsequent investigations.

To be successful in this course you will need to spend the time outside of class watching the videos.
