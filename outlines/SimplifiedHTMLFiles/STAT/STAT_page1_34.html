Course Description

STAT 372:

Design and analysis of surveys. Management of sample and non-sample error. Simple random sampling and stratified random sampling. Additional topics in survey sampling. Observational and experimental studies. Principles for the design of experiments. Analysis of variance, factorial experiments, and interaction. Application to problems in business.

Suggested textbooks:

My slides are fairly extensive, and should thus suffice in the majority of cases. Nevertheless, I recognize that it is also useful to have alternate sources of explanations, and below are two suggestions of books. Note that these two textbooks are suggestions/recommendations, they are not required textbooks.

Lohr, Sharon L. (2022), Sampling: Design and Analysis, 3rd edn., CRC Press, Boca Raton, FL. (ISBN: 9780429298899)

  • Though it is at a slightly lower/simpler level than what we will discuss in class, it is an excellent and very readable textbook.
  • A reprint of the 2nd edn. is available online via the UW Library by clicking here (from a UW IP address or using the Get access from anywhere service from the Library).
  • The 3rd edn. is available from Amazon.ca (at ≈$130), from Chapters Indigo (at ≈$130), and from the publisher (in both hardcover and eBook formats).
  • Additional resources (including datasets) can be found on the author’s website; datasets can downloaded here (.csv format as a zip file).
  • There is a companion book, entitled R Companion for Sampling Design and Analysis, which can be downloaded for free (pdf format) here.

 

Särndal, C.-E., Swensson, B. & Wretman, J. (1992), Model Assisted Survey Sampling, Springer, New York, NY. (ISBN: 978-0-387-40620-6; Call #: QA276.6.S37 1992)

  • Carl-Erik Särndal was my master thesis supervisor, and his book greatly influenced my Stat 372 slides and teaching. It is an excellent book, but a harder read than Lohr (2010).
  • It is available from Amazon.ca (at ≈$170) and from Chapters Indigo (at ≈$250).
  • I strongly encourage you to Google the title to see if there are better pricing options.
  • One copy of S¨arndal et. al (1992) is on a 1-day loan at the Davis Library.

 

Kutner, M., Nachtsheim, C.J., Neter, J. & Li, W. (2005), Applied Linear Statistical Models, 5th edn., McGraw-Hill College, Boston MA. (ISBN: 0-07-238688-6; Call # QA278.2.K87 2005)

  • This is a truly excellent textbook, which explains concepts and theory extremely well and nicely. I used it when I was an undergraduate student (a very long time ago), and this was by far the best statistics book I purchased as a student.
  • It is available from Amazon.ca (at $160).
  • I strongly encourage you to Google the title to see if there are better pricing options.

 

Student Assessment

Assignments:

  • The 35% will not be divided equally between the 4 assignments. The weight of each assignment will be proportional to its number of points. For example, if assignments #1 & #3 are on a total of 40 points each, and assignments #2 & #4 are on a total of 60 points each; then, assignments #1 & #3 will each count for 20% of your overall assignment grade, whereas assignments #2 & #4 will each count for 30% of your overall assignment grade. Since assignments are worth 35% of your overall Stat 372 grade, assignments #1 & #3 will each count for 7% of your overall grade, whereas assignments #2 & #4 will each count for 10.5% of your overall grade.
  • You will have (about) 1 week to complete each assignment after it is posted on the Stat 372 LEARN website.
  • You are allowed and encouraged to collaborate with fellow classmates on assignments. However, everyone must hand in their own assignment and show that they understood what they wrote. You are not allowed to collaborate with anyone who is not a fellow Stat 372 classmate; see Avoiding Academic Offenses & Academic Integrity.
  • You will thus have to upload your assignments to Crowdmark. To ensure smooth and efficient grading, please make sure to upload each question individually. I like the additional flexibility provided by Crowdmark; in particular, that students can submit their assignments without being on campus.
  • Assignments submitted late will be penalized. Assignments submitted after the solutions have been posted or after the graded assignments have been returned to students will not be marked and will receive a grade of 0. Note that Crowdmark allows for late submissions.
  • However, I recognize that some of you might be dealing with multiple deadlines on or around the same date. I am thus allowing each one of you a one time 3 hour, 1-day or 2-day extension on an assignment with a reduced lateness penalty. That reduced penalty will be 1% for an additional 3 hours, 5% for an additional 24 hours and 10% for an additional 48 hours. Please know that you have to email me (at least) 6 hours before the original due date to take advantage of the reduced 1-day or 2-day lateness penalty. Since this is meant to be a last minute thing, you do not need to email me in advance to take advantage of the 1% (3 hours) lateness penalty. However, since it counts towards your one-time reduced lateness penalty, you still need to email me to inform me that you want to take advantage of this option.
  • If you are unable to hand in an assignment due to illness (with supporting documentation) or other extenuating circumstances (again with proper documentation), the weight of that assignment will be shifted to your other assignments (proportionally; see first bullet under assignments); no alternate/make-up assignment will be granted in this case. If you do not have proper documentation, you will receive a grade of 0 for that assignment. Since you will have (about) one week to complete any given assignment, I do not expect that illnesses will be much of an issue.
  • In the above bullet, by supporting/proper documentation, I am mostly (but not exclusively) referring to completing a Verification of Illness Form (VIF) or a self-declared short-term absence in Quest (note that COVID-19 now falls under this). Also, make sure to email me as soon as possible. That being said, I recognize that we are all currently experiencing extraordinary circumstances, and I will do my best to be understanding/accommodating.
  • If you are unable to hand in 2 (or more) assignments (due to illness or other extenuating circumstances), the weight of the first missed assignment will be shifted to your other assignments as described in the above bullet point. However, the weight of the other missed assignments will be shifted to the final exam.
  • You have 2 weeks from the day the graded assignment is handed back (via Crowdmark) to appeal your grade.
