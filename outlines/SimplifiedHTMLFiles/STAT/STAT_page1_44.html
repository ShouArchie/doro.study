Course Description

STAT 440:

Introduction to and application of computational methods in statistical inference. Monte Carlo evaluation of statistical procedures, exploration of the likelihood function through graphical and optimization techniques. Topics include expectation-maximization, Bootstrapping, Markov Chain Monte Carlo, and other computationally intensive methods.

Tentative Course Schedule

The following computational inference techniques will be illustrated with several examples.

Maximum likelihood estimation: review of MLE asymptotics and confidence intervals, optimization, profile likelihood, expectation maximization algorithm (approx. 3.5 weeks).

Monte Carlo Methods: rejection sampler, importance sampler, bootstrap, jackknife (approx. 3 weeks).

Monte Carlo Markov Chain: Gibbs sampler, Metropolis-Hastings algorithm, Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (approx. 3 weeks).

Graphical models and Causal inference (approx. 2.5 weeks).

The course grade will be based on four equally weighted assignments (4 X 15% = 60%), and a final project (40%). 

Assignments: Assignments are due by 11:59 pm via Crowdmark on the due date stated on the assignments on Crowdmark. The previous table provides an approximate due dates for the assignments to help you plan your course work accordingly. You can resubmit your assignment as many times as you like before the deadline. It is your responsibility to make sure your solutions are visually clear. After the deadline, you cannot resubmit your assignment. Unless prior arrangements are made, no late assignments will be accepted and a grade of zero will be awarded. Any arrangements for extenuating circumstances are at the discretion of the instructor. Students are encouraged to discuss assignment exercises with each other, the TA, and the course instructor. However, each student must write their own solutions, including code (where relevant) and the solutions should not be copied from any source or from each other.

Final Project: Detailed instructions concerning the project will be provided later in the term. STAT 840/CM 761 students must come up with their own projects. STAT 440 students will be able to pick from prespecified options.
