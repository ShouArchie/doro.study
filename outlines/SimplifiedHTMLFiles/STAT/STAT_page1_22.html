Course Description

STAT 332:

Designing sample surveys. Probability sampling designs. Estimation with elementary designs. Observational and experimental studies. Blocking, randomization, factorial designs. Analysis of variance. Designing for comparison of groups.

View requirements for STAT 332

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Sampling: explain the effect of the finite population correction
Sampling: Design and analyze a stratified random sample
Experimental: Explain randomization, Blocking, and replication
Experimental: Conduct and analyze A/B experiments
Experimental: Explain factorial design
Experimental: Compare and contrast experimental design and observational studies
Experimental: Analyze experimental design using regression

Student Assessment

Component Value
Project on experimental design (incl. peer review) 44%
Midterm 44%
Assignments 10%
Activities supporting in-class analysis (incl. data collection) 2%

Project:  This is a group project with 2-3 group members. The outline contains a due date for project. The project deliverable is an <=10 min video. You will also peer review videos of your fellow students.

Activities supporting in-class analysis: The in-class analysis will require data collection (and possibly other activities). The marks are for contributing to data collection (and possibly other activities). 

Assignments: If you miss assignments for any reason, including "my dog ate the homework", I will shift the weight to the midterm. Likewise, for  VIFs and self-declared absences I will also shift the weight to the midterm.

Missed midterm:  Any missed midterm will result into an oral exam.

Failed midterm+project: If you fail both the midterm and the project, you fail the course. Your grade will be the average between midterm and project.

Assignment Screening

Text matching software (Turnitin) may be used to screen assignments in this course. This is being done to verify that use of all material and sources in assignments is documented. In the first week of the term, details will be provided about the arrangements for the use of Turnitin and alternatives in this course. See Administrative Policy below for more information and links.

ChatGPT/Generative AI: Feel free to use such tools for learning. What you hand in (project / assignment) should be written by you, not by ChatGPT or similar programs. Software that detects whether or not your project is auto-generated essay maybe used.

Administrative Policy

Project: Team members must contribute to the project in broadly equal proportions. All team members must meaningfully contribute to the analysis and to coding.  For video projects, no team member may read a script that another team member wrote. Projects must not use data sets that one of the team members has used for similar projects in previous courses.

Assignments: 

Collaboration: Collaboration with other students on assignments is encouraged. You must write up solutions using your own words and write your own programs. 

Handing-in: Upload scanned assignment to www.crowdmark.com.  (Uploading the assignments in the last seconds/ minutes before the deadline is at your own risk. Please note that if you upload one file for one question before the deadline, you will not be able to upload any additional files to other questions after the deadline.) Assignment are typically due 9 days after they come out.

Late assignments/ project: You lose 2% of the marks on late assignments per hour late.

Solutions: I am no longer able to supply solutions because a minority of students post them on the Internet. If you have questions about the marked assignments, you can ask the TA’s or myself.

Appeals: You have 7 days from the time the assignment/midterm is handed back to you to appeal a midterm or an assignment. (e.g., If you get an assignment back on a Tuesday, I will take appeals until and including the following Tuesday). 

Please send an email to the instructor support coordinator  Divya Lala <divya.lala@uwaterloo.ca> who will coordinate with the TAs.

The email must contain your name, student id, and  Waterloo email address.

Give a clear explanation as to why you think you deserve a different mark. It is helpful if the subject line of the email is “STAT xxx: regrade request A#” where # is the number of the assignment. Note: You can gain or lose marks on assignments. Students are frequently surprised that losing marks is not uncommon.
