Course Description

STAT 371:

Practical and theoretical aspects of simple and multiple linear regression models. Model building, fitting, and assessment. Process thinking and improvement. Strategies for variation reduction such as control charting. Process monitoring, control, and adjustment. Applications to problems in business.

View requirements for STAT 371

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Develop appropriate and meaningful linear regression models to fit to data from business, finance, and other fields for the purpose of answering relevant questions about relationships between the response and explanatory variables in a population or process of interest.
Use the programming language, R, to fit regression models and to explore course concepts.
Interpret regression results from R output, draw conclusions from these results in the context of the study, and effectively communicate these results and conclusions to disparate audiences and interest groups.
Use graphical and numerical analyses to determine whether the fitted model is appropriate with respect to its underlying model assumptions.
Employ model selection methods and model improvement techniques to improve the fit of the model.
Explain the mathematical theory behind the results and output from a regression analysis, including the derivation of the distributions of model parameter estimators.
To understand and apply the statistical concepts, theory and techniques necessary to monitor and assess process performance.

Final Grades: A final grade is a measure of the knowledge and understanding of course material the student has demonstrated on course assessments. Mathematics Faculty INC Grade Policy: A grade of INC is awarded to a student who has completed course work during the term well enough that they could reasonably be expected to earn a passing mark in the course, but who was unable to complete end-of-term course requirements (usually the final exam) for reasons beyond his or her control. See: https://uwaterloo.ca/math/current-undergraduates/regulations-and-procedures/incomplete-procedure for more information on the Faculty of Mathematics' INC policy.

Instructor INC Policy for STAT 371: In the case of a missed final exam with supporting documentation, an INC grade will only be awarded if both the following conditions are satisfied:

  • A minimum (cumulative) average of 50% over all term grading components (assignments/quizzes/midterm)
  • A minimum 50% grade on the midterm (note that the midterm must be written to remain eligible for an INC)

Any student who misses the final exam due to illness and who does not meet the above criteria will receive a failing grade of DNW (Did Not Write, with an associated grade of 32), regardless of the severity of the illness, or whether or not they have medical documentation. Exceptions to this policy will be made at the discretion of the instructor.
