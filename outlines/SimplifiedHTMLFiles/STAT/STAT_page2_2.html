Course Description

STAT 923:

Multivariate normal and Wishart distributions, estimation and hypothesis testing of mean vectors and covariance matrices and their extensions to high-dimensional setting (n<p), principal component analysis (PCA), factor analysis, canonical analysis, discrimination, and classification with a focus on linear discriminant analysis (LDA) and quadratic discriminant analysis (QDA). Precision matrices for high-dimensional data, high dimensional, and sparse PCA, Nonlinear PCA.

ASSIGNMENT POLICIES

The three assignments will be due by 11:59 pm EDT via Crowdmark on February 2nd, March 2nd and March 30th, respectively. You will receive a personal link to submit your assignment via email one week before the due date. 

  • Submissions before the deadline: You can resubmit your assignment as many times as you like before the deadline.  It is your responsibility to make sure your solutions are visually clear (if your answers cannot be read you will receive a grade of zero) and that each question’s answer is uploaded separately into the correct section. Be sure to give yourself ample time to upload your solutions before the deadline.
     
  • Late Assignments: Late Assignments will not be graded and will be awarded a grade of zero. 
     
  • Missed Assignments: Arrangements will be made if you miss an assignment due to a valid, documented, reason. See the section on Accommodation due to illness below. Note for your circumstance to be deemed valid you must have been incapacitated for a significant portion of the 7-day assignment window.
     
  • Retrieving Marked Assignments: A link to your graded assignment will be sent to you via email.
     
  • Rules for Collaboration: Students are encouraged to discuss assignment exercises with each other, the TAs, and the course instructor. Any assistance must be limited to discussion of the problem and sketching general approaches to a solution. Each student must write their own solutions. Consulting another student’s solution is prohibited, and submitted solutions may not be copied from any source. In particular, submitting solutions copied in whole or in part from an assignment submission or solution key from a previous offering of this course, or from an offering of any other course, is forbidden, even if a student is resubmitting their own work. These and any other forms of collaboration on assignments constitute cheating. Suspected cases of cheating will be immediately forwarded to the Associate Dean. If you have any questions about whether some activity constitutes cheating, please ask the instructor.
     
  • Requests for re-grading: You may appeal the grading of an assignment or test by sending an email request to the TA within three days of the date the assessment is returned. You must provide an explanation of why you feel you deserve a different mark. Your grade may go up or down as a result.

 

 

Presentation POLICIES

The presentation will be scheduled at weeks 12 - 13 (tentatively). 

  • Papers of interest for presentations will be provided to you two weeks before your individual presentation date.
     
  • Your presentation date and time will be randomly assigned.
     
  • You have 20 minutes to present and 5 minutes for Q&A (This is subject to change depending on the number of students who will make a presentation).
     
  • Your presentation will be evaluated based on your comprehensive understanding of the paper and the clarity of your presentation.

 

Project POLICIES

  

  • Project reports (based on some real data analysis) need to be typed and submitted to the Dropbox folder at LEARN. More details (e.g., data sets etc.) will be announced by March 15th.
  • Alternative Options for Written Report:
    If you are interested in ideas in journal papers related to the course, you are also allowed to write a
    review paper. Additionally, for students who have original ideas on multivariate analysis, a research
    essay is acceptable as well. Make sure that the paper or the research topic chosen by you is
    approved by me.
  • Grading of the Projects
    General clarity and correctness of writing are required for all three options.
    • For the first option, we will grade your reports on the statement of problem and data, statistical analysis performed, and conclusions.
    • For the second option, we will be more concerned about whether you can make critical comments on the paper you choose to review.
    • For the third option, we will focus on originality of your ideas in your research essay.
     
  • It will be due due by 11:59 pm EDT on April 13th.

Administrative Policy

Changes to in-class Meeting Schedule and other Covid related Circumstances:

It is possible, due to Covid-19, that the university may need to cancel classes for a short period of time and/or transition from in person instruction to an online format during the term (and with short notice). In the event of a university required change, using an appropriate technology platform we will adopt an alternative meeting schedule that mirrors/matches the existing in-person meeting schedule for the course/section as much as possible, . 

Any on-campus in-person assessments that require modification due to a university mandated change connected to in-person gathering will, if necessary, proceed in a manner that best achieves the intentions of the original assessment format. Any necessary changes will be communicated as soon as we reasonably can during the course of the term. 

If you are required to self-isolate as a result of Covid-19 and unable to attend in person classes, the expectation is that all other course obligations can be met except for anything related to direct in-person attendance. In circumstances where your ability to complete course obligations are impacted, you should use the Accommodations due to Illness and related instructions below in order to provide notification of the circumstances to your instructor. 

The university has a self-declaration process that can be followed for anyone who is diagnosed with Covid-19 during the term. This self-declaration is a first step for any necessary course related accommodations for any student experiencing related symptoms. 

 

Accommodation due to Illness: Students self-isolating due to flu-like or COVID-19 symptoms should be completing short-term absence declaration form on Quest. This form allows students to self-declare an absence of 48 hours with no documentation required. Students are limited to submitting once per term, and only during the lecture period.

When you believe your illness (different from COVID-19) or circumstances will impact your academic studies you must follow the instructions on the Faculty of Mathematics' Accommodations webpage. In verified cases, typically the weight of the missed assessment will be moved to the final exam, however the decision is at the discretion of the instructor.
