Instructor & TA (Teaching Assistant) Information

 

Course Description

STAT 936:

This course covers methods for analyzing data in which repeated measures have been obtained for individuals in health studies over time. Different methods will be discussed to handle both continuous and discrete longitudinal response data, with examples from biomedical and population health datasets. Some of the approaches covered will include linear, non-linear, and generalized linear mixed effects models, as well as generalized estimating equations and transition models, with distinctions drawn between subject-specific and population averaged approaches for generalized linear longitudinal response data. Also, there will be coverage of exploratory methods, evaluation of model assumptions and adapting to assumption violations, approaches for handling missing data, and treatment of advanced topics such as semiparametric and nonparametric models for longitudinal data. Software (e.g. R or SAS) will be used throughout the course.

View requirements for STAT 936

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Recognize the added complexity of longitudinal data versus typical univariate response data seen in introductory regression and generalized linear models courses, including the importance of the covariance structure for longitudinal responses.
Learn about techniques for analyzing data that is collected over time.
Learn the difference between subject-specific and population-averaged models.
Learn how to analyze longitudinal data in R.

There will be no required textbook. Content will be delivered primarily through lecture slides, posted on LEARN.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assignments 45% (3 x 15%)
Report 30%
Presentation 25%

Assignments: There will be 3 assignments, each worth 15% of your final grade. They will be a combination of theoretical/analytical problems, data analysis, R coding and/or simulations. They are tentatively due at 5pm EST on the following dates:

  • Assignment 1: 5pm Thursday, February 6, 2025
  • Assignment 2: 5pm Thursday, February 27, 2025
  • Assignment 3: 5pm Thursday, March 20, 2025

Assignments will be released roughly 1.5 weeks before they are due and must be submitted via Crowdmark. You may discuss assignments with other students but you must submit your own unique solution written in your own words, and write who you collaborated with on the front page. Assignments up to 24 hours late will receive a penalty of 50%. Assignments more than 24 hours late will not be graded, and you will receive a zero.

Rules for Collaboration: Students are encouraged to discuss assignment exercises with each other, but any assistance must be limited to discussion of the problem and sketching general approaches to a solution. Each student must write their own solutions, including code (where relevant). Consulting another student’s solution is prohibited, and submitted solutions may not be copied from any source. In particular, submitting solutions copied in whole or in part from an assignment submission or solution key from a previous offering of this course, or from an offering of any other course, is forbidden, even if a student is resubmitting their own work. These and any other forms of collaboration on assignments constitute cheating. Suspected cases of cheating will be immediately forwarded to the Associate Dean. If you have any questions about whether some activity constitutes cheating, please ask the instructor.

Illness and Extenuating Circumstances: If you are unable to complete an assignment due to severe illness or extenuating circumstances, please email the instructor before the deadline. 

Remark Requests: You may appeal the way an assignment was marked by submitting it to the instructor via email within one week of it being returned. Note that remark requests can result in a grade higher or lower than the original. When requesting a remark request, the following information is required:

  • First and Last Name
  • Userid or Student #
  • Course code
  • Assessment Name
  • Question(s) for Remark • Reason(s) for Remark

Final Presentation & Report: There will be a final presentation and a final report. Detailed instruction will be shared later during the term. The final report will be due before the final lecture, and the presentations will take place during lecture time.
