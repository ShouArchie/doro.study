Course Description

AVIA 310:

An exploration of aviation non-technical skills and their impact on aviation safety and operational performance. Components explore workload, management, situational awareness, decision-making, and crew co-ordination.

View requirements for AVIA 310

This course offers an in-depth exploration of key human factors in aviation, focusing on the interaction between human capabilities, limitations, and aviation environments. Emphasizing the roles of psychology, physiology, and ergonomics in pilot performance and safety, students will analyze human limitations and collaborate to address critical issues in aviation human factors.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain the scope and fundamentals of human factors science and describe its relationship to aviation
Understand sensory and cognitive processes
Evaluate the role of aircraft design and automation on human capabilities and limitations
Assess human factors in training, situational awareness
Explore strategies for managing stress, workload and fatigue
Communicate contemporary aviation human factors insights through research and content creation

Participation:  Refers to the active involvement and engagement of students during classroom activities, discussions, and exercises. It encompasses actively participating in class by asking questions, answering questions, sharing opinions, offering insights, and contributing to class discussions and activities.

Test 1 and Test 2: Two tests will be given throughout the semester after we have finished covering course material. The tests will cover all lecture information in the course, including presentations, lectures, videos, etc., up until the test day. Test format may include multiple choice, fill-in-the-blanks, short answer, and/or essay questions.

Make-up sessions will be arranged for students who miss a test for valid reasons.

Human Factors Spotlight: This is a team* assignment. Each team is expected to create  a social media-style video content that explores a current problem, challenge, or opportunity in the aviation or aerospace sector from a human factors perspective. The video should be engaging and concise, analyzing the chosen topic by demonstrating its impact on safety, performance, or efficiency in the industry. In the final week of the course, each team will give a brief introduction to their topic, followed by showing their video. This assignment is to encourage you to think critically and creatively, applying your knowledge of human factors to communicate effectively in a social media format. The objective of teamwork in this assignment is to foster collaboration, improve organization and communication skills, and leverage diverse perspectives to create a comprehensive and impactful analysis of the chosen topic.

  • You are expected to submit a summary of your chosen topic, a plan for content creation, and a description of the roles and responsibilities of each team member. This submission will help ensure that the project is focused and on track. Late submissions will incur a penalty of 1 point for each hour past the due date.
  • You may choose your team as you wish. However, students who have not chosen a team by the deadline will be randomly assigned to a team by the instructor. All members of your team must make substantial contributions to the assignment.
  • The value of this assignment is 35% in total (10 % topic, summary, and plan submission, 20% presentation and video content 5% peer feedback).

Trivia Questions: Each team* is expected to submit questions for the trivia that will be held on week 10 of the course. 

Trivia Wining Team Bonus (3% BONUS): This bonus is for the members of the winning team of the Trivia.

Aviation Community Engagement (2.5% BONUS): To support and promote the aviation community at the University of Waterloo, you are eligible to earn a 2.5% bonus on your grade in AVIA 310 by supporting community- and research-building activities.  Examples of activities that would be eligible for earning the full 2.5% bonus grade would include:

  • Volunteering as a participant in an aviation-applied research project at the University of Waterloo (1 study = 1.25% bonus, 2+ studies = full 2.5% bonus),
  • Volunteering for a University of Waterloo Aviation Society event [1 hour = 0.5% bonus, up to 5 hours = full 2.5% bonus],
  • Volunteering in the local community (such as engaging with the local air cadet squad, volunteering for an event at the Waterloo Wellington Flight Centre, etc.) [1 hours = 0.5% bonus, up to 5 hours = full 2.5% bonus],
  • Other activities (please discuss with your TAs or Professor).

*Each student will be part of only one team. The same teams will participate in both the HF Spotlight assignment and the Trivia.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.

Research Ethics: The University of Waterloo requires all research conducted by its students, staff, and faculty which involves humans as participants to undergo prior ethics review and clearance through the Director, Office of Human Research and Animal Care (Office). The ethics review and clearance processes are intended to ensure that projects comply with the Office’s Guidelines for Research with Human Participants (Guidelines) as well as those of provincial and federal agencies, and that the safety, rights and welfare of participants are adequately protected. The Guidelines inform researchers about ethical issues and procedures which are of concern when conducting research with humans (e.g. confidentiality, risks and benefits, informed consent process, etc.). If the development of your research proposal consists of research that involves humans as participants, the please contact the course instructor for guidance and see: https://uwaterloo.ca/research/office-research-ethics
