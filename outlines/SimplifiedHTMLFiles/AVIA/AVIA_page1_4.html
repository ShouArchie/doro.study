Course Description

AVIA 374:

A course offered in a particular branch of aviation, subject to availability of instructor.

View requirements for AVIA 374

This hybrid course will guide you in your post graduate planning and career preparation. In addition to financial planning, topics will also include: understanding how money works, lifestyle opportunities in aviation, budgeting, professionalism, preparing you for unforeseen life events and so much more. This course is a must for pilots starting out in their career. With insight from industry professionals, you are sure to get real-life, relevant, and valuable information that is applicable for the rest of your career. This course is offered as a hybrid course. The instructor will advise throughout the course which weeks will be in person or online. 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Identify personal financial and lifestyle goals.
Set and monitor financial goals with a budget.
Understand the opportunities within the aviation industry that are in line with your personal and financial goals.
Understand the aviation economy and how it impacts the pilot career.
Plan for unforeseen circumstances and awareness of available resources.

Professionalism (20%): Attendance, professional attitude, participation, and punctuality.

Ice breaker (10%): Submission of class introduction and biggest financial concern or goal. Max 100 words for online submission for submission mark.

Resume, Cover letter, and Job Listing (30%): Submission of a one page pilot resume, one page cover letter, and pilot job listing. Online submission for submission mark. 

Course Feedback (10%): Complete course feedback and submit online for submission mark. Course Feedback due April 4, 2025.

Final Exam (30%): Multiple choice online final exam. 

Proof of Action (2% BONUS): Use the knowledge from the course for real world action. Submit online for bonus marks.

 

Late submissions will not be accepted and will receive a mark of zero, unless an exception has been approved and appropriately documented.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used for assignments in this class with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Recommendations for how to cite GenAI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
