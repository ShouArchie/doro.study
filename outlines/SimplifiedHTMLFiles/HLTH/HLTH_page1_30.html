Course Description

HLTH 608, Health and Risk Communication in Public Health, is an overview of theories, research findings, and applications of health communication and risk communication in public health. The course examines theories of group interaction and mass communication related to community perceptions of public health problems and practices, the impact of new technologies on public health communication, intercultural issues in health communication, health literacy, social marketing, and links between health communication and public health policy.

PLEASE NOTE: If you have not had a course, or equivalent, that addresses health behavior change theories (they are typically presented in depth in courses on health promotion or social, cultural, & behavioural aspects/determinants of health) you will be at a disadvantage and should expect to put in additional time and readings in the initial weeks of the semester. These include the Health Belief Model, Social Cognitive Model, Theory of Reasoned Action/Planned Behaviour, the Transtheoretical Model, and Diffusion of Innovations. We will provide a supplementary module, including recommended reading materials and videos, for students who lack this background or would like a review.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Describe key theories in scholarship about interpersonal and public health communication, including theories from media studies
Demonstrate understanding of health literacy and its relevance to health as well as health communication
Apply change theories to analyze and design health communication campaigns, messages, ad materials
Describe the social marketing planning framework and what its key features are
Design the basics of a social marketing communication plan to promote behavior change
Demonstrate understanding of communication for advocacy by producing an op-ed piece advocating a health- or environment-related policy change
Describe the importance of partnerships and community engagement for successful health communication campaigns
Describe electronic and digital innovations such as use of eHealth and mobile phone-based information technology
Describe pitfalls in risk communication and how to avoid them
Demonstrate understanding of explanatory models and patients’ illness narratives

This course is designed to build MPH students’ core competencies in: strategies for preventing disease and promoting health; core concepts in population and public health; and systems perspectives and critical thinking.
