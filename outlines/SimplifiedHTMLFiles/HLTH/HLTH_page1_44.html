Course Description

HLTH 652:

This course explores qualitative method approaches to program evaluation and public health practice. A critical analysis will inform health policy and practice based on methodological approaches most appropriate to the type of evaluation being conducted. Data collection tools, such as focus groups or interviews will be taught, as well as useful techniques for data analysis, interpretation of results, and presentation of findings. Mixed methods are introduced.

View requirements for HLTH 652

HLTH 652 applies qualitative approaches to program evaluation and public health practice within a broader context of mixed method evaluation studies. The course will address epistemological approaches used in qualitative research and provide useful strategies for data collection, analysis and presentation of findings. Students will have the opportunity to carry out and analyze a short piece of qualitative evaluation research, using critical approaches to inform health policy and practice. At the end of the course, students will appreciate how and when to use qualitative research methods and recognize appropriate designs for different evaluation questions.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Appreciate the value of alternative critical interpretations, varied voices, and stakeholders in evaluation practice
Be familiar with a range of ontological, epistemological, and methodological orientations to mixed and qualitative research and evaluation
Identify evaluation questions that are appropriately answered using qualitative and mixed method paradigms
Describe different qualitative approaches (e.g., thematic analysis, grounded theory, narrative analysis, critical discourse analysis)
Discern data gathering and sources (e.g. interviews, focus groups, field observation, and texts including media and legal documents)
Become acquainted with iterative and inductive analytic approaches
Determine how qualitative data analysis software (e.g. NVivo) organizes data
Consider ethics and responsibilities throughout the qualitative research process
Appreciate participatory approaches to evaluative inquiry and analysis
Assess the credibility and transferability of qualitative evidence
Report and present qualitative research
