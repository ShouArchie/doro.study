COURSE DESCRIPTION 

Health Studies (HLTH) 640 Professional Experience Practicum (1.5) PRA (Course ID: 012544): 

The supervised practicum is intended to provide professional program students with an opportunity to apply course learning in a health or health system setting. The placement may involve any of the activities or functions of students' field of study: public health, health informatics, and health evaluation. 

A contract stipulating practicum objectives, and work to be completed and evaluated to meet these objectives, must be jointly approved by the student, the student's Practicum Placement Supervisor, and the course instructor. A written report by the student, together with products produced during the practicum and interim and final feedback from the placement site supervisor, are used by the course instructor to evaluate student performance. 

This practicum is graded on a Credit/Non-credit basis and is open to School of Public Health Sciences students only. Completion of all prerequisite coursework is required before commencing the practicum. Where this is not possible due to constraints within your practicum placement, you must discuss this with the course instructor and/or practicum coordinator of the challenges and expected submission date. See the Frequently Asked Questions section below for more information. 

Catalogue Description from University of Waterloo (link to UW Catalogue). 

 

Learning Outcomes

The professional experience practicum placement is intended to provide students with an in-depth opportunity to build graduate-level competencies in a health practice setting. This experience is also intended for students to acquire skills and competency training in the practical realities of their professional field of study.
MPH students:
MPH students will build skills and competency training in public health as a broadly defined and general field of study.  MPH placements may involve any of the multiple different activities or functions of public health, such as surveillance, policy development, program evaluation, communications, etc.
MHE students:
MHE students will build skills and competency training in health program and policy evaluation.  MHE placements may involve any type of evaluation, e.g. process, outcome, economic, and/or comprehensive evaluation activities, and involve any of the evaluation competencies that have been taught in the MHE program.
MHI students:
MHI students will build skills and competency training in health informatics.  MHI placements may involve any of the activities or functions of health informatics, such as system analysis, information modeling and interface development, project management, and communications, etc.

Note: 

In rare, exceptional circumstances, students may be permitted to undertake a practicum before their prerequisite coursework is complete.  In these cases, students may request permission to waive remaining pre-requisites by emailing the Practicum Coordinator and Course Instructor with. Please see review the list of example scenarios below. 

If you have only one core course remaining, it is acceptable to complete your final core course concurrently with HLTH-640, or in a term following HLTH-640, particularly if necessitated by scheduling issues.  If this is your situation, you do not require approval and can enroll in HTLH-640 directly, without any further communications. 

If you have three or more core courses remaining, you are ineligible to take HLTH-640 at this time.  Under no circumstances will such requests be approved, and further correspondence requesting such approvals may not receive a response. 

If you have two core courses remaining, you may request to take the practicum if you meet the following conditions: 

  • you are in good academic standing (not in conditional standing);
  • you have a practicum opportunity arranged, that would not be possible in later terms;
  • your practicum opportunity does not rely on material to be learned in the missing core courses; and
  • you have a plan to complete the remaining courses.

In these cases, you may request permission to take the practicum before or concurrent with the remaining core course by emailing the Practicum Coordinator and Course Instructor with:  

  • a list of the specific core courses you would need “waived”;
  • a description of the practicum opportunity, including the timing in relation to your remaining pre-requisite courses;"

It is important to note that not all requests will be granted.

PLACEMENT HOURS & OTHER CONSIDERATIONS:  

Required contact hours: The required number of contact hours required for the MPH / MHE / MHI practicum is 420-hours over a minimum of 12-weeks.  

Other Considerations:  

Unpaid placements:  

If you are completing an unpaid practicum, an Affiliation Agreement must be signed (or already be in place) between the University (Faculty of Health) and the host organization.  As soon as you have a host organization identified for an unpaid placement, email the Experiential Learning and Communication Specialist to begin this process.  Note that it can take days to months for these agreements to be signed, so plan far in advance.   

It is a University of Waterloo requirement that you complete the insurance forms (regardless of other circumstances, e.g., other insurance you may carry). See Forms & Documents section.  

Note that there is no cost to you or your placement organization for this insurance (the forms ensure that you are covered by Waterloo’s insurance so your host organization doesn’t have to cover you). You simply need to complete the forms, including Practicum Placement Supervisor signatures where noted, and submit the completed forms at the same time as your Learning Contract. See Forms & Documents. 

Part-time placements: 

Practicum placements that extend beyond one term (e.g., over two terms, at half-time hours) are considered part-time. Note that if you do a part-time placement, you must remain in ‘active’ status (and thus pay tuition) for both terms. Note that you are also required to follow the course outline (i.e., syllabus) for the term in which you enroll, which is the first term of a two-term practicum. You may not take more than two terms to complete your placement. 

Placements at your place of employment: 

Ideally, the practicum placement activities should be separate from your work (i.e., not your regular job), and your practicum placement supervisor should be different from your regular manager. You do not have to give up your current position at your workplace while completing your practicum (e.g., you may simply assume a new temporary role with a different supervisor for the purposes of your practicum). 

Placements Outside of Canada: 

If you plan an out-of-country practicum placement, there are special international travel safety and security requirements that must be completed prior to approvals to proceed with your international practicum.  

Prior to travel, review the Canadian Government's Travel Advice and Advisories for the specific country/region.  Confirm the advisory level for your destination.  Travel to countries identified as either 'Avoid non-essential travel' or 'Avoid all travel' is not normally allowed.  

Waterloo International requires you to complete the Pre-departure Travel Form.  Once the form has been submitted a member of the Safety Abroad Team will initiate completion of your International Travel Checklist.  Waterloo International will work closely with you to ensure you have completed all of the required steps in order for you to meet your safety and security responsibilities and will inform the practicum coordinator when your International Travel Checklist is complete. 

As part of the Practicum Learning Contract, you must agree to complete the international travel safety and security requirements as outlined by Waterloo International prior to departure.  This includes but is not limited to completion of pre-departure orientation and assessment in LEARN, registration with the travel safety and security provider, and registration with the government of the country of the passport you are travelling on.

ASSESSMENTS & COMPETENCIES 

Assessments: The practicum is graded on a Credit/Non-credit (pass/fail) basis.  To receive 'Credit' (i.e., pass), students must complete the following: 

  1. submit all required documents in a timely manner, and have all documents approved;
  2. receive a recommendation for a 'pass' from your practicum placement supervisor on your Final Evaluation; and
  3. successfully achieve your identified five or more competencies, as evidenced by your two or more submitted products and reflection. 
    MPH students from the 2019 cohort onwards must also:
  4. receive approval for required PebblePad submission(s).

Receiving a grade: At the end of the enrolled term (see Important Dates), all students enrolled in the course will receive an official course grade (i.e., credit) within Quest. Official course grades, credit, and academic standing can be viewed in QUEST at the end of the term. The LEARN course website is not the official location to view official grades.  

Students completing a part-time (i.e., >1 term) placements, or students completing a full-time (i.e., 1-term) placement who did not submit all required documents (including any revisions) by the end-of-term deadlines, will be given an INC (incomplete).  Then, once your placement is complete, and your final documentation approved, we will change your grade accordingly.  

If you have an INC in a course, you must remain in 'active' status for the duration of the INC.  Thus, if this is the last term of your degree, it is particularly important to ensure you meet the end-of-term deadline, to avoid paying tuition next term.  Note that documents that miss the end-of-term deadline will be reviewed in the first 1-2 weeks of the following term. 

Required competency development: Through your practicum placement, you must develop five or more professional competencies specific to your degree, i.e., MPH / MHE / MHE, and demonstrate how you have gained the competencies via submission of two or more products, and an accompanying reflection. 

  • Public Health (MPH) Competencies (see LEARN course website for MPH Competencies).
  • Health Evaluation (MHE) Competencies (see LEARN course website for MHE Competencies).
  • Health Informatics (MHI) Competencies (see LEARN course website for MHI Competencies).

A. LEARNING CONTRACT 

Once you secure your practicum, discuss with your Practicum Placement Supervisor your learning goals (including the competencies you want to attain) and placement opportunities/activities, and begin drafting the Learning Contract. While you may use Learning Contracts from prior terms as templates to help guide your draft (e.g., as available on the LEARN community site), you must use the specific Learning Contract form that is posted in the LEARN course website for the term in which you are enrolled. 

In your Learning Contract, you must identify five (or more) competencies that will be developed during your practicum. Take care to ensure you are using the up-to-date competencies relevant to your program (MPH/MHI/MHE). You must also identify two (or more) products / deliverable that you will submit as evidence that you developed the identified competencies (see Student Final Report section below). All competencies chosen must have a linked product that will be used to demonstrate their development.  

Students should plan to submit their completed Learning Contracts (and other University forms, if needed) approximately 2-4 weeks before the start of their practicum placement. This is to help avoid delays in approvals, and to provide the time for any necessary revisions.

B. PLACEMENT INTERIM EVALUATION 

At the halfway point of your placement, you and your Practicum Placement Supervisor should meet to discuss your progress. Your Practicum Placement Supervisor will need to complete and sign the Interim Evaluation form, which will provide an assessment of your progress to-date.  

This is the time at which you should discuss any current or anticipated challenges for the completion of your practicum. This is also the recommended time to flag any inter-professional issues and discuss with the Practicum Coordinator (cc’ing the Course Instructor), fortunately, though, this is a rare occurrence. 

  • Interim Evaluation word document form (see LEARN course website).
  • Interim Evaluation dropbox (see LEARN course website).

C. STUDENT FINAL REPORT (aka “reflection”) and PRODUCTS/DELIVERABLES  

Student are required to demonstrate how they met five (or more) identified competencies from their Learning Contract, using the Student Final Report document. For each competency, students will briefly describe how they attained the competency, and substantiate their description by citing the relevant parts of the two (or more) products / deliverables from their practicum as evidence of competency attainment. 

Students must submit a minimum of two products / deliverables from their practicum but can submit as many as needed to demonstrate that they met each of the competencies. ‘Products / deliverables’ are items generated for the placement organization during the activities of the placement. A wide range of products / deliverables are acceptable, such as reports, evaluations, analyses, presentations, and many others. Students are encouraged to choose the ‘products / deliverables’ that are specific to their practicum and the work that was performed. If a product / deliverable you generated included many contributors, i.e., a group product / deliverable, submit only the portion that you created (e.g., the draft literature review you wrote for a larger report). If this is not possible, contact the Course Instructor to discuss options. 

  • Student Final Report word document (see LEARN course website).
  • Student Final Report Dropbox (see LEARN course website).

D. PLACEMENT FINAL EVALUATION 

At the end of your placement (but in advance of the end-of-term deadline!), you and your practicum placement supervisor should meet to discuss your achievements. Your practicum placement supervisor will need to complete and sign the Final Evaluation form. Within this form, they will provide feedback about your competency attainment, and will recommend a pass/credit or fail/no-credit on your practicum. In the rare case of a ‘fail’, you will need to meet with the Practicum Coordinator and Course Instructor to develop a plan to achieve the ‘pass’ for the course. 

  • Final Report word document (see LEARN course website).
  • Final Report Dropbox (see LEARN course website).

XI. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES RELATED TO THE PLACEMENT 

Student Responsibilities:  

You are expected to actively engage in finding and negotiating an appropriate practicum to meet your learning and career development needs, and are expected to: 

  • If needed and early on, work with the Practicum Coordinator to explore personal and professional learning needs.
  • Develop a practicum of interest and make contact with the Practicum Placement Supervisor.
  • Work with the Practicum Placement Supervisor to establish a Learning Contract, prior to the start of practicum.
  • Seek ethics or other approvals, as required, for any of the practicum-related activities.
  • Agree to, and sign, the Learning Contract together with the Practicum Placement Supervisor.
  • Conduct activities to meet learning objectives, service expectations activities, deliverables, etc. as set out in the Learning Contract.
  • Identify, if applicable, where the practicum placement is not meeting your learning needs with respect to competency development.
  • Meet professional standards of conduct, including:
    • Respect for the confidentiality of health or other information on individuals that you may encounter as part of your placement;
    • Respect for the confidentiality of agency information;
    • Behave responsibly regarding attendance, work hours, work ethic, and workplace expectations;
    • Actively and in an ongoing manner seek clarity and feedback on expectations, with your Practicum Placement Supervisor; and
    • Show interest in agency activities.
  • Participate in Interim Evaluation and Final Evaluation with the Practicum Placement Supervisor.
  • Identify any issues or areas of concern immediately as they occur, including involving the Practicum Coordinator if needed.

Practicum Placement Supervisor Responsibilities:  

The Practicum Placement Supervisor ensures the necessary practice and learning environment for you, and provides ongoing guidance, support and supervision.  They are expected to: 

  • Identify potential projects, activities, and learning experiences within their organization.
  • Negotiate the details of the Learning Contract with you.  This should include discussing: your educational and practice needs; the competencies you want to develop; their expectations and needs; expected activities and responsibilities; professional conduct expectations; any deliverables; any relevant policies or procedures related to the practicum and the organization; and any stipend or resources available to support your activities.
  • Provide input into the Learning Contract and review it with you prior to signing it.
  • Make necessary arrangements to provide for space, computer, phone, supplies, and any other resources you need to accomplish the work of the practicum.
  • Orient you to the workplace, organizational structure, and functions.
  • Facilitate your access to individuals and work teams, and support opportunities for ongoing activities (e.g., Board and other relevant meetings, field visits, investigations, seminars) and practice experiences.
  • Meet with you regularly (suggested minimum supervisory time is one hour weekly) to review your progress and provide feedback and direction. Ideally this would be in person, but interaction by phone or other electronic means can be used on occasion when it is not feasible to meet in person.
  • Suggest supplementary readings or projects to enhance your experience.
  • Discuss your needs and any concerns with the Practicum Coordinator and Course Instructor, as necessary.
  • Conduct your Interim Evaluation and discuss it with you, including any areas for improvement.
  • Conduct your Final Evaluation and discuss it with you and recommend a pass or fail.

Experiential Learning and Communication Specialist Responsibilities: 

The Practicum Coordinator oversees the administration aspects of the practicum component of the degree, including identifying and disseminating opportunities to you, and supporting you throughout the entire practicum process (from any initial questions you may have early in your degree, to any issues or questions you have through to the end of your placement).  They will: 

  • Assist you, as needed, to identify realistic goals for the practicum.
  • Assist you in identifying potential placements.
  • Answer questions you may have before you enrol.
  • Be the first point of contact for students and be available to address any concerns or difficulties experienced during the practicum.
  • If needed, attend meetings at your request, or the request of the Practicum Placement Supervisor.
  • Review, provide feedback on, and approve required University of Waterloo forms (e.g., insurance forms).
  • Seek student input about the practicum experience (e.g., via an end-of-term survey).

Course Instructor Responsibilities: 

The Course Instructor oversees the academic aspects of your practicum to ensure it meets both your learning needs and the academic requirements of the MPH, MHE, or MHI program.  They will:   

  • Review and approve the Learning Contract, ensuring that it meets academic standards and expectations.
  • Be available to address any concerns or difficulties experienced during the practicum.
  • If needed, attend meetings at the request of the Experiential Learning and Communication Specialist.
  • Review, evaluate, and provide feedback on the submitted Learning Contract, and provide approval once any required revisions are sufficiently addressed.
  • Review and approve the Interim Evaluation, and follow up on any concerns identified (together with the Practicum Coordinator)
  • Review and approve the Final Evaluation, and follow up on any concerns identified (together with the Practicum Coordinator)
  • Assess the Student Final Report and Products, and use these, together with the other types of informal assessments, to assign a final grade.

XIII. COURSE & UNIVERSITY POLICIES  

Submission Times: Please be aware that the University of Waterloo is located in the Eastern Time Zone (GMT or UTC-5 during standard time and UTC-4 during daylight saving time) and, as such, the time that your activities and/or assignments are due is based on this zone. Late Submissions:  

If you are late submitting your Learning Contract (and any additional required forms, e.g., insurance forms for unpaid practicums), your practicum start date (and potentially the end date) will be delayed. 

If you are late submitting your Interim Evaluation, you and your Practicum Placement Supervisor will receive an email from the Practicum Coordinator and/or Course Instructor as a reminder of the importance of submitting the form in a timely manner. 

If you are late submitting your Student Final Report, Products, or Final Evaluation, you will be given a grade of INC (incomplete) for the course and may be required to enrol as an active student (and thus pay tuition) in the subsequent term until your final documentation is submitted and approved.

Students' submissions are stored on a U.S. server, therefore students must be given an alternative (e.g., scaffolded assignment or annotated bibliography), if they are concerned about their privacy and/or security. Students will be given due notice, in the first week of the term and/or at the time assignment details are provided, about arrangements and alternatives for the use of Turnitin in this course.

It is the responsibility of the student to notify the instructor if they, in the first week of term or at the time assignment details are provided, wish to submit alternate assignment.
