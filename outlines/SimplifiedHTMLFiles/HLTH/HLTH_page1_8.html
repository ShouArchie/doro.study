Course Description

HLTH 341:

An introduction to the study of biological factors governing disease in humans that will use selected diseases to identify risk factors and illustrate pathogenic mechanisms. The role that behaviour has in modifying biological response to disease may also be considered.

View requirements for HLTH 341

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Evaluate the etiology of selected pathophysiological conditions and explain whether it is determined by internal human-host factors, external environmental factors or a combination of factors.
Differentiate between modifiable versus non-modifiable risk factors that contribute to the onset illness and progression of disease of various pathophysiological conditions.
Explain and describe key pathophysiological aberrations that occur during the onset of illness, the continued impairment of health early and the process of disease progression.
Determine opportunities for public health intervention at multiple stages of disease progression.

Course Design

Our HLTH-341 course for Winter 2025 will be a flipped course. All lectures will be pre-posted as power-point slides with accompanying screencasts (i.e., videos). The power-point slides and videos will cover important content and follow along with the posted textbook readings. With our flipped course design, it is expected that students will cover course content in the posted slides and videos independently and on their own during our Monday at 8:30am class time. Student in-person attendance is not required. I will be fully available to all of you during that time. During our Wednesday at 8:30am class time together, we will meet on-campus and in-person all together to go over the content, review major concepts, work through questions, and students can apply understanding through case studies. For our Wednesday class time together, some additional, non-textbook, readings may be posted as interesting articles or articles for some discussion. Other resources may also be provided throughout the term to facilitate student learning. The in-person, on-campus, class time will not be recorded. Students that are unable to attend class can utilize the power-point slides, textbook and other readings, as well as course website, and their peers to supplement anything that was missed. This course design is intended to encourage and promote student engagement and active learning with the goal to move students away from memorization. 

Participation and Engagement 

Participation points will be based on your attendance and participation in our clicker or poll questions during our class time. There will be many opportunities to earn these points. The poll questions will be content-based multiple choice, or other short-response, questions. Students are not required to answer the question correctly since points are awarded simply for attending, participating, and attempting the question. If you miss a class or miss answering questions, that is okay as there is no penalty. You are only required to attend and participate in nine of the twelve Wednesday classes to receive full points. 

Concept Quizzes  

Students will be assessed on their knowledge and comprehension of course content through a series of concept quizzes. The content covered on each Quiz will be approximately 1 to 2 Modules of course content. Questions will focus on the content covered in the Lectures and Module content with content from the readings designed to supplement student understanding. The online quizzes will be administered through the course website. Students are expected to abide by the honor system to complete these quizzes independently and without any resources. The purpose of these quizzes is for students to ‘self-check’ their understanding of the material that was covered during the class sessions. Questions will predominantly be in the format of Multiple-Choice, or other Quick answer questions. There will not be any written or essay questions. Quizzes will open on the date specified in the course schedule and remain open for 48-hours with due dates at the end of the week on Friday by 11:59pm. Students can complete the quiz at any point during that time-period.

Marks and Grades

Questions or concerns regarding marks for any component of the course, must be addressed with your TA within 7-days of the posted mark. After that time, assignment re-marking is not permitted. When requesting re-marking, students are required to clearly outline and explain their request in writing with rationale and support for the request. It is not sufficient to simply say: “I feel I deserve the marks”. Provide supportive evidence of where and why marks were erroneously deducted.

Grades are based on the University of Waterloo Grading Scale. All final marks are automatically rounded-up by LEARN to the next highest point (i.e. 69.5% to 70%).

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot in teaching, learning, and education is still evolving and changing. In our class, we will discuss the expectations surrounding gen-AI.

Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

Students are encouraged to reach out to campus supports if they need help with their coursework including: 

  • Student Success Office for help with skills like notetaking and time management
  • Writing and Communication Centre for assignments with writing or presentations
  • AccessAbility Services for documented accommodations
  • Library for research-based assignments
