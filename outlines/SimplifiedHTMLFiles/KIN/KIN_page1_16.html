Course Description

KIN 221L:

This laboratory course provides students with advanced practical tools used in the biomechanical assessment of human movement. The labs have been designed to assist with students' knowledge and understanding of material discussed in the KIN221 lectures.

View requirements for KIN 221L

Laboratory Instructions: 

KIN 221L lab instructions will be available on-line via LEARN. Each lab will have multiple resources to help you prepare and complete the lab. Prelab talks, will help orient you to the lab, how to videos are available to help you with calibrating and using equipment. Data will be collected and analyzed via MATLAB in the same manner done in KIN 121L. Read the lab material before you attend a lab. It is your responsibility to come to lab prepared. You are unlikely to complete a lab should you not come prepared, and your marks will reflect this. Please print the required material before attending labs or bring a device to lab to access the documents.

The laboratory exercises in KIN 221L are designed to give you advanced experience in the measurement and modelling techniques used in the field of biomechanics. The labs supplement the lectures and will provide a means for you to apply your knowledge in a practical manner. They will help you to identify the benefits and limitations of the experimental approaches used and provide you with a greater understanding of the topics covered in lecture. The materials covered in the labs represent experimental methods that are routinely performed in both clinical and research settings. The labs will therefore equip you with the knowledge and skills necessary to work with these techniques after you have graduated from Kinesiology and Health Sciences at the University of Waterloo. 

Each lab will contain a set of objectives. You will be expected to meet these objectives and will be tested on them throughout the course. The labs will use knowledge and skills gained from KIN 121 and KIN 121L. Further, the labs in this course will build on each other so you are expected to use the knowledge gained from prior labs to perform subsequent labs. 

The labs will cover topics such as:

  • EMG collection, processing, and analysis
  • Joint modelling concepts, muscle mechanics, force-length / force-velocity relationships
  • Dynamic versus static task analysis, including image analysis and multi-segment rigid link modelling
  • Problem solving skills

The labs are designed to give you experience with the equipment and the signals you are measuring. You will make mistakes, and this is ok. It is part of the learning process and will ultimately help you gain a greater understanding of the topic at hand. 

We are very fortunate to have access to the equipment and measurement techniques used in these labs. Many programs have limited staff and equipment for such purposes. Embrace this opportunity, as it is a unique one that will put you ahead of your peers at other institutions.

Lab Schedule:

 

In-Person Labs:

In-person labs are 2 hours in duration, on alternate weeks, and begin the week of Jan 13th, 2025.  All in-person labs are held in EXP 3689. The first lab consists of a mandatory review / introduction of equipment, calibration, data collection software, etc. Labs begin the second week of classes.

  • Lab # 1 consists of a mandatory review / introduction of equipment, calibration, data collection software, etc. and a short lab report assignment to be handed in for grading via the drop boxes on LEARN.

 

  • Labs 2, 3, 4, 5 will consist of experimental data collection and assignments to be handed in for grading via the drop boxes on LEARN.

 

  • All lab assignments are due 1 week after your assigned lab time by 11 PM. For example, you have a lab on Tuesday, so your lab report is due the following Tuesday by 11 PM.  All labs must be handed-in to the correct dropbox on LEARN, be in PDF format and consist of 1 complete file.

 

  • Lab attendance is mandatory. You must attend a lab to hand in an assignment. The only exception is with the expressed written permission of the lab instructor. You cannot use other student’s/group’s data to create your lab report. Any data presented in the lab videos on LEARN are for demonstration purposes only and cannot be used for your assignment without written permission from the lab instructor. If you fail to attend more that 20% of the labs for undocumented reasons, you will not be awarded credit for the course.

 

  • Punctuality is also very important.  If you arrive more than 15 minutes after lab has begun, you will not be permitted to stay for the lab. If your late arrival is due to unforeseen circumstances, please speak to the lab instructor.  You may be asked to attend another lab section.  If you do arrive late without a valid explanation, you will not be able to attend lab and will receive a mark of 0% for that assignment.

 

  • All Practical Lab Exams will take place the week of Mar 31, 2025 during your regular lab time no matter which week your scheduled lab takes place. If you have a conflict with the Lab Exam time, you must let the lab instructor know as soon as possible, no accommodations are guaranteed during these exams.

 

The Practical Lab Exam will test the set-up, collection, processing, and analysis skills learned in the lab. The practical exam will be 10 minutes in duration. You will enter the lab and be asked to proceed to 1 of 5 stations, where you will perform the activities described by the examiner at that station. You will be graded on your activities and will be asked to provide oral responses to questions. Concurrent examinations will take place in each lab section, so please refer to the PDF document posted on LEARN towards the end of term for more details regarding the scheduling of the practical exam. Failure to attend your assigned Practical Exam time will result in a grade of zero on the exam. If you are ill during your exam time, follow the producers outline below, you may be required to complete the Lab Exam the next time the course is offered.

Laboratory Work:

It is very important that you attend the lab section in which you are registered. If you are not able to attend your lab section, you are asked to contact the head lab instructor in advance and provide a VIF or other appropriate documentation.  Failure to provide appropriate documentation will result in a mark of zero for the lab. All laboratory assignments are due 1 week after you attend the lab, by 11:00 PM, and are to be handed-in to the corresponding dropbox on LEARN. All work submitted after that time will be deducted 20% per day with no exceptions, except as indicated below under the Illness and Documentation sections.

We understand that the laboratory data is collected and processed in a group setting and we encourage group discussions on laboratory topics. That being said, your laboratory write-ups should represent independent work. We will not tolerate students copying whole or part of others’ laboratory assignments. If you are working with classmates on the laboratory assignments, make sure that the work you hand in is independent and represents YOUR work only. You may work with your peers to seek out answers (e.g., finding websites, or text references). However, the creation of graphs/tables, written answers and all other submitted work associated with the lab report are to be completed by the individual. 

Handing in the work of others represents cheating and is a punishable academic offense. Data may be presented in the lab videos for demonstration purposes only, and you cannot hand in a lab report using this data without the written consent of the lab instructor.  Your lab reports must use the data you collected in the lab. Only your own group’s data is acceptable to use in your lab report. You will be reported to the Associate Dean of Undergraduate Studies for disciplinary action should you fail to adhere to the lab policies above.

 

Accidents and Injury in the Lab: 

The risk of injury while performing these lab activities is extremely small. However, if an accident does occur, it and any injury or safety concerns must be reported to the course Instructor that same day. These will be reported to the course instructor and will be acted on accordingly. If you feel that our actions do not satisfactorily address your safety concerns, you should report them to the Associate Chair for Undergraduate Studies (Dr. Chris Vigna) or the Undergraduate Program Manager (Lee Chandler).

Administrative Policy

Alternate Arrangements if In-Person Activities are Suspended:

It is possible that an interruption to in-person activities may occur this term based on public health guidelines and/or a decision by the University to suspend in-person activities. Below are the contingency plans that will be implemented in KIN 221L should an interruption occur:

 

  • A short-term (e.g., one-week) cancellation of in-person classes, whether for the course or University-wide: Lab material will be provided online via LEARN.

 

  • A longer-term cancellation of in-person meetings, whether for the course or University-wide: Lab material will be provided online via LEARN.

 

  • Cancellation of in-person examinations, whether for the course or University-wide: Examinations will occur online.

 

  • Self-isolation of a student: An accommodation plan will be made on a case-by-case basis and may include re-weighting of course components, a change of lab attendance date, or other options based on the length of absence from on-campus activities.

Academic Regulations and Accommodations

In addition to illness, there may be events that preclude students from meeting academic obligations. The following are examples of valid circumstances and required documentation that may warrant academic accommodation:

 

  • A member of a varsity team travelling for competition: a letter/email from the Head Coach verifying the departure and return dates and times.
  • Death of a family member: a copy of the death certificate, a copy of the obituary or a link to the online obituary.
  • Travel: from time-to-time students are required to travel during an academic term for reasons NOT related to pleasure/vacation. If you are travelling and require accommodation, you must inform the instructor at least 2 weeks in advance of your travel, indicate the reason for travel and provide a boarding pass upon return to campus.

Important note: Travel that results in arrival to campus after the first day of class, absence before or after a reading break or departure from campus during the final exam period DO NOT warrant accommodation and any missed academic obligations will receive a grade of zero.

 

Co-op Interviews

Academic obligations take priority over co-op interviews. Whenever possible students are encouraged to swap interview times to avoid conflicts with academic obligations. For details and procedures on what to do if you have an interview conflict, please see the Interview Conflict page on UW’s website.

 

If you are not able to resolve your interview conflict with a lab or tutorial, you may request to swap lab/tutorial sections for only the day/week in question. Please provide your instructor a screenshot from WaterlooWorks such that your name and the date and time of the scheduled interview are visible. If the interview was scheduled outside of WaterlooWorks, a letter/email from the prospective employer with the details outlined above will suffice. 

 

If you're feeling overwhelmed with your interview schedule, you can contact your co-op advisor to discuss available options and/or fill out a Request for Interview Relief Form on WaterlooWorks. Depending on your situation, you may be able to choose to be removed from one or more of your upcoming interviews by having your job application(s) withdrawn.

 

Illness and Documentation 

From time to time, students will encounter extenuating circumstances such as significant illnesses, ongoing medical conditions, or religious observations that prevent them from meeting academic obligations. The University is committed to assisting students who experience these events.

COVID-19 Related Absence

Students must complete the illness/isolation declaration if they are seeking consideration from an instructor for missed course requirements due to COVID-19 or self-isolation. This is a self-declaration available on Quest and is to be completed only for an absence related to an COVID-19 related illness or an absence as a result of a requirement to self-isolate. The number of self-declared COVID-19-related absences is limited to one per student per academic term. Absences resulting from other conditions or longer than 14 days must be verified using the Verification of Illness form (see below).

 

Self-declaration of short-term absence (2 days)

Students cannot use their short-term self-declared absence for accommodations related to classes scheduled with a component of Laboratory (LAB) or Clinic (CLN). For more information please visit the self-declaration of short-term absence website.

 

Verification of illness form (VIF)

For other illnesses or health conditions, students are required to provide their instructors a Verification of Illness form. 

Regardless of the nature of the absence or the form submitted, it is the student’s responsibility to notify their instructor(s) of their absence within 48 hours of a deadline/due date. Failure to do so will result in late penalties or an accommodation may not be permitted. Adjustment of due dates or deferrals of academic course elements are not automatic upon the self-declaration of illness or short-term absence.
