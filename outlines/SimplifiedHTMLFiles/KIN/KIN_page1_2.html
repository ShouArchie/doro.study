Course Description

KIN 100:

Regional anatomy of the limbs and trunk, including a brief introduction to the anatomy of the central nervous and cardiovascular systems. The course focuses on the bones, muscles, and neurovasculature of the limbs and trunk.

View requirements for KIN 100

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Identify bony landmarks of the limbs and vertebral column.
Identify attachment sites and nerve supply for muscles of the limbs and trunk.
Understand the actions produced by muscles as they contract and shorten.
Trace the path of blood from the heart to the upper limb and back.
Trace the path of blood from the heart to the lower limb and back.
Draw and label the brachial plexus.

In Class Questions (ICQs)

Participating in the ICQ component requires you to purchase the Top Hat course materials and participate by being present in class.

ICQs will be used during lectures for the following purposes:

  • As a way to practice the process of “retrieval practice”
  • To identify concepts that might need additional review by the instructor
  • To assess knowledge gained in “flipped classroom” activities
  • To encourage students to attend classes

I will provide 3-5 questions per class. There are approximately 30 lectures in the term so this should provide you with more than 120 ICQs. Students will earn 1 mark for each response given and an additional 0.5 marks for correct responses. Therefor, each ICQ is worth 1.5 marks. 

If you miss a class you will receive zero for that set of questions. It is not possible to submit ICQ responses using paper. 

Please note that responding on behalf of another student (i.e. bringing an extra device to class with you) is considered an academic offense and will be reported to the Associate Dean of Undergraduate Studies.
