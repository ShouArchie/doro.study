Course Description

KIN 351:

This course will develop theoretical knowledge and practical skills in the rehabilitation of orthopedic injuries. The course will build upon knowledge related to orthopedic injuries gained in core and elective courses. The focus will be on a population that was active and healthy prior to injury.

View requirements for KIN 351

This course is designed to develop theoretical knowledge and practical skills related to rehabilitation of orthopedic conditions and injuries. Lecture material will be comprised of recorded lectures in LEARN. The lectures are based upon several different sources, including the recommended text. I will provide narrated lecture slides in LEARN in video form for downloading and/or viewing along with ppt slides of the video lecture. It is expected that you view/make notes on said lectures and slides in advance of the in person labs each week. Evaluations will take place during our designated lecture time so please do not make other arrangements or commitments during this time for other courses, jobs, etc. as we will not accommodate you for those circumstances. 

During lab periods, at which attendance is 100% mandatory, you will complete practical skills. Due to the number of students and the allotted space, there is no switching of lab sections. Please note that you cannot use STA for labs per UW policy. 

LEARN is arranged in modules by topic/by week. I will make the modules available in LEARN later in the week for the upcoming week so you have time to prepare for lab. Please refer to the schedule above for a general timeline, which is subject to minor changes as we proceed. There are some hard deadlines to follow, such as the quiz dates and test dates, that will remain static.    

You will be added to the 351 MS Team automatically by IST which will allow you to join any potential synchronous sessions, should they be necessary as a contingency, during the term.  

Concerns with marking or questions about tests, assignments and practicals must be addressed with your instructor within 1 week of the mark posting in LEARN. No further review is permitted after that time.    

* It is assumed that students are familiar with the pertinent anatomy and orthopedic conditions. There will be a very brief review of each area, but anyone NOT familiar with the anatomy, at the level it is being discussed, is strongly encouraged to consult an anatomy textbook, review course notes or the course text. Anatomy is CRITICAL in this course. A base level of knowledge and skills related to exercise instruction and prescription is also assumed from KIN 204. Also note that we will be covering the management of conditions covered in KIN 340 so it is expected that you are familiar with those injuries. If not please refer to previous notes and slides from KIN 340.  

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. Understand the evidence informed practices and techniques associated with rehabilitating musculoskeletal (MSK) injuries
2. Become familiar with the technologies/modalities/techniques employed to aid in the rehabilitation of musculoskeletal injuries
3. Comprehend and apply theoretical concepts to practical scenarios related to the rehabilitation of MSK injuries
4. Practice manual therapy skills and techniques for injury rehabilitation
5. Appreciate the natural healing process of humans post injury
6. Confidently plan and implement a rehabilitation program for a client with any orthopedic injury

Evaluation Details

Submissions are individual, not group efforts, unless specifically indicated in the description of the submission- e.g. Submission #5. Submissions will be outlined and communicated in lab. Please submit well in advance of the deadline.

Quizzes and tests will be completed in LEARN, during lab and lecture time, in person, so please ensure your electronic device- laptop, tablet, phone, is updated, powered, connected to Eduroam, and ready to go for each session and test. They are 7 question quizzes on the content covered in LEARN each week including lectures, videos and readings, to name a few. You must be in person to complete each quiz. These should be at the same time/date as all others in the class per the schedule. You must have a device to complete the quiz on during the lab or lecture time slot. Failure to bring a device will result in a grade of 0 for that evaluation. 

Tests will potentially consist of the following question types:

Illness and Documentation 

From time to time, students will encounter extenuating circumstances such as significant illnesses, ongoing medical conditions, or religious observations that prevent them from meeting academic obligations. The University is committed to assisting students who experience these events.

Student Absences – Accommodation for illness or other extenuating circumstances

  1. Short-term Absence - a self-declared short-term absence will excuse students from their academic responsibilities for up to 2 consecutive calendar days during the formal lecture period. Students may choose to participate in some course activities that fall during the short-term absence, and still record an absence for other activity in that period. During the 2-day academic consideration period, the instructor cannot require completion of any of the missed academic responsibilities. Students must contact the instructor no later than 24 hours after the missed assessment(s). Self-declared short-term absences will not be accepted for the course/class components of Clinic (CLN), Laboratory (LAB), and Studio (STU). No accompanying documentation is requested or required. Only one short-term absence can be self-declared per academic term. Thereafter, the student will be required to submit a University of Waterloo Verification of Illness Form (VIF) or register for academic accommodations with AccessAbility Services (depending on the nature of the reasons for the absence).  Undergraduate students only.
  2. COVID-19-related Absence - an absence of 10 days or less due to COVID-19 related illness (including vaccination side-effects) or a requirement to self-isolate. Participation in online course elements may be reasonable. Students can self-declare one COVID-19-related absence in an academic term.
  3. For absences that do not meet the short-term absence or COVID-19 criteria, for self-declared absences that exceed the maximum number permitted per term, or for (retroactive) reports of illness, students are required to submit the Verification of Illness Form. A high-quality photo of documentation should be uploaded to the Verification of Illness or Extenuating Circumstances Intake Form.
  4. Some students may seek academic accommodations on religious grounds. Students must consult with their instructor(s) within two weeks of the announcement of the due date for which accommodation is being sought.
  5. If the student’s absence is due to extenuating circumstances, they should use the VIF intake form via https://vif.uwaterloo.ca. Students will be required to upload a PDF or high-quality photo of their documentation to the intake form.

All faculties are using the Verification of Illness or Extenuating Circumstances Intake Form through https://vif.uwaterloo.ca. Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as manual therapy skills and exercise prescription Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
