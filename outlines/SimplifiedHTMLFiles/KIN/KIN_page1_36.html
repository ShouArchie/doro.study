Course Description

KIN 356:

This course will examine how different regions of the nervous system interact to shape our perception, decisions, memories, movements, and consciousness. The focus will be placed on the role of the sensory systems in transducing external stimuli into neural activity, how sensory information is processed within and across sensory modalities (i.e., multisensory integration), and the implications for nervous system function. Topics will be considered from developmental, injury, disease, and aging perspectives.

View requirements for KIN 356

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
To be able to describe each of the sensory systems in terms of classification and mechanistic function
To distinguish between sensation and perception and explain how these processes affect behaviour
To identify which neural structures are associated with different stages of sensory information processing
To apply the basic principles of classical psychophysical techniques to measure sensory thresholds
To compare and contrast principles, theories and methods as they pertain to sensory systems neuroscience
To explain different evidences for multisensory integration and how they relate to different neural areas
To show how sensory information processing can be influenced by attention and past experiences
To become familiar with how sensory information processing shapes our decisions and actions
To better understand changes in neural function from a sensory information processing perspective
