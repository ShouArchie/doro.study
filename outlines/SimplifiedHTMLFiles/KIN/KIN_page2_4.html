Course Description

KIN 630:

A course focused on the core principles of research design (populations, sampling, experimental designs) and statistical applications (descriptive and inferential) specific to Kinesiology and its sub-disciplines.

View requirements for KIN 630

A course focused on the core principles of research design (populations, sampling, experimental designs) and statistical applications (descriptive and inferential) specific to Kinesiology and its sub-disciplines.

 

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. Identify the appropriate research design for a given research question.
2. Describe the strengths and weaknesses of various research designs and identify potential threats to internal and external validity
3. Describe the theoretical basis and assumptions of commonly used analytical approaches
4. Apply the appropriate analytical approach for a given research question and set of data
5. Employ the statistical package R to conduct data analyses and interpret the resulting statistical output

R Statistical Software Package:

We will be using the R statistical package throughout the course to organize, display, and analyze data. R is a free statistical package available for download from The R Foundation. All assignments must be completed using R. The final exam will also require students to use R output to answer questions. For the final exam, students will be given R output; students WILL NOT be required to do any programming for the final exam.

Assignments

The assignments and R programming are intended to supplement the lecture material by providing students with the opportunity to apply course concepts in a critical manner.

To provide all students with an equal footing, students will be provided with supplemental video tutorials covering R's basics and the relevant functions required for each assignment (through the course site on LEARN). Students are expected to review the supplemental materials to complete the assignments.

All assignments will be posted using the Dropbox on the LEARN course site. Students will submit all assignments via the Dropbox using the .R file template included in the assignment Dropbox. Assignments are to be submitted by 4 pm on the date indicated. Late assignments will be penalized 20% per day starting at 4:01 pm on the day the assignment is due. 

Final Exam

The final exam will consist of two parts. Part 1 will assess your understanding of fundamental statistical concepts. Part 2 will assess your ability your critical thought ability and the ability to interpret results to answer research questions.

Part 1 of the final exam will occur on Tuesday, April 1st. Part 2 of the final exam will take place on Thursday, April 3rd.  Both parts of the exam will be closed book.

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods is a useful tool for troubleshooting R programming code. 

GenAI may be used for assignments in this class. However, students must use GenAI in the context of the assignment (e.g. use any specific R programming packages required in the assignment). Students are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work they submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI. 

Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may, therefore, contain inaccuracies or reflect biases.
