Course Description

KIN 320:

Task analysis describes methodologies that can be applied to identify, describe, and analyze how people interact with their environment and with each other. Task analysis includes a broad range of descriptive and analytic techniques from which the practitioner must select the most appropriate one for their purposes and setting. This skills-based course will guide students in learning task analysis methodologies and associated techniques using a combination of didactic, case-study, and experiential learning-based teaching methods. The application of task analysis from the perspective of a Human Factors & Ergonomics (HFE) specialist will be emphasized.

View requirements for KIN 320

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. Identify practical and ethical issues that emerge when applying task analysis
2. Describe different task analysis methodologies
3. Choose task analysis methodologies appropriate for a given setting and purpose
4. Perform a range of task analysis methods

Administrative Policy

Late Policy

A penalty of 1% per hour late will be applied up to a maximum of 48 hours. After 48 hours a grade of 0 will be applied.

Self-declaration of short-term absence

The Registrar's Office is piloting a short-term accommodation process. Students may experience short-term absences for several reasons for which they cannot participate or complete their academic responsibilities. For any assessments during the formal lecture period, students may self-declare their short-term absence within Quest using the Self-Declaration of Absence Form. Students will be permitted to self-declare short-term absence once per academic term, during the formal lecture period. A self-declared short-term absence will excuse students for up to 48 hours. The timing of which does not start when you submit your form. For example, if you declare a Tuesday as your first day absent, the Wednesday would be your second day and you would be expected to return to your studies on the Thursday (two full days absent; Tuesday and Wednesday). You are permitted to attend lectures, labs, tutorials, etc. However, you are not permitted to submit any assignments/assessments. For more information please visit the self-declaration of short-term absence website.
