Course Description

KIN 232:

An introduction to research design, statistical analysis, and their application to kinesiology and clinical health practice. A primary focus is to provide students with the tools to critically evaluate sources of information pertinent to their chosen profession.

View requirements for KIN 232

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
To demonstrate competency in using Microsoft Excel and other data analysis software
To appraise data by organizing and analyzing data using descriptive statistics
To develop critical thinking and analysis skills to interpret research
To explain the scientific method
To describe various types of research designs, their strengths and weaknesses
To determine the appropriate statistical test to use with a research design
To be able to analyze and interpret statistical outcomes, including:
Confidence intervals
Analyses of differences (t-tests, ANOVA) with independent groups and repeated observations
Analyses of association (correlation, regression)
Non-parametric analyses

Tutorials

The tutorial is a required part of the course. Students must be on time and present for their scheduled tutorial sections to earn the grades associated with the tutorial assignment. Tutorials are your opportunity to interact with the course material and gain practice at developing your research design, statistical analysis and critical analysis skills! They will consist of a variety of activities including group work, practice questions, data manipulation in Excel and calculations. 

Missing a tutorial: If you know in advance that you have a valid scheduling conflict with your tutorial (e.g., co-op interview), you may request to swap into an alternate tutorial section for that week. Please follow this link https://forms.office.com/r/PUqppjnHdq and complete the Microsoft Form. If you miss a tutorial due to illness (as outlined below), please email Siyavash within 48 hours of missing your assigned tutorial section. 

The tutorials are designed to encourage a cooperative and collaborative learning environment. At times, students are encouraged to work with their peers within the tutorial section to seek and compare answers and assist in developing answers/components to questions. However, each student is required to independently create and submit their own assignment. Some of the tutorial assignments are completed within the tutorial itself and are to be submitted by the end of the tutorial session. 

Late submissions: Please note that late submissions of assignments are subject to a 20% deduction per 24-hour period in which they are late. Please also note that dropboxes within LEARN can lag when multiple submissions are occurring at the same time (which tends to happen when many submissions occur near the deadline). Therefore, if a student submits an assignment at 10:47 pm but the assignment does not appear in the dropbox until 11:03 pm, then the assignment is considered late. Moral of the story – submit early! Multiple submissions to the dropbox are allowed, and the most recent submission is what the instructional team will mark.

Generative AI

The use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence

Absences in Quest

Religious or creed-related absence

Undergraduate students can now submit a Religious Observance Self-Declaration in Quest. This allows students to register the dates of potential conflict with coursework. As the dates of important religious observances are generally known well in advance, students must consult with their instructors within two weeks of the announcement of the due date or scheduled examination date for which academic accommodation is being sought. 

Short-term absence (once/term)

Students can self-declare one short-term absence per term on Quest for any reason (no documentation required). A short-term absence covers up to two calendar days during the formal lecture period. Laboratory (LAB), Clinic (CLN), and Studio (STU) courses/class components do not qualify for accommodation as a result of a self-declared short-term absence. There is no expectation that a self-declared short-term absence be total; that is, students may elect to participate in any course and complete any course element(s) despite not being required to. It is the student’s responsibility to contact their instructors within the first 24 hours after submitting their self-declaration to discuss accommodations for missed course components.

COVID-19-related absence (once/term)

Students can self-declare an absence of up to 10 days due to COVID-19-related illness. No in-person course activity is permitted during the period of a self-declared COVID absence. Participation in online components is permitted but should not be required if the student notifies the instructor that they are too ill to participate in the online work. Submitting the self-declaration through Quest will notify your instructors of your absence. It is the student’s responsibility to contact their instructors within the first 48 hours after submitting their COVID-related absence, or as soon as they are well enough, to discuss arrangements for missed course components.

Absence due to illness  

When students are unable to meet academic obligations due to illness, they are required to complete the Verification of Illness or Extenuating Circumstances web form (log in required) and upload their Verification of Illness Form (VIF) to the site. The University of Waterloo Verification of Illness Form (VIF) is normally the only medical documentation accepted to support requests for academic consideration. Students should not submit their VIF or any other medical documentation to their instructors, teaching assistants, or advisors. To request and arrange accommodations, it is the student’s responsibility to contact the instructor within 48 hours of the date of any missed course component, or as soon as the student is able given their health condition. Once approved, the student and their instructors will receive a confirmation email of the student’s absence. Course instructors will determine how missed course components are accommodated.

Absence due to extenuating circumstances

For absences due to extenuating circumstances (bereavement, family illness, varsity sports, etc.), students are required to complete the Verification of Illness or Extenuating Circumstances web form (log in required) and upload their official documentation (e.g., plane tickets, obituary, court documents, email from varsity coach, police reports, etc.) to the site. To request and arrange accommodations, it is the student’s responsibility to contact instructors within 48 hours of the date of any missed course component, or as soon as the student is able given their circumstances.
