Course Description

KIN 100L:

Regional gross anatomy of the limbs and trunk, including a brief introduction to the central nervous and cardiovascular systems. The anatomy is explored using pre-dissected human cadavers. For the limbs and trunk, the emphasis is placed on structures (muscles, nerves, and bones) involved in gross movement.

View requirements for KIN 100L

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Use anatomical terminology to describe location of structures, types of movements, and basic injuries.
Identify the function of a muscle based on its attachments and fibre direction.
Describe the basic structure of the heart and the great vessels
Predict the deficits of common neurological impairments based on muscle and skin innervation.
Identify anatomical structures seen in cadaveric specimens and images.

The final examination will be scheduled by the Registrar’s Office. The final exam is cumulative (covering Labs 1-11), closed book, no aids are allowed. More detail will be provided in class and on Learn.

Lab Attendance: 

Attendance at your scheduled lab is mandatory. When you come to lab, you will need to sign in. Simply attending lab will be worth 1% of your final grade. If you have a valid reason for missing lab, you will need to complete the Form for Missing Lab and attend another lab section or open lab review to cover the material. Otherwise, you will not receive the attendance mark. Since this grade category is worth 10% and there are 11 Labs, you are able to miss on lab without a valid reason and still receive full attendance marks.

Pre-Lab Readings and Questions:

Prior to each week of labs, you will be required to complete the section Pre-Lab Reading for that particular lab on Top Hat. There are several questions that you will need to complete for each Pre-Lab Reading. The questions are mostly in the form of Image Matching and Fill-in-the-Blank. At the end of the term, your lowest mark in this category will be dropped. The Pre-Lab Reading sections are all assigned at the beginning of the term. They are due at the beginning of the first lab of the week (Mondays @ 10:30am). We will be dropping your lowest grade, so if you forget to complete it one week, it will be dropped from your final grade calculation.

Post-Lab Assessments:

Weekly Post-Lab Assessments are to be completed on Top Hat as soon as possible following your scheduled lab section. Assessments are available to be completed online at the end of the first lab section of the week until 4:30pm the following Wednesday (each assessment is available for 10 days).

Assessments must be completed individually, but you may use your notes as needed. You will be given questions related to the lab content that was presented in that same week. You will have 30 minutes to complete each assessment. Once you have started the assessment you must finish it within the same session.

Should you encounter technical difficulties while completing an assessment please contact Jeremy Roth (jroth@uwaterloo.ca) immediately. To avoid unnecessary hassle, please ensure that you are at a secure and reliable internet connection when you complete your assessment.

Bellringer Exams:

There are two bellringer exams for this course. They will be the same format. The midterm bellringer is scheduled for Friday, February 28th from 4:30pm - 6:00pm, room TBD. This exam will cover content from Labs 1-5. The final exam will be scheduled by the Registrar’s Office during the final exam period and is cumulative, covering content from all 11 Labs. Midterm and final exams are closed book, no aids are allowed. More detail will be provided in class and on Learn.  Each question will be projected on a PowerPoint slide for 30 seconds. You will be expected to answer the question on an answer sheet in the time provided. This is not a multiple choice exam. Once the 30 seconds have passed, the next question will be displayed and you will not be able to return to previously viewed questions. Some questions will be simple identification questions, while others will ask you to describe function, innervation, attachments, or other key concepts, so read the questions carefully.

Illness and Documentation 

From time to time, students will encounter extenuating circumstances such as significant illnesses, ongoing medical conditions, or religious observations that prevent them from meeting academic obligations. The University is committed to assisting students who experience these events.

Self-declaration of influenza-like illness

Students must complete the illness/isolation declaration if they are seeking consideration from an instructor for missed course requirements due to influenza-like illness or self-isolation. This is a self-declaration available on Quest and is to be completed only for an absence related to an influenza-like illness or an absence as a result of a requirement to self-isolate. This form is to be used for an absence up to 14 days. Absences resulting from other conditions or longer than 14 days must be verified using the Verification of Illness form (see below).

Self-declaration of short-term absence

You are not able to use this self-declaration for LAB courses as per the self-declaration of short-term absence website.

Verification of illness form (VIF)

For any non-influenza-like illnesses or health conditions, students are required to provide their instructors a Verification of Illness form. 

Regardless of the nature of the absence or the form submitted, it is the student’s responsibility to notify their instructor(s) of their absence within 48 hours of a deadline/due date. Failure to do so may result in late penalties or an accommodation may not be permitted. Adjustment of due dates or deferrals of academic course elements are not automatic upon the self-declaration of illness or short-term absence.

Accommodations

False claims of illness or false claims of the circumstances outlined above and/or the submission of false supporting documentation of extenuating circumstances constitute an academic offence that will result in disciplinary action under Policy 71. 

Adjustment of due dates or deferrals of term tests or final examinations are not automatic upon the presentation of suitable documentation. Instructors will use this documentation along with all information available to them when determining whether accommodation is warranted. When instructors elect to provide an accommodation, the options available to students vary based on the nature of the extenuating circumstances they are facing, and on the type of evaluation they were unable to complete on time. 

For in-term assignments, instructors may use their discretion and allow an extension. If the instructor does not grant an extension and an element is missed, it is recommended that the weight of the missed element – an assignment, a laboratory report, or other evaluation mechanism – be transferred to similar types of elements due later in the term. If this option is not available, the weight of the missed assignment may be transferred to a test or the final examination. If students are granted an accommodation for a test, the weighting of the missed test is typically added to the final examination weighting or spread over the remaining tests.
