Course Description

KIN 312:

An introduction to selected neurological disorders and their implications for physical activity. The neurological disorders examined include those which accompany neuromuscular and perceptual-motor impairment, intellectual disability, cardiovascular and respiratory disease.

View requirements for KIN 312

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. To use appropriate clinical/scientific terminology to define and describe the neurological disorders covered in this course including etiology, epidemiological patterns, subjective symptoms, objective presentation and treatment approaches.
2. To summarize the characteristics of healthy movement and healthy gait and appraise how impairments related to various neurological disorders may impact these characteristics.
3. To describe, compare and contrast the pathophysiological mechanisms underlying the neurological disorders discussed in this course.
4. To identify the components of a basic neurological assessment and relate the importance of normal and abnormal findings
5. To describe the symptoms of various neurological disorders and their relationship to changes in the neuromuscular system.
6. To describe the impact of neurological disorders and associated symptoms on movement and an individual's daily life activities.

Administrative Policy

Class Conduct:

This is a large class but you are not a small part of it! To make our time together as valuable as possible, we both have to work hard at it. The following basic principles may give us some guidelines:

  1. Every student has the right to learn as well as the responsibility not to deprive others of their right to learn.
  2. Every student is accountable for his or her own actions.

In order for you to get the most out of this class, please consider the following:

  • Attend all scheduled classes and arrive on time. Late arrivals and early departures are very disruptive and violate the first basic principle.
  • Please do not schedule other activities during this class time. I will try to make class as interesting and informative as possible, but I can’t learn the material for you.
  • Please let me know immediately if you have a problem that is preventing you from performing satisfactorily in this class.
  • Please refrain from carrying on separate conversations with your peers during class time. If it is urgent and cannot wait until class is over, please quietly excuse yourself from class so as not to disrupt the learning of others.
  • If your private discussion is related to class content please feel free to raise your hand and ask a question. Questions are an important part of the learning process and chances are other members of the class have the same question as you and would benefit from the clarification.
  • Please turn the ringer off your cell phone during class time so that it won’t be a disruption.

I am looking forward to working with you this term.

 Illness and Documentation 

From time to time, students will encounter extenuating circumstances such as significant illnesses, ongoing medical conditions, or religious observations that prevent them from meeting academic obligations. The University is committed to assisting students who experience these events.

Self-declaration of influenza-like illness

Students must complete the illness/isolation declaration if they are seeking consideration from an instructor for missed course requirements due to influenza-like illness or self-isolation. This is a self-declaration available on Quest and is to be completed only for an absence related to an influenza-like illness or an absence as a result of a requirement to self-isolate. This form is to be used for an absence up to 14 days. Absences resulting from other conditions or longer than 14 days must be verified using the Verification of Illness form (see below).

Self-declaration of short-term absence

The Registrar's Office is piloting a short-term accommodation process. Students may experience short-term absences for several reasons for which they cannot participate or complete their academic responsibilities. For any assessments during the formal lecture period, students may self-declare their short-term absence within Quest using the Self-Declaration of Absence Form. Students will be permitted to self-declare short-term absence once per academic term, during the formal lecture period. A self-declared short-term absence will excuse students for up to 48 hours. The timing of which does not start when you submit your form. For example, if you declare a Tuesday as your first day absent, the Wednesday would be your second day and you would be expected to return to your studies on the Thursday (two full days absent; Tuesday and Wednesday). You are permitted to attend lectures, labs, tutorials, etc. However, you are not permitted to submit any assignments/assessments. For more information please visit the self-declaration of short-term absence website.

Verification of illness form (VIF)

For any non-influenza-like illnesses or health conditions, students are required to provide their instructors a Verification of Illness form. 

Regardless of the nature of the absence or the form submitted, it is the student’s responsibility to notify their instructor(s) of their absence within 48 hours of a deadline/due date. Failure to do so may result in late penalties or an accommodation may not be permitted. Adjustment of due dates or deferrals of academic course elements are not automatic upon the self-declaration of illness or short-term absence. 

Academic Regulations and Accommodations

In addition to illness, there may be events that preclude students from meeting academic obligations. The following are examples of valid circumstances and required documentation that may warrant academic accommodation:

  • A member of a varsity team travelling for competition: a letter/email from the Head Coach verifying the departure and return dates and times.
  • Death of a family member: a copy of the death certificate, a copy of the obituary or a link to the online obituary.
  • Travel: from time-to-time students are required to travel during an academic term for reasons NOT related to pleasure/vacation. If you are travelling and require accommodation, you must inform the instructor at least 2 weeks in advance of your travel, indicate the reason for travel and provide a boarding pass upon return to campus.

Important note: Travel that results in arrival to campus after the first day of class, absence before or after a reading break or departure from campus during the final exam period DO NOT warrant accommodation and any missed academic obligations will receive a grade of zero.

Co-op Interviews

Academic obligations take priority over co-op interviews. Whenever possible students are encouraged to swap interview times to avoid conflicts with academic obligations. If you swap interview times or you cannot find a student to swap with, you must complete an Interview Conflict form. Follow this link for more information on interview process and procedures. You can submit an Interview Conflict form by following these steps:

  1. Login to WaterlooWorks
  2. Select 'Submit a form' from your dashboard
  3. Select the category 'Pre and Post Interview support'
  4. Select the sub-category based on your current situation. There are two sub-categories to choose from:
  • Interview Conflict - another student agreed to switch times
  • Interview Conflict - unable to switch times with another student
  • Interview location accommodation required - interview at UW interview site other than my home campus (for Architectural Engineering and Architecture students)

If you're feeling overwhelmed with your interview schedule, you can contact your co-op advisor to discuss available options and/or fill out a Request for Interview Relief Form on WaterlooWorks. Depending on your situation, you may be able to choose to be removed from one or more of your upcoming interviews by having your job application(s) withdrawn.

Accommodations

False claims of illness or false claims of the circumstances outlined above and/or the submission of false supporting documentation of extenuating circumstances constitute an academic offence that will result in disciplinary action under Policy 71. 

Adjustment of due dates or deferrals of term tests or final examinations are not automatic upon the presentation of suitable documentation. Instructors will use this documentation along with all information available to them when determining whether accommodation is warranted. When instructors elect to provide an accommodation, the options available to students vary based on the nature of the extenuating circumstances they are facing, and on the type of evaluation they were unable to complete on time. 

For in-term assignments, instructors may use their discretion and allow an extension. If the instructor does not grant an extension and an element is missed, it is recommended that the weight of the missed element – an assignment, a laboratory report, or other evaluation mechanism – be transferred to similar types of elements due later in the term. If this option is not available, the weight of the missed assignment may be transferred to a test or the final examination. If students are granted an accommodation for a test, the weighting of the missed test is typically added to the final examination weighting or spread over the remaining tests.
