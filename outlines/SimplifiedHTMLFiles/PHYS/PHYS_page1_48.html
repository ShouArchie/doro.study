Course Description

PHYS 394:

Lasers: semi-classical interaction, properties, cavity, Gaussian beams. Radiation and Detectors. Introduction to nonlinear optics.

View requirements for PHYS 394

Coherence:

                  - Classical description and how it applies to lasers

                  - temporal coherence - monochromaticity for CW lasers and transform limited bandwidth for pulsed lasers

                  - spatial coherence - collimation 

Lasers:

                  - Laser Components: gain medium, pumping, laser cavity, output coupler

            - laser properties - monochromaticity, collimation, coherence, Gaussian beam propagation

 

            Gain Medium:

                  - semi-classical description of Absorption, Stimulated and Spontaneous Emission

                  - population rate equations - Laser Amplification

                  - types of pumping

 

            Laser Cavities:

                  - Etalons, Confocal cavity, ouput coupler

                  - cavity modes; spatial and temporal

                  - cavity losses

 

Nonlinear and Electro-Optics

            - Q-switching, 

            - mode-locking, 

            - frequency doubling (if time allows)

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
•Illustrate laser properties using common applications
•Appraise the accepted definition of coherence in terms of laser radiation
•Explain how a laser works to generate coherent radiation above an oscillation threshold
•Solve specific laser design criteria
•Explain why nonlinear optics was not observed before the laser

Tentative Course Schedule

Week 1, overview of topics

week 2, Stimulated emission, transition rate probability, rate of absorption, spontaneous emission

week 3, 3-level laser, 4-level laser, rate equations, saturation of lasing transition, laser oscillation

week 4, Coherence: temporal and spatial

week 5, laser cavities, cavity stability, spatial modes, gaussian mode

week 6 confocal cavity

week 7 gaussian beams

week 8 gaussian beam propagation, ABCD matrices for gaussian beam propagation

week 9, line shapes, homogeneous and inhomogenous broadening. spatial and spectral hole burning

week 10, transient laser behaviour, q-switching

week 11, mode-locking, nonlinear optics

(two inclass midterms account for 12 week)

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assignments            10%
Written report                      20%
2 term Tests              30%
Final Examination  40%

Evaluation: The course grade will be based on 5 assignments, one written report, 2 in-class tests and a final examination, which will be held during the Official Examination Schedule. 

 

 Dates of term in-class tests are Feb. 8, 2025, Mar. 13, 2025

*note: if either midterm is missed due to  48hour absence then the final exam will be worth 55% of the final grade.

 

Assignments: The 5 assignments will be assigned every other week and will cover the lecture topic covered in those two weeks.

Rules for Group Work in Assignments: Students can work in groups, but each student needs to submit his/her own version of the working and results.

 

Assignment Deadline: Assignments are to be handed in to the instructor during the Friday class of that week. 

Late Submissions: If the TA has not completed marking, late assignments will be accepted. Assignments cannot be handed in after the solutions are posted which will typically happen the following week.

Pick up Marked Assignments: Marked assignments will be picked up in class

 

Written Report: The report will be your opportunity to display your understanding of laser properties as they relate to a common application.  In your report, you must describe which laser characteristics are needed for the application and why that particular type of laser can deliver the needed laser specifications. The topics to choose from are:

 

  1. Excimer lasers are being used for lithography, but now there is an interest in using them for laser fusion.  At least 2 US companies have formed recently to develop excimer lasers for fusion even though Los Alamos National Lab shut down their excimer laser fusion development project years ago.  Why is there now great interest in excimers for laser fusion?
  2. Why are Er doped fiber amplifiers used in fiber optics communications?
  3. Google data centers can have a million lasers working in them. What kind of laser and why?

 

The reports will be marked by the instructor.  As this report is worth as much as the 5 assignments, it is expected that you will be working on this report during the weeks without assignments.  For the report you will be asked to 

 

  1. choose the topic by Jan. 16, 2025 (5% of report grade)
  2. have the three main points and the 5 references (not websites) that you are using by Feb13, 2025 (35% of report grade)
  3. Hand in the 5-page report by March 20, 2025 (60% of report grade)
