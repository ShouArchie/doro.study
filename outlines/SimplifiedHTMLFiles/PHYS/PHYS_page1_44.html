Course Description

PHYS 375:

Stellar distances, masses, ages. Stellar interiors and atmospheres, star formation and evolution. Supernovae, white dwarfs, neutron stars, black holes.

View requirements for PHYS 375

Lecture notes will be posted on Learn. Attendance is strongly encouraged.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain and apply methods for observationally determining stellar properties.
Describe stellar classification, the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram, and key features, such as the Main Sequence.
Identify and explain the key physics that governs these properties.
Understand the equations of stellar structure and radiation.
Make order of magnitude estimates of stellar properties using the equations of stellar structure.
Describe the evolution of stars, supernovae and stellar remnants.

Student Assessment

There is no defined grading scheme for this course.

 

 

Assignments must be submitted to the Dropbox on LEARN, by the deadlines given below.

 

  • Policy regarding missed graded element: 
  • A missed assignment or term test will be given a grade of zero unless a VIF form is submitted.  Acceptance of the VIF as a valid excuse will be at the discretion of the instructor. 
  • Late assignments will be graded to half marks.  Alternative test dates cannot be accommodated.
  • If a short-term absence is declared on a quiz day, the remaining two quizzes will be used.  No makeup will be offered.
  • Should a student declare a short-term absence on an assignment due date, the assignment must be submitted the day following the last day of the absence to avoid penalty.
  • Short-term absences must be declared in Quest. 
  • No alternative form of assessment will be offered beyond what is stated in this syllabus.
  • Check appropriate uW websites for details concerning various dates (e.g., final examination, drop deadlines).
