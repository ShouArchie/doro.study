Course Description

PHYS 242L:

For students who have taken or are taking PHYS242.

View requirements for PHYS 242L

This lab course is designed to provide direct experience with the physical phenomena studied in the PHYS 242 course. You will learn how to use standard physical instruments, combined with systematic methods of data collection and analysis and the clear, accurate reporting of results to demonstrate and clarify the theory used in the experiments. 

Students will perform 5 experiments in-person during their scheduled lab period, and write a report on each afterwards. Each experiment is designed to test a certain theoretical expectation and are listed in this outline under "tentative course schedule".

You must check your Quest schedule for the exact date and time of each experiment for the specific section in which you are registered.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Connect, calibrate and use standard and specialized lab equipment
Apply methods of careful data collection and reasonable data analysis
Report quantitative and qualitative experimental results clearly and accurately
Interpret the physical meaning of experimental results, and validate their integrity, using related theory
Assess uncertainty in experimental measurements, and demonstrate how to calculate compound uncertainties
Identify and explain the concepts that are critically relevant to the experimental methods employed

Intro videos will be made available online several days before the experiment is performed. The intro videos must be watched before attending the scheduled lab period as they will be replacing the usual in-person introduction to the experiment and equipment. STUDENT ABSENCES:

Illness: If you are sick, please do not come to lab. If you are ill and do miss an experiment you must submit a Verification of Illness Form (VIF) to the Absence Database in order to receive an accommodation. This form can be filled out by a healthcare practitioner at Campus Wellness OR  your own family/private healthcare practitioner. Please only submit your forms to the Absence Database, do not email them to your Instructor. It is your responsibility to contact your Instructor to make them aware of your absence. You must make accommodations with your instructor within 1 week of the end of your VIF or else no accommodation can be guaranteed as the term progresses.

Creed/Religion: If you will be unable to attend a lab or meet a deadline due to a creed or religious observance you must complete the Religious Observance Self-Declaration Form in Quest. As the dates of important religious observances are generally known well in advance, students must consult with your Instructor within two weeks of the announcement of the due date or lab for which academic accommodation is being sought. Since all the deadlines of this course are outlined in this Course Outline the two weeks will be counted from the first day of the term, if you reach out to your instructor after the two weeks there is no guarantee that an accommodation will be able to be made.

Extenuating Circumstances: Students can request academic considerations for extenuating circumstances (bereavement, emergencies, University of Waterloo sanctioned academic or athletic events, etc.), and requests will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis. You must provide and submit official documentation to the Absence Database, the supporting documentation will be considered similarly to a VIF. Documentation is required before any action can be taken. The supporting documentation should provide a reason for the absence and the impacted dates, some examples are: plane/train/bus tickets for emergency travel, court documents, police reports, death certificates/obituaries/letters from funeral directors, and counsellor letters. The previous lists is not exhaustive. It is your responsibility to contact your Instructor to make them aware of your absence.

Short-Term Absences: Students have the ability to declare a two-day absence to excuse themselves from most academic responsibilities for up to two consecutive calendar days. This does not apply to LAB courses, if a student declares a self-declared short-term absence and it overlaps with a component of this course they are still expected to complete/attend.

More information about absences can be found here.

Other absences: Any other absence is considered an "unapproved absence", and there will be no guarantee that an accommodation will be provided and is up to the discretion of your Instructor. Absences related to the course work or expectations of other classes--such as studying for midterms or completing assignments in other courses--will not be accommodated

If you have a chronic or on going issue that impacts your academics I would highly recommend you reach out to AccessAbility services (more information at the end of this outline) to determine a more comprehensive accommodation plan.

Instructor Absence: If the instructor is unable to attend a live lab session (e.g. for COVID-19 self-isolation) then an alternate instructor/TA will take their place. If one is not available at short notice, then the lab session may be rescheduled and/or moved to a remote format. Please monitor your email and LEARN announcements for this type of information.

Student Assessment

Component Value

GENERAL EXPECTATIONS:

  • On LEARN, read "Instructions to Students" under "Manual" and watch the course introduction video(s)
  • Students are expected to complete all the experiments (1-5) assigned to them and submit a report for each experiment. The reports must be written and submitted individually
  • Students are expected to have read through all lab materials, watched relevant experiment videos, and be fully prepared before attending their session.

POLICIES RELATED TO REPORTS:

  • Lab reports must be submitted to the correct LEARN dropbox folder by your due date
  • The due date for your report is 11:59pm, one week after your scheduled live session.
  • Report submissions must be a single file in .pdf format only. Please copy and paste all data tables and/or scanned images into this one file (do not upload spreadsheets, JPEGs, Jupyter notebooks, etc.).
  • Each report is graded out of 20 total marks
  • Late reports will be penalized at a rate of 1 mark per day up to a maximum of 8 late marks.
  • You are encouraged to discuss and critically analyze ideas with your colleagues in order to better understand the principles and concepts in these labs, but your report must reflect your own understanding, and be written independently (see below under “Academic Integrity” for more information).

FINAL EXAM:

In addition to the five lab reports, there will be a Final Exam that will take place during the exam period and scheduled by the Registrars Office. More information about the Final Exam will be released later in the term.

Such permission may only be granted for legitimate reasons (including, for example, verified illness, family emergencies, varsity travel). Equity and Inclusion: In this course, you should expect to be treated fairly and with respect by everyone on the instructional team and all your peers. You should expect an equal opportunity to learn. You are also expected to behave fairly and respectfully toward everyone else and avoid behaviours that make it harder for others to be comfortable. Everyone in this course has equally earned their place. Please help to create a welcoming and inclusive environment by, for example, refraining from discussing a person’s appearance or making assumptions based on gender, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, disability, or other personal characteristics. If you have a concern about how you are being treated, some of the people you can contact include (in order of escalating seriousness): your instructor, the current Chair of the Department of Physics and Astronomy, the University of Waterloo’s Sexual Violence Prevention and Response Office at svpro@uwaterloo.ca or the police on- or off-campus.

Generative AI: This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as analysis and discussion of experimental results. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, or images, like Chat GPT, or DALL-E, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
