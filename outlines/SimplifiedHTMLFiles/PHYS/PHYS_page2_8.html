Course Description

PHYS 705:

Phase transitions. Fluctuation phenomena. Kubo's theory of time correlation functions for transport and spectral properties; applications selected from a variety of topics including linearized hydrodynamics of normal and superfluids, molecular liquids, liquid crystals, surface phenomena, theory of the dielectric constant, etc. Students who have not taken PHYS 704 but have an equivalent background in Statistical Physics may seek the instructor's consent to register in this course.

View requirements for PHYS 705

Ignore the above course description.  Topics covered will include:

  • Introduction: Phase transitions, order parameters, spontaneously broken symmetry.
  • Mean field theory, critical exponents.
  • Derivation of Landau theory via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation.
  • Perturbation theory of fluctuations and its breakdown.
  • Dimensional analysis and scaling.
  • Real-space and Wilsonian renormalization group.
  • Calculation of critical exponents in d = 4 − ε dimensions.
  • Continuous symmetry, Goldstone modes, Mermin-Wagner theorem.
  • Non-linear sigma model.
  • Kosterlitz-Thouless transition, vorticies and topology.
  • Classical gauge theories.

Tentative Course Schedule

This course will be a flipped classroom, with asynchronous video lectures, and one scheduled "office hour" per week.  The scheduled office hour will occur in-person in room PHY 308, typically on Thursday at 2:30pm unless otherwise noted.  Students who cannot attend in person are encouraged to attend through Zoom.

Additional office hours with the instructor are available by appointment.

Online lectures can be found on PIRSA. The course pace will cover approximately two recorded lectures per week, unless otherwise indicated by your instructor.

Up-to-date course announcement will occur on UW LEARN.
