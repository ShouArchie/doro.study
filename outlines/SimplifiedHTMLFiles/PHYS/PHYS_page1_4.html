Course Description

PHYS 111:

An introduction to physics for students intending to concentrate their further studies in biology, dentistry, medicine and paramedicine; includes particle kinematics and dynamics, energy and momentum conservation, and rotational mechanics.

View requirements for PHYS 111

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Students should develop an awareness of the importance of the concepts in physics in everyday life, and the tools for making predictions on the outcome of physical events. Students will develop their quantitative skills and use those skills to solve problems.

Tentative Course Schedule

Course Material and Timing (approximate):

Week 1: Vectors, scalars, velocity and acceleration

Week 2: Kinematics 1D, 2D

Week 3: Relative velocity, Newton’s Laws

Week 4: Forces. Tension/Friction

Week 5: Circular motion

Week 6: Energy/Power

Week 7: Momentum

Week 8: Centre of Mass/ Rotation

Week 9: Rotational Kinematics/ Torque

Week 10: Angular Dynamics/Rolling/Angular momentum

Week 11: Statics

Student Assessment

There is no defined grading scheme for this course.

Course Assessment:

Course assessment will be provided by a combination of continuous assessment and exams.  

Weekly assignments on Webassign (online)                                     10%

Weekly quizzes (last 25 minutes of tutorial) hand marked               20%

Weekly feedback submitted on Learn                                                5%

Midterm                                                                                              25%

Final exam                                                                                           40%

 

Alternate grading schemes: If a student misses a deadline for any assessment component (including single quizzes or assignment) for a reason that is acceptable to their instructor then the weight for that particular component will be transferred to the final exam. In order to allow for the fact that different students may learn at different rates, total weight from weekly quizzes (not at the individual quiz level) and/or the midterm can be transferred to the final exam. This will be considered automatically and will only be used to benefit students.

 

Students who are registered with accessibility services and have been granted more time for quizzes will use crowdmark to hand them in online.

Course Statement on Collaboration and Cooperation

In any workplace setting, problem solving is a team effort.  Cooperation and collaboration are important parts of the problem solving process.  In keeping with this idea, We encourage intensive discussion of assignment problems. HOWEVER, cooperation and collaboration is distinct from copying.  Any work handed in MUST be your own, and you should understand everything you hand in.

In this course, you should expect to be treated fairly and with respect by everyone on the instructional team and all your peers.  You should expect an equal opportunity to learn.  You are also expected to behave fairly and respectfully toward everyone else and avoid behaviors that make it harder for others to be comfortable.  Everyone in this course has equally earned their place.  Please help to create a welcoming and inclusive environment by, for example, refraining from discussing a person’s appearance or making assumptions based on gender, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, disability, or other personal characteristics.
