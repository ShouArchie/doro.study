Course Description

PHYS 365:

Complex variables, Cauchy-Riemann conditions, Cauchy integral formula, Taylor and Laurent expansions, residue theorem, contour integrals and applications. Fourier and Laplace transforms with applications. Green's functions.

View requirements for PHYS 365

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
perform contour integrals
employ Green functions to solve differential equations
perform Fourier and Laplace transforms
construct and manipulate complex power series expansions+

POLICY ON HOMEWORK ASSIGNMENTS

  • Homework assignments can be submitted until midnight of the day indicated on the assignment.  Assignments must be uploaded (either as a scanned copy or a .pdf file) to crowdmark.  
  • You are encouraged to work with your peers to solve the problem sets. However, the solutions must be your own—copying solutions constitutes a breach of academic integrity.
  • The penalty for late assignments/laboratories is 10% per day past the deadline unless a university-approved verification of illness form is given to the appropriate TA.  After the solutions are posted, no late submissions will be accepted. Solutions to the problem sets will be released no sooner than 1 week after the due date. There may be exceptions to accommodate, e.g., exam scheduling. These special cases will be announced.

All deadlines will be published on LEARN.  Papers can be submitted without penalty up to 11:59 pm of the deadline date.

POLICY ON MISSED EXAMS

Missed exams will result in a grade of 0%, unless there are verifiable extenuating circumstances— A verification of Illness Form (VIF) is required.

University Policy
