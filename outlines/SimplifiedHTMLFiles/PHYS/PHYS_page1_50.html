Course Description

PHYS 396:

Introduction to imaging concepts in biophysics with emphasis on the interrelationship between the physics principles of an imaging modality and the associated image reconstruction; methods for imaging at macroscopic to microscopic scales; computed tomography, magnetic resonance imaging, ultrasound, PET, optical imaging, optical and fluorescence microscopy, scanning probe microscopy (AFM, STM), optical tweezers, electron microscopy.

View requirements for PHYS 396

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain the fundamental physical principles underlying key medical imaging modalities, including radiography, computed tomography, nuclear medicine, ultrasound, magnetic resonance, and optical imaging.
Interpret mathematical representations of imaging processes, such as Fourier transforms and convolution, and relate them to practical imaging applications
Compare the capabilities, limitations, and applications of different medical imaging techniques in clinical and research contexts
Conduct a comprehensive literature review on a specific imaging topic, synthesizing current research to identify trends and gaps
Prepare and submit a structured and concise abstract for a mock scientific conference to meet professional standards
Design and present a visually engaging and scientifically accurate conference poster summarizing research findings from an existing paper
Deliver an effective mini-presentation that communicates technical content clearly to a professional audience
Apply critical thinking to interpret and discuss the biological and clinical implications of imaging technologies

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assignments x 7 50%
Mock Conference Project: Medical Imaging Applied to Biological Disease 50%

Additional details regarding each type of assessment metric is provided below for your reference.

ASSIGNMENTS:

  • Frequency: 7x Throughout the Term
  • Performance Metric: Individual
  • Level of Collaboration Allowed: None - Screening for Plagiarism Active
  • Description: Approximately every 1-2 weeks, you will be asked to submit an assignment for grading. These assignments provide you with a chance to apply the knowledge you have learnt to foundational problems for various topics. All assignments are to be submitted to Crowdmark for grading.
  • Grading: Based on accuracy of solutions, quality/clarity of solutions
  • Late Penalty: 10% per day up to 30% (Day 3), then 100% grade deduction unless a VIF or approved exemption is provided

MOCK CONFERENCE PROJECT:

  • Frequency: Throughout the Term
  • Performance Metric: Group of 2-3 Students for Groupwork Components | Individual for Individual Components
  • Level of Collaboration Allowed: Free to Discuss and Collaborate within Group | Cannot Plagiarize/Copy from Others
  • Description: Throughout the term, you will be asked to prepare material for a mock conference to mimic the experience of a researcher or graduate student going to present their work at a conference. Typically, this involves doing the research (in your case this would be a literature review to review different research that has been done), preparing an abstract that summarizes the work, practicing a presentation for your talk/poster, and submitting a paper for publication. Given that we need to do all this within the limitations of a course, the project will be broken up into the following components:
    • Milestone 1 (Group): Selection of Group and Topic | Students will be asked to pick a disease and at least 2 methods (diagnostic+ treatment for example) that can be paired well to study that disease
    • Milestone 2 (Group): Abstract Submission | Students will be asked to submit a short paragraph and a list of references to share what they wish to base their project on after reviewing literature on reputable platforms
    • Milestone 3 (Individual): Video Presentation | Students will be asked to prepare a 10 minute presentation to be recorded and posted on LEARN to summarize the disease they have chosen and what kinds of methods (fundamental knowledge + pros/cons) can be used to image, diagnose and/or treat the chosen disease. Peer evaluations are mandatory on Bongo / LEARN.
    • Milestone 4 (Individual): Conference Paper Submission | Students will be asked to submit an 8-10 page (single-spaced) paper formatted according to a specific journal style (more details to be provided on LEARN) to mimic a typical manuscript submission process. The paper must provide the following information:
      • Introduction: a short review of the specified medical condition
      • Methods: a discussion of how at least three medical imaging and/or biophysical technologies reviewed in this course can apply to the specific medical condition, and what diagnostic information can be obtained through the use of different imaging technologies
        • Subsections must provide insight into (a) invasiveness of the procedure, (b) image quality, (c) complexity of instrumentation / methodology, and (d) cost and time considerations
      • Results: a summary of how some of the methods chosen have been used to study the medical condition; at least 2-3 studies must be reviewed here
      • Conclusion: a summary of which methods are best suited to provide the most diagnostically relevant and detailed information about the medical condition
      • References: include references to any published work
    • Milestone 5 (Group): Conference Poster Presentation | After having done individual research on medical conditions and imaging technologies, students will be asked to prepare a 36"x24" poster summarizing the best content out of all their group's work. Support and guidance will be provided on how to properly prepare poster presentations. Printing fees will be covered by Dr. Lee if and only if you submit your final poster in digital format (PDF) by the specified deadline. Dr. Lee will then bring the printed posters to class on the last week, where we will mount them to the walls of the classroom (or on the tables) and hold our mock conference. Peer evaluation rubrics will be provided for all students so they can walk around evaluating others' posters and ask/answer questions. 
      • Dr. Lee has secured a group discount with a local printing company that requires a minimum of 5 days lead time. If you do not provide Dr. Lee with the poster files by the specified deadline, you will be responsible for printing and paying for the poster yourself as the discounted cost is only available with the bulk purchase. Conference posters of this size are typically $25-50 + HST depending on the vendor and quality of paper.
  • Grading: Detailed grading rubrics will be provided on LEARN for each milestone of the project
  • Late Penalty: 100% for any late submissions, email for extenuating circumstances that require accommodations
