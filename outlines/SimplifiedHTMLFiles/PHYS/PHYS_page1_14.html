Course Description

PHYS 122:

Simple harmonic motion, resonance, damped harmonic motion, complex numbers, wave motion and sound, electrostatic force and potential, electric current and power, capacitors, DC circuits, LRC circuits, introduction to magnetic fields Lorentz Force.

View requirements for PHYS 122

Overview
This course covers oscillations, waves, electricity and an introduction to magnetism. We cover a chapter a week for almost all weeks of the course. See the schedule below for more details. Students will practice problem solving using an on-line homework system called MasteringPhysics which is available from the commercial publisher, Pearson. These assignments are due weekly, and there are weekly tutorials that must be attended in person. There will be a midterm exam, final exam, and four quizzes. Please make note of the dates below.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand and correctly apply concepts related to oscillations and waves including sound. (See the schedule below for more detail.)
Understand and correctly apply concepts related to electrostatics and magnetism. (See the schedule below for more detail.)
Apply problem solving approaches to write clear and correct solutions to quantitative problems involving oscillations, waves, electrostatics and magnetism, as well as topics from the first term of mechanics.
Apply appropriate mathematics in solving problems involving oscillations, waves, electrostatics and magnetism including using derivatives, integration, dot and cross products and complex numbers.
Work effectively and collaboratively in diverse groups.
Effectively communicate your understanding of scientific and technical information in writing and orally, using mathematical symbols and words.

Quizzes
There will be four quizzes scattered throughout the term. Two will be before the midterm exam and two will be after. The quizzes must be taken in your scheduled tutorial section (Friday, Monday or Tuesday) unless Dr. Cummings has given you prior permission otherwise.  The Friday quiz dates are as follows: Jan. 24, Feb. 7, March 7 and March 21. The Monday dates are as follows: Jan. 27, Feb. 10, March 10 and March 24.  The Tuesday dates are as follows: Jan. 28, Feb. 11, March 11 and March 25. (Or see the schedule above.)  Everyone gets to drop one quiz grade with, or without, a VIF/valid university excuse.  If you miss more than one quiz, please send the VIF covering the second or subsequent absence(s) to  Dr. Cummings.  

Midterm Exam
The midterm will cover Weeks 1-5 and is scheduled for Wednesday Feb. 25 from 7-9 pm.  Room and seating information will be distributed in the weeks before the midterm.  You MUST notify Dr. Cummings immediately if you know you have a conflict with the date.  We will NOT consider conflicts for those who provide us with less than 7 days notice unless it is an emergency. If you get sick or have some other last minute emergency and miss the midterm with an accepted, valid excuse, then the weight will be shifted to the final exam. 

Final Exam 
This will be cumulative and is scheduled during the final exam period by the Registrar's Office.  As per university policy, if you miss the final exam with a valid excuse, you must take the make-up during or before the university make-up days which are held near the start of the next term.  If the final exam is not completed in this time period a grade of DNW will be submitted.

Mastering Physics 
There will be a MasteringPhysics assignment DUE WEEKLY, most weeks of the term.  The assignments will each contain about 15 required questions that should take approximately 2 hours to complete.  In addition, there will be one or more bonus question(s) that can be completed if desired.  Answering these bonus questions can make-up for points missed elsewhere on any MasteringPhysics assignment, or can result in a score for MasteringPhysics that is greater than 100%.  Scores over 100% “roll-over” into other Mastering Assignments, and even the other categories.  (Mastering is worth 10% of your grade.  If you end up with a score of 110% for Mastering, you will get 11% for the category which effectively just raises your overall grade by 1%).  10 points for the required questions will be dropped as well. Assignments are due on the Monday at 11:59 pm typically two weeks after the topic starts.  For example, the material covered in Week 1 is due on Monday in Week 3, on January 20.  You get 8 submission attempts.  Your score on multiple choice questions goes down each try.  Your score on numerical submission is not reduced if you get the answer within 8 attempts. Unfortunately, we can not give extensions on Mastering Physics assignments but the late penalty is small.  Your score is reduced by 0.2% for every hour (1% for every 5 hours late, or about 5% per day). The first assignment is due on January 20.  See the course calendar above for additional dates.

Generally, excused absences and AAS accommodations for missed course components do not excuse a student from a MasteringPhysics Assignment but rather only extend the period of time the student has to complete the assignment by the number of days covered by the excused absence.  So, please, keep working on your assignments unless you are debilitated. If you have incurred a late penalty on a MasteringPhysics assignment that you believe should be forgiven based on a VIF or other excused absence including AAS accommodation, please send an email to karenc@uwaterloo.ca at the end of the term with the following information: Assignment number, VIF or other documentation of excused absence attached, or state the number of days covered by the VIF or excuse.  Any late penalties will be adjusted accordingly.  Please put "grade adjustment" in the subject line. 

Tutorials
Your grade in tutorial is calculated as 
5% Artifact score
10% TA Check outs/Attendance

More information is below:
Group Activities: You will meet in groups once per week on Friday, Monday or Tuesday.  Group work will consist of various activities but often we will do either a conceptual tutorial or problem solving practice.  We will take short quizzes four times during the term.
Group Structure: You will be placed into learning groups of 3-6 people which will be changed at midterm.   
Learning Artifacts:  After your tutorial you will submit a written record of your own work on the tutorial, or your notes from your group meeting, as a “learning artifact".  Artifacts are submitted through CrowdMark as soon as possible after your tutorial session, but no later than Friday at 11:30  pm following your tutorial.  You are not allowed to submit the written work of a group member as part of your artifact. You talk and think with your group members, but you only submit your own work and notes. Work must not be typed unless an AAS accommodation is in place. If you miss a meeting you can still submit your own work as your artifact for credit. 
TA Check-outs:  Students should not leave their group meeting without having seen a TA for (at least) attendance. Group members who are absent receive a score of zero for the check-out.  There are no make-ups.  
Important Note Regarding TA check-out: For students who are present at the tutorial, these “check-outs” will be scored on a scale of 1,2,3,4 out of 4.  The default score will be a 3 out of 4 or 75%.  Most groups in PHYS122 will be quite good and will still get this score of 75%. Only the very best groups (top 10%) each session will get a 4 out of 4 or 100%. Groups who are not collaborating effectively will be reduced in score to a 2 or even a 1 in some cases. Please try to be collaborative and productive in your groups and don't expect a 100% in tutorial.  The typical score for tutorial is about 83%.
Missed Tutorial Work
If you have a VIF, 48-hour absence or other excused absence, we will drop both your TA Check-out/Attendance and Artifact scores for the week. In fairness to those students who do not get sick, we will drop one score for TA Eval and one for Artifact for everyone.  A VIF is not required for this, and a VIF or 48-hour absence does not get you additional drops.  If you have VIFs covering more than one missed tutorial, please contact Professor Cummings at the end of the term.

Academic Integrity Comments:   Your answers for artifacts and tests must be hand-written unless you have accommodations. For tutorial artifacts, you work as a group in tutorial, but you write and submit your own artifact.  This can be done on a tablet if you wish, so long as it is hand-written.  

You can work with the internet and peers as much as you like for the MasteringPhysics assignments.  We encourage you to work with peers on those problem sets.  But, you must ultimately submit your own answers.  And, don't confuse getting the assignment done with understanding the material! You won't be cheating if you get tons of help, but you also won't be getting ready for the tests.
