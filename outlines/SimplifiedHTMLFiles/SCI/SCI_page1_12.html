Course Description

SCI 300:

These special topics courses will cover material relevant to all students in the Faculty of Science. Course content is not specific to an individual program.

View requirements for SCI 300

Graduating students from the Faculty of Science will be expected to navigate what’s been called a VUCA world; one marked by volatility, uncertainty, complexity and ambiguity (VUCA). To thrive in this world, graduates will need to be self-directed learners able to adapt to changing landscapes, develop resilience, grapple with ambiguity, interdependencies, and inter-relationships. 

 The course uses the student-led, individually created course (SLICC) framework allowing students to co-create their learning journey by intentionally and explicitly planning for the development of key lifelong learning competencies (listed below and outlined in the Future Ready Talent Framework) while volunteering or working in their chosen setting. 

To participate in this course, students MUST have secured the position before the start of the term and ensure that they are able to complete a minimum of 100 hours (10 or more hours per week) during the term working on developing the key competency they’ve chosen.  

The future ready talent framework (FRTF) defines competencies as a combination of skills, abilities and knowledge.  Key competencies are established for successfully navigating the emerging global workplace.  For this course, students should select one key competency from the list below to develop during their work placement. 

The key competency is then developed using the SLICC framework.  This will guide students through the process of planning and executing how they will successfully demonstrate that they have met the learning outcomes for the course that are listed below. 

 

FRTF Key Competencies 

Initiative: 

  • Seek learning opportunities, both formal and informal; 
  • Take initiatives to connect with others about career opportunities. 

Communication: 

  • Articulate thoughts and ideas clearly in oral and written forms to persons inside and outside of your organization; 
  • Listen actively and ask questions to better understand other person’s viewpoints. 

Career Development: 

  • Set career learning goals and identify opportunities to achieve those goals; 
  • Explore how personal values and interests align with occupational demands, tasks, goals and environments. 

Information Literacy: 

  • Synthesize data from multiple sources into meaningful information; 
  • Present this synthesis to members of your organization to seek feedback. 

Technological Agility: 

  • Apply a new technology to achieve a better result; 
  • Advocate for the use of innovative technologies. 

Implementation and Collaboration: 

  • Structure, coordinate, organize your projects and tasks; 
  • Share responsibility with other team members to solve the problems of others. 

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
(Analysis) - analyze their initial skills in a chosen key competency area and reflect on the value this competency can bring to a successful experience.
(Application) - successfully apply a range of relevant skills towards a goal and identify areas of personal improvement.
(Demonstration) - demonstrate skills improvement in the chosen key competency area.
(Recognition) - recognize the different approaches needed to overcome obstacles with positive outlook and perseverance.
(Evaluation) - evaluate and critically reflect on the approach used to develop skills in the chosen key competency area.

Platforms to be used in SCI300

LEARN (learn.uwaterloo.ca) - LEARN will be used as the primary platform for accessing resources associated with this course.  It will also be used for announcements.  Please adjust your LEARN settings to ensure you receive notifications when news about the course is posted.  To see how to do this check this uWaterloo IST Knowledge Base webpage

PebblePad - Weekly Reflective Writing, Proposal, Midterm Reflective Report, Final Reflective Report will all be recorded on PebblePad, which will be accessed through a link in the course LEARN site.  Many important resources, examples and tips are available through your Pebblepad Workbook.  Please make sure to familiarise yourself with this platform.

Microsoft Teams - The course has a MS Teams site.  This allows communication between students and with the instructor in a University sanctioned space.  Given the self-directed nature of the final 2/3 of the course, this is a valuable tool to remain connected with other course members and the instructor.  Periodic check-ins and additional resources may be posted here.  Please ensure you have notifications enabled so as not to miss anything or make sure you check-in regularly.

 

Course Deliverables

The main course deliverables that contribute to your overall course grade are:

Weekly Reflections - These are entries in your PebblePad workbook that contain evidence of your thinking and learning as you develop your FRTF key competency area.  They are the building blocks on which your Midterm and Final Reflective Reports are written.  A workshop on developing your reflective writing ability takes place in Week 2

Proposal - In this deliverable you are required to explain how you will achieve each of the learning outcomes in this course with respect to the FRTF key competency that you are developing, and how you will demonstrate that you have done so.

Midterm Reflective Report - In this report you are required to explain, with evidence, how you are achieving each of the learning outcomes in this course with respect to the FRTF key competency that you are developing.   

Final Reflective Report - In this summative report you are required to explain, with evidence, how you have achieved each of the learning outcomes in this course with respect to the FRTF key competency that you set out to develop.

Deadlines for all deliverables are provided in the course calendar on LEARN.

Notes on Course Grading

  • Overall grade for Weekly Reflections will not simply be an aggregate of the individual grades.  The grade assigned will assess and demonstrate your growth in learning to critically and deeply reflect on an issue.
  • The Midterm Reflective Report grade may be transferred to the Final Reflective Report if there is evidence that feedback from the Midterm Report has been used to significantly improve performance.
  • Because of the subjective nature of grading in this course, the overall course grade will be rounded to the nearest decile (e.g. 60, 70, 80, 90, 100).

In the case of illness:  

If students are not able to complete the full 100 hours in a term, they can submit VIF for this absence, and contact the instructor to determine the timeline for completion of the course.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will be discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
