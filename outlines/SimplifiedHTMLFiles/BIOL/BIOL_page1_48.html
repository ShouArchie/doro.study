Course Description

BIOL 449:

How the health of human populations is influenced by microbial communities. Aspects of the epidemiology of infectious diseases, factors influencing the emergence of microbial pathogens and antibiotic-resistant strains, means to control undesired microbial populations in habitats such as water, biodegradable materials, and food will be discussed. Approaches to the regulation and monitoring of microbial agents will also be examined.

View requirements for BIOL 449

Other current topical issues in infectious diseases, including zoonosis, safe drinking water, and the relevance of One Health in disease surveillance, will also be explored in this course.

Prereq: BIOL 241

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Describe the activities of microorganisms and their influence on the health of human populations
Identify and assess how society makes efforts to understand, prevent and control infectious diseases
Calculate the measurements of illnesses and death in human populations due to infectious diseases
Identify approaches to the cure and prevention of infectious diseases
Apply knowledge of zoonosis to emerging infectious diseases and assess their threat to human populations
Explain microbial risk analysis through the example of drinking water
Assess strategies and methodologies used to control infectious diseases within the field of public health microbiology

Student Assessment

Component Value
Weekly quizzes 15%
Group activity (case study) 20%
Group evaluation 5%
Midterm 20%
Final exam 40%

Weekly quizzes: Students are expected to actively engage with the course material through weekly activities. Each quiz will address the topic covered during that week and must be completed exclusively in class. Failure to comply with this policy may result in a grade of 0% for that week or for the entire quiz component of the assessment, at the instructor's discretion.

Group activity: To ensure students comprehend the application of course content in real-life situations, there will be participation in a group-based case study. These case studies are related to the topics covered in the course. You will enhance your understanding of course content by discussing your group-selected case study together with your group members. Details are posted on LEARN. Refer to the “Assignment Instructions” tab for additional information, including due dates.

Group evaluation: To ensure that all group members actively participate in the case study, each member will have the opportunity to rate the participation of their fellow group members.

Exams: Exams will consist of multiple choice and multi-select questions. To answer these questions, you will need to understand rather than just memorize the course content. Please see the course LEARNING OBJECTIVES above that will inspire my test questions. You may bring a non-programmable calculator. The final exam will be cumulative. Missed exams (midterm or final) will result in a grade of 0, except under special circumstances outlined below.

Final grades will not be rounded up beyond university policy and mathematical conventions. 

Make-up: There will be no make-up for the midterm. If the midterm is missed, due to illness, its weighting may only be added to the final examination upon submission of a verification of illness, which must be submitted prior to the midterm. 

Make-up final exams will only be considered in cases of illness; no other reasons will be accepted. A missed final exam will disqualify a student from receiving a final grade and may result in an incomplete (INC), at the discretion of the instructor. The student will only be eligible to retake the exam in the next term the course is taught to receive the final grade.

When midterm or final exam is missed due to illness, the student should be prepared to provide a verification of illness. This information will be used to determine if the student is eligible for consideration described above. The dates affected and degree of incapacitation will be used to determine follow-up action. Students should ensure that their health care provider indicates this type of information on the medical certificate. For example, “was seen” or “attended emergency Dept on...” are not acceptable. The University of Waterloo verification of illness form is available online. Any University of Waterloo student can go to University of Waterloo Health Services.

Although illness may constitute a valid reason for missing an exam, it does not automatically entitle a student to an alternate exam.

Additional assessment details may be posted to the course LEARN site.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, may be used in this course with proper documentation, citation, and acknowledgement. Permitted uses of and expectations for using GenAI will discussed in class and outlined on assignment instructions. 

Recommendations for how to cite generative AI in student work at the University of Waterloo may be found through the Library: https://subjectguides.uwaterloo.ca/chatgpt_generative_ai. Please be aware that generative AI is known to falsify references to other work and may fabricate facts and inaccurately express ideas. GenAI generates content based on the input of other human authors and may therefore contain inaccuracies or reflect biases. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. Exercise caution when using large portions of content from AI sources, especially images. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence 

You are accountable for the content and accuracy of all work you submit in this class, including any supported by generative AI.
