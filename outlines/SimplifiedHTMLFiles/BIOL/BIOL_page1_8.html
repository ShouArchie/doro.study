Course Description

BIOL 220:

An introduction to plant diversity, and the anatomy and physiology of vascular plants. The course will include a description of major cell and tissue types, and their organization in roots, stems, and leaves. Topics such as the processes of water and ion uptake, photosynthesis, long distance transport, and growth regulation will also be covered in lectures and through laboratory experiments.

View requirements for BIOL 220

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the importance of the relationship between plants and humans in the environment and appreciate the species richness and diversity of plants
Explain the transition from green algae to land plants and be able to recognize the distinctive features that distinguish each group
Describe the characteristics that distinguish plants from other living organisms
Recognize and identify various cell types and how they account for the structure and function of tissues and organs
Understand how plant function: photosynthesis, water movement, hormones, reproduction, tropism, abiotic and biotic interactions

Term Tests: The format for the term tests will be a) multiple choice, b) labelling/fill in the blank and c) short answer questions. The term tests are NOT cumulative, each will test one module (three modules total). Term Test 1 will include information from Lectures 1-7. Term Test 2 will include information from Lectures 8-14. Term Test 3 will include material from Lectures 15-21. If a student completes all three term tests, the lowest test score will be dropped and the final grade calculated with the two highest scoring tests. 

Policy for Missed Term Tests: You have the following three options for declaring an absence, in all three cases, you are expected to notify the  course instructor before or within 24 hour of the missed term test, failure to do so can result in an INC and zero (0) grade for missed test:

  1. Short-term Absence: you can self-declare, through Quest, an absence for 48 hours or less, during the Formal Lecture Period; a short-term absence can be self-declared for any reason that prevents a student from meeting their academic obligations; students are permitted only one self-declared short-term absence accommodation per academic term, no documentation is required. This DOES NOT apply to labs.
  2. Covid-19 Related Absence: students can self-declare, through Quest, an absence for 10 days or less if they test positive for Covid-19 or related symptoms. Any participation needs to be consistent across all courses; no documentation is required. Can only be declared once a term.
  3. VIF Submission: for absences that do not meet the short-term absence criteria, for short-term absences that exceed the maximum number permitted per term, or for (retroactive) reports of illness or other extenuating circumstances, students must submit a Verification of Illness Form (VIF) and/or other supporting documentation electronically to the Science Undergraduate Office (SUO). For extenuating circumstances that are not illness related, for which the VIF is not appropriate, other official documentation is necessary (for example: plane/train/bus tickets, court documents, police reports, death certificates/obituaries/letters from funeral directors, counsellor letters). Science maintains a page with up-to-date guidelines for Accommodations for Illness or Extenuating Circumstances and can be reviewed as necessary.

Note: filing documentation with the SUO does NOT automatically excuse the missing of a test/assignment. The course instructor will consider the information provided on the documentation when deciding whether a student should be excused. Generally, no make-up term tests will be given. If you have missed a term test for reasons that can be validated, the value of the missed test will be distributed proportionally across the other two term tests. To meet course requirements, you MUST complete two(2) term tests to pass this course.

Bonus Quizzes:  Every Wednesday at 12:20, excluding Week 4, 9 and 13, bonus quizzes will be posted to LEARN and available until Friday at 19:00. If a student is unable to participate in a week's lab due to documented absence, the weight value of the missed course element will be redistributed to the remaining preparation assignments.
