Course Description

BIOL 240:

Introduction to the biology of bacterial and archaeal organisms. Topics include cell structure and function, methods of cultivation, genetics, phylogeny and taxonomy, and metabolic and genetic diversity.

View requirements for BIOL 240

Topics this course will cover will include: microbial cell structure and function, methods of cultivating microbes, the influence of environmental parameters on microbial activity, regulation of microbial cell activity, genetics, phylogeny and taxonomy, an overview of metabolic and genetic diversity in the microbial world. The course is intended to provide basic knowledge of the world of microorganisms and consideration of how we study the activities of these small inhabitants of the planet.

The knowledge gained in this course will also prepare you to succeed in more advanced courses in microbiology, microbial ecology, and molecular biology.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain the impact of microbiology on the evolution of life and on human society.
Describe the diversity of bacteria, archaea, and microbial eukaryotes and differentiate between the three domains of microbial life based on key characteristics.
Explain how viruses were discovered and identify key viral characteristics.
List nutritional categories of microbial metabolism and identify appropriate methods for cultivating microorganisms from these categories.
Provide examples of notable microbial species and describe their importance for industry, the environment, or human health.
Explain how microorganisms regulate gene expression in order to compete for limited energy and nutrients.
List methods and mechanisms for exchange and manipulation of microbial DNA.
Describe how modern molecular methods have impacted microbiology.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Assignments 12%

Final Examination 25%

The format for the course assessments can be found on the BIOL 240 LEARN site. If you miss an assessment you will receive a grade of zero (0). 

If the assessment was missed due to illness or another valid, documentable reason, you may be granted accommodation through the following process:

  1. Contact your instructor within 24 hours of the missed assessment.  Emails can be received 24 hours per day. Accommodations for missed assessments will not be granted if you do not make contact via email.
  2. If you are ill, please follow the Faculty of Science illness guidelines. There is a self-declaration system for COVID-19 related illness or short-term absence. For non-COVID-19 illness please have submit a UW Verification of Illness Form (VIF). The VIF must not be retroactive (i.e. cannot have note completed after the date of the exam). Severe illness is the only excuse for missing an exam. Note that instructors are under no obligation to accept your medical documentation, and they should not be viewed as a “get out of jail card”, if an instructor has reason to believe you were not actually ill, it can be rejected.
  3. For absences not related to illness, please consult the FAQ on the Science webpage for suggested documentation.
  4. Submit your documentation to the Science Undergraduate Office (SUO) within one week from the exam date and complete their process. An automated Illness Certificate will be sent to your instructor by email.
  5. If your VIF or documentation is not submitted within one week, or is not valid, a grade of zero (0) will be recorded for the missed assessment.

The weight of the missed term test(s) or Achieve Online Assignment(s) will be added to the final exam weight once proper documentation has been submitted.  There are no make-up or alternative term tests. Student travel plans are not considered acceptable grounds for granting an alternative examination time (see https://uwaterloo.ca/registrar/final-examinations).

Academic Integrity

The weekly quizzes are NOT open book and must be done alone, without any outside help whatsoever. If not, you will be in breach of academic integrity. Specifically, do not use your course notes to help you while completing the quiz, do not discuss the quiz with other classmates, and do not participate in any quiz group work. Please be advised that it is considered an academic offence to share copies of past quizzes or quiz questions. Saving a copy of a quiz or quiz question is also an academic offence.
