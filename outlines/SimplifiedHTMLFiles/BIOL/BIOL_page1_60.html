Course Description

BIOL 610:

A critical evaluation of selected research topics in evolution and the diversity of life and practical applications. Topics may include macroevolution, population genetics, evolution of behaviours, ecological evolution, phylogenetic reconstruction and/or phylogeny of the eukaryotic and prokaryotic organisms.

View requirements for BIOL 610

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Students will be exposed to a variety of topics in evolution through reading and class discussions centered on the book: The Rise and Fall of the Dinosaurs, A new history of the lost world; a term paper on an evolution topic of their choice, as well as an in-class presentation.

Self-reflection on The Rise and Fall of the Dinosaurs. A new history of the lost world

 

Due on Monday April 7th, 2025 -  by 7:00 pm (email to instructor).  Reflection will be no more than two pages double spaced.  More will be shared in class.

 

Presentation and Term Paper:

 

  • Select one article from the Annual Review of Ecology, Evolution and Systematics or one article from Trends in Ecology and Evolution that have been published no earlier than 2017.
  • Please select your papers by Monday February 3rd and share the title and the PDF with the instructor. 
  • Presentation and class discussion on presentation (during the last day of class):
    • Present a 15-20 minute overview of your selected paper to the class 
    •  Your presentations should include the following:
      1. An overview of the background to the topic
      2. A summary of the important aspects of the paper
      3. Any important research, if applicable, that you feel the authors should have included but did not
      4. Your opinion on whether the author(s) accurately and adequately summarized the topic. Did they have any biases, such as overstating their own research and understating the work of others?
      5. A summary of the conclusions, or future directions provided by the author(s)
      6. A summary of any important research that has been done on the topic after the paper was published (a cited literature search will be helpful here). 
      7. Do you feel that the authors review had an impact on research conducted on the topic after the publication of their paper?
      8. After you present your paper, lead a 10-15 minute class discussion on it 
  • Term Paper:
    • Write a 9-12 page (maximum, double spaced) review of your selected paper, using the same guidelines above. A copy of your paper should be submitted to the instructor (by email) no later than Monday April 7, 2025 by 7:00 pm
