Course Description

BIOL 211:

An introduction to the structure, evolution, and development of vertebrate organ systems, explored through lectures and laboratory exercises.

View requirements for BIOL 211

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Understand the diversity of vertebrates that exist in the world
Know some of the adaptations in each of the major groups (jawless fishes, cartilaginous fishes, bony fishes, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals)
Know some of the anatomy, physiology, reproductive strategies, behaviour and ecology of vertebrates
Be familiar with the basic techniques of dissection through laboratory sessions

Administrative Policy

Please see  syllabus on the course Learn site for complete course description. 

 

  1. You must achieve an average grade of at least 45% in the unit tests to pass the course.  If the average of your unit tests is less than 45.0%, your final course grade will be the average of the unit test scores.
  2. Please check for course announcements on learn which might also be made in the lectures.
  3. Unit tests will be held during classtime.  However, in the event the university mandates online delivery, tests will be administered in Learn and they will be ‘closed book’ assessments- no aids of any sort or collaboration will be permitted.  To access the tests on Learn you will be required to pass an Academic Integrity quiz before each test.
  4. Unit tests may include any combination of multiple choice, true/false, matching and questions requiring written responses.
  5. Unit tests will not be returned to the student. 
  6. If you miss a unit test or a quiz due to illness or another valid, documentable reason, you may be granted accommodation through the following process:
    1. Contact your instructor via email within 24 hours of the missed exam. 
    2. Follow instructions at the Faculty of Science webpage on “Consideration for absences due to illness or other extenuating circumstances” at this link: https://uwaterloo.ca/science-undergraduate-office/academic-policies-and-procedures/accommodation-illness-or-other-extenuating-circumstances
    3. At the instructors discretion, grades for no more than one missed unit test may be transferred to the other assessments (there are no make-up unit tests).  Grades for the final exam cannot be transferred to other assessments.
    4. Student travel plans are not considered acceptable grounds for granting an accommodation.
