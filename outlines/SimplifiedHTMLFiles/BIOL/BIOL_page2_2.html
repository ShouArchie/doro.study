Course Description

BIOL 636:

Discussion of current advances in selected topics in immunology. The areas to be covered will include cell-mediated immunity, humoral immunity, comparative immunology, and other topics of current interest.

View requirements for BIOL 636

  1. Classes comprise student-led presentations and/or student-led discussions. 
    1. Research paper - (~50 min) on the research paper, providing enough general background (including techniques) to make the presentation clear. The presenter will then lead the discussion on the results and conclusions of the paper
    2. Lecture on chosen topic students will choose a specific aspect of immunity (i.e interleukins, major histocompatibility complex, etc) and present a seminar (~50 min)
  2. Based the research paper topic, students will prepare an NSERC style grant application proposing a program of study in immunology.

Guidelines for research presentations

  1. Paper selection: Students should choose papers that have been published in the last 5 years. Papers should have 4-7 figures and contain at least 3 or 4 different techniques. As the term progresses, students should aim to select papers with different topics and techniques from those papers previously presented. 
  2. Presentation: Should include an introduction to the topic, a methods section in which the presenter should describe the methods as well as their strengths and limitations (only if methods have not been previously covered), present the results from the paper and a summary of the major findings of the paper. Students should also be able to describe one or two future experiments (and the methods that would be used) that could extend the study.
  3. Participation: All students are expected to bring questions/discussion about the methods used, their limitations, strengths and alternative methods that could have been used to address the same questions. Students should be able to interpret figures and determine if all the proper controls are in place, whether the authors have interpreted the results or if there are alternative interpretations, whether the results support the conclusions/findings presented in the discussion. Students should also generate ideas for future studies that could extend the paper being presented.

 

Guidelines for literature review paper

  1. Maximum of 10 double spaced pages (not including references), 2.5 cm margins (sides, top, bottom) in Times New Roman, 12 pt font.
  2. Should include a title, name of student, abstract, introduction, body sections (however many are needed), and conclusions section which identifies the impact of the research, current knowledge gaps and future directions in the field. 
  3. References can be in any style, however they must be consistent. 

 

Guidelines for NSERC grant 

  1. The topic of the NSERC grant will be based off of the paper selected for the research presentation.
  2. Students should refer to the sample NSERC grant provided on the course UW Learn webpage as a guide during preparation of their grant.
  3. NSERC grant: should be a maximum of 5 single spaced pages (not including references), with 2.5 cm margins (sides, top, bottom) in Times New Roman, 12 pt font. The sections of the grant should include background, objectives, hypothesis, specific aims, a description of these aims, and an impact of research section. Figures are encouraged! Depending on the approach students take, it may be appropriate to include a progress report section. References can be in any style, however the format must be consistent. No budget or budget justification is needed.
