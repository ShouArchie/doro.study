Course Description

BIOL 365:

This course covers bioinformatics methods with an emphasis on analysis of high-throughput "-omics" data. Topics include analysis of genome-scale gene and protein expression, construction of species and gene trees from molecular sequence data, and analysis of biological systems using bioinformatics tools. Practical experience with bioinformatics tools and datasets will be provided through computational laboratory exercises.

View requirements for BIOL 365

We will be using the R statistics package for some aspects of this course. You will need access to a suitable computer and be able to download R and Rstudio software packages and have access some web-based tools.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Use computational tools for the analysis of biological data
Understand the underlying theory and assumptions of bioinformatics tools
Analyze DNA, RNA and protein sequences
Understand and compare high throughput methods for DNA sequencing
Use bioinformatics tools within the statistics package R

Tentative Course Schedule

Week 1 - Introduction, biological sequence databases 

Week 2 - Alignments and scoring functions

Week 3 - BLAST and search algorithms 

Week 4 - Genomes and gene finding

Week 5 - Multiple sequence alignments and HMMs 

Week 6 - High throughput sequencing and assembly 

Week 7 - Reading week

Week 8 - Midterm (in class): Mon Feb 24th

Week 9 - Phylogenetic trees, models and likelihood 

Week 10 - Genome variation

Week 11 - Gene expression and differential expression analysis 

Week 12 - Clustering and descriptive stats

Week 13 - Current and emerging bioinformatics tools + review

Final exam - in person during the final exam period.
