Course Description

BIOL 359:

A study of the processes of evolution; the differentiation of populations and the origin of new forms of life.

View requirements for BIOL 359

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Describe and explain the various agents of evolution
Demonstrate how these processes may result in adaptation, speciation and extinction
Analyze and interpret cases of evolution
Apply evolutionary concepts of conservation biology

Administrative Policy

Please see course syllabus for complete course description

 

  1. To receive a passing grade on this course you must pass the final exam.  If you do not pass the final exam the final course grade will be your final exam grade.
  2. Please check for course announcements on learn which might also be made in the lectures.
  3. Unit tests will be held during class time. In the event the university mandates online delivery, tests will be administered in Learn and they will be ‘closed book’ assessments- no aids of any sort or collaboration will be permitted.  To access the tests on Learn you will be required to pass an Academic Integrity quiz before each test.
  4. Quizzes will be held remotely.
  5. Quizzes, unit tests and the final exam may include any combination of multiple choice, true/false, fill-in-the-blanks, matching and questions requiring written responses, possibly calculations.
  6. Quizzes, unit tests and final exam will not be returned to the student. 
  7. If you miss a unit test or a quiz due to illness or another valid, documentable reason, you may be granted accommodation through the following process:
    1. Contact your instructor via email within 24 hours of the missed exam. 
    2. Follow instructions at the Faculty of Science webpage on “Consideration for absences due to illness or other extenuating circumstances” at this link: https://uwaterloo.ca/science-undergraduate-office/academic-policies-and-procedures/accommodation-illness-or-other-extenuating-circumstances
    3. At the instructors discretion, grades for no more than one missed unit test may be transferred to the other assessments (there are no make-up unit tests).  Grades for the final exam cannot be transferred to other assessments.
    4. Student travel plans are not considered acceptable grounds for granting an accommodation.
