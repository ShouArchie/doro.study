Course Description

BIOL 465:

Analysis of proteins using structural and bioinformatics approaches. Topics explored in lectures and labs include methods of protein structure determination and visualization, structure comparison and prediction, prediction of protein function and interactions, molecular dynamics, and protein design.

View requirements for BIOL 465

From the course calendar: Biol 465 –Structural Bioinformatics

Analysis of proteins using structural and bioinformatics approaches. Topics include methods of protein structure determination and visualization, structure comparison and prediction, prediction of protein function and interactions, molecular dynamics, and protein design. [Offered: W]

Prereq: BIOL 266/366 or BIOL 365; CHEM 233 or CHEM 237 or BCS Bioinformatics specialization or BMath(CS)Bioinformatics specialization

Basically, structural biology spans two interwoven approaches lying along a continuum. At one end, experimental structure determination and at the other, purely computational approaches.

The goal is to blend these two extremes and examine complementary nature of the two

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Have a deeper understanding of protein structure
Be familiar with the methods of structure determination
Be familiar with the software for protein structure visualization and analysis
Understand how various bioinformatics methods can be used for protein functional analysis
Have the ability to critically evaluate current peer reviewed literature and data and information sources related to structural biology
Developed their ability to design, carry out, and analyze an experiment utilizing structural informatics tools.
Enhanced scientific writing, presentation, and evaluation skills

Other Materials

Kritik peer assessment will be used as a component of the assessment for the group presentation. You will be required to obtain this software.

The text is not required but for those with a deeper interest in structural biology or limited background in biochemistyry, it is a great resource for understanding the concepts presented. There will also be copies of the text on reserve in the Davis Library.

In the tutorials we will be making use of several pieces of software including the molecular visualization and analysis software package ChimeraX and the crystallographic refinement and model building package CCP4. These software packages are available as downloads at no cost to the student and run on most platforms (Windows, Mac, Linux). Other software utilized in the tutorials will be accessed via web interfaces.

Additional material will be drawn from peer reviewed literature and online resources.

Course materials used in lectures will be available through Waterloo LEARN (http://learn.uwaterloo.ca), or through links provided to specific websites.

NOTE: You should have a laptop computer on which you can download and run ChimeraX and other software. If you do not have a suitable computer available, please contact me to see if other arrangements can be made.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Tutorial Assignments 25%
Midterm Exam (during class) 25%
Group Presentation 20%
Final Report 30%

As a general rule, late assignments will be penalized 10% per day or part thereof. Assignments submitted after 48h will not be accepted.

Midterm Exam

The midterm exam will be held during class time on Feb 12th, 2025 @ 11:30 am. The format will be short answer.

Presentations

Presentation material will be based on a recent peer-reviewed journal article of your choosing. This will be a group presentation of ~4-5 individuals. Kritic will be used for peer evaluations which will form a part of the overall presentation grade. Further details will be provided in-class.

Final Report

The final report will take the place of a final written exam. The report is intended as a summary of an independent project of your own design that will be developed throughout the term using skills developed over the course of the semester that will involve the bioinformatic analysis of a protein/protein family. The due date of the report will be April 11th.

Assignment Screening
