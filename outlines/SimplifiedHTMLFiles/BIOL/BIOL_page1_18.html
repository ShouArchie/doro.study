Course Description

BIOL 302:

A hierarchical approach to biological structure with an emphasis on functional morphology. Starting with the cell, the fundamental unit of structure and function, the material progressively develops how cells organize to form tissues such as epithelium, connective tissue, and muscle. Emphasis on how these tissue building blocks cooperate to form the major organs and organ systems of the human body. The laboratory component includes preparation and staining of tissues for microscopy, and use of virtual tools for viewing high resolution tissue scans.

View requirements for BIOL 302

COURSE SUMMARY

This course will introduce you to the cellular components and building blocks that make up tissues and organs. You will learn how the structure and function of cells (microscopic) can be applied to understanding and identifying tissues (macroscopic) through exposure to histology slides.

 

PREREQUISITES

BIOL 302 requires BIOL 130 and BIOL 273. It is assumed that students have a basic understanding of cell biology and cell function. Students that don’t find this course very challenging. Make sure you know your cell biology, particularly how to identify a nucleus.

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
1. Recognize distinguishing features of basic tissue & cell structure
2. Connect the relationship between structure & function in cells & tissues
3. Identify unknown tissues through recognition of cells and their functions

Administrative Policy

ATTENDENCE POLICY - LABS & READINGS (BIOLUCIDA)

Do the Lab work and Readings. Lab Attendance is required to submit your final report. It is important that you look at slides and look at as many as possible. Most of the images in your text are very clear and you will see markedly different views of the same tissues in the Lab/Biolucida. Ask questions. Work as a group. Take your time and find everything more than once. It is in the Lab and the online slides (Biolucida) that you will learn to identify cells and tissues. Lecture tells you what they look like, how to find them, what they do. Biolucida, the Reading assignments, and the Lab complement Lectures. Skipping/Missing either will make this course very difficult. In the past lab attendance and slide viewing has directly correlated with grades. 

Note: Tissue slides are now available online at much higher resolution than in-person viewing on a microscope in previous years. Through Biolucida, you have access to other online libraries. Strongly recommend that you use them too.

CHEATING

There are no excuses and no tolerance for cheating. Cheating includes searching for answers (or even ‘double-checking’ them) during online exams, tests, assessments, etc. This also includes discussing the course through any means during the exam period (text, reddit, carrier pigeon, etc.). So, if the exam/quiz is open for 24 h, you cannot discuss it with anyone until it’s over. If you have to ask yourself if what you’re doing may constitute as cheating, it’s probably considered cheating.
