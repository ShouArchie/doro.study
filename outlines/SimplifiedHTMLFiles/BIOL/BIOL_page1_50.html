Course Description

BIOL 450:

Ecological processes and evolutionary adaptation are explored in the world's largest and most diverse ecosystems. The major ocean habitats will be characterized, stressing their importance as resources, moderators of climate and reservoirs of biodiversity.

View requirements for BIOL 450

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Know and describe the ecology and evolution of marine organisms in the context of the habitat that they occupy and the selection pressures that have led to these adaptations.
Explain and illustrate major oceanic processes (e.g. light, water, currents, temperature, primary productivity) and how they result in the current distribution and adaptations of marine organisms.
Understand and discuss the major impacts that human activities have on marine organisms and the habitats that they occupy.
Understand how global governments interact to make decisions around climate impacts.
Have an appreciation for the immense diversity of life in Earth's largest ecosystem.

Tentative Course Schedule

The course consists of two 80 minutes long lectures each week and will roughly follow chapters from the textbook: Marine Biology: Function, Biodiversity, Ecology, Sixth Edition, by Jeffrey S. Levinton. Before the midterm and the final exam there will be a recap session in class. A few lectures may also be delivered by guest lecturers on topics that are of relevance to the course material.

Student Assessment

Component Value
Midterm 25%
Final Exam 45%
Group Project 30%

Exams Format- The format for both the midterm and final exam may be multiple choice, true/false, matching, and short answer questions. The midterm will assess material covered until reading week. The final will cover material from the entire course (i.e., pre and after midterm lectures) and will be cumulative. Check announcements on LEARN closer to the dates of the two exams for further details.

Practice questions- Practice questions for the midterm and final exam will be posted in advance on LEARN so that you can test your understanding and see the style of questions that will be in the exams. I will first post the questions and give you a few days to go over them, and then post the answers. 

Recap sessions- We will have recap sessions during the course, one before reading week prior to the midterm, and one at the end of the course in the second half prior to the final exam. These recap sessions will be during class time and are an opportunity for us to go again together over concepts that require further clarification. We will also go over the outline of the midterm and final exam during those sessions. To make the best use of time during those sessions, I ask that students email me specific questions that they have in advance prior to the session so that I can bring the relevant slides back to class and answer specifically on what is being asked. Note that these recap sessions ARE NOT intended to go over again entire lecture topics so please be specific with what you are asking (you can point to specific slide numbers if easier). If I don’t get a lot of questions for these recap sessions, then we will move over to new material so please make sure to attend those sessions. 

Misusing the midterm- Absence from the midterm will result in the transfer of the weight to the final exam. A make-up midterm will not be available. Students do not need to submit a VIF for a missed midterm. The final exam must be written and will be cumulative. 

Missing the Final Exam

  1. Contact me by email within 24-48 hours of the missed final exam. 
  2. If you are ill, please follow the Faculty of Science illness guidelines. There is a self-declaration system for COVID-19 related illness. For non-COVID-19 illness please submit a UW Verification of Illness Form (VIF). The VIF must not be retroactive (i.e. cannot have note completed after the date of the assessment). Severe illness is the only excuse for missing an assessment. 
  3. For absences not related to illness (e.g. extenuating circumstances), please consult the FAQ on the Science webpage for suggested documentation.
  4. Submit your documentation to the Science Undergraduate Office within one week from the exam date and complete their process. An automated Illness Certificate will be sent to me by email. Please email me also a copy of the VIF.
  5. If your VIF is not submitted within one week, or is not valid, a grade of zero (0) will be recorded for the missed exam.

Administrative Policy

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills, such as [fill this in with your discipline-specific skills]. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
