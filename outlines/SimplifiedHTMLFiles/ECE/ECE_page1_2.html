Course Description

ECE 109:

This course will help students understand the relationship between the microstructure of common semiconductor and optoelectronic materials, and the electrical, optical, and magnetic properties exhibited by the materials. Topics include structure of atoms, models of the atom, electronic configuration, band theory, atomic bonding, dielectric properties, optical transparency, magnetic properties, molecular bonding, metals and alloys, and activation energy.

View requirements for ECE 109

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Explain electrical, magnetic and optical properties of materials in terms of atomic structure, interatomic bonding and bonding energy.
Identify the fundamental crystal structures and the association to atom diffusion for various solid materials.
Determine the electrical and the magnetic properties of conductors, insulators and semiconductors.
Understand the relationship between the microstructure of the materials and the electrical, optical, and magnetic properties exhibited by the material.
Apply the appropriate laws and conditions to solve problems in the field of materials chemistry, electrical engineering and chemical engineering.

Assignments

Assignments are designed to reinforce content covered during the lectures and tutorials. These multi-choice assignments will be administered with the start of a new unit through LEARN quizzes tool and can be accessed as many times as the student wish but have only one time attempt for submission. In addition, the students must submit the FULL SOLUTION on LEARN Dropbox. No late assignments will be accepted! Each assignment will be open for completion until the scheduled due date for approximately two (or three)-week-period, depending on the unit length. The assignments are evenly weighted from the total assignment mark. After the due date, the multi-choice assignments will be marked automatically through LEARN and the grades will be posted on the gradebook in LEARN. Some full solutions submission will be screened and checked by the TAs. The students will have access to the solution of the assignments after the grades are released.

Assignments due are at 11:59 PM on Fridays: (1) Jan. 24; (2) Feb. 7; (3) Mar. 14; & (4) Apr 4.

ONLINE Quizzes and Final Exam

There will be two multiple-choice-question quizzes that will be administered using the LEARN quizzes tool. In addition, the students must submit the FULL SOLUTION on LEARN Dropbox. The quizzes will be available for 24-hour window, it will open at 12:00 am EST on the scheduled date and closed at 11:59 pm EST on the same day. Students will have two hours to submit their responses from the time they start the quiz. The quiz cannot be stopped or paused once it has been started. In general questions on the quizzes will not be answered during quiz time, however, there is exemption for technical questions. If you find that a quiz question is unclear, email your assumptions and a screenshot of the unclear quiz question to the instructor and/or the TAs within an hour of completing the quiz. The quizzes are evenly weighted to form the total quiz mark. Students can view their quiz marks on the gradebook in LEARN. No make-up quiz for missed one will be conducted. The mark of the missed quiz with an acceptable reason will be shifted toward the other quiz. During quiz you may consult your textbook, handwritten course notes, and materials posted in the course LEARN site. You may use any type of calculator. Use of any other resource is prohibited. You may not communicate directly or indirectly with any person except the course instructor or TA. Final exam will be determined by the registrar office and will happen during the final exam period.

Administrative Policy

TIPS FOR SUCCESS

To be successful in this course you must be engaged with the course content both in and out of class. It is highly recommended that you:

• Complete relevant textbook readings and review your class notes.

• Attend the lectures and tutorials.

• Complete assignments independently.

• Seek help and ask questions, both in-class and outside of class as needed.
