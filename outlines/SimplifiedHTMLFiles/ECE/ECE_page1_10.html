Course Description

ECE 205:

Fourier series. Ordinary differential equations. Laplace transform. Applications to linear electrical systems.

View requirements for ECE 205

This course contains five main topics:

  • First order differential equations
  • Second order differential equations
  • Laplace transforms
  • Fourier series
  • Linear first and second order partial differential equations with constant coefficients.

 

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Solve first order differential equations by means of separation of variables and integrating factor
Solve second order both homogeoneous and non-homogeneous differential equations with constant coefficients
Understand the Laplace transform and use it to solve Initial Value Problems
Compute the Fourier series of the given function
Solve the linear heat and wave equations using method of separation of variables

Written Assignments: There will be 8 written assignments. The assignments will be posted on Learn page at least one week prior the due date. Assignments must be scanned and submitted electronically via Crowdmark. They are due at 9:00 PM EDT on the Fridays of the weeks listed in the course schedule. Late submissions will not be accepted, regardless of  circumstances. To account for short term absences, your lowest written assignment will be automatically dropped. This allows you to freely use your short term absence for your other classes. Written assignment problems must be completed without any outside help other than piazza posts and discussions with the instructor. 

 

Midterm Exam : The midterm examination will be held on Monday, February 24 from 12:30 to 1:45 PM. Information regarding the room and your seat will be distributed before the exam on Odyssey. This will be a closed book exam and no calculators allowed.

Missed Midterm assessment : There will be no make-up midterms. Any valid, documented, absences will result in the weight of the midterm being transferred to the final exam.

Final Exam: The Final exam will be scheduled by the RO during the Final Examination Period. This will be a closed book exam and no calculators allowed.

Administrative Policy

Short-term Absences: If you submit a short-term absence for an assignment, that assignment will automatically count as your dropped assignment. This policy is why we decided to drop the lowest assignment. If you miss the midterm exam and use your short-term absence, its weight will be shifted to the final exam. There will be no make-up midterm.

Verification of Illness Forms (VIF): If you submit a VIF and you wish to be exempt for an assessment, here is what will happen. You must first contact your instructor, notifying them that you have submitted a VIF. 

If you miss an assignment during the absence range, the weight from that assignment will be shifted to the other assignments. Explicitly, we will drop that assignment in addition to dropping your lowest assignment.

If you miss the midterm exam, its weight will be shifted to the final exam. There will be no make-up midterm.

Other accommodations are at the discretion of your instructor.

Generative AI

This course includes the independent development and practice of specific skills. Therefore, the use of Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) trained using large language models (LLM) or other methods to produce text, images, music, or code, like Chat GPT, DALL-E, or GitHub CoPilot, is not permitted in this class. Unauthorized use in this course, such as running course materials through GenAI or using GenAI to complete a course assessment is considered a violation of Policy 71 (plagiarism or unauthorized aids or assistance). Work produced with the assistance of AI tools does not represent the author’s original work and is therefore in violation of the fundamental values of academic integrity including honesty, trust, respect, fairness, responsibility and courage (ICAI, n.d.). 

You should be prepared to show your work. To demonstrate your learning, you should keep your rough notes, including research notes, brainstorming, and drafting notes. You may be asked to submit these notes along with earlier drafts of their work, either through saved drafts or saved versions of a document. If the use of GenAI is suspected where not permitted, you may be asked to meet with your instructor or TA to provide explanations to support the submitted material as being your original work. Through this process, if you have not sufficiently supported your work, academic misconduct allegations may be brought to the Associate Dean. 

In addition, you should be aware that the legal/copyright status of generative AI inputs and outputs is unclear. More information is available from the Copyright Advisory Committee: https://uwaterloo.ca/copyright-at-waterloo/teaching/generative-artificial-intelligence
