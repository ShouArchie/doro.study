Course Description

ECE 340:

Electronic circuits and their limitations including, metal-oxide-semiconductor (MOS) and bipolar junction transistor (BJT) differential pairs, biasing, the cascode configuration and active loads. Differential and multistage amplifiers. Feedback, stability, and compensation.

View requirements for ECE 340

Instructor's Description:

ECE 340 is the third core course in a three-course sequence (after ECE 140 and ECE 240) on electronic circuits. ECE 340 focusses on the analysis and design of transistor amplifiers using integrated circuit (IC) technology, where transistors and other electronic components are built together on a semiconductor chip. We study amplifiers, and their supporting circuits, because these are essential building blocks for many analog application-specific ICs (ASICs), including sensor interfaces, baseband-communication systems, power-delivery circuits, and data converters. We will study transistor amplifiers built using metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect transistors (MOSFETs) and bipolar junction transistors (BJTs).

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
Determine the gain and bandwidth of an op amp to achieve the desired frequency response.
Perform dc operating point and small-signal ac analyses of MOSFET and BJT circuits.
Analyze and design single-stage and multi-stage MOSFET and BJT amplifiers with passive and active loads, including cascodes.
Analyze and design current mirrors to set the dc operating point of an amplifier.
Analyze the frequency response of transistor amplifiers.
Perform feedback analysis of amplifiers.
Operate appropriate lab equipment to build, debug, and characterize basic electronic circuits.

Exam and Quiz Conditions:

  • Closed book and closed notes
  • Non-programmable, non-graphing scientific calculator is allowed
  • A formula sheet will be provided

Policy for Missed Quiz and Midterm Exam:

  • You may miss the quiz and/or midterm exam without academic penalty by declaring your absence according to University policies. In this case, the weight of the missed quiz and/or midterm exam will be added to the weight of the final exam. Consequently, calculation of your overall grade in the course may not follow the two grading schemes shown above. 
  • You will receive a grade of zero on the missed quiz and/or midterm exam if you do not declare your absence according to University policies.

Lab Policies:

  • Labs will be done by individual students (not in groups). Therefore, you need to bring your own ECE 240 lab kit to your session.
  • Lab manuals will be posted on LEARN several days before the Monday of each lab week.
  • Labs 2-5 have a "pre-lab" section which involves answering questions related to the lab content. Your pre-lab is due by 12:30 PM on Tuesday of each lab week, regardless of the day of your scheduled lab session.
  • All labs contain an "in-lab" section which involves building and measuring various circuits and reporting results. Your lab report is due by 1:30 PM one day after the day of your scheduled lab session.
  • You will have a scheduled quiz in some lab sessions. The lab instructor will provide more details about these in Lab 1.

Policy for Missed Labs: 

  • You may miss a lab without academic penalty by declaring your absence according to University policies. As a result, the weight of each missed lab will be added to the weight of the final exam. Please also inform the course instructor and lab instructor by email if you will be absent for a lab.
  • If you miss one lab without declaring your absence according to University policies, then you will receive a mark of zero for the lab you missed.
  • If you miss two or more labs without declaring your absence according to University policies, then you will (1) receive a mark of zero for each missed lab, and (2) your overall course grade will be reduced by 25%.

Problem Sets:

  • A new problem set will be posted on LEARN roughly every week.
  • Problem sets do not need to be submitted and are not graded.
  • Most problem sets will use the end-of-chapter problems from the required textbook.
  • Numerical solutions not included in the textbook will normally be provided upon release of the problem set. Worked-out solutions to selected problems will normally be provided about one week after the problem set is posted.
